LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Speedrunning, One Month In: 69 Runs and One Runaway Leader

Published: July 2, 2026 · 6 min read

Back in early June, we covered the moment the Speedrun.com leaderboards for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight came out of embargo with zero runs on the board and 68 followers watching. A month later, the board looks completely different. As of this week, the game's official stats page shows 69 total submitted runs from 16 active players, with 83 people now following the game's Speedrun.com page.

69 Total Runs Submitted
16 Active Runners
83 Community Followers

Full Game vs. Individual Levels

Of the 69 runs on the board, 23 are full-game completions and 46 are individual level (IL) runs, spread across the 18 level boards that were already set up when the leaderboards opened. That split lines up with what we predicted in our original coverage: IL runs have a much lower barrier to entry than a full-game attempt, so it makes sense that they'd outnumber complete runs roughly two to one this early in the game's competitive life. Combined, every run submitted so far adds up to 2 days, 20 hours, 23 minutes, and 55 seconds of recorded run time on the leaderboard — a genuinely large body of routing and execution work from a community that's only been racing for about four weeks.

Zero runs have been submitted in the Challenge category so far, which tracks with a young leaderboard — challenge modes tend to attract competitive attention later, once runners have exhausted the more straightforward Any% and IL routes.

Redniko's Hot Streak

One name currently dominates the recent activity feed: redniko, competing under the Valhalla flag. In the past several days alone, redniko set first-place times across multiple level boards in the "Story Solo, PC" category, including a 5-minute 16-second run on "The League of Shadows Returns," 3 minutes 9 seconds on "Tumbler Chase," 4 minutes flat on the "Joker" level, 1 minute 18 seconds on "The Penguin," and 2 minutes 51 seconds on "Mimes." Sweeping five separate level boards in a short window is an unusually strong showing, and it's currently redniko's name to beat across a meaningful chunk of the individual level leaderboard.

Why IL sweeps matter: A runner topping multiple individual level boards in the same category usually signals they've found or refined routes that others haven't matched yet. Expect other competitors to study redniko's runs and attempt to counter-route these specific levels in the coming weeks.

A Smaller Moderation Team

One quieter change since launch: the moderator roster has shrunk. When the leaderboards opened in June, six moderators were listed — super moderators chimkin and gustative, alongside CaptainPaxo, colten8, kwazrr, and Siedemnastek. The current stats page now lists five: chimkin and gustative remain as super moderators, joined by colten8, kwazrr, and Siedemnastek. CaptainPaxo no longer appears on the moderator list. Moderator turnover a month into a new game's leaderboard isn't unusual — verifying runs and settling category disputes is unpaid, time-intensive work — but it's worth noting for anyone submitting runs and wondering who's reviewing the queue.

What This Growth Actually Means

Sixteen active players and 69 runs is a modest number in absolute terms compared to leaderboards for years-old LEGO titles like LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga, but the trajectory is the more meaningful signal. The board went from a follower count with no runs to a functioning competitive scene — full-game category, 18 individual level boards, an active moderation team, and a runner already setting a pace across multiple levels — inside a single month. That's a healthy early growth curve for a game that launched May 22, 2026, and it suggests the leaderboard has staying power rather than being a one-week novelty that fizzles out.

The next real inflection point for the competitive scene will likely be the Mayhem Collection DLC, arriving September 18, 2026, with Joker and Harley Quinn as new playable characters and a new Arkham Asylum story mission. New playable characters historically mean new routing options and, often, entirely new categories — something worth watching for runners who want to get in early on a fresh leaderboard the way redniko and the current top runners did back in June.

How to Get Involved

If you've been sitting on a personal-best time without submitting it, now is a good moment — the board is active enough to have real competition, but still young enough that plenty of levels don't have a heavily optimized time locked in yet. Submissions go through Speedrun.com directly, require video proof, and go through the moderator queue described above before appearing on the board.
