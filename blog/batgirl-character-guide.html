Batgirl in LEGO Batman Legacy: Unlock, Gadgets & Why She's Essential for 100%

Published: July 13, 2026 · 6 min read

Batgirl is one of the most requested characters to unlock in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, and unlike some of the Bat-Family, she doesn't make you work for it — no side quest, no hidden collectible, no purchase. She also happens to be the character the game quietly needs you to use if you're chasing full completion, since her hacking kit is tied directly to revealing collectibles across Gotham. This guide covers exactly when Barbara Gordon joins your roster, what her gadgets actually do, how she stacks up against the rest of the seven-character lineup, and who voices her.

How to Unlock Batgirl

Batgirl becomes playable at the very start of Chapter 4: Knight at the Museum. There's no side quest to complete, no collectible to find, and no purchase required — reach Chapter 4 in the main story and she's automatically added to your roster. The mission itself opens with Batman and Batgirl teaming up at the Gotham City Museum to rescue partygoers caught in a Firefly arson attack, and from that point on she's available for both story missions and Free Play.

Tip: If you want to reach Batgirl faster, stick to main story missions on your first run and save open-world collectible sweeps for after Chapter 4 — her hacking kit makes those sweeps significantly faster anyway.

Batgirl's Gadgets & Abilities

Batgirl's loadout is the most technology-focused on the roster, and it's flavored by her backstory: unlike most of the Bat-Family, her in-game character description frames her as a self-taught hero who built her own costume and gear, making her the team's de facto tech support.

💻 Hackarang

Puzzle-Solving + Combat

A tech-enhanced batarang that interfaces with electronic panels, overloads enemy tech, and distracts robotic or electronic enemies. It's the gadget behind most of Batgirl's terminal-hacking puzzle solutions.

🔁 Drone

Traversal + Puzzle-Solving

Deploys a flying drone that scans areas from above, follows arrow-marked drone paths, and reaches switches other characters can't get to. Batgirl is also required to interact with district radio towers, which populate the map with all nearby activities and collectibles once hacked.

🎯 Focus Blast

Ranged Combat

A ranged attack option that gives Batgirl a way to deal with enemies from a distance rather than closing to melee range, according to GAMES.GG's breakdown of her kit. Combined with the Drone, it makes her one of the few roster members built around not engaging enemies head-on.

Why Batgirl Is Essential for 100% Completion

Unlike Nightwing or Robin, who mostly matter for specific traversal puzzles, Batgirl's Drone-hacked radio towers are effectively mandatory for full completion. Each district's tower reveals every nearby collectible on your map once hacked, which means players who skip using her end up manually hunting for content that a five-minute tower sweep would have marked automatically. If you're planning an open-world completion pass, the recommended order is to unlock Batgirl at Chapter 4, then immediately hack the nearest tower before doing anything else in that district.

Batgirl vs. the Rest of the Roster

Dexerto's character tier list for the game places Batgirl in A-Tier, alongside Nightwing — one rung below Batman and Talia Al Ghul in S-Tier, and one above Catwoman and Robin in B-Tier, with Jim Gordon alone in C-Tier. The reasoning tracks with her kit: she's not built to out-fight Batman in melee, but her ranged Focus Blast and Drone give her options that pure brawlers on the roster simply don't have, and her hacking is the closest thing the game has to a mandatory traversal tool.

Full roster context: Batgirl joins a seven-character playable lineup that also includes Batman, Jim Gordon, Catwoman, Robin, Nightwing, and Talia Al Ghul. For where each character unlocks, see our All Characters & Unlocks Guide, and for how Nightwing — the other A-Tier pick — compares, check our Nightwing Character Guide.

Who Voices Batgirl?

Batgirl / Barbara Gordon is voiced by Savannah Beckford, according to cast listings compiled by Behind The Voice Actors — marking her first video game voice role after prior on-screen work including an episode of The Buccaneers.

Practical Tips for Using Batgirl

  • Hack towers the moment you get her. Each district's radio tower reveals all nearby collectibles once hacked with the Drone — do this before manually hunting for Riddler trophies or WayneTech Caches.
  • Use the Hackarang on electronic enemies and terminals. It's her primary tool for both combat against robotic foes and environmental puzzles gated behind panels.
  • Lean on Focus Blast to avoid melee. If you'd rather not close the distance on tougher enemies, Batgirl's ranged option is one of the few on the roster built for that playstyle.
  • Upgrade her early. Her base stats are solid, but her hacking and ranged tools scale noticeably once you invest skill points in her upgrade tree, according to GAMES.GG's guide.

Verdict: Is Batgirl Worth Using?

Batgirl isn't optional in the way some roster members are. Her tech-focused kit — Hackarang, Drone, and Focus Blast — makes her the strongest pick for puzzle-heavy sections and any content gated behind hacking, and her tower-scanning ability is close to mandatory if you're aiming for full open-world completion. Pair her with a melee-focused character like Robin or Nightwing in co-op, and you cover most situations the game throws at you.
