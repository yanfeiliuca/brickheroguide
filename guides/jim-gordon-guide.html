Jim Gordon unlocks at the start of Chapter 1 and is immediately underestimated by most players. A man who brought a glorified can of expanding foam to a gunfight looks unimpressive next to Batman's gadgets. But once his Rebound Launcher is upgraded, Gordon transforms into the best character in the game for clearing groups — his bouncing bolts chain between every enemy in an area with minimal aiming required. This guide covers his full upgrade path and the situations where he genuinely outperforms every other character.

Who Is Jim Gordon in This Game?

Gordon is your second active character during Chapters 1 and 2, before Catwoman takes that spot. He stays in your roster for the entire game and is particularly strong in:

  • AR Combat Challenges — his Rebound Launcher's chain-hit mechanics trivialize multi-enemy challenges
  • Crowd control — Foam Sprayer traps slow groups and create space for Batman to finish them
  • The Dodgeball Batcave challenge — only he can complete it
Hidden strength: Jim Gordon has the widest array of area-of-effect and multi-enemy targeting damage in the whole roster. Players who skip his upgrades miss one of the most powerful crowd-clear tools in the game.

Foam Sprayer — Full Upgrade Tree

The Foam Sprayer is Gordon's primary weapon. It fires globules of expanding foam that immobilize enemies on contact. Raw damage is low, but crowd control value is high — especially once upgraded.

Rebound Launcher — Full Upgrade Tree

This is the upgrade tree that makes Jim Gordon exceptional. The Rebound Launcher fires bolts that bounce between enemies on contact. Upgraded, these bolts split and chain repeatedly, hitting every target in a room from a single shot.

UpgradeCostEffectPriority
Splitter6 chipsEach bolt splits into two when it bounces — effectively doubles the number of targets hit per shotBuy First
Rebound Power4 chipsIncreases the power and number of bounces per bolt — more bounces means more total hitsBuy First
Scope6 chipsAdds precision targeting scope — more useful in open-world encounters than indoor missionsLater
Focus Shot12 chipsSupercharged bolt that chains between multiple enemies in sequence — Gordon's best single-use abilityLate Game
Why Splitter + Rebound Power is so powerful: A single Rebound Launcher shot with both upgrades active can bounce 4–6 times, splitting at each bounce. In a room of 8 enemies, one shot can hit all of them. This makes Gordon the best AR challenge character in the game — challenges that require you to defeat waves of mixed enemies become trivial with a fully upgraded Rebound Launcher.

Best Build: Priority Upgrade Order

Here is the recommended spend sequence for Gordon, assuming you have already prioritized Batman's core upgrades:

  1. Rebound Power (4 chips) — cheapest meaningful upgrade; buy immediately when Gordon joins in Chapter 1
  2. Splitter (6 chips) — doubles effective hits; the two Rebound upgrades together cost only 10 chips and transform Gordon's output
  3. Bigger Clip (6 chips) — more sustained foam output before reload pauses
  4. Goop Trail (6 chips) — passive crowd control; buy when you start doing open-world crime events
  5. Surrounding Splat (4 chips) — situationally useful, cheap
  6. Focus Shot (12 chips) — buy when you have excess chips in Phase 3
  7. Focus Splat (12 chips) — last, as it is less versatile than Focus Shot
  8. Scope (6 chips) — skip or buy last; the bouncing mechanic works fine without precision aiming

Total minimum investment for a powerful Gordon: just 10 chips (Rebound Power + Splitter). That is the best value-per-chip ratio of any character in the game.

Jim Gordon in AR Combat Challenges

AR (Augmented Reality) Combat Challenges are timed wave-clearing events found throughout Gotham City and in the Batcave training area. They reward Gold Bricks and Studs for completion, with bonus rewards for clearing waves under par time.

Once Gordon's Rebound Launcher is upgraded with Splitter and Rebound Power, his approach to every AR challenge becomes the same:

  1. Let enemies group together — do not engage immediately
  2. Fire one Rebound Launcher bolt into the centre of the group
  3. Watch it chain and split between every enemy in the area
  4. Clean up any survivors with Foam Sprayer
  5. Repeat for the next wave
Mixed enemy type challenges: The Rebound Launcher's bounce mechanic ignores enemy type — it hits standard enemies, armoured enemies, and shield-carriers with equal effectiveness. Gordon handles mixed groups better than any character whose attacks are blocked by shields.

Best Co-Op Pairings

⭐ Gordon + Batman (Best Overall)

  • Batman handles stealth and single targets
  • Gordon clears groups and suppresses crowds with foam traps
  • Covers every combat scenario
  • Best pairing for Chapters 1–2

Gordon + Catwoman

  • Catwoman's Call Kitty distracts enemies
  • Gordon walks in and fires into the distracted group
  • Excellent for stealth-optional missions
  • Good for open-world crime events

Gordon is a weak solo pick for stealth missions — his Foam Sprayer makes noise and his kit lacks traversal tools. He shines in open combat, which makes Batman, Nightwing, or Catwoman the best partner choices depending on whether your current mission leans toward stealth or open confrontation.

Jim Gordon's Batcave Challenges

Gordon is the required character for two Batcave Mural challenges:

  • Dodgeball — Foam Spray an enemy until they curl into a ball, then throw them at other enemies. Works in any open combat area with 3+ enemies.
  • Bouncer (partial) — Technically a Nightwing challenge (Rebound Bolt), but Gordon's similar mechanic means players sometimes confuse the two. Make sure you are using Nightwing's Rebound Launcher for this one, not Gordon's.
