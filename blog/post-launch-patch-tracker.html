Post-Launch Patch Tracker: Every Fix in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight

📅 June 1, 2026  |  ⏱ 6 min read  |  BrickHeroGuide

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight launched to near-universal acclaim on May 22, 2026, and for most players the experience has been smooth and joyful. But no open-world game ships without rough edges, and TT Games has already begun rolling out fixes in the first weeks post-launch. At the same time, the Steam community and speedrunning community have catalogued a handful of bugs that are still outstanding as of June 2026.

This page tracks every confirmed patch, every known community-reported bug, and the current fix status — so you know exactly what's been addressed and what to watch out for on your playthrough.

📌 Last Updated: June 1, 2026. This tracker will be updated as TT Games releases new patches. Bookmark it if you're mid-playthrough and want to know whether a specific bug has been resolved.

Patch History

Since launch, TT Games has pushed two confirmed updates to all platforms. Here's what each one addressed.

Build 23314029 — Pre-Load Patch
May 21, 2026  ·  All Platforms

Pre-Launch Stability Prep

  • Silent infrastructure patch pushed ahead of the May 22 general release
  • No official patch notes published; depot file changes logged on SteamDB
  • Likely addressed pre-load configuration and initial server readiness
Update 1.005 (1.005.000) — Day 1 Patch
May 22, 2026  ·  PS5 / Xbox Series X|S / PC

Quality of Life & Crash Fixes

  • General stability improvements across all platforms
  • Gameplay performance optimizations
  • Addressed multiple crash-to-desktop scenarios
  • Other minor tweaks (specifics not detailed in official notes)
Unnamed Patch — Suits Progress Fix
Late May 2026  ·  All Platforms (reported)

100% Completion Counter Correction

  • Fixed a bug where players with 100 out of 101 suits unlocked displayed an incorrect progress percentage of 99.8% instead of the correct value
  • Players affected by this bug should now see accurate progress on the suits collection screen
  • Reported and confirmed by JayShockblast on X: "If you had 100/101 suits and progress was at 99.8, it is now correct. I am sure there are plenty of other fixes."
Update 1.006 (1.006.000) — Stability & Performance
June 2, 2026  ·  PS5 / Xbox Series X|S / PC

Stuttering, Crashes & Performance Fixes

  • Added general stability fixes
  • Added gameplay performance improvements
  • Addressed game stuttering issues (particularly open-world traversal in densely packed Gotham districts)
  • Fixed game loading crashes (most reported on Xbox Series X|S during area transitions)
  • Addressed in-gameplay crashing issues
  • Other minor tweaks (full changelog from TT Games pending)

⚠ 100% Completion Warning: The disappearing Tricorner world chest at coordinates 139/181 is the most serious outstanding bug for completionists. Players have reported it as the final item needed for open-world 100%. If you're affected, hold off on that area until a patch is confirmed — re-entering the chapter after a game restart may restore the chest for some users, according to community reports.

Workaround for the Character Input Swap Bug

The most frequently reported gameplay-disrupting bug (outside of the chest issues) is the character input swap disappearing on PC. This affects left-handed players and custom control configurations especially, since Tab is the default switch key and may not work in all setups. Here's how players have been resolving it:

  • Open the in-game settings menu
  • Navigate to Controls
  • Select "Reset to Default"
  • Re-apply your preferred input changes
  • The input prompt should reappear for the current session

This is a session-level workaround only — the bug may recur after a few hours of play. TT Games has not issued a fix for this issue as of June 1, 2026.

What's Still Needed

The overall bug picture is relatively mild for a major open-world launch. The Day 1 patch addressed crash stability, and the suits progress fix came quickly — both are signs that TT Games is actively monitoring feedback. The more persistent issues (disappearing chests, wonder chest soft-locks) affect a smaller subset of players but are particularly frustrating for those pursuing 100% completion.

Looking ahead, the next major content update will be the Mayhem Collection DLC, slated for September 2026 as part of the Deluxe Edition. That update will introduce Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters with their own story mission. It's likely that a broader bug-fix pass will accompany that release, which is standard practice for LEGO game post-launch support.

📋 About the Nintendo Switch 2 Version: The Switch 2 version of the game is planned for a later 2026 release and has not yet launched. Bug reports on this page pertain to the PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC versions only.

How to Report Bugs to TT Games

If you've encountered a bug not listed here, TT Games accepts player reports through the Steam Discussions board for PC players, and through official support channels linked on the official game site. Including your platform, game version, and a brief description of the steps to reproduce will help the team prioritize fixes.

We'll keep this page updated as new patches drop. If you've confirmed a fix that isn't reflected here, or found a workaround for an open bug, let us know through the contact page.
