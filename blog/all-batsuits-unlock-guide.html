All Suits in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — Complete Unlock Guide

Published: June 16, 2026 · 7 min read

With over 63 suits and outfits in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — not counting DLC — there’s a staggering amount of costume variety to collect across all seven playable characters. From Batman’s Golden Age grey-and-blue to the Zur-En-Arrh purple-and-yellow, and from Catwoman’s 1966 TV series look to Nightwing’s Spyral spy disguise, the game’s wardrobe doubles as a love letter to 86 years of Batman in every medium.

This guide compiles every confirmed base-game suit for all seven characters, organized by character with their specific unlock requirements, based on information from GameRant’s post-launch suit database. DLC outfits from the Legacy Collection (Arkham Trilogy, Batman Beyond, and Party Music packs) are covered in a separate section below.

How to Change Suits

Changing your character’s outfit is available any time you’re in the open world and not inside a vehicle. Here’s the exact button sequence:

  1. Press Start / Options to open the pause menu.
  2. Press R1 (PS5) / RB (Xbox) to navigate to the Characters tab.
  3. Press Square (PS5) / X (Xbox) to open Suit Options.
  4. Select any unlocked suit to equip it instantly — no load screen required.
Quick tip: You can switch suits freely during free-roam sections of Gotham City without returning to the Batcave. This also applies to Legacy Collection DLC suits once they’re registered to your account.

How to Unlock Suits

Suits are earned through a variety of in-game activities — there’s no single grind path, which means natural story progression will unlock a good chunk automatically. Here’s every unlock method:

  • Complete Main Story Missions — Each chapter completion rewards specific suits for multiple characters simultaneously.
  • Collect Lost Zoo Animals — Scattered across Gotham, finding these unlocks several Batman and Batgirl suits at milestone counts (3, 9, 15 animals).
  • Bat-Mite’s Batcave Shop — Spend Studs to purchase suits directly. Prices range from 30,000 to 55,000 Studs per outfit.
  • AR Trials — Complete Augmented Reality challenge courses to earn suits at 2, 5, and 15 trial milestones.
  • Riddler & Cluemaster Puzzle Rooms — Solve all 61 Riddler Puzzles or all 60 Cluemaster Puzzles for specific suits.
  • WayneTech Caches — Unlock scattered caches across Gotham at 10, 30, 50, 70, 80, 90, 110, 140, 150, 160, and 180 cache milestones for various suits.
  • Wanted Poster Activities — Complete 2 or 4 Wanted activities for specific outfits.
  • Falcone Missions — Complete 3 or 9 Falcone side missions for Catwoman costumes.
  • DLC / Pre-Order — Several suits require purchasing the Deluxe Edition or connecting accounts.

Efficient Unlock Strategy

If you want to maximize your wardrobe without grinding endlessly, here’s a sensible priority order. Story progression is the most efficient starting point — simply completing the main campaign unlocks the majority of suits for Batman, Catwoman, Robin, Batgirl, and Nightwing without any extra effort. WayneTech Caches are the biggest investment for Talia, who gates most of her suits behind high cache counts (up to 180). If you’re planning a completionist run, check our 100% Completion Guide for the optimal cache collection route through Gotham.

For Studs-based purchases, the Bat-Mite shop items range from 30,000 to 55,000 Studs per suit. If you’re low on Studs, see our fast Stud farming guide — the Batcave Cache route and Joker’s Funhouse Free Play can generate enough to clear the Stud shop in a single session.

The Riddler and Cluemaster puzzles are the most time-intensive unlocks — both require completing their full sets (61 and 60 puzzles respectively) for a single Batman suit each. These are long-term goals worth saving for a dedicated collectibles pass rather than chasing mid-playthrough.

Mayhem Collection (September 2026): The upcoming Mayhem Collection DLC will add Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, each with their own gadgets and suit options. Deluxe Edition owners get it included; standard edition players can purchase it separately when it launches on September 18, 2026 alongside the Nintendo Switch 2 version.
