LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight features the largest open-world Gotham City ever built for a TT Games title. Built on Unreal Engine 5, the city is more vertically layered and district-dense than any previous LEGO open world. This guide covers every island, traversal method, fast travel system, activity type, and navigation strategy for clearing Gotham efficiently.

Scale & Layout

Gotham in Legacy of the Dark Knight is split across four distinct islands connected by bridges and the SubWayne underground transit system. The city is built to feel genuinely metropolitan — tall towers cluster in Central Island's business district, industrial infrastructure dominates Tricorner's waterfront, residential blocks stack vertically across South Island, and North Island's wealthier neighborhoods slope upward toward Arkham Asylum on its northern peninsula.

The UE5 engine enables draw distances and environmental detail that make navigating from rooftop height practical — you can spot activity icons, vehicle paths, and terrain features from altitude in a way that was not possible in older LEGO games. Developing a habit of climbing high before exploring each new area pays off significantly.

The Four Islands of Gotham

Tricorner Island — Industrial South

The southernmost island, dominated by the Gotham docks, warehouses, and heavy industry. The Red Hood Gang and Falcone crime family have strong presences here during Chapters 1–2, making it the densest combat district early in the game. Side activities include waterfront zoo animal rescues (animals from the Gotham Zoo have escaped onto the docks), several Riddler Puzzle Rooms hidden in warehouse interiors, and AR Trial terminals on elevated crane structures. The SubWayne station here is one of the easier unlock puzzles in the game — a short Batgirl terminal sequence on the platform level.

South Island — Residential & Commercial

The central-south borough covering Gotham's residential neighbourhoods, markets, and mid-tier commercial strips. This island has the highest density of Riddler Puzzle Rooms of any district and multiple Cluemaster investigation sequences that require Gordon's evidence-gathering ability. The SubWayne network runs through South Island most extensively — three of the nine stations are here, making it the easiest island to fast-travel within once unlocked. The Flugelheim Museum, setting for Chapter 2's Joker confrontation, is on South Island's eastern waterfront.

Central Island — The Heart of Gotham

The main business district — skyscrapers, the Gotham City Police Department, Wayne Enterprises tower, and the densest pedestrian environment in the game. Most of the story's major set pieces occur here. Central Island has the tallest buildings in Gotham, making it both the most impressive district to traverse and the one with the most rooftop collectibles. Batgirl's towers here reveal the largest collectible count of any island. AR Trials on this island are the most technically demanding — the obstacle courses use the district's vertical geometry extensively.

North Island — Wealthy Heights & Arkham

The wealthiest borough, with Wayne Manor visible on the northern slopes (accessible via the Batcave tunnel rather than the open world directly). Arkham Asylum occupies the northern peninsula and is the setting for Chapter 5. North Island has fewer side activities than the southern islands but higher individual reward per activity — the Villain Encounters here drop more Gold Bricks than anywhere else in the open world. The Gotham Zoo (whose animals escape across all four islands) is located here, making North Island the origin point for the zoo rescue activity chains.

Traversal Systems

Three primary traversal methods cover different movement needs:

  • Grapple Hook — Batman and several other characters fire a grapple to any anchor point (yellow rings on ledges, rooftops, and overhead structures). This is the primary vertical movement tool. Grapple range upgrades in the WayneTech tree directly extend how far the hook reaches, opening higher ledges and bridging longer gaps. Investing in grapple range early is the single most impactful upgrade for open-world navigation.
  • Cape Glide — Jumping from any height and holding the glide button deploys Batman's cape. Gliding from rooftop level lets you cover entire city blocks without touching the ground, crossing between islands via bridge level or scanning rooftop collectibles from altitude. Glide speed and distance improve with traversal upgrades.
  • Batmobile & Vehicles — The Batmobile and other unlocked vehicles handle street-level travel at high speed. The Batmobile is particularly effective in Central Island's wide avenues. Vehicle traversal does not replace grapple and glide for collectible hunting — most items are elevated — but dramatically speeds up travel between distant SubWayne stations during the open-world sweep.
Traversal tip: The fastest route across Gotham is grapple → glide → grapple again at the lowest safe altitude. Maintaining altitude by grappling to a new anchor just before the glide descent stalls lets you cross entire districts without touching ground. Practice this rhythm in Chapter 1's open world sections before Gotham fully opens.

SubWayne Fast Travel Stations

Nine SubWayne stations are distributed across all four islands. Each must be unlocked individually by solving a short environmental puzzle at the station entrance — typically a Batgirl terminal sequence, a grapple-and-switch mechanism, or a destructible barrier cleared with explosives. Unlocked stations appear on the map and can be instantly fast-travelled between from the pause menu.

Station distribution by island: two on Tricorner, three on South Island, two on Central Island, two on North Island. Unlocking all nine is required for a trophy and contributes toward the overall completion percentage. Community consensus (from r/legogaming) is that South Island's three stations should be prioritised first due to their central position making every other island faster to reach from there.

Unlock stations before sweeping. Each unlocked SubWayne station makes all future open-world work faster. Dedicate 20–30 minutes at the start of your open-world phase to unlocking every station before beginning district sweeps.

Batgirl Towers

Each district contains at least one Batgirl-hackable tower. Reaching the summit via grapple or glide and completing Batgirl's terminal hack reveals all nearby collectibles, Riddler Puzzle Rooms, zoo animals, and AR Trials in that district on the minimap — turning a blind search into a checkbox sweep.

Tower activation is the single highest-value action at the start of any district sweep. Without it you are effectively searching blind; with it every remaining activity in the district is pinpointed. Batgirl must be in your party for the hack — she does not need to be the active character during traversal, just available for the terminal interaction.

Some towers require traversal upgrades to reach their summits. If a tower looks inaccessible, check whether a grapple range or wall-run upgrade would open the route before looking for an alternate path.

Side Activity Types

Each district contains a mix of the following recurring activities:

  • Escaped Zoo Animals — Animals from Gotham Zoo have scattered across all four islands. Detective Vision highlights them through obstacles. Each returned animal earns a Gold Brick. Animals can appear on rooftops, in back alleys, on building ledges, and (on Tricorner) in warehouse interiors. This is the most commonly incomplete activity type at the end of a district sweep because animals in unusual locations are easy to overlook.
  • AR Trials — Timed traversal and combat challenges accessed via glowing blue AR terminals. Completing the trial earns a Gold Brick; beating a bonus time target earns additional Studs. Each district has 2–3 AR Trials. They range from pure grapple-and-glide courses to combat-wave challenges with environmental obstacles.
  • Riddler Puzzle Rooms — Green question mark entrances hidden throughout each district. These do not appear on the minimap until you are within close range, making them the most commonly missed activity. Each room contains an environmental logic puzzle that rewards a Gold Brick and occasionally a suit unlock. Use Detective Vision and walk each district slowly to trigger their proximity markers.
  • Villain Encounters — Scripted boss fights that trigger as you explore each district. These are marked on the map after tower activation and reward Gold Bricks plus a Batcave villain trophy on completion.
  • Cluemaster Investigations — Gordon-specific investigation chains where Detective Vision traces clue trails leading to a hidden Gold Brick cache. These require Gordon to be in your party and are the most narrative-rich side activities in the open world.
  • Vehicle Races — Batmobile and Batcycle time trials on marked street circuits. Completing each race earns a Gold Brick; beating the gold time earns a bonus. Central Island has the longest circuits due to its wide road layout.

District Sweep Strategy

The most efficient approach to clearing Gotham's open world:

  1. Unlock all SubWayne stations first (20–30 min) — eliminates travel overhead for the entire sweep
  2. Activate Batgirl's tower in each district before doing any activities — reveals the full activity list before you start
  3. Clear Villain Encounters first in each district — they are the largest Gold Brick rewards and often happen naturally while traversing between other objectives
  4. Do zoo animals and AR Trials next — both are map-marked after tower activation and straightforward to route efficiently
  5. Do Riddler Puzzle Rooms last with a slow walk-through — proximity-trigger only, so requires ground-level coverage of every street and alley
  6. Cluemaster Investigations anytime Gordon is in your party — complete them when the clue trail is nearby rather than making separate trips
Completion tracker: The map's district completion percentage is your authoritative guide. If a district still shows incomplete after you believe you have done everything, a Riddler Puzzle Room or zoo animal is the almost certain culprit. Use the slow walk-through method described above to find it.

Notable Locations & Landmarks

Wayne Enterprises Tower (Central Island)

The tallest building in Gotham. Reaching its summit rewards a Gold Brick and offers the best vantage point in the game for planning traversal routes across all four islands. The building's exterior has multiple grapple anchor stages — it is effectively a platforming challenge disguised as a landmark.

Gotham City Police Department (Central Island)

Gordon has special interactions at the GCPD that trigger Cluemaster investigation chains not available elsewhere. Visit with Gordon in your party after completing Chapter 1 to unlock these.

Flugelheim Museum (South Island)

The Chapter 2 Joker mission location. In the open world post-story, the museum is accessible and contains two Red Brick hiding spots that are missed by most players during the story run — one behind a Batgirl terminal inside, one on the roof via Nightwing wall-jump.

Gotham Zoo (North Island)

Returning zoo animals here is required for the zoo rescue Gold Bricks. The zoo itself has a small Riddler Puzzle Room at its eastern entrance that many players walk past without noticing because it faces away from the main path.

FAQ

Can I access all four islands from the start?

The full open world opens gradually during the story — Tricorner and parts of South Island are accessible from Chapter 1, Central Island expands through Chapters 2–3, and North Island including Arkham Asylum becomes fully explorable after Chapter 4. By the end of the story all four islands are open with no locked gates.

Do I need specific characters to complete every open-world activity?

Yes. Batgirl is required for tower activations and some Riddler Puzzle Room puzzles. Gordon is required for Cluemaster Investigations. Catwoman's lockpick opens certain side caches. Most activities are accessible to Batman alone, but 100% requires all characters at some point. See the Characters & Villains Guide for a full ability breakdown.

What is the fastest way to navigate between islands?

SubWayne fast travel is fastest for crossing multiple islands. For adjacent islands, the grapple-glide-grapple chain across the bridge structures is faster than driving. The Batmobile is fastest for pure street-level traversal within a single island but slower than glide for multi-island movement.
