5 Reasons to Start LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Now — Before the September DLC

Published: June 20, 2026 · 7 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has been out for just under a month, and the Mayhem Collection DLC — which adds The Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters — doesn't drop until September 18, 2026. If you've been sitting on the fence about picking up the game, that gap is actually working in your favor. Here's why the next three months are the ideal window to dive in.

Reason 1: The Base Game Is Excellent — and Long

It's easy to frame LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight as a game you buy for the DLC. Don't fall into that framing — the base game is a complete, content-rich experience that stands entirely on its own.

According to data aggregated on HowLongToBeat, the main story takes between 10 and 15 hours. An average playthrough with moderate side activity runs around 16 hours. Full 100% completion — collecting every Red Brick, unlocking all suits, clearing all Gotham side missions and Batmural Challenges — stretches to 25–50 hours depending on your pace.

The game's open-world Gotham is divided into four explorable islands, each with a distinct atmosphere. The story chapters weave between characters including Batman, Jim Gordon, Catwoman, Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, and Talia al Ghul, each bringing a different combat and puzzle style. There are 101 base suits to collect, dozens of WayneTech upgrades to research, and a dedicated Dark Knight difficulty mode for players who want a genuine challenge. This is not a short game padded out by DLC promises.

New to LEGO games? Check our Beginner's Guide for 10 essential tips covering difficulty modes, Detective Mode, Red Bricks, and how the WayneTech upgrade system works.

Reason 2: Update 1.006 Fixed the Rough Launch Edges

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight launched on May 22 with some notable performance issues — particularly PC stuttering during open-world traversal, game-loading crashes on certain hardware configurations, and inconsistent frame pacing. If you heard about those problems at launch and held off, that's an understandable call. The situation has improved substantially.

Update 1.006, released June 2, 2026, addressed the most-reported issues across all platforms. According to the official patch notes, the update delivered general stability fixes, gameplay performance improvements, stuttering corrections during Gotham open-world traversal, and fixes for crash conditions that some players encountered on initial loading. The June 2 patch is the most significant post-launch update so far. Additional smaller hotfixes targeting GPU-specific issues on PC had already landed in the days immediately following launch.

The upshot: if you buy the game today, you're getting a meaningfully more stable experience than the one reviewers played at launch. Most major outlets reviewed on a pre-release build; the launch-window performance criticisms largely no longer apply.

PC players: See our PC Requirements Guide for a full breakdown of the patch history, performance settings, and GPU-specific tips including the latest Update 1.006 PC patch notes.

Reason 3: The Deluxe Edition Upgrade Is Genuinely Good Value

If you're on the fence about whether to pick up the Standard or Deluxe Edition — or to upgrade if you already own Standard — the math has become cleaner now that the September 18 DLC date is officially confirmed.

The Deluxe Edition upgrade costs $24.99 on Steam, PS Store, Xbox Marketplace, and Epic Games Store. It unlocks two separate DLC bundles:

  • Legacy Collection (available immediately): 21 cosmetic suits across three themed packs — an Arkham Trilogy Pack, a Batman Beyond Pack, and a Party Music Pack — plus three Batmobiles and 15 Batcave decorative props.
  • Mayhem Collection (September 18, 2026): The Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters, a new Arkham Asylum breakout story mission, Mayhem Mode (an open villain sandbox for causing chaos across Gotham), the Sinister Pack of 7 villain-themed suits, and a new Batmobile.

According to Warner Bros. Games' June 3, 2026 press release, no standalone Mayhem Collection listing has been confirmed on any storefront. The Deluxe Edition upgrade is currently the only confirmed route to the September content for Standard Edition owners. At $24.99 for two content drops — one of which adds substantial new playable characters and a new game mode — the upgrade represents solid value for anyone who enjoys the base game.

The practical implication: buying in now and upgrading to Deluxe gets you the Legacy Collection suits immediately while you play through the story, and the Mayhem Collection waiting for you in September. The timing works out well.

Reason 4: The Community Is Hitting Its Stride

One month post-launch, LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has a thriving fan community that makes the experience richer.

On the speedrunning side, Speedrun.com opened official leaderboards on June 7, 2026, after a two-week post-launch embargo while the community established categories and rules. The game has attracted 13 active runners with 39 total runs submitted across full-game and individual-level categories, according to Speedrun.com stats. Watching world-record attempts is a genuinely fun way to discover shortcuts and sequence breaks in a game you're playing yourself.

On PC, the Nexus Mods community has been particularly active. By mid-June, the community had produced a cinematic reshade preset, a battle-damaged "The Dark Knight Returns" skin for Batman, an Arkham City Batman skin, and several costume packs. According to a roundup from our June Mod Roundup, the mod scene was already producing high-quality work within three weeks of launch. If you're on PC, launching the game today means you have access to all of that.

The broader player milestone is also worth noting: the game sold 1.2 million copies in its first week, per data reported by Alinea Analytics, making it one of the better-selling LEGO games on record. That commercial success strongly signals that TT Games will support the game with the planned DLC roadmap and additional patches going forward.

Reason 5: Three Months Is the Right Amount of Time

Here's a practical argument that's easy to overlook: September 18 is approximately 90 days from now. That's the right amount of time to play this game thoroughly before the Mayhem Collection arrives.

A story-only run takes 10–15 hours. A comfortable 100% completion run, working through Gotham's four islands at a relaxed pace, takes most players 30–40 hours. Spread that over three months — roughly 2.5 to 3 hours per week — and you arrive at September 18 having fully experienced the base game, unlocked Joker and Harley Quinn's confirmed villain-focused content fresh, and have a character roster and Gotham knowledge that makes the DLC's Arkham Asylum breakout story mission and Mayhem Mode immediately accessible.

Starting the game on, say, July or August means you'll likely be somewhere mid-game when September 18 arrives and the Mayhem Collection drops. The June-to-September window is, mathematically, the ideal entry point for the complete story experience.

The Case Against Waiting Until September

To be fair to the counter-argument: some players prefer to enter a game once all content is available, avoiding the situation where they reach the end of the base game and then wait months for more. That's a legitimate preference, particularly for players who tend to play a game intensely then move on.

The case against waiting is that LEGO games, by design, reward ongoing exploration more than binge sessions. The open-world loop in Legacy of the Dark Knight — finding collectibles in Gotham, chasing Batmural Challenges, gradually unlocking WayneTech upgrades for each character — is well-suited to playing a few sessions per week over an extended period rather than a concentrated 20-hour sprint. The structure of the game rewards the patient approach.

Additionally, if you're on PC and interested in mods, the mod ecosystem will be even richer by September. But the flip side is that the base game at full price plus a $24.99 upgrade will cost exactly the same in September as it does now. There's no financial incentive to wait.

The Bottom Line

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is a strong game that's been improved by post-launch patches, supported by a healthy community, and priced at a point where the Deluxe Edition upgrade represents genuine value. The Mayhem Collection's September 18 arrival creates a natural three-month window to play through the base game comfortably before new content lands.

For players who have been on the fence since launch, that window is open now. The game launched in a playable state, Update 1.006 improved it meaningfully, and the best version of this game — base game fully patched, mods available, community active — is available to play today.
