WayneTech Chips & Skill Bricks: Complete Currency Guide

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has three distinct currencies that power its progression system. Understanding how each one works — and where to spend them — is key to unlocking everything efficiently. This guide breaks down every currency, the best ways to earn them, and a recommended spending priority so you don't waste resources early on.

Currency System Overview

Here's a quick summary of the three currencies before we dive into detail:

⬤

Studs (Silver, Gold, Blue, Purple)

The classic LEGO currency. Dropped by breaking objects, defeating enemies, and completing missions. Used to purchase extras, vehicles, and Red Brick effects at the Batcave.

⚙

WayneTech Chips

Found hidden in levels and rewarded for completing mission challenges. Used to upgrade the Batcave facilities, unlock gadget enhancements, and expand your hub capabilities.

🧩

Skill Bricks

Earned through combat performance and special objectives. Used to unlock nodes on each character's individual skill tree, improving their abilities and unlocking new moves.

Studs: The Basic Currency

Studs are everywhere and they're the currency you'll interact with most. Every breakable object drops studs, and their color indicates their value:

  • Silver Studs: 10 each — the most common drop
  • Gold Studs: 100 each — found in specific objects and secret areas
  • Blue Studs: 1,000 each — rare, usually hidden or behind puzzles
  • Purple Studs: 10,000 each — extremely rare, typically 1-2 per level in well-hidden locations

Each mission has a "True Hero" stud threshold. Reaching it earns a gold brick and a bonus stud reward. Always aim for True Hero on your first playthrough — it's usually achievable by thoroughly smashing everything in sight.

Tip: Studs are also used as your "score" for each mission. Check the True Hero threshold in the pause menu — if you're close, spend an extra minute breaking objects before finishing the level.

What Studs Buy

  • Red Brick effects (multipliers, invincibility, collectible detectors)
  • Bonus vehicles for the Batcave display
  • Character costumes and cosmetic variants
  • Hints and map reveals from the Batcomputer

WayneTech Chips: Upgrade Currency

WayneTech Chips are the key to upgrading your Batcave and expanding your gameplay options. They're rarer than studs and require deliberate effort to collect.

Where to Find WayneTech Chips

  • Hidden in levels: Each story mission contains 3-5 hidden WayneTech Chips, often behind puzzles or in hard-to-reach areas
  • Mission challenges: Each mission has 3 optional challenges (displayed in the pause menu). Completing all 3 awards bonus chips
  • Batcave exploration: The Batcave hub itself contains WayneTech Chips that become accessible as you unlock new areas
  • Free Play rewards: Replaying missions in Free Play and finding all collectibles earns chip bonuses

What WayneTech Chips Upgrade

  • Vehicle Display (5 chips): Unlocks passive stud bonuses for every mission. Upgrade this first!
  • Gadget Workbench (8 chips): Enhances character gadgets — Batarang becomes multi-target, Whip extends range, etc.
  • Training Room (10 chips): Unlocks combo practice and new combat moves for all characters
  • Batcomputer Tier 2 (12 chips): Reveals collectible locations on the minimap during missions
  • Trophy Room (15 chips): Displays your progress and unlocks bonus challenge missions
  • Batcomputer Tier 3 (20 chips): Reveals ALL hidden objects and secrets during missions
Important: WayneTech Chips cannot be farmed infinitely. There is a fixed number in the game (approximately 120 total). Plan your spending carefully, especially early on.

Skill Bricks: Character Abilities

Each of the 7 playable characters has a unique skill tree with 12-15 nodes. Skill Bricks are the currency you spend to unlock these nodes and power up your characters.

How to Earn Skill Bricks

  • Combat performance: Achieving high combo counts (10+, 25+, 50+ hits) during missions awards Skill Bricks
  • Character-specific challenges: Each character has 5 personal challenges (check the character menu). Completing them awards bonus bricks for that character
  • Boss fights: Defeating bosses without taking damage awards extra Skill Bricks
  • Daily Batcave challenges: The Training Room offers rotating challenges once unlocked

Skill Tree Structure

Every character's skill tree has 3 branches:

  • Combat Branch: Improves attack damage, combo length, and unlocks new combat moves
  • Traversal Branch: Enhances movement abilities — longer glides, faster wall climbing, higher jumps
  • Special Branch: Upgrades the character's unique ability — better Detective Vision range for Batman, faster Tech Hack for Robin, etc.
Tip: Skill Bricks are character-specific. Bricks earned while playing as Batman can only be spent on Batman's tree. Switch characters regularly to level everyone up evenly.

Best Ways to Earn Each Currency

Studs: Fastest Earning Methods

  1. Stud Multiplier Red Bricks — By far the most impactful. Even a 2x multiplier doubles your income immediately
  2. Replay early missions — Mission 2 and Mission 4 are particularly stud-rich and quick to complete
  3. Break everything — Develop the habit of smashing every object you pass. Ambient studs add up fast
  4. True Hero bonuses — Hit the True Hero threshold on every mission for flat stud bonuses

WayneTech Chips: Collection Strategy

  1. Story first, chips second — Play through the story once, then revisit with full character roster in Free Play to reach all chip locations
  2. Complete all mission challenges — Check the pause menu for the 3 optional challenges per mission
  3. Explore the Batcave thoroughly — New areas unlock as the story progresses, each hiding 2-3 chips

Skill Bricks: Leveling Strategy

  1. Focus on one character at a time — Unlock their Special Branch first for the biggest gameplay impact
  2. Chase high combos — Use crowd-control-friendly characters (Batman, Nightwing) in crowded fights
  3. Don't skip personal challenges — 5 per character, each worth significant Skill Brick rewards

What to Spend On First (Priority Guide)

Early in the game, resources are tight. Here's the recommended spending order to maximize your progress:

WayneTech Chips — Priority Order

  1. Vehicle Display (5 chips) — Passive stud income boost pays for itself quickly
  2. Gadget Workbench (8 chips) — Enhanced gadgets make collectible hunting much easier
  3. Batcomputer Tier 2 (12 chips) — Minimap collectible markers save enormous time in Free Play
  4. Training Room (10 chips) — Unlocks daily challenges for steady Skill Brick income
  5. Everything else — Fill in the rest as you collect more chips in Free Play

Studs — Priority Order

  1. 2x Stud Multiplier Red Brick — First priority, always. Doubles all future stud income
  2. Collectible Detector Red Brick — Highlights nearby minikits and red bricks during gameplay
  3. 4x Stud Multiplier — Stacks with 2x for 8x total income
  4. Character costumes — Purely cosmetic, buy these last

Skill Bricks — Priority Order Per Character

  1. Special Branch (first 3 nodes) — Biggest immediate gameplay impact
  2. Traversal Branch (first 2 nodes) — Better movement makes every mission faster
  3. Combat Branch — Nice to have but not essential for progression

Stud Multiplier Red Bricks

The multiplier Red Bricks are the most powerful items in the entire game. They stack multiplicatively, meaning combining all five gives you a staggering 3,840x stud multiplier:

  • x2 Multiplier: Found in Mission 2 — cost to activate: 500,000 studs
  • x4 Multiplier: Found in Mission 5 — cost to activate: 1,000,000 studs
  • x6 Multiplier: Found in Mission 8 — cost to activate: 2,000,000 studs
  • x8 Multiplier: Found in Mission 10 — cost to activate: 4,000,000 studs
  • x10 Multiplier: Found in Mission 11 — cost to activate: 5,000,000 studs

Combined: 2 x 4 x 6 x 8 x 10 = 3,840x. With all five active, a single mission replay can net tens of millions of studs.

Pro Tip: Find and activate the x2 multiplier as early as possible. It halves the time to earn everything else. For the x2 location in Mission 2, see our Red Bricks Location Guide.

Best Farming Methods

Once you've unlocked stud multipliers and Free Play mode, here are the most efficient farming strategies:

Best Mission for Stud Farming

Mission 4: Arkham Breakout — This mission has the highest density of breakable objects and can be completed in about 8 minutes. With a x2 multiplier active, you can earn roughly 200,000 studs per run. With all multipliers, this skyrockets to over 300 million per run.

Best Method for Skill Brick Farming

Training Room Daily Challenges — Once unlocked, the Training Room offers 3 rotating combat challenges daily. Each awards 5-10 Skill Bricks depending on performance. The "Combo Challenge" is the most efficient: aim for 50+ hit combos using Nightwing (his Escrima Sticks chain quickly).

Batcave WayneTech Chip Sweep

After completing the story, revisit the Batcave and explore every corner. New passages, rooms, and hidden areas unlock as you progress. A thorough Batcave sweep typically yields 15-20 additional WayneTech Chips you may have missed.
