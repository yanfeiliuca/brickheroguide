Gotham City in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is divided into 9 districts, each with its own tower to activate and its own collectible pool. Activating a tower immediately reveals every WayneTech Cache, collectible, and side activity in that district on your map — making tower activation the single highest-value action you can take when starting open-world exploration. This guide covers all 9 districts, how to activate each tower, and the most efficient order to sweep Gotham clean.

How Towers Work

Each district contains one Activity Tower — a large antenna or signal tower visible from a distance. Activating a tower triggers a hacking minigame, after which every collectible icon in that district appears on your map automatically.

What towers reveal: WayneTech Cache locations, Red Brick locations, Gold Brick locations, crime event zones, AR Challenge sites, and Stud Cache positions. Bat-Symbol locations are the only collectible type that towers do not reveal — you need to find those manually or use Detective Mode.

Tower activation does not count as a collectible or completion percentage item. Hacking all 9 towers is not tied to any trophy or achievement — but it cuts your open-world collectible hunting time by 80% or more by eliminating all blind searching.

Tower Activation Requirements

You cannot interact with any district tower until Batgirl joins your roster in Chapter 4. Her Hackarang gadget is required to trigger the hacking minigame. The minigame itself is similar to the electrical puzzles in story missions — connect the circuit correctly within the time limit.

Fast unlock tip: The Security Bypass upgrade for Batgirl (3 chips — the cheapest upgrade in the game) reduces tower hacking time significantly. Buy it immediately when Batgirl unlocks and activate all 9 towers before doing anything else in the open world.

All 9 Districts Overview

District 1
Old Gotham South
Island: Old GothamStory: Early Access

The oldest part of the city and the one you explore most during the main story. Dense architecture makes traversal interesting — lots of rooftop hopping and narrow alleyways. Home to the Monarch Theater (needed for the Theatrical Batcave challenge) and several early-game crime event zones.

Tower tip: The tower is on a mid-height rooftop in the western section. Grapple up from street level.
District 2
Old Gotham North
Island: Old GothamStory: Early Access

Wealthier area bordering Wayne Tower. Contains several hackable terminal puzzles guarding WayneTech Caches — bring Batgirl for efficient cache clearing. Gentleman Ghost can appear here as a random encounter (needed for the Paranormal Investigator Batcave challenge).

Tower tip: Located near the northern docks. Accessible via grapple from the adjacent waterfront warehouse.
District 3
Old Gotham West
Island: Old GothamNotable: Wayne Tower

Home to Wayne Tower, the tallest structure in Gotham — and the target of the "The High Life" Batcave challenge. The jetstream surrounding Wayne Tower's upper floors is the easiest way to stay airborne for the "Floor Is Lava" challenge (60 seconds of gliding).

Tower tip: The Wayne Tower area has the most height in this district — the tower itself is nearby. Use the Wayne Tower jetstream to reach the antenna height.
District 4
The Cauldron South
Island: The CauldronEnemy Density: High

Industrial district with warehouses, docks, and factories. Extremely high density of breakable objects — ideal for the Demolisher challenge (60 objects in one minute). Multiple vehicle-accessible areas where the Batmobile can be driven through structures for rapid stud farming.

Tower tip: Located on a factory rooftop. Accessible by grappling up the south face of the main warehouse complex.
District 5
The Cauldron North (Ace Chemicals)
Island: The CauldronStory: Mission Location

The most story-significant district in the open world. Ace Chemicals occupies the northern section and is the setting for several key story missions. Post-story, it contains a dense concentration of WayneTech Caches including several puzzle caches requiring power cell solutions.

Tower tip: On top of the Ace Chemicals main structure. Enter via the side service ladder and grapple to the roof antenna.
District 6
Gotham Village (Robinson Park)
Island: Gotham VillageTerrain: Open / Parkland

The greenest district in Gotham — Robinson Park and surrounding residential areas. More open terrain than the other districts means fewer grapple points but easier vehicle navigation. Contains several zoo animal side quests that reward Gold Bricks.

Tower tip: Freestanding tower on the park's eastern edge. No climbing required — the base has a direct interaction point at ground level after the hacking minigame.
District 7
Newtown
Island: NewtownDifficulty: Medium

A mid-density commercial district with a mix of ground-level and rooftop cache locations. Several caches here require Nightwing's electric cable to connect power sources — bring him for efficient clearing. Newtown has a high concentration of crime events relative to its size.

Tower tip: Located centrally on a shopping mall rooftop. Easily reachable by grappling from the adjacent multi-story car park.
District 8
East End (Amusement Mile)
Island: East EndNotable: Tricorner Bridge

Home to Amusement Mile — the carnival-themed entertainment district that appears in several story missions. The Tricorner bridge connecting this island to Old Gotham South is the longest straight road in the game, ideal for the Turbo Batcave challenge (30 seconds of uninterrupted boost).

Tower tip: On top of the Amusement Mile Ferris wheel structure. Requires multiple grapple points up the ride structure.
District 9
Tricorner
Island: TricornerNote: Known Bug

The southernmost district and the last one most players reach. Tricorner has a known bug in the June 2026 patch (1.006) where one world chest may disappear after loading a save — if you encounter this, reload the auto-save from before entering the district or check for a patch update.

Tower tip: Coastal location on the southern tip of the island. Directly accessible from the main road — the tower's base has an interaction point at street level.

Recommended Exploration Order

The most efficient order for tower activation and district sweeping minimises fast-travel time by working island by island rather than jumping between them:

  1. Old Gotham South → North → West (one island, three districts, covers the story-relevant area first)
  2. Cauldron South → North (second island, highest cache density in the game)
  3. Gotham Village (single-district island, quick to clear)
  4. Newtown (single-district island)
  5. East End → Tricorner (last island, includes the Amusement Mile missions)
Do all towers before collecting anything. Activate all 9 towers first during one dedicated session, then return for systematic collection sweeps. Seeing all icons at once is far more efficient than activating towers one at a time and partially clearing each district.

Efficient Collection Strategy

  • Use your Sense ability constantly — it highlights WayneTech Caches with an audio ping that grows louder as you approach, even before tower activation
  • Bring Catwoman for lockpick caches, Batgirl for hacking caches, Nightwing for electric caches — knowing which character you need before entering a district saves switching time
  • Vehicle + missile for breakable environment objects — in industrial areas, the Batmobile's missiles break larger structures that hands cannot
  • Follow power cables from locked caches — use Detective Mode or Sense to see cables running from a locked cache to its power source; the cable leads you directly to the solution
  • Do one island completely before moving — the mental overhead of tracking partial completion across multiple islands slows you down significantly
