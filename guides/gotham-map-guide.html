LEGO Batman Legacy of the Dark Knight -- Open-World Gotham City Overview

Last updated: May 28, 2026 · 5 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight features the largest open-world Gotham City ever built for a TT Games title. The game launched May 22, 2026, and players have now fully explored its open world — here's what we know about navigating every corner of Gotham.

The Biggest Gotham Yet

Multiple previews have confirmed that this Gotham City is larger than any open world in a previous TT Games title. The move to Unreal Engine 5 has allowed the developers to create a denser, more vertically layered city than was possible in older LEGO games.

Traversal: Grappling, Gliding, and the Batmobile

Reviews confirm three primary traversal systems:

  • Grappling Hook: Batman and certain other characters can grapple to ledges, rooftops, and anchor points throughout the city. This is the primary vertical movement tool.
  • Gliding: The cape glide returns, allowing players to soar between buildings. The UE5 engine enables longer draw distances, making navigation-from-above more practical.
  • Batmobile: Drivable vehicles including the Batmobile let you traverse streets at high speed. The Batcave serves as the central hub where vehicles are stored and customized.

The Batcave: Your Central Hub

The Batcave serves as the game's hub area, a confirmed feature where players can customize vehicles, switch between Batsuits, view collected trophies, and access missions. The cave acts as the connective tissue between story missions and open-world exploration.

City Layout: The Four Islands of Gotham

Gotham City is split across four main islands, each with its own distinct neighborhoods, activities, and collectibles:

  • Tricorner Island — The southernmost island, home to the docks and industrial zones. Heavy Red Hood Gang and Falcone crime family presence in the story's early chapters.
  • South Island — Central residential and commercial districts. Contains multiple SubWayne stations and a high density of Riddler and Cluemaster puzzles.
  • Central Island — The heart of Gotham, including the Gotham City Police Department, Flugelheim Museum, and the main business district. Many story missions are set here.
  • North Island — The wealthiest borough of Gotham, home to Wayne Manor (accessible via tunnel from the Batcave) and Arkham Asylum on its northern peninsula.

SubWayne Fast Travel Stations

The game's fast travel system is built around SubWayne stations — nine underground transit points scattered across all four islands. Each station must be unlocked by solving a nearby environmental puzzle before it can be used. Once active, you can travel between any unlocked station instantly from the map screen.

Tip: Prioritize unlocking SubWayne stations early. They dramatically reduce travel time during collectible hunts and are especially useful for backtracking to missed items after you unlock new characters or gadgets.

Towers and Points of Interest

Each district contains at least one Tower. Activating a Tower — usually by reaching its summit via grapple or glide and solving a short puzzle — reveals all nearby collectibles, activities, and points of interest on your map. Some revealed content requires specific characters or traversal abilities to access, so expect to revisit towers in Free Play after unlocking more of your roster.

Key activity types revealed by towers include:

  • Riddler Puzzles — Environmental riddles that reward WayneTech tokens and studs
  • Cluemaster Puzzles — Detective-style investigation sequences scattered across Gotham
  • WayneTech Caches — Hidden tech crates containing upgrade materials
  • Races — Vehicle and on-foot time trials across the city streets
  • Side Activities — Character-specific challenges and citizen rescue events
