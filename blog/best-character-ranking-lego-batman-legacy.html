All 7 LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Characters, Ranked — Tier List & Abilities Guide

Published: June 20, 2026 · 6 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight takes a notably focused approach to its roster: rather than the hundreds of characters found in earlier LEGO games, it gives you just seven playable heroes and anti-heroes, each with a distinct set of abilities built around unique gadgets and skill trees. Every character shares the same baseline — hand-to-hand combat, a grappling hook, gliding, and vehicle access — but their unique tools create genuinely different experiences in both story missions and Free Play collectible hunting.

So which characters are worth investing in first, and who shines brightest for 100% completion? Drawing on both the tier list published by Dexerto and the hands-on impressions from COGconnected, here is a full breakdown of all seven characters — ranked, with abilities explained.

Important note: These rankings reflect the base-game roster. The Mayhem Collection DLC (September 18, 2026) will add The Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, which may shake up the meta. Check back after launch.

Quick Tier Reference

Before we go character by character, here’s the consensus tier placement based on combat strength, traversal, and collectible utility:

Tier Character Key Strength
S Batman Best all-rounder; most costumes and vehicles; deep skill tree
S Talia Al Ghul Most distinct moveset; excellent for puzzle variety
A Batgirl Best for collectible hunting; drone creates unique traversal
A Nightwing Electrified upgrades of Robin’s toolkit; strong in combat
B Robin Solid foundation; best story intro mission
B Jim Gordon Most fun to play; rebound launcher is uniquely satisfying
C Catwoman Niche puzzles; use when required for collectibles
Tier caveat: Because all characters share the same baseline mechanics, these rankings reflect overall versatility. Catwoman is still perfectly enjoyable to play — she just has a narrower set of unique puzzle roles than the top-tier characters.

Character Breakdown: All 7, Ranked

S-Tier — Rank #1

👸 Batman

Unique Abilities: Batarang · Batclaw · Explosive Gel

The Dark Knight is the character you’ll spend the most time with across the campaign, and TT Games built out his toolkit accordingly. His batarang and batclaw are standard but deeply upgradeable through Legacy of the Dark Knight’s per-character skill trees. His explosive gel opens cracked-wall passages locked to him alone, which matters a great deal in Free Play. Beyond his gadgets, Batman has the largest selection of costumes and vehicles in the game — well over 20 of the 63+ base-game suits belong to him. If you are aiming for 100% completion, Batman is the character you will be switching into most often.

S-Tier — Rank #2

⚔ Talia Al Ghul

Unique Abilities: Ninja Disc · Blow Dart

Talia joins the roster later in the story than the rest of the cast, which might tempt players to overlook her. That would be a mistake. Where the Bat-family members share overlapping traversal toolkits, Talia brings something genuinely different: a ninja disc and a blow dart that interact with the environment in ways no other character can replicate. After spending hours with birdarangs and grappling hooks, her movement and puzzle style feel like a welcome breath of fresh air. The Dexerto tier list ranks her alongside Batman at the top, and that’s well-earned.

A-Tier — Rank #3

👾 Batgirl

Unique Abilities: Drone · Hackarang

If your priority is finding every collectible in Gotham City, Batgirl should be your go-to Free Play character. Her drone is unlike anything else in the game: it can create deployable grapple anchor points mid-air, opening traversal routes locked off to every other character. Her hackarang can unlock radio towers distributed across Gotham’s four islands, and each tower reveals the locations of nearby collectibles on the minimap. According to GamesRadar, this ability directly reduces the collectible-hunting grind more than any other single unlock in the game. Batgirl’s combat takes time to come online (she needs a few skill upgrades before she feels snappy), but the payoff is significant.

A-Tier — Rank #4

🔧 Nightwing

Unique Abilities: Electrorang · Electric Cable Launcher

Nightwing is essentially Robin with an electrical upgrade: his electrorang replaces the birdarang, and his electric cable launcher does the same for Robin’s standard cable. The electricity adds meaningful puzzle interaction — powering generators, stunning enemies through water — that Robin cannot replicate. In terms of raw fun, COGconnected noted that Nightwing can feel slightly redundant if you’ve been playing Robin for hours, since the core loop is similar. But for Free Play, the electrical toolkit gives him a distinct and useful role. He ranks above Robin purely on collectible utility.

B-Tier — Rank #5

👤 Robin

Unique Abilities: Birdarang · Cable Launcher

Robin’s toolkit is the most conventional in the roster — a birdarang thrown like a boomerang and a cable launcher that can attach to enemies or the environment. What elevates him is his story content: COGconnected’s reviewers rated Robin’s introduction mission as the best of any character in the game, praising how it weaves tutorial and narrative without feeling like either a hand-hold or an obstacle course. As a Free Play character, Robin’s utility is solid but narrower than Nightwing’s electrified version of the same toolkit. He’s the best character for enjoying the early game, even if he gets outpaced later.

B-Tier — Rank #6

👮 Jim Gordon

Unique Abilities: Foam Sprayer · Rebound Launcher

Gordon is the ranking’s biggest wildcard. Dexerto places him firmly in C-tier based on raw mechanical versatility. But COGconnected’s Michael Chow named him the most enjoyable character in the entire game — and it’s easy to see why once you get your hands on the rebound launcher. This weapon fires a rubber projectile that bounces between enemies and environmental objects, clearing clusters of LEGO bricks in a single shot in a way that is deeply satisfying. His foam sprayer immobilizes enemies and machines, giving him meaningful crowd-control utility. The split between “tier list ranking” and “fun to play” has never been wider. He lands in B here as a compromise: mechanically limited for completionists, but genuinely a joy in combat.

C-Tier — Rank #7

🐈 Catwoman

Unique Abilities: Whip · Summon Cats · Wall Climbing · Glass Slicing

Catwoman lands at the bottom of most rankings, though the gap between her and the B-tier characters is smaller than a strict tier label suggests. Her whip, wall climbing, and ability to slice through glass windows handle a set of specific collectible puzzles that no other character can unlock. Her most distinctive tool — summoning cats to crawl through vents — is thematically delightful but mechanically limited in scope. What hurts her ranking is that her puzzle interactions feel less inventive than Batgirl’s drone or Gordon’s rebound launcher. You will still switch into Catwoman regularly during Free Play, but primarily because she holds the keys to specific locks rather than because she transforms how the game feels.

Choosing Characters for Free Play

If you are working toward 100% completion, the practical answer to “which character should I use?” is: whichever one unlocks the collectible in front of you. That said, the most efficient team composition for a Free Play sweep is:

  • Batman — explosive gel walls, broadest vehicle access, most costume variety
  • Batgirl — radio tower reveals, drone traversal, hackarang interactions
  • Nightwing — electric generator puzzles, electric enemy interactions
  • Catwoman — glass slicing, vent cats, whip targets
  • Talia — ninja disc and blow dart puzzles, unique movement options

Robin and Gordon can be swapped in when specifically required by a mission objective, but the five above cover the widest range of ability-locked content across Gotham’s four islands.

Skill tree tip: Each character has their own independent skill tree purchasable with WayneTech Points earned through gameplay. Prioritize unlocking Batgirl’s radio tower ability early — it dramatically reduces the time spent locating collectibles on foot across all four islands.

Bottom Line

Legacy of the Dark Knight’s seven-character roster is one of the most mechanically cohesive in the LEGO game series. Because every character shares the same core traversal abilities, the game never locks you out of areas in a frustrating way during the story. The differences emerge in Free Play, where Batgirl’s radio towers, Batman’s explosive gel, and Talia’s unique toolset quietly account for a large percentage of hidden collectibles.

For players working through the story, the character order matters far less than you might expect — you’ll enjoy them all. For completionists, Batgirl and Batman are the two indispensable tools, with Nightwing and Catwoman filling essential niche roles in Free Play. Gordon, meanwhile, is the one you play because it’s fun — and that counts for a lot.
