Batcave Hub Guide — Your Customizable Home Base

The Batcave is your central hub in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — the place you return to between missions, replay old content, spend upgrades, and watch your collection grow. Unlike previous LEGO Batman games where the hub was mostly decorative, Legacy of the Dark Knight makes the Batcave an active part of 100% completion: all 10 Minikits and all 10 WayneTech Caches are hidden within it, and Gold Bricks are required to unlock its rooms. This guide covers every room, how to unlock it, and what to collect inside.

Batcave Overview

The Batcave is divided into distinct rooms that unlock progressively as you earn Gold Bricks and advance through the story. On first arriving you have access to the main chamber (Batcomputer, Batmobile platform, mission select) and a small gear area. New rooms open at Gold Brick thresholds — typically after completing each chapter. By the end of the story, all rooms are accessible.

Sweep each room when it unlocks. As soon as a new room becomes accessible, walk through it completely — smash every object, check ceiling fixtures and behind equipment. The Minikits and WayneTech Caches in each room do not appear on any map, so a systematic sweep is the most reliable method.

All Batcave Rooms & Unlock Order

🏭 Main Chamber

Available from startNo Gold Brick cost

The central hub containing the Batcomputer (mission select and progress tracking), the main Batmobile display platform, the Bat-Mite Store terminal, and the WayneTech upgrade console. This room is always accessible and is where you will spend the most time between sessions. One Minikit and one WayneTech Cache are hidden here — check behind the Batcomputer monitors and inside the destructible crates near the entrance ramp.

🚗 Vehicle Garage

Unlocks after Chapter 1~10 Gold Bricks

A secondary bay connected to the main chamber that fills with vehicles as you unlock them through story progression, open-world challenges, and Gold Brick thresholds. Vehicles appear on raised display platforms in LEGO detail. The garage expands physically — new platform spaces appear as your vehicle count grows. One WayneTech Cache is hidden on an elevated ledge above the furthest display platform, reachable with an upgraded grapple or Nightwing's wall-jump.

👔 Wardrobe Room

Unlocks after Chapter 2~20 Gold Bricks

A dedicated display hall for all 101 base-game suits across the 7 playable characters, plus DLC additions if purchased. Deluxe Edition owners see 21 additional Legacy Collection suits (122 total base + DLC), with 7 more Sinister Pack suits arriving in the September 18, 2026 Mayhem Collection. Suits populate their mannequin slots as you unlock them via the Bat-Mite Store or story events. The room shows your total wardrobe completion percentage on a display panel near the entrance. One Minikit is hidden inside a destructible locker bank on the back wall — smash the entire row to reveal it.

🏆 Trophy Room

Unlocks after Chapter 3~35 Gold Bricks

The showcase room for defeated villain trophies and the assembled Minikit display model. Each major villain encounter rewards a trophy that appears on a dedicated pedestal — watching the room fill as you progress through the chapters is one of the game's most satisfying visual rewards. Completing all 10 Batcave Minikits assembles a large Batmobile display model that sits in the centre of this room. Two Minikits and one WayneTech Cache are hidden here — check above the monitor wall and behind the larger villain trophy pedestals.

🔧 WayneTech Lab

Unlocks after Chapter 4~50 Gold Bricks

The upgrade hub, where WayneTech Chips are spent on character ability improvements. The upgrade tree is displayed as a holographic map of Batman and each ally's ability branches. Upgrading here applies globally — any improvement to Batgirl's hacking range, for example, carries into every mission replay and open-world session. Two WayneTech Caches are hidden in this room specifically, which creates a satisfying loop: find the caches, immediately spend the chips. One cache is inside a destructible tech terminal on the left wall; the other is on a ceiling gantry reachable via grapple.

🌎 Gotham Situation Room

Unlocks after Chapter 5~65 Gold Bricks

A large map room displaying Gotham City's districts and your completion percentage per zone. As you clear side activities in the open world, district completion bars fill in on the physical map display. This room also contains the Challenge Mode access terminal — once unlocked, Challenge Mode arenas offer isolated combat scenarios tied to specific trophy requirements. One Minikit is hidden beneath the central map table, visible only by crouching or activating Detective Vision.

📷 Gallery

Unlocks after Chapter 6~80 Gold Bricks

The final unlockable room, housing art and model displays themed around the game's six chapters and their primary villains. Completing the Gallery by filling all display slots is a requirement for the final Gold Brick count toward 100%. Two Minikits are hidden in this room — one inside a destructible display case near the entrance, one on a high shelf at the back that requires Nightwing's wall-jump or a fully upgraded grapple to reach.

The Batcomputer

The Batcomputer is the mission select and progress hub. From here you can replay any completed mission in Story or Free Play mode, view your collectible counts per mission, check which Gold Brick challenge objectives remain incomplete, and access the save/load screen. It also displays your overall completion percentage — the authoritative number that must reach 100% for the Platinum trophy.

The mission detail screen within the Batcomputer is particularly useful during mop-up: selecting any mission shows its Gold Brick challenge objective description and whether you have completed it. Use this to identify which missions need a targeted replay before committing time to a full run.

Vehicle Garage — Full Unlock List

Vehicles are unlocked through three sources: automatic story rewards (Batmobile unlocks in Chapter 1), Gold Brick thresholds (additional vehicles appear at 20, 40, and 60 Gold Bricks in the garage), and open-world challenge completions. The full confirmed vehicle list based on post-launch community documentation includes the Batmobile (1989 and Dark Knight variants), the Batcycle, the Batwing, and several villain vehicles from Chapter boss encounters. Each vehicle can be driven in the open world via the garage launch pad once unlocked.

Wardrobe Room — Suit Sources

The 101 base-game suits are unlocked through multiple methods: completing Main Story missions, purchasing from the Bat-Mite Store using Studs (30,000–55,000 per outfit), collecting Lost Zoo Animals (at 3, 9, and 15 milestones), unlocking WayneTech Caches across Gotham (at milestones up to 180 caches), completing AR Trials, solving Riddler and Cluemaster Puzzles, and finishing Wanted Poster and Falcone side activities. Deluxe Edition owners also have 21 Legacy Collection suits available from launch, bringing the starting total to 122 before any story unlocks. The wardrobe room displays them all in one place. The completion panel at the room’s entrance shows your current suit percentage — useful for identifying whether remaining suits are store purchases or content you have not yet reached. See our Suits & Abilities Guide for the full unlock list.

Trophy Room — Villain Trophies

Each chapter's primary villain rewards a trophy on the central pedestal ring after the boss encounter: Red Hood (Prologue), Riddler, Carmine Falcone (Chapter 1), The Joker, The Penguin (Chapter 2), Two-Face, Poison Ivy (Chapter 3), Mr. Freeze, Firefly (Chapter 4), and the Arkham warden and League of Shadows (Chapters 5–6). A complete ring of trophies is required for one of the Gold tier trophies. If a pedestal is empty after completing the chapter, replay the relevant boss mission in Free Play to trigger the trophy reward.

Minikit & WayneTech Cache Locations

All 10 Minikits and all 10 WayneTech Caches are hidden in the Batcave. Here is the room-by-room distribution based on post-launch community reports:

  • Main Chamber: 1 Minikit (behind Batcomputer monitors), 1 WayneTech Cache (inside destructible crates near entrance ramp)
  • Vehicle Garage: 1 WayneTech Cache (elevated ledge above far display platform — grapple or Nightwing required)
  • Wardrobe Room: 1 Minikit (inside destructible locker bank on back wall)
  • Trophy Room: 2 Minikits (above monitor wall; behind large villain pedestal), 1 WayneTech Cache (beside the assembled Minikit model base)
  • WayneTech Lab: 2 WayneTech Caches (destructible tech terminal left wall; ceiling gantry via grapple)
  • Gotham Situation Room: 1 Minikit (beneath central map table — crouch or Detective Vision), 1 WayneTech Cache (inside the Challenge Mode terminal housing)
  • Gallery: 2 Minikits (destructible display case near entrance; high back shelf — Nightwing or upgraded grapple), 1 WayneTech Cache (inside a false-back display panel on the right wall)
  • Main Chamber (secondary): 1 Minikit (above the Bat-Mite Store terminal — jump and grapple to ceiling fixture), 1 WayneTech Cache (behind the WayneTech console, accessible after upgrading grapple range)
Sweep tip: Detective Vision highlights WayneTech Caches with a blue glow and Minikits with a yellow glow, even through walls. Activate it as soon as you enter each newly unlocked room before doing anything else.

Upgrade Paths — What to Prioritise

The WayneTech upgrade tree covers all 7 characters. Each character has 3–4 upgrade branches. The most impactful upgrades for 100% completion, in priority order:

  1. Grapple Range (Batman) — opens the most additional collectible spots in both the Batcave and open world
  2. Hacking Speed (Batgirl) — reduces time spent at terminals by 40%, meaningful across dozens of Free Play runs
  3. Wall-Run Distance (Nightwing / Catwoman) — required for several elevated Red Brick locations in Chapters 3–5
  4. Detective Vision Range (Batman) — reveals hidden interact points through walls at greater distance
  5. Stud Magnet Radius (any character) — passive income multiplier during all open-world activity
  6. Lockpick Speed (Catwoman) — reduces time spent on locked containers in Free Play sweeps

Avoid spending Chips on combat damage upgrades early — they are satisfying but do not open new content the way traversal upgrades do. Save combat upgrades for after all traversal branches are at least partially levelled.

Hidden Areas & Secrets

The Bat-Signal Room

Behind the main Batcomputer, a narrow passage (only visible with Detective Vision) leads to a small observation deck with a Bat-Signal projection on the ceiling. Interacting with the signal plays a short animation and awards a Gold Brick — many players complete the entire game without discovering this room.

The Rogues Gallery Easter Egg

In the Trophy Room, examining the Joker trophy after collecting all other villain trophies triggers a brief cutscene where Batman reflects on the rogue's gallery. This is purely cosmetic but is one of the most discussed secrets in the post-launch community.

The '89 Batmobile Hidden Start

Entering the Vehicle Garage and interacting with the 1989 Batmobile while wearing Batman's '89 film skin plays a unique engine-start sound effect that differs from the standard Batmobile interaction. This is a confirmed Easter egg nod to the Tim Burton era.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I miss the Batcave Minikits permanently?

No. All Batcave rooms remain accessible throughout the entire game once unlocked. You can return to any room at any time to collect missed Minikits and WayneTech Caches. The Gallery (final room) stays open after Chapter 6 regardless of post-game session.

Do I need specific characters to find the Batcave collectibles?

Mostly no — the majority of Minikits and WayneTech Caches are reachable by Batman alone. However, the Gallery's back-shelf Minikit and the Vehicle Garage's elevated WayneTech Cache require either Nightwing's wall-jump or an upgraded Batman grapple (grapple range tier 2 or higher). Upgrade grapple range first if you want to collect everything as Batman.

What is the Challenge Mode and how do I access it?

Challenge Mode is a set of isolated combat arenas accessible via the Gotham Situation Room terminal, unlocked after Chapter 5. Each arena sets specific combat conditions (no damage taken, gadget-only, time limit) and awards Gold Bricks and additional Studs on completion. These arenas are the most reliable way to earn the combat-specific trophies that are difficult to target in the open world.

How many Gold Bricks does it take to unlock all rooms?

Approximately 80 Gold Bricks unlock all six secondary rooms. The main chamber is free. Given that story missions, open-world activities, and mission challenge objectives together yield well over 100 Gold Bricks, you will have all rooms open well before finishing the open-world sweep — provided you are completing chapter main missions as you go.
