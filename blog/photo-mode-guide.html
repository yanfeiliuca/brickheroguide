LEGO Batman Legacy Photo Mode: How to Use It (and Unlock the Collecting Evidence Trophy)

Published: July 17, 2026 · 6 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight shipped with a full Photo Mode, and two months into its life the feature has quietly become one of the game's more useful — and more overlooked — tools. TT Games' own LEGO Games News account confirmed the feature at launch with a simple post: "LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has a PHOTO MODE." It's a small thing to announce on its own, but it's also the gateway to a bronze trophy nobody stumbles into by accident, and it's the reason Gotham City has been showing up looking so good in community screenshot roundups.

Here's how to open it, what it lets you adjust, the specific move that unlocks the "Collecting Evidence" trophy, and where your captures actually end up if you're playing on PC.

How to Access Photo Mode

Photo Mode is reached through the pause menu during normal gameplay. According to a detailed community breakdown from In Game News, opening it freezes the action at whatever moment you're in and hands you a set of camera adjustments, including depth of field, exposure, and color grading. That's enough to turn a mid-combo screenshot or a rooftop skyline shot into something closer to a composed piece of key art, without needing any external editing software.

The camera also rolls — you can tilt it off the level horizon in either direction, which is a small but deliberate inclusion, since it's tied directly to an unlockable trophy (more on that below).

Unlock the "Collecting Evidence" Trophy

If you haven't picked up the bronze trophy called Collecting Evidence yet, it has nothing to do with detective work despite the name. According to PowerPyx's trophy guide for the game, it unlocks when you roll the Photo Mode camera more than 20 degrees in either direction. That's it — no specific location, no story requirement, just open Photo Mode anywhere and tilt the camera.

Fastest way to get it: Pause the game the moment you gain control at the start of the story, open Photo Mode, and roll the camera hard to one side. You'll never have to think about this trophy again.

Where Your Screenshots Are Saved (PC)

On PC, Photo Mode captures don't go through Steam's overlay screenshot system by default — they're written to a local folder. Players on the Steam Community discussions boards have documented the path as:

PC Screenshot Location
  • C:\Users\<YourUsername>\AppData\Local\Dinner\Saved\Screenshots\Windows\

The "Dinner" folder name is a holdover from the game's internal development codename rather than a typo — it's consistent with how TT Games has structured save and capture directories on past titles. If you've been using Photo Mode and can't find your images in your usual Steam screenshots folder, this is almost certainly where they went instead.

On PS5 and Xbox Series X|S, captures taken while in Photo Mode are handled through each console's own native capture and share tools rather than a separate in-game gallery, so they'll show up alongside your other screenshots in the PlayStation or Xbox media gallery.

Why Photo Mode Has Become a Community Fixture

Photo Mode's biggest audience so far hasn't been completionists chasing the Collecting Evidence trophy — it's been the screenshot community. The tool's depth-of-field and lighting controls have been credited as instrumental to the PlayStation "Share of the Week" submissions built around the game, letting players isolate a single character model or a specific corner of Gotham City that would otherwise blur past during combat. We rounded up some of the standout community captures in our PS Share of the Week community screenshots piece if you want to see what's possible once you start experimenting with the settings.

Looking ahead: When the Mayhem Collection DLC arrives September 18, 2026, with The Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, expect Photo Mode to get a fresh workout — two brand-new character models and an Arkham Asylum-set story mission are exactly the kind of content that tends to flood the screenshot boards in the first week after a DLC drop.

Quick Recap

  • Open Photo Mode from the pause menu at any point during gameplay.
  • Adjust depth of field, exposure, and color grading to frame your shot.
  • Roll the camera more than 20 degrees to unlock the Collecting Evidence trophy.
  • On PC, find saved captures at AppData\Local\Dinner\Saved\Screenshots\Windows\.
  • On PS5/Xbox, captures route through your console's standard screenshot and share tools.
