How Skill Trees Actually Work in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight

Published: July 5, 2026 · 6 min read

Skills in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight work differently than in earlier LEGO games, where a single shared upgrade pool applied to whichever character you happened to be controlling. Here, progression is locked to specific characters and, more precisely, to the two unique gadgets each of them carries. If you've been holding onto Gold Skill Bricks without a plan for where to spend them, or you're not sure why Robin's upgrades don't seem to affect Batman at all, here's how the whole system actually fits together.

Where to Actually Spend Skill Bricks

Skills are unlocked at the upgrade workbench inside the Batcave, located to the right of Batmite's shop — look for the blue workbench icon while in Detective Mode. According to GamesRadar+, every playable character has their own separate tree, and the trees are entirely tied to that character's two unique gadgets rather than to the character as a whole. Spend Batarang chips on Batman, and Robin's Birdarang tree doesn't move an inch — there's no shared or universal pool.

The currency for these trees is the Gold Skill Brick, which according to TheGamer you earn one of every time you complete a mission. You spend them from the map screen by pressing the map button and scrolling to the Upgrade arrow. Individual skills cost anywhere from around 5 to 12 bricks depending on how powerful the effect is, so a full gadget tree takes a meaningful chunk of story progress to max out.

Every Character Has Two Gadget Trees

Rather than one generic skill list, each hero's abilities are split across their two signature tools. A few confirmed examples, per GamesRadar's breakdown:

  • Batman — Batarang and Batclaw. Batman has the largest overall skill count of any character. Notable unlocks include Concussive Batarang (sticks to enemies before a blast), Scatterang (throw multiple Batarangs at once), and Alarmarang (draws in nearby enemies).
  • Robin — Birdarang and Cable Launcher. His trees mirror Batman's structure: Extra Targets increases how many enemies a single Birdarang can hit, and Bungee Cable turns the Cable Launcher into a bounce-and-slam tool.
  • Commissioner Jim Gordon — Foam Sprayer and Rebound Launcher. Goop Trail leaves sticky traps as Foam Zorbs roll, while Rebound Launcher upgrades add force and area to his ricochet attacks.
  • Catwoman — Whip and Call Kitty (which summons a small cat usable for combat or stealth). Speedy Spin sends enemies into a crowd-clearing spin with a single crack of the Whip.

Every skill inside these trees is additionally tagged as either a Combat or an Exploration skill, which matters when you're deciding what to prioritize — a Combat-heavy build clears fights faster, while an Exploration-heavy one speeds up traversal and collectible hunting.

Best Skills to Unlock First

Because Gold Skill Bricks are earned slowly through mission completion, spreading them thin across every character early on is the main mistake to avoid — you end up with several underpowered heroes instead of one reliable frontliner. Per TheGamer's early-game recommendations, the standout picks in the opening chapters are:

  • Combat Focus (Combat) — builds Focus meter faster for quicker takedowns.
  • Healthy Hero (Combat) — improves survivability against tougher enemies.
  • Perfect Counter (Combat) — adds extra damage on a well-timed counter.
  • Stud Magnet (Exploration) — pulls in more Studs automatically, useful for anyone working toward WayneTech Chip purchases.
  • Dive Bomb (Exploration) — adds an aerial attack option useful for both combat openers and traversal.

Best Skills for Later in the Game

Once you're further into the story and tougher enemy types show up, TheGamer's guide points to a second wave of priorities: Extra Focus and Dodge Attack (both Combat, for sustaining takedown chains against larger enemy groups), Glide Takedown (Combat, for finishing enemies mid-glide), Focused Maneuvers (Exploration, more Focus generation), and Hyper Combo (Exploration, extra Studs per combo). These late skills tend to cost more Gold Bricks than the early picks, so it's worth banking a surplus rather than spending every brick the moment you earn it.

Don't confuse this with WayneTech: Gadget skill trees (this article) run on Gold Skill Bricks earned from missions. WayneTech Chip upgrades — a separate system covered in our WayneTech priority guide — run on chips collected from the game's 200 hidden caches and are spent at a different workbench menu. The two systems don't share currency.

Why This Matters for Your Build

Because trees are gadget-specific rather than character-generic, the practical takeaway is to decide early which one or two characters you'll actually play the most — likely Batman, given his gadget count — and fund their trees first before spreading Gold Bricks across the rest of the roster. For co-op sessions where a second player is controlling Robin or Catwoman for most of the campaign, it's worth checking in on their trees separately, since neither playthrough progress nor Focus/Exploration priorities carry over between characters.

For everything else once you're deciding how to build your roster, our Beginner's Guide and Suits & Abilities Guide cover the rest of the progression systems, and the WayneTech priority guide handles the separate gadget-chip economy.
