Kevin Conroy’s Voice Is Hidden in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — and TT Games Just Confirmed It

Published: June 25, 2026 · 6 min read · Easter Eggs & Tributes

Kevin Conroy voiced Batman in over 60 projects across three decades — from Batman: The Animated Series in 1992 through the Arkham game series and beyond. He passed away in November 2022. Now, more than a month after LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight launched, fans and a TT Games developer have confirmed that his voice lives on in the game in not one, but two hidden ways.

The first tribute was hiding in plain sight. The second took the community weeks to find. Together, they make Legacy of the Dark Knight one of the most heartfelt tributes to Conroy’s legacy in gaming history.

The Billboard: “After Hours With Conroy — The Voice of Gotham’s Night”

As players explored Gotham City’s open world, many noticed a billboard advertising a fictional late-night radio program: Gotham Radio After Hours with Conroy, billed as “The Voice of Gotham’s Night.” The image on the billboard shows a LEGO minifigure in front of a radio microphone.

The wordplay is deliberate and touching. Conroy spent decades voicing Gotham’s Knight — with a K — and the billboard quietly transforms that into a radio host who is the voice of Gotham’s Night. It’s the kind of subtle, loving detail that only true Batman fans would write.

DC.com writer Joshua Lapin-Bertone was among the first to flag it publicly in his June 8 feature on the game’s Easter eggs: “This is a tribute to the late Kevin Conroy. Words can’t express his impact on Batman’s legacy, and I love that he has a presence in this LEGO version of Gotham City.”

The Credits: Conroy and West Listed Alongside the Main Cast

The billboard was meaningful, but observant players noticed something even more significant in the game’s credits: Kevin Conroy and Adam West are both listed by name, alongside the game’s main voice cast.

Adam West, who died in 2017, is credited for a voice line that plays during the 100% completion phone call — a detail long-time LEGO game fans will recognize as a classic franchise tradition. His inclusion in the credits confirmed that archival recordings were used.

The same logic applied to Conroy. If he was credited, his voice had to be in the game somewhere. The community went searching.

The Discovery: Two Archival Voice Lines in Arkham Asylum

On June 18, 2026, a player going by Bluenightbeyond posted a video on X showing what they had found: two archival Kevin Conroy voice lines embedded in the Arkham Asylum level of Chapter Five.

The lines are drawn from Conroy’s recordings in the original Batman: Arkham Asylum game series:

  • Line 1 — Security Alert: Upon Batman’s first arrival at Arkham with the Joker, an intercom announces: “Security alert detected.” The voice is unmistakably Kevin Conroy’s.
  • Line 2 — Elevator Malfunction: When the player enters the elevator inside Arkham, the intercom says: “Elevator is currently experiencing difficulties. We apologize for any inconvenience.” Again, Conroy’s archival recording.

They are easy to miss — background intercom announcements in a busy action level. But they are unmistakably him.

“I’m glad someone finally discovered the use of Kevin Conroy’s voice within the game during the Arkham Asylum level.”

— Mike Consalvey, Senior Level Designer at TT Games, posting on X, June 18, 2026

The confirmation from TT Games itself — from the designer who worked on the very level containing the Easter egg — sent fans across Reddit, X, and YouTube into an emotional reaction. The post from Bluenightbeyond, accompanied by a photo of Conroy’s LEGO billboard, garnered thousands of responses.

Why the Arkham Asylum Chapter Was the Right Place

The choice of location is not accidental. Chapter Five of Legacy of the Dark Knight recreates the opening sequence of Batman: Arkham Asylum — the 2009 Rocksteady game that Conroy voiced, and which is widely regarded as one of the greatest superhero games ever made.

By placing Conroy’s archival voice in that specific level, TT Games created a moment of continuity: the version of Batman that Conroy helped define, echoing through the halls of Arkham one more time. It’s a quiet acknowledgment from one developer to another — and from the whole development team to the man who was Batman for so many people.

Where to find the voice lines: Play through Chapter Five until you reach the Arkham Asylum level. Listen carefully to the intercom announcements when you first arrive with the Joker, and again when you enter the elevator. The voice lines are short, so turn up your volume.

A New Batman, But a Lasting Legacy

In Legacy of the Dark Knight, Batman is voiced by Shai Matheson, who brings a fresh energy to the role for the LEGO universe. Meanwhile, Robert Pattinson — who played Bruce Wayne in The Batman (2022) — is preparing for a theatrical sequel.

But no change in casting erases what Conroy built. For a generation of fans, his voice is Batman. The Animated Series, the Arkham games, and dozens of other projects were shaped by the warmth, intelligence, and authority he brought to the role. TT Games clearly understood that when they made Legacy of the Dark Knight.

The billboard tribute. The credits listing. The two hidden intercom lines in Arkham. Each of these is a small thing on its own. Together, they tell you that the people who built this game loved Kevin Conroy — and they built a permanent place for him in Gotham City.

Community Reaction

The response across the gaming community has been overwhelmingly emotional. GamingBible, Screen Rant, NME, TheGamer, and OpenCritic all ran dedicated articles on the discovery within 24 hours of Consalvey’s confirmation. Many fans on Reddit’s r/LegoGaming and r/batman described the moment as moving them to tears.

For a game that was already being praised for its love of Batman’s full cinematic and animated history, the Conroy tributes feel like the emotional core of everything Legacy of the Dark Knight set out to accomplish. You can tell this was a game made by people who grew up with Kevin Conroy’s Batman — and who wanted to honor him the right way.

According to GamingBible, the discovery was first widely shared by the LEGO content creator Ashnflash before Consalvey’s confirmation made it official. The Screen Rant and TheGamer write-ups include additional community quotes and reactions for anyone who wants to relive the moment.
