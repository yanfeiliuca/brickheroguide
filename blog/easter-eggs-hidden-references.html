Every Easter Egg & Hidden Reference in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight

Published: May 21, 2026 · 5 min read

TT Games didn't just make a great LEGO Batman game — they stuffed Legacy of the Dark Knight wall-to-wall with hidden references, layered jokes, and deep-cut pop culture nods. Since the Deluxe Edition dropped on May 19, players have been sharing clips of the best ones online. Here's a rundown of every confirmed Easter egg found so far, with the context that makes each one especially clever.

🔎 Still finding more? The community is actively discovering new references daily. These are the confirmed Easter eggs as of May 21, 2026 — more are likely hiding in Gotham's open world.

The Easter Eggs, Ranked by Layers of Cleverness

1. The Penguin / Danny DeVito / It's Always Sunny Triple Play

Reference: It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia

This one has three layers. When you fight the Penguin, he delivers the line "so anyway, I started blasting" — a reference to the iconic meme from It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia, spoken by Danny DeVito's character Frank Reynolds. The twist: DeVito played the Penguin in the 1992 film Batman Returns. So it's a Batman villain quoting a meme from a show starring the actor who once played that exact villain. TT Games clearly did their homework.

2. Bruce Wayne's Business Card / American Psycho / Christian Bale

Reference: American Psycho (2000)

In one scene, Bruce slides a business card onto a desk. An NPC immediately starts admiring its craftsmanship, asking if it's "eggshell stationary." This is a direct shot-for-shot homage to the famous business card scene in the 2000 film American Psycho — which starred Christian Bale, who of course went on to play Batman in Christopher Nolan's Dark Knight trilogy. Another joke that only lands if you know both references at once.

3. Alfred Quotes Michael Caine's Viral Tweet

Reference: Michael Caine's 2024 tweet

Alfred quotes what has become one of the internet's favourite Batman memes: "Why do we fall? So we can learn to pick ourselves up. Batman Begin." This is verbatim from a tweet posted by Michael Caine — who played Alfred in the Nolan trilogy — in August 2024. The original tweet misspelled Batman Begins as "Batman Begin," which made it go viral. Having the in-game Alfred deliver this line is a perfect tribute to the real-world actor who made the character iconic.

4. Bat-Mite as the Resident Evil 4 Merchant

Reference: Resident Evil 4 (2005 / 2023 Remake)

Bat-Mite — the interdimensional Batman superfan — appears in the game wearing a purple bandana over his face, mirroring the iconic Merchant character from Resident Evil 4. He even attempts to say the Merchant's famous "What are you buying?" line, before adding that it's hard on his vocal chords. It's a funny fourth-wall nod that works on its own, but the design detail makes it genuinely impressive.

5. Batman Fights an Animatronic Bear (Five Nights at Freddy's)

Reference: Five Nights at Freddy's

Batman stumbles upon a malfunctioning animatronic bear that attacks him — a clear nod to the horror series Five Nights at Freddy's, in which animatronic animals at a children's restaurant attempt to kill the night-shift security guard. Batman dealing with the FNAF bear in full LEGO form is exactly the kind of absurd crossover only TT Games would attempt.

6. Black Mask's Car / Street Fighter 2 Bonus Stage

Reference: Street Fighter 2 (1991)

During a confrontation with Black Mask, Batman destroys his car in a sequence that's a direct tribute to Street Fighter 2's famous Bonus Stage — where players could beat up a car between rounds to score extra points. It was one of the most iconic moments in fighting game history, and seeing it recreated in LEGO form is a treat for anyone who grew up in arcades.

7. Poison Ivy Boss Fight / Jurassic Park Kitchen Scene

Reference: Jurassic Park (1993)

One of the subtler references: during the Poison Ivy boss fight, her giant snapdragon plant's two heads snap at each other in what appears to be a shot-for-shot recreation of the velociraptor entrance in the kitchen scene from Jurassic Park. If you know the scene, you'll spot it immediately. If you don't, it still works as a funny bit of animation — but the homage rewards those who do.

8. The Diner and 55 Burgers (I Think You Should Leave)

Reference: I Think You Should Leave with Tim Robinson

A diner NPC asks if the restaurant has any food challenges. The waiter replies that if they can eat "55 burgers, 55 shakes, and 55 tacos," they'll be asked to leave — a reference to the "Pay It Forward" sketch from Netflix comedy series I Think You Should Leave with Tim Robinson, in which a man attempts to order an enormous amount of fast food. The specific numbers are identical to the sketch.

Why So Many References?

It's worth noting that Batman himself has always been a character who sits at the intersection of pop culture history. The Nolan trilogy reshaped blockbuster filmmaking. The 1966 Adam West TV series became a camp icon. The 1989 Tim Burton film defined an era. By making a game that spans "decades of Bruce Wayne's life," TT Games created a natural excuse to weave in references from every era — game, film, TV, and internet culture alike.

According to Kotaku, there are many more Easter eggs still being found. The community has been actively sharing clips since early access opened on May 19, and the full launch on May 22 will bring even more eyes to Gotham's open world. Expect this list to grow.

🏆 Fun fact: Legacy of the Dark Knight currently holds a Metacritic score of 84, making it the highest-rated LEGO game of all time — surpassing LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga's record after four years. The dense Easter egg content is one reason critics have praised it as a love letter to Batman's entire history. (GamesRadar+)
