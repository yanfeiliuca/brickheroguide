One Month In: How LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Has Held Up

Published: June 22, 2026 · 7 min read

Exactly one month ago today, LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight launched in full on PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC. (Deluxe Edition players got a head start on May 19.) It's been a busy thirty days: a major stability patch, a confirmed DLC date, Twitch Drops, free HBO Max unlocks, and a Steam community that has left over 11,000 reviews. Here's the full post-launch picture.

Critical Reception: A Strong Showing

The reviews that landed around launch have held up well. Multiple major outlets called Legacy of the Dark Knight the most ambitious LEGO game to date, praising the Arkham-style combat overhaul, the density of Gotham's open world, and the sheer breadth of Batman mythology packed into a single title. IGN awarded it an 8/10, GamesRadar+ gave it 4 out of 5 stars, and Game Informer called it “Batman, Built Different” in a glowing assessment.

On Steam, the game currently sits at Overwhelmingly Positive, according to data at the time of writing, with more than 11,600 English-language reviews. That puts it among the highest-rated LEGO titles on the platform. The most common threads in positive reviews cite the story's emotional weight — particularly the young Bruce Wayne sequences — and the surprising depth of the combat system. Critical reviews tend to focus on occasional camera issues in tight spaces and some texture pop-in during fast traversal.

Post-Launch Patch: Update 1.006

TT Games shipped Update 1.006 on June 2, roughly ten days after the standard edition launch. According to the patch notes on SteamDB, the update addressed general stability, fixed game loading crashes, resolved stuttering issues that had been widely reported on PC, and included other minor tweaks. The patch was broadly well-received by the community, with players noting that the stuttering fix in particular made Gotham's open world significantly smoother to traverse.

As of writing, no second content patch has been announced, though TT Games and Warner Bros. Games have both indicated that ongoing support is planned through at least the Mayhem Collection DLC launch in September.

Free Unlocks: HBO Max, WB Games & Redeem Codes

One of the quieter stories from the first month has been the suite of free cosmetics available to players who connect external accounts. According to Game Rant and confirmed by the WB Games rewards page, active HBO Max subscribers can unlock two exclusive Batsuits — the Dark Knights of Steel Batsuit (inspired by DC's Dark Knights of Steel comic series) and the Black Lantern Batsuit (tied to HBO's Lanterns) — simply by linking their HBO Max and WB Games accounts from the in-game starting menu.

The process is straightforward: from the main menu, select the HBO Max announcement in the lower left corner, scan the QR code or open the link, sign in, and the suits appear in your collection within moments. Players also unlock a Golden Age Batsuit just for creating or linking a WB Games account, no subscription required.

Still valid: The HBO Max and WB Games account links remain active — if you haven't claimed your free suits yet, they're still available. Head to the main menu and look for the banner in the lower left corner. More info on the WB Games rewards page.

Physical LEGO set owners can also redeem codes for bonus Batmobiles and Batcave props via the “Redeem Code” option on the starting menu. Select LEGO sets sold at retail include a code card with these unlocks. Check our dedicated redeem codes guide for the full list.

Twitch Drops: What Was Available and What Came Next

A Twitch Drops campaign ran from May 19 (Deluxe Edition early access) through May 31, offering eight free Batcave prop rewards. Players earned drops by watching LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight streams with Drops Enabled, with each reward requiring 30 minutes of watch time. Two of the eight rewards were exclusive to the official DC Twitch channel.

The campaign has since ended, but WB Games has used Drops campaigns for multiple games and has not ruled out future rounds. If you missed May's campaign, keep an eye on the official Twitch Drops page for any announcements tied to the Mayhem Collection DLC launch in September.

What's Next: Mayhem Collection DLC on September 18

The biggest post-launch milestone is still ahead. The Mayhem Collection DLC has been officially confirmed for September 18, 2026, arriving simultaneously with the Nintendo Switch 2 version of the game. According to the LEGO.com launch announcement, the Mayhem Collection includes:

  • A new Mayhem Mode with The Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, each with their own abilities, gadgets, and takedowns
  • A new Story Mission set inside Arkham Asylum, following the villain duo's breakout attempt
  • The Sinister Pack: seven new suits, five new Batcave props, and a new Batmobile

Deluxe Edition owners receive the Mayhem Collection as part of their purchase. Standard Edition players can buy it separately. For a full breakdown of confirmed content, see our Mayhem Collection DLC deep-dive.

The Verdict After 30 Days

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has had a strong first month. Critical reviews have held firm, the Steam community is one of the most enthusiastic of any LEGO game in recent memory, and TT Games delivered a meaningful stability patch within the first two weeks. Free cosmetic unlocks via HBO Max and WB Games accounts have given players extra reasons to engage, and the roadmap through September gives the game clear momentum.

The next major marker will be September 18, when the Mayhem Collection DLC drops alongside the Switch 2 version. If that release goes smoothly — and if the Joker and Harley Quinn's Arkham Asylum mission delivers on its premise — Legacy of the Dark Knight looks set to sustain its positive reputation through the end of 2026 and beyond.
