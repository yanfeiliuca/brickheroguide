All 23 Red Brick Locations in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight

Published: June 30, 2026 · 6 min read

Red Bricks have been a LEGO game staple for decades, but LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight changes how they work in one important way: they are no longer cheat codes. Forget the Stud multipliers and Score x10 cheats from the earlier LEGO Batman titles. In Legacy, all 23 Red Bricks are purely cosmetic — they unlock new colour schemes and visual effects you can apply to any suit or vehicle you own. Think of them as a second wardrobe on top of the 101 base suits already in the game.

Fourteen Red Bricks are hidden inside story missions, each locked behind a short puzzle. The remaining nine are purchased directly from Bat-Mite's Shop using Studs. This guide covers every location and puzzle solution so you can collect them all in a single playthrough.

How Red Bricks Work in Legacy

Once you collect or purchase a Red Brick, you equip it through the character menu. Press Options (PS5) or Menu (Xbox) to open the map, then press R1/RB to navigate to the Characters tab. From there, press Square/X to view your Suits, then press Square/X again to open your Red Brick selection. On the vehicle side, the same path applies but choose Triangle/Y at the Suits step to switch to Vehicles instead.

Red Bricks apply a visual skin to the item currently equipped — a Ninja brick gives your active suit a dark blue ninja palette, while the Showbiz brick wraps it in a spotlight-yellow stage aesthetic. These stack well with the more unusual unlockable suits and can dramatically change how your build looks without affecting any stats or gameplay mechanics whatsoever.

Shop unlock timing: Bat-Mite's Shop in the Batcave becomes accessible at the start of Chapter 2. However, some of the purchasable Red Bricks only appear in the shop's random rotation after you complete the main story. If you are mid-playthrough and certain bricks are missing from the podium, check again after the credits roll.

All 14 Mission Red Bricks

Each story mission contains exactly one Red Brick hidden behind an optional puzzle. You don't need to collect these during your first pass — all missions are replayable in Free Play mode, so if you lack the right character ability, you can return later with your full roster.

Mission Red Brick Puzzle Solutions

Ninja — Prologue: Infiltration

Chapter: Prologue

After escaping the temple and building the ballista to form a tightrope, ignore the path forward. Instead, follow the ledge on the right side of the building to find a secret room with two statues. Pick up the books from each side of the room and place them on the two pressure plates. Then have Bruce Wayne and Talia al Ghul stand on the remaining pressure plates simultaneously to pop the Red Brick out.

Nautical — Chapter 1: New Beginnings

Chapter: 1 (Docks section)

Near the end of the mission when you are tasked with building a battering ram to reach Flass, look left for a building with yellow and black hazard signs. Grapple to the roof and drop through the open skylight to collect the Red Brick inside without triggering the battering ram sequence.

Filthy Rich — Chapter 1: Carmine Falcone

Chapter: 1

After speaking with Flass in the large open room, look for a vault with a handwheel directly opposite the exit door. Spin the handwheel until the combination reads 5-9-6 — the code is hinted at by two pages visible in the room reading "59X" and "X96." The safe opens to reveal the Red Brick.

Toxic Waste — Chapter 1: The Red Hood Gang

Chapter: 1

In the room with the glass floor corridor (enemies visible partying below), enter the hallway and immediately turn right to find a vent. Crouch and enter it. Use Jim Gordon's Foam Sprayer to plug the two holes oozing toxic waste, then use Batman's Batclaw on the wall above. Use Gordon's Foam Sprayer one final time to clear the last obstruction and collect the brick.

Feline — Chapter 2: Mimes

Chapter: 2

In the store section where the only forward path requires building, look right to find the Gadgets Store. On the floor near it, find a wire and plug it into the monitor. A mini-game appears — use the red arrows to guide the green cat to the bottom-right red square while avoiding the orange dog. Once the cat reaches the square, the monitor rewards you with the Red Brick.

Gothic — Chapter 2: The Penguin

Chapter: 2

Past the exploding penguin area, in the warehouse with the Cobblepot painting, destroy the objects to the right of the painting to expose a vent. Climb through. In the new room, use Catwoman to cut the glass on the right and retrieve the red umbrella. Place the umbrella on the podium and use Catwoman's Whip to rotate it toward the Red Brick in the cage, then trigger the pressure plate to explode the cage.

Artist — Chapter 2: Flugelheim

Chapter: 2

After entering through the locked doors with the ticket, when the shutters drop over the Joker statue, use Catwoman's Call Kitty ability to send a cat through the vent on the right into the security room. Leap from the desk to the shelf and follow it to the ceiling, where another vent leads to scaffolding holding the Red Brick.

Showbiz — Chapter 2: Joker

Chapter: 2

On the right side of the street, find a building with a yellow flowerbed. Play as Catwoman, grapple up, cut the window, and enter. Use the Call Kitty gadget to jump the cat from the coffee table to the TV. Switch to Batman and push the TV left, then control the cat again to pass through the vent and collect the Red Brick from the shelf.

Clown — Chapter 3: Two-Face

Chapter: 3

After detonating the first bomb and clearing the ensuing enemy encounter, find the strength test machine at the back of the area. Have Robin jump on the pressure plate to reveal three targets. Use Robin's Birdarang to highlight and hit all three targets simultaneously — the Red Brick drops immediately.

Power Plant — Chapter 3: Kite Man

Chapter: 3

Where the killer plant is located, head downward and find a door that Robin can pry open with his staff. Inside, aim the Cable Launcher at the battery and the empty power generator on the right. This activates an air vent Batman can glide up to snatch the Red Brick from above.

Flower Power — Chapter 3: Poison Ivy

Chapter: 3

Past a set of locked doors, enter the greenhouse on the left. To break the glass case holding the Red Brick, place the red flower on the left pedestal and the green flower on the right pedestal. The case shatters and the brick is yours.

Fire Breather — Chapter 4: Firefly

Chapter: 4

Near the Ticket Office objective, turn right into the planetarium room. Four lights are projected onto a central planet. Turn all the handwheels in the centre of the room until every light shows the red planet (Mars). The planet cracks open and the Red Brick is inside.

Ice Age — Chapter 4: Mr. Freeze

Chapter: 4 — after boss fight

After defeating Mr. Freeze, you control Nightwing and Batgirl. Before building the device, look at the wall behind you for a red panel — use Batgirl's Hackarang on it to reveal three targets. Fire Nightwing's Electrorang to hit all three targets at once (it auto-targets nearby enemies). The Red Brick falls from the left wall.

Bats — Chapter 6: The League of Shadows Returns

Chapter: 6

After resurfacing from the sewers, turn left to find a socket where Nightwing can insert his staff. Then hack the nearby garbage truck with Batgirl — it moves right. Use the truck as cover to sneak past the Tumblers' sightlines and grab the Red Brick behind the gate without being spotted.

All 9 Bat-Mite Shop Red Bricks

Bat-Mite's Shop unlocks in the Batcave at the start of Chapter 2, and additional Bat-Mite kiosks appear scattered around Gotham City's districts. The front podium at each location offers a rotating selection — visit regularly and ensure you have enough Studs banked, as the entire shop rotation requires completing the main story before all nine bricks appear.

Why Bother With Red Bricks?

If Red Bricks don't affect gameplay stats, are they worth hunting? For completionists, yes — collecting all 23 is a requirement for 100% completion and contributes toward the game's collectible trophy milestones. For casual players, they are an optional cosmetic layer on top of the already-extensive suits system.

The more compelling reason to hunt them is the puzzle design itself. Each mission Red Brick puzzle is a compact, self-contained challenge that uses the mission's active characters in creative ways — the Feline brick's cat-guiding mini-game, the Filthy Rich safe combination, and the multi-character pressure-plate puzzle in the Infiltration prologue are all memorable enough to be worth experiencing even if you don't care about the cosmetic reward. They are the closest the game comes to classic LEGO multi-character environmental puzzles, and they represent some of the best optional design in the entire campaign.
