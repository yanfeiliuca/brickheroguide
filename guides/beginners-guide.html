LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is the most mechanically ambitious LEGO game TT Games has made — with Arkham-inspired combat, a full open-world Gotham, and a suite of gadgets that feed into environmental puzzles. Here's everything you need to know before you start, to avoid the most common beginner mistakes.

Choose Your Difficulty Mode

When you first start the game, you'll choose one of three difficulty modes. You can change this at any time from the pause menu.

Easiest

⭐ Classic

Infinite respawns. Minimal enemy variety — no shield-bearing or advanced enemy types. Crowd sizes are kept small. The traditional LEGO experience with zero punishment for failing.

Recommended for: Young players, casual play
Default

🦇 Caped Crusader

Unlimited lives but significantly larger crowds with mixed advanced enemy types — shield bearers, counter-specialists, and gadget-required encounters. Combat requires reading enemy indicators.

Recommended for: Most players — start here
Hardest

🔥 Dark Knight

No lives safety net. One failed combat scenario means restarting from checkpoint. Enemy variety and crowd density are at maximum. Reviewers note it's still easier than Arkham Knight's standard mode.

Recommended for: Action game veterans

💡 Pro Tip: Difficulty does not affect trophies or the Platinum. You can complete everything on Classic and still earn 100% completion. Change freely without penalty.

Detective Mode — Use It Constantly

Activate Detective Mode by clicking the right stick (R3 on PlayStation, RS on Xbox). It triggers a sonar pulse that scans the area around you, revealing enemy positions, collectibles, puzzle targets, and hidden secrets through walls and obstacles.

Detective Mode operates in three visual sub-modes:

  • X-Ray mode — sees through thin walls, reveals hidden enemy positions
  • UV mode — highlights invisible LEGO textures, Riddler glyph clues, and environmental hints
  • Thermal mode — detects heat signatures from machines, enemies behind cover, and interactive vents

Detective Mode feeds directly into the stealth system: it shows you enemy patrol routes and awareness cones, letting you plan takedowns before combat starts. Using it before every major encounter is consistently the smarter play than charging in blind.

Red Bricks — Find These First

Red Bricks unlock game modifiers that make everything else easier or more rewarding. There are 23 Red Bricks total: 14 hidden in the open world and 9 purchasable from the Bat-Mite Store after collecting WayneTech Chips.

Priority Red Bricks to find early:

  • Stud Multipliers (×2, ×4, ×6, ×8, ×10) — stack these together for exponential Stud income. Activating all multipliers simultaneously yields ×80 Studs, making the 1 million Stud target for buying suits trivial.
  • Collectible Detector — shows nearby collectibles on your minimap, dramatically reducing time spent hunting minikits.
  • Invincibility — eliminates respawn frustration entirely on Dark Knight mode.

💡 Tip: Red Bricks have a compounding benefit — the earlier you activate Stud Multipliers, the more Studs you accumulate during the rest of your playthrough. Prioritize finding them in Chapters 1–2 if possible.

Combat Fundamentals

The combat system is Arkham-inspired with free-flow combos. A few things to learn immediately:

  • Counter prompt — the blue flash above an enemy's head means they're about to attack. Hit the counter button to deflect and chain into your combo.
  • Grapple Slam — the single most important move against shield enemies. Hold the grapple button to vault over a shielded enemy, then tap the attack button to slam them to the ground, bypassing their shield completely. Unlock this upgrade from Batman's skill tree as soon as possible.
  • Gadgets in combat — Jim Gordon's Foam Sprayer freezes enemies for easy combos. Batgirl's Hackarang stuns electronic enemies. Talia's Blowdarts put guards to sleep without triggering combat at all.
  • Environmental interactions — look for glowing LEGO objects near enemy groups. Pulling a barrel with the Batclaw or detonating Explosive Gel near a cluster is faster than fighting individually.

Building Your Stud Income

Studs are the main currency for purchasing suits from the Bat-Mite Store. You need over 1 million Studs to buy all 101 base suits. Tips to accumulate faster:

  • Smash everything in the environment — furniture, vehicles, bins, and LEGO objects all drop Studs.
  • Activate Stud Multiplier Red Bricks as early as possible and stack them.
  • Replay completed missions in Free Play — missions with dense enemy encounters generate the most Studs per minute.
  • Gold Bricks from missions can be exchanged at the Bat-Cave for bonus Stud payouts.

Character Switching Tips

Switch your Player 2 companion freely in the Bat-Cave between missions. The most common beginner mistake is staying with Jim Gordon for too long into Chapter 2 and 3 — once Catwoman unlocks, her wall climbing, glass cutting, and Call Kitty abilities solve puzzles Gordon simply cannot.

If you hit a puzzle that feels stuck, the answer is usually a character ability you haven't tried yet. Check which character's gadget applies before searching online.

15 Essential Tips

1
Start on Caped Crusader

Classic mode strips out too much of the combat depth that makes this game interesting. Caped Crusader is the designed experience — unlimited lives with real combat stakes.

2
Click R3 before every puzzle

Detective Mode scans the area for interactive objects. Most puzzles have their solution highlighted in UV or X-Ray mode if you scan first.

3
Upgrade Grapple Slam immediately

Shield enemies appear from Chapter 2 onwards. Grapple Slam bypasses shields instantly. Without it, shield encounters are unnecessarily frustrating.

4
Don't stress about collectibles on first playthrough

Many collectibles require abilities from characters you haven't unlocked yet. Play the story first, then revisit in Free Play with the full roster available.

5
Activate Stud Multipliers as early as possible

Red Brick Stud Multipliers stack multiplicatively. All five active simultaneously gives ×80 Studs. This single action changes the economy of the whole game.

6
Smash everything in the environment

LEGO Batman's environments are dense with breakable objects. Smashing environment objects before every combat encounter tops up your Stud count fast.

7
Counter the blue flash, not the attack animation

The counter window opens on the blue flash above an enemy's head — not when they swing. Countering on the flash chains into combo multipliers; countering late just blocks.

8
Use Catwoman's Call Kitty for any tight-space puzzle

If you can see a vent opening but can't enter, switch to Catwoman and use Call Kitty. The cat navigates any duct the game marks — look for the small paw icon.

9
Orange glow = Explosive Gel

Cracked LEGO walls glow faintly orange when Batman is nearby. These are always solved with Explosive Gel — if you see orange, that's your cue.

10
Complete early side activities before Chapter 3

Side activities in Gotham unlock Batmobile upgrades and Gold Bricks. Doing 2–3 side activities per chapter before moving on keeps Batman's vehicle options expanded.

11
Talia is your best Free Play character

For mission replay in Free Play, Talia's Ninja Dash lets her skip platforming challenges other characters must navigate carefully. She dramatically cuts replay time.

12
Red Bricks have compounding value — prioritize them

Every Red Brick you find early applies to everything you do after. A Stud Multiplier found in Chapter 1 earns you far more than the same multiplier found in Chapter 5.

13
13
Gold Bricks unlock character skill upgrades

Gold Bricks earned from completing missions fund character skill upgrades in the Bat-Cave. Prioritize Batman's combat upgrades and Catwoman's passive ability extensions first.

14
There are no missable collectibles

Nothing is missable. Every collectible, Red Brick, and suit can be obtained via Free Play after the story ends. Play the story naturally without anxiety about missing things.

15
The Bat-Cave is your hub — visit between every mission

The Bat-Cave is where you swap characters, unlock Red Brick modifiers, purchase suits, apply Gold Brick upgrades, and access the Bat-Mite Store. Check it between missions to apply any newly unlocked Red Bricks before continuing.
