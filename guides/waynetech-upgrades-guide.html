WayneTech Chips are LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight's upgrade currency — spend them at any Batcave Workbench to make every gadget more powerful. With 200 chips spread across the open world and story missions, you have plenty to work with, but early on chips are scarce. Spending them in the wrong order means struggling through mid-game fights with an underpowered kit. This guide tells you exactly which upgrades to buy first, why, and how to efficiently collect every chip in Gotham.

How WayneTech Chips Work

Chips come from WayneTech Caches — glowing containers hidden across Gotham's 9 districts and inside every story mission. There are exactly 200 caches in total: 160 in the open world and 40 inside missions (5 per mission). Each cache holds exactly one chip.

Find caches faster: Activate the District Tower in each of Gotham's 9 regions to reveal all nearby cache locations on your map. Your sense ability also highlights cables running from locked caches to their power sources — follow them to unlock puzzle caches without wandering.

To spend chips, head to any Workbench station inside the Batcave. Open the upgrade menu, pick a character, and purchase whatever upgrades you can afford. Upgrades activate immediately and apply in all future gameplay, including during mission replays.

⚔ Playing on Dark Knight Mode? The priority order below changes significantly. Dark Knight Mode imposes a 20% stud death penalty on every life lost, which makes offensive upgrades riskier to rely on — you need survivability before damage output. Prioritise health bar extensions and counter timing upgrades in Phase 1 before any offensive tree. The Focus Bat Swarm is still excellent, but don't race toward it at the expense of defensive options. See our Dark Knight Mode Survival Guide for the full priority breakdown.

Priority Upgrade Phases

Think of your upgrade journey in three phases. The core rule: Batman first, always — he is always one of your two active characters, so his upgrades deliver value in every single fight.

🔴 Phase 1 — Story Mode (Chips 1–40)

Estimated chips: 5–15 story chips + early open world

Buy Batman's Stealthy Stun (5 chips) and Carbon Fiber Tips (7 chips) first. Together these extend stun duration on Batarang hits and improve their base stopping power, making early fights noticeably smoother. If you have spare chips, grab Brute Claw (6 chips) so Batman can yank large Brute enemies off balance for follow-up combos.

🟠 Phase 2 — Mid Game, Chapters 3–4 (Chips 40–100)

Estimated chips: 40–100 after tower activations

Now save up for Focus Bat Swarm (12 chips) — the single best upgrade in the game. Activating it summons a swarm of bats that damages and distracts multiple enemies simultaneously, while also regenerating your Focus meter quickly when chained into melee attacks. This creates a self-sustaining combat loop. After Focus Bat Swarm, spend on Scatterang (9 chips) for multi-target crowd control and Batclaw Fling (9 chips) to clear tight corridors. Begin investing in your co-op partner — Jim Gordon's Splitter (6 chips) and Bigger Clip (6 chips) pay dividends fast.

✅ Phase 3 — Free Roam Cleanup (Chips 100–200)

Estimated chips: 100–200 from district clears + mission replays

At this stage you unlock Nightwing, Batgirl, and Talia al Ghul. Focus on each character's 12-chip Focus ultimate — these are expensive but transform them from solid supports into screen-clearing threats. Tackle sidekick upgrades in the order you use each character most in co-op or free-roam missions.

What NOT to Buy First

Skip these early: Raw damage upgrades like Weighted Batarangs (6 chips) and the Alarmarang (6 chips) give marginal returns compared to stun and crowd-control upgrades at the same price. Focus on upgrades that change how a weapon works before upgrades that just increase its numbers.

Also hold off on Focus ultimates (all 12-chip upgrades) until Phase 2 or later. They are the most powerful options available, but you will not have enough chips to afford them early without starving yourself of the foundational upgrades that make mid-game fights comfortable.

The Alarmarang is a situational utility pick — it draws enemy attention for stealth setups, but only useful if you are playing a pure stealth route. Most players will never feel its absence. Buy it last, if at all, during Phase 3 cleanup.

How to Farm WayneTech Chips Fast

The 200 available chips is more than enough to buy every upgrade in the game if you explore thoroughly. Here is the most time-efficient approach:

  • Complete story missions in order — each contains exactly 5 caches (40 guaranteed chips total from missions), with zero backtracking required
  • Start open-world farming in Otisberg District first — it has the highest concentration of surface-level caches reachable without upgraded mobility gadgets, making it ideal before you unlock the Grapnel Boost or Sonar Pulse upgrades
  • Activate all 9 District Towers — this immediately marks every open-world cache on your map, eliminating aimless searching
  • Move to Tricorner Island waterfront second — second-highest cache density in the game, and several caches here are invisible without the upgraded Sonar Pulse Reveal ability
  • Use your Sense ability upon entering any new area — nearby caches ping audibly, and the sound grows louder as you approach
  • Follow power cables from locked puzzle caches to their power source using Sense — solves most cache puzzles in under a minute
  • Replay missed missions via the Batcomputer in the Batcave — any mission cache you skipped on the first pass can be retrieved in Free Play with the full character roster
District priority: Otisberg → Tricorner Island → Diamond District is the community-recommended order for chip farming. Otisberg's flat layout and surface-level caches reward players who haven't yet upgraded traversal. Once you have the Grapnel Boost, Diamond District's vertical cache clusters become the most efficient grind. Note: a Tricorner Island cache chest that previously failed to spawn was fixed in Update 1.007 — make sure you're patched before writing off a missing chip there.
Purple stud trick: When you spot a purple stud worth 10,000, do not collect it immediately. First raise your Stud Combo multiplier to its current maximum (up to 4x with Hyper Combo upgrades from the Exploration tree), then collect it for up to 40,000 studs in a single pickup.

With these priorities in place, you will have a fully powered Batman by the end of Chapter 3, solid sidekick upgrades by mid-Chapter 4, and enough chips left over to finish every character's upgrade tree during the end-game free-roam phase. No chip should ever feel wasted.
