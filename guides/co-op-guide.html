LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight supports two-player local co-op throughout the entire game — story missions, open-world Gotham, and free-roam collectible hunting. There is no native online multiplayer, but PC players have access to Steam Remote Play Together and PlayStation players have Share Play as workarounds. This guide covers everything you need to get two players into Gotham and playing effectively together.

How to Start Co-Op

1

Connect a second controller

Plug in or pair a second controller to your console or PC before or during gameplay.

2

Press Start on the second controller

At any point during gameplay — even mid-mission — press Start (or Options on PS5) on the second controller. The game immediately assigns Player 2 to the companion character in the current level.

3

The screen splits automatically

The screen divides vertically: Player 1 on the left, Player 2 on the right. Both players can now move independently through the level.

4

Drop out any time

Player 2 can drop out by pressing Start again or disconnecting the controller. The game returns to single-player seamlessly.

Drop-in at any time: You do not need to start a mission from the beginning to play co-op. Player 2 can join mid-combat, mid-exploration, or anywhere in the open world. This is particularly useful for co-op collectible farming where one player does the puzzle and the other covers enemies.

How Split-Screen Works

The game uses a vertical split-screen, dividing the screen down the middle. Both players have full independent movement, which means you can explore different parts of a level simultaneously. There is no tether or distance limit — Player 2 can run across the map while Player 1 works on a puzzle.

Puzzle solutions, however, often require coordination. Many two-player puzzles are designed so one player activates something on one side of the screen while the other does the same on their half. When the split-screen gap gets confusing, pause the game and regroup at a shared location first.

No online multiplayer: LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight does not have built-in online co-op. The game is local split-screen only. See the Online section below for workarounds.

Best Character Combinations

The strongest co-op pairings pair one combat-focused character with one utility/mobility character. This covers all mission types — stealth, open combat, and puzzle sections.

Batman + Jim GordonS Tier
Available from Chapter 1

The default pairing and the strongest for Chapters 1–2. Batman handles stealth takedowns and single-target pressure; Gordon's upgraded Rebound Launcher clears entire rooms in one or two shots. Batman covers the stealthy angles, Gordon walks in after and finishes the survivors. This combination has zero weaknesses during early game.

Batman + NightwingS Tier
Available from Chapter 4

The highest-damage co-op pairing. Batman handles focus-intensive single targets while Nightwing's Electrorang Overcharge and Focus Circuit create chain electricity through enemy groups. Best for high-difficulty Dark Knight Mode runs where enemies need to be eliminated quickly before they become dangerous. Both characters have strong stealth tools, so this pairing works in all mission types.

Batman + CatwomanA Tier
Available from Chapter 2

Excellent for stealth-heavy missions. Catwoman's Call Kitty distracts enemy patrols; Batman moves in for stealth takedowns. Catwoman's Distract upgrade (5 chips) is one of the most useful co-op tools in the game since it creates safe windows for Batman to clear entire rooms without being detected.

Robin + NightwingA Tier
Available from Chapters 3–4

Both characters have cable launchers, enabling a unique combo: Robin tethers a small enemy to a Brute (Buddy System challenge), while Nightwing uses the electric cable to shock both simultaneously. This combination also covers the Multitasker Batcave challenge easily — one player holds the cable line while the other throws Birdarangs.

Batgirl + Talia al GhulA Tier
Available from Chapter 4–5

The stealth-specialist pairing. Talia's Teleport Takedown silently eliminates guards while Batgirl hacks terminals and distracts remaining enemies with her drone. Both characters have quiet gadgets and no loud weapons, making this the cleanest approach for players doing stealth-only runs.

Catwoman + Talia al GhulB Tier
Available from Chapters 2 and 5

Fun and chaotic. Talia's Disco Powder makes enemies dance; Catwoman's spinning whip attacks chain through the disoriented group. Low combat efficiency but extremely entertaining. Use for free-roam and open-world crime events where combat stakes are low.

Playing Online (No Native Multiplayer)

The game does not support online co-op natively, but two workarounds let you play with a remote partner:

Steam Remote Play Together (PC)

On Steam, right-click the game in your library, select Remote Play Together, and invite a friend. Steam streams your screen to them and treats their controller input as a second local controller. The guest does not need to own the game. Input latency depends on both players' internet connections — works well on stable connections under 50ms, becomes difficult above 100ms.

PlayStation Share Play (PS5)

On PS5, open a Party and select Share Play from the options. Choose "Give Controller to Friend" — your PS5 treats the remote player's DualSense as a second local controller. Share Play sessions are limited to 60 minutes per session but can be restarted immediately.

Best for online play: Both Remote Play Together and Share Play work well for co-op in slower-paced exploration and puzzle sections. Fast combat becomes more challenging at high latency — prioritize the utility-character role (Jim Gordon, Catwoman, Batgirl) for the higher-latency player, since their gadgets are more forgiving to input delay than Batman or Nightwing's melee combos.

Co-Op Tips & Strategy

  • Divide role responsibilities clearly — one player on combat, one on puzzles/utility. Trying to both do the same thing causes chaos in split-screen
  • Player 1 controls Batman — he is always present and his story dialogue carries the narrative; having the more experienced player on Batman avoids confusion
  • Player 2 takes the support role — Jim Gordon clearing groups, Catwoman distracting guards, or Batgirl hacking terminals all contribute without requiring Batman's stealth precision
  • Split up for collectible hunting — once both players know the area, splitting the vertical split-screen to cover different parts of a level halves the time needed to find WayneTech Caches
  • Coordinate puzzle timing — many two-player puzzles require simultaneous button presses; use voice chat or count down verbally before activating
  • Share upgrades mentally — upgrades unlock for both players, so spending chips on whichever character the second player prefers is a worthwhile co-op investment

Co-Op Collectible Farming

Co-op is genuinely faster for collectible farming than solo play. The split-screen allows both players to cover different parts of a level simultaneously, effectively doubling your scanning area. The most efficient approach:

  1. Activate all District Towers first (requires Batgirl, unlocked in Chapter 4)
  2. Enter co-op with one player as Batgirl (for hacking puzzle caches) and one as Nightwing (for electric puzzle caches)
  3. Split the map — Player 1 takes the top half, Player 2 takes the bottom
  4. Meet at any remaining puzzle caches that need both characters

This approach can cut a district's cache collection time by 40–50% compared to solo play.
