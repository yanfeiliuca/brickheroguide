🔍 Detective Mode

Detective Mode Explained — Every Feature & How to Use It Effectively

Detective Mode is one of the most useful tools in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — and one of the most ignored. Many players activate it when stuck, see a highlight appear, and move on. But Detective Mode does far more than that: it reveals enemy positions through objects, pings nearby collectibles with audio cues, highlights interactive environmental elements, and feeds directly into the stealth system. This guide breaks down every function and explains where it genuinely changes how you play.

How to Activate Detective Mode

PC (Controller)R3 / RSSame as console

Activating Detective Mode triggers a sonar pulse that radiates outward from your character. The effect is temporary but can be re-activated immediately — there is no cooldown. The pulse works even when playing as characters other than Batman.

All Features Explained

👀
Enemy Detection

The sonar pulse reveals the positions of all enemies within range, including those behind walls, in adjacent rooms, and above or below your current floor. Detected enemies appear as orange silhouettes visible through solid surfaces for several seconds after the pulse. This lets you count enemies and plan your approach before entering a room.

🔥
Interactive Object Highlighting

Environmental objects you can interact with — grapple points, breakable walls, pressure plates, levers, and hidden switches — all glow when the sonar pulse passes over them. This is the primary "puzzle hint" function. If you cannot figure out what to do next, activate Detective Mode and look for the highlighted object you have not touched yet.

📥
WayneTech Cache Audio Ping

When a WayneTech Cache is within range, Detective Mode triggers an audio ping in addition to the visual effect. The ping frequency increases as you move closer — like a hot-and-cold proximity indicator. Inside story missions where cache locations are not shown on a map, this audio cue is the primary way to locate caches you have not found yet. Follow the ping's rhythm: slow pinging = far away, rapid pinging = within a few meters.

🔎
Objective Markers

If you lose track of your current objective or miss a waypoint, Detective Mode highlights the next required interaction in the environment. This is particularly useful in open areas where objectives can be on different floor levels from where you are currently standing.

❓
Character Requirement Indicator

If Detective Mode highlights an object but shows a question mark over it instead of an interaction prompt, that object requires a character you have not unlocked yet. The question mark is sometimes replaced by a character portrait if the game can identify which character is needed. This prevents wasting time trying to solve puzzles designed for characters you do not yet have.

Using Detective Mode in Combat

Before engaging any combat encounter, activate Detective Mode from a safe distance. This gives you:

  • Enemy count — know exactly how many enemies are in the room before walking in
  • Enemy type identification — shield-carriers and Brutes show different silhouettes from standard enemies
  • Patrol patterns — detected enemies continue to show through walls for several seconds, letting you observe patrol routes before committing to stealth
  • Environmental hazard markers — explosive canisters and interactive crowd-control elements (pushable crates, swingable chandeliers) are highlighted, letting you plan environmental takedowns
Stealth combat workflow: Activate Detective Mode from cover → count enemies and mark their positions → identify isolated targets for stealth takedowns → execute takedowns before the pulse fades → re-activate before approaching the next group. This loop makes Dark Knight Mode stealth missions significantly more manageable.

Using Detective Mode for Exploration

In open-world Gotham, Detective Mode is your primary tool for finding hidden items between tower activations (which are only available after Chapter 4). Use it:

  • Every time you enter a new area — activate immediately to get a baseline read on what is interactive and what is background decoration
  • When the cache audio ping starts — if you hear the ping while walking through a district, activate Detective Mode and turn slowly until you find the highlighted cache
  • At the top of buildings — caches placed on rooftop equipment are easier to spot from height since the sonar pulse radiates outward, not downward
  • Near locked doors and chained objects — Detective Mode often reveals the power source or switch that unlocks them

Using Detective Mode for Puzzles

Many of the game's environmental puzzles have solutions that are technically visible without Detective Mode but much easier to find with it active. Specifically:

  • Power cable puzzles — cables running from a locked WayneTech Cache to its power source glow brightly under Detective Mode, making the cable path easy to follow even through complex geometry
  • Multi-switch puzzles — when a room requires activating several switches, Detective Mode highlights all of them simultaneously even if some are partially hidden
  • Batgirl hacking terminals — the terminal itself glows blue under Detective Mode before Batgirl interacts with it, distinguishing hackable terminals from similar-looking non-interactive panels

Advanced Uses Most Players Miss

These are the three Detective Mode behaviours that most guides do not cover:

1. The Question Mark Means "Come Back Later"

When Detective Mode shows a question mark over an interactive object, most players assume it is a bug or an inaccessible area. It is not — it means a specific character ability is required. In Free Play (available after completing each mission once), you have access to the full character roster. Items blocked behind a question mark in Story Mode are the ones to revisit in Free Play. Tracking which areas had question marks is a more efficient way to plan Free Play sweeps than walking through the entire mission again hoping to spot locked items you missed.

2. Character Hints in the Portrait

Sometimes the question mark is replaced by a small character portrait showing which specific character is needed. If you see Catwoman's portrait over a lock, you need her lockpick. Batgirl's portrait means a hack terminal. Nightwing's portrait means an electric cable connection. The portrait does not always appear — it depends on whether the game can definitively identify the required ability — but when it does, it eliminates all guesswork.

3. Pulse Reveals Batcave Trophy Locations Mid-Mission

WayneTech Caches are not the only items that ping under Detective Mode. Batcave Trophy animals (escaped zoo animals, in-world hidden objects) also produce proximity audio cues when the pulse passes near them. If you hear the cache ping but cannot find a glowing cache, look for a small hidden animal or trophy item — they produce an identical audio frequency.
