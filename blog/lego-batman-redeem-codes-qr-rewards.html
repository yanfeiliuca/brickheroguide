LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — All Redeem Codes & QR Rewards Explained

Published: May 23, 2026 · 5 min read

If you've started up LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight and spotted a "Redeem Code" option sitting on the main menu, you might be wondering what it's for. Unlike the cheat codes of older LEGO games, this system works differently — and knowing the details will save you time and set realistic expectations for what you can unlock.

The short answer: the Redeem Code system is tied to QR codes found inside select physical LEGO Batman building sets. Scanning those QR codes through your LEGO account generates a one-time code you can enter in-game to unlock exclusive cosmetics. Here's everything confirmed so far, sourced from Game Rant's launch-week coverage.

All Confirmed LEGO Sets & In-Game Rewards

At the time of writing, four Batman LEGO sets include QR codes that unlock in-game rewards. The codes are printed inside the physical building instructions booklets — not on the box — so you'll need to build (or at least open) your set to find them.

LEGO Set Set Number In-Game Reward
Batman Logo 76330 Golden Batman outfit (character skin)
Batman v Superman Batmobile 76331 Batman v Superman Batmobile + golden variant
The Batman Batmobile 76332 The Batman Batmobile + golden variant
Batman & Robin Batmobile 76333 Batman & Robin Batmobile + golden variant

Notice that the Batman Logo set (76330) is the only one that rewards a character outfit rather than a vehicle. The three Batmobile sets each give you both the standard film-accurate version of the car and a golden variant, making them especially attractive for collectors who want to fill out their Batcave garage.

Good news: According to Game Rant, redeem code rewards appear to be bonus cosmetics only — they do not count toward 100% completion or trophy/achievement progress. So if you don't have access to these sets, you won't be locked out of a platinum or full-clear.

How to Redeem Your QR Code (Step by Step)

The process runs through LEGO's official Insiders membership platform before you ever touch the game's main menu. Here's the full flow:

  1. Visit lego.com and sign into your LEGO Insiders account — or create a free one if you don't have it yet.
  2. Click the minifig head icon in the top-right corner to access your My Membership page.
  3. Click the down arrow next to Menu to expand your account options.
  4. Select My Collection, then click Add a set.
  5. Choose Scan QR code and scan the code from inside your LEGO set's building instructions. This generates a unique one-time game code.
  6. Back in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, select Redeem Code from the main menu and enter your code to claim the reward.

The QR codes in the building instructions link directly to your LEGO account and convert into a game-redeemable string. Keep your building instructions safe — you'll need the QR code to generate the code in the first place.

Note on digital purchases: If you purchased a LEGO set and lost the instructions, LEGO.com often hosts digital copies of instruction booklets. However, it's currently unclear whether digital booklet PDFs include a scannable version of the QR code. Physical instructions are the safest option.

Do the Codes Expire?

Yes. According to reporting by Brick Fanatics (via Game Rant), each redeem code is single-use and set to expire on March 1, 2029. That's nearly three years from the game's launch, which is a reasonably generous window, but it's worth redeeming sooner rather than later if you already own one of the qualifying sets. LEGO's official product pages confirm the QR-code digital unlock system, though the official expiration date hasn't been published on LEGO's site directly at the time of writing.

Because each code is one-time use, you won't be able to share a single physical set's code across multiple accounts or transfer it after redemption. Plan accordingly if you're buying a set specifically for the in-game reward.

Is There a Free Reward for Everyone?

Yes — and this one doesn't require purchasing anything. The game includes a LEGO Group Online Survey option buried within the menus. According to Game Rant's coverage, completing this survey rewards any player with a free Stud Cache, no LEGO set purchase required. It's a small bonus, but it's the one genuinely free unlock available at launch for all players regardless of whether they own any physical sets.

To find it, look through the game's settings or extras menus for the survey prompt. The exact menu path may vary slightly depending on your platform.

What These Rewards Mean for the Game

The Redeem Code system reflects a broader trend in gaming — physical merchandise tie-ins that add optional digital bonuses without gating critical content behind paywalls. The rewards here (cosmetic suits, vehicle variants) are genuinely desirable for fans of the film adaptations they represent, but TT Games has kept them out of the core progression loop, which is a player-friendly design choice.

It's also worth noting that the three Batmobile rewards each pull from a specific film: the Batman v Superman Batmobile, the Robert Pattinson-era The Batman Batmobile, and the 1997 Batman & Robin Batmobile. These match the game's central theme of celebrating Batman's entire cinematic history, from the campy to the gritty. If you're a collector of LEGO Batman sets anyway, the in-game reward is a nice bonus on top of an already appealing physical product.

As TT Games potentially expands the redeem code system with additional sets or future DLC, we'll keep this page updated with any new entries. For now, the four confirmed sets represent the full roster of QR-linked rewards at launch.
