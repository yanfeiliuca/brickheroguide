Unlike traditional LEGO games where you can pick from dozens of characters freely, LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight takes a more focused approach — you unlock 7 handcrafted characters across the game's 5 story chapters, each with a distinct gadget set and playstyle. This guide covers every character's unlock condition, gadgets, passive abilities, and best uses.

Character Overview

All 7 characters unlock automatically through story progression — you cannot miss any of them. Batman is always locked to Player 1 and cannot be swapped out. All other characters occupy the Player 2 slot and can be freely switched in the Bat-Cave between missions.

Batman (Bruce Wayne)

The Dark Knight — always Player 1
🔓 Prologue / Chapter 1

You'll play as young Bruce Wayne in the Prologue's flashback sections, but Batman's cowl and full gadget set don't appear until Chapter 1 begins. Batman is permanently locked to the Player 1 slot — he cannot be swapped out.

Batman's combat is Arkham-inspired: counter-attacks, grapple slams, and a free-flow combo system that rewards timing over button mashing. He also gains access to a rotating fleet of Batmobiles as you progress through the story.

Gadgets:

🪃 Batarang 🪝 Batclaw 💥 Explosive Gel 🔍 Detective Vision
Batarang
Standard throw for activating switches, hitting enemies, and triggering LEGO targets at range.
Batclaw
Pulls grapple points and LEGO objects, used in platforming and puzzle interactions.
Explosive Gel
Breaches cracked LEGO walls — look for the orange-glow texture to know where to apply it.
Detective Vision
X-ray, UV, and Thermal modes reveal hidden clues, enemies through walls, and heat signatures.
🔦

Jim Gordon

Commissioner of the GCPD
🔓 Complete Prologue

Jim Gordon is your default Player 2 companion throughout all of Chapter 1. He's the Bat-Family's liaison with law enforcement and brings a utility-focused gadget kit focused on crowd control and puzzle solving rather than pure combat.

🧴 Foam Sprayer 🏀 Rebound Launcher
Foam Sprayer
Seals pipes, stops active machinery, and traps enemies in place with sticky foam. Used constantly in Chapter 1 puzzles.
Rebound Launcher
Fires a projectile that bounces off surfaces — useful for hitting targets around corners and behind cover.

💡 Tip: Gordon's Foam Sprayer is surprisingly powerful as a crowd-control tool in combat — freezing enemies in place gives Batman easy combo opportunities.

🐱

Catwoman (Selina Kyle)

The Cat Burglar of Gotham
🔓 Chapter 2 — "Mines" Mission

Catwoman makes her entrance during the Mines mission in Chapter 2 — she's impossible to miss. She arrives in style by stealing Jim Gordon's Batclaw, which gives you a hint at her thieving nature. Catwoman has the most passive abilities of any character, making her uniquely flexible in both combat and exploration.

🪢 Whip 🐱 Call Kitty 🧗 Wall Climbing 🪟 Glass Cutter 🔐 Safe Cracker
Whip
Rotates special LEGO screws and LEGO bricks, pulls distant objects, and can whip-grab enemies in combat.
Call Kitty
Summons a LEGO cat to crawl through small vents and tight spaces inaccessible to other characters.
Wall Climbing
Passive ability — Catwoman can scale special textured walls marked with cat-claw scratch marks.
Glass Cutter (Passive)
Can slice through reinforced glass panels that block other characters.
Safe Cracker (Passive)
Opens locked safes found throughout Gotham — often containing Studs, collectibles, or Bat-Family tokens.
🐦

Robin (Dick Grayson)

The Boy Wonder
🔓 Enter Chapter 3

Robin unlocks automatically when you begin Chapter 3 and visit Haley's Circus. Dick Grayson starts the chapter in civilian clothes, but after a training montage the Dynamic Duo is officially born. Robin plays similarly to Batman but with key multi-target and mobility upgrades.

🪃 Birdarang 🪢 Cable Launcher
Birdarang
Like the Batarang, but can target multiple enemies or switches in a single throw — great for clearing groups and activating twin-target puzzles.
Cable Launcher
Creates a tightrope-style cable between two anchor points — walk across it to reach platforms or use it as a zipline in combat.

💡 Tip: The Cable Launcher solves several puzzles where you need to create a crossing between two buildings. If you see two glowing anchor points across a gap, that's Robin's cue.

💻

Batgirl (Barbara Gordon)

Tech genius of the Bat-Family
🔓 Enter Chapter 4

Batgirl joins the team at the very start of Chapter 4 — without her father Jim Gordon's knowledge. Her gadget set is the most technology-focused of the roster, opening up puzzle solutions that no other character can access. She fights in a style similar to Batman and Robin but with a more technical edge.

💻 Hackarang 🚁 Drone
Hackarang
A tech-enhanced batarang that interfaces with electronic panels, overloads enemy tech, and distracts robot or electronic enemies.
Drone
Deploys a flying drone to scan areas from above, navigate arrow-marked drone paths, and reach switches in inaccessible locations.
⚡

Nightwing

Dick Grayson — grown up and electric
🔓 Chapter 4 — "Mr. Freeze" Final Mission

Nightwing is Dick Grayson's evolution as a hero — he unlocks as a separate character during the final mission of Chapter 4. You still retain Robin as a swappable option. Nightwing's gadgets are electrified upgrades to Robin's toolkit, letting him jumpstart electronics and create electrified cables for puzzle solving.

⚡ Electrorang 🔌 Electric Cable Launcher
Electrorang
An electrified Birdarang that can activate electrical panels, stun enemies, and charge LEGO machinery that Robin's version cannot.
Electric Cable Launcher
Creates a live electrical conduit between two anchor points — conducts power across the cable to charge distant devices.

💡 Tip: Nightwing's electric gadgets are the only way to power certain machines in Chapter 4 and 5 puzzles. If Robin's Cable Launcher didn't solve it, try switching to Nightwing.

🥷

Talia al Ghul

League of Shadows — the stealth specialist
🔓 Enter Chapter 5

Talia is briefly playable during the Prologue in flashback training sequences, but she doesn't become a permanent roster character until Chapter 5 begins. She's the most mechanically distinct character in the game — the only one built around stealth rather than combat, and the only one who doesn't fit the "bat-gadget" template.

💨 Blowdarts 🥷 Ninja Dash
Blowdarts
Puts enemies to sleep silently — essential for stealth sections where alerting guards triggers a combat restart.
Ninja Dash
A short-range teleport ability — lets Talia cross gaps, dodge attacks, and reach platform positions the other characters can't jump to.

💡 Tip: Talia is the best character for Free Play stealth sections. Her Ninja Dash means she can bypass many platforming challenges entirely, saving time during mission replay for collectibles.

Secret Bonus Characters (Community Discovery)

Beyond the 7 official playable characters and the confirmed September 18, 2026 DLC duo (Joker & Harley Quinn — see our full Mayhem Collection breakdown), the community has discovered three additional secret characters hidden in the game’s mission-select system: Alfred Pennyworth, Lucius Fox, and Bruce Wayne (civilian suit). These are not listed anywhere in the game’s UI and were uncovered by players digging into mission-select mechanics, confirmed by community reporting in June 2026.

Note: These secret characters work only in free-roam Gotham. They cannot be used in story missions or replayed chapters. None have combat abilities — they are purely a novelty unlock for open-world exploration.

How to Unlock (The D-Pad Trick)

The method is consistent across all three: enter the specific mission section where the character appears, select them via the character swap screen, hold Down on the D-pad for ~2 seconds, and exit the mission. The game registers the character as "selected" and adds them to your free-roam roster on exit.

Alfred Pennyworth

Mission: The Mimes → Section: “Home Sweet Home”

Attacks with a broom. No gadgets. Fully driveable vehicles. Cannot grapple or glide.

Lucius Fox

Mission: Wayne Industries → Section: “WayneTech R&D”

No combat moves (flees enemies). Unique ability: can access WayneTech terminals that normally require Batman's authorization — useful for collectible hunting.

Bruce Wayne

Mission: Wayne Industries → Section: “WayneTech R&D” (separate run from Lucius)

No combat moves. Unique interaction: can enter the locked Wayne Manor drawing room, which contains a secret easter egg room accessible to no other character.

For the full step-by-step unlock guide for all three characters, see our dedicated blog post: 3 Secret Playable Characters You Probably Missed.

Character Tips & Best Practices

Switching characters: In Free Roam, open the Start menu → navigate to the Character tab → select your Player 2 companion. In co-op, each player controls their own character slot independently.

Batman is always Player 1: You cannot remove Batman from the Player 1 slot. All story missions and collectibles are designed around Batman being present at all times.

Gadget synergies in co-op: The game rewards character pairing. Batman + Catwoman is the most versatile duo for exploration (his Explosive Gel + her Call Kitty covers most puzzle types). Robin + Batgirl is ideal for tech-heavy Chapter 4 missions. Nightwing + Talia is the stealth powerhouse for Chapter 5.

No missable unlocks: All 7 characters unlock through mandatory story progression. You can complete all character-specific puzzles in Free Play after the credits roll if you missed any during your first playthrough.
