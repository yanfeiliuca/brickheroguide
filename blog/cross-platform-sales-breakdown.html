LEGO Batman Legacy Sells 1.2 Million Copies in Week One: PS5 Leads, Xbox Beats Steam

📅 June 8, 2026  |  ⏱ 5 min read  |  BrickHeroGuide

Analytics firm Alinea Analytics has released its first-week sales estimates for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, and the numbers paint an interesting picture: approximately 1.2 million copies sold across PlayStation 5, Xbox Series, and PC in the game's opening week, generating roughly $83 million in gross revenue. According to Alinea, the game is performing in line with what they'd call a "household title doing what household titles do" — solid, but not spectacular by blockbuster standards.

Dig into the platform split, however, and there's a genuinely surprising data point: Xbox outsold Steam. For a major multiplatform title in 2026, that's an unusual outcome — and it sheds light on exactly who is playing this game and where.

📊 Source: Sales estimates from Alinea Analytics, reported by FRVR and Windows Central. These are third-party estimates, not official figures from Warner Bros. Games.

PlayStation's dominance is the least surprising part of this story. PS5 has the largest active install base among the current-gen consoles, and LEGO games have historically skewed toward PlayStation in the European market, where Sony has a particularly strong foothold. A 51% share for PS5 is almost exactly what you'd predict from base rates alone.

Why Xbox Beat Steam: A Family Game on a Family Platform

The more newsworthy finding is Xbox outperforming Steam by roughly 40,000 copies. On most multiplatform releases these days — especially games with a PC-savvy audience — Steam tends to be competitive with or superior to Xbox. Not here.

Alinea offered a clear explanation: LEGO games are, fundamentally, household titles. They're bought for living-room televisions, played on the family console, and shared between parents and kids. Xbox, with its living-room positioning and Game Pass ecosystem, is a natural home for that audience. PC gaming, by contrast, skews toward a demographic that is older and more solo-focused — a different profile from the co-op family audience that defines the LEGO fanbase.

📋 Note on Epic Games Store: The Alinea figures reportedly cover Steam sales only for PC. Legacy of the Dark Knight is also available on the Epic Games Store, which could add a further (unquantified) slice to the total PC install base. The $83M revenue figure is believed to span all platforms including EGS.

This pattern isn't unique to LEGO Batman. LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga similarly showed strong console dominance over PC when its sales data was analyzed. For TT Games, the lesson is consistent: build for the couch, and the console audience will show up. Steam is a secondary market.

How Does 1.2 Million Compare to Expectations?

The obvious benchmark is LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga, which sold approximately 5 million copies by June 2022 — just two months after launch in April of that year. Against that bar, 1.2 million in week one looks like a significant shortfall.

But context matters. Skywalker Saga was the first major LEGO title after a multi-year drought, carried by the most recognized entertainment franchise on Earth, and had been in development for several years with enormous pre-launch coverage. Legacy of the Dark Knight, by comparison, was announced in August 2025 and launched roughly nine months later. It didn't have the same years of anticipation or the same cultural gravitational pull as Star Wars.

Alinea's own read on the numbers was notably measured: "Our launch estimates for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight are healthy without being spectacular — the audience composition data tells us this is a household title doing what household titles do." They also flagged that release timing was a headwind, with the game launching in close proximity to Forza Horizon 6, which drew away a chunk of the Xbox audience during the same window.

🏒 Holiday Long Tail: LEGO games are historically strong sellers throughout the holiday season, often matching or exceeding their launch week sales during November–December discounts. Skywalker Saga, for instance, saw sustained chart appearances years after launch. The 1.2 million week-one figure is likely the floor, not the ceiling.

The Switch 2 Wild Card

One significant variable is still missing from these numbers: Nintendo Switch 2. Warner Bros. Games confirmed in early June 2026 that Legacy of the Dark Knight will launch on Switch 2 on September 18, 2026 — the same day as the Mayhem Collection DLC across all platforms. Nintendo's audience has historically been highly receptive to LEGO titles; the original Nintendo Switch versions of Skywalker Saga and LEGO Marvel contributed meaningfully to that game's long-term totals.

If the Switch 2 version performs at even half the rate of the PS5 version, it could push the game's cumulative total well past 2 million copies by year's end. The September release window — just before the holiday shopping season — is well-timed to maximize those numbers.

The Bigger Picture: A Solid Launch, Not a Breakout

Taken together, the 1.2 million figure represents a real commercial success for TT Games after several years away from a major release — but it also reveals the ceiling that a non-Star Wars LEGO game currently faces. Batman is popular, but he's not a universal cultural phenomenon in the same league as the franchise that defined a generation of LEGO games.

The more heartening signal is qualitative: with a 97% positive rating on Steam and a Metacritic score of 84 — the highest ever for a LEGO game — player satisfaction is genuinely exceptional. Word-of-mouth from happy players, combined with the September DLC drop and the Switch 2 launch, gives the game a meaningful runway to grow beyond its week-one numbers.

For fans, the takeaway is simple: the game is selling well enough to support the Mayhem Collection DLC, the Switch 2 port, and hopefully more post-launch content. The lights are on at TT Games for the foreseeable future.
