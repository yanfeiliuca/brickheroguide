Beginner's Guide: What to Know Before You Play

Last updated: May 19, 2026 · Based on pre-release coverage and confirmed game details

Jumping into LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight for the first time? Unlike previous LEGO games, this fourth installment makes significant changes — a smaller roster of deeply developed characters, Arkham-inspired combat, and an open-world Gotham built in Unreal Engine 5. Here's what you should know before starting.

1. Understand the Combat System

Combat in Legacy of the Dark Knight takes heavy inspiration from the Batman: Arkham series' freeflow system, but adapted to be more approachable. Rather than trying to replicate Arkham's technical depth, the game encourages using a combination of abilities and gadgets (like batarangs and environmental actions) during fights.

If you've played Arkham games, the rhythm will feel familiar but more forgiving. If you haven't, don't worry — the game is designed to be accessible to players of all skill levels.

Tip: Experiment with different gadgets and environmental interactions during combat. The game rewards creative approaches over button-mashing.

2. Master Traversal in Open-World Gotham

Gotham City is a fully open world you can explore freely. Movement around the city uses grappling hooks, gliding, and vehicles like the Batmobile. Get comfortable chaining these traversal methods together — grappling to a rooftop, then gliding across the street, then summoning the Batmobile to drive to the next district.

3. Use Each Character's Unique Abilities

Unlike previous LEGO games that offered hundreds of characters with similar movesets, Legacy of the Dark Knight features only 7 playable characters, each with distinct abilities:

  • Batman — Batarangs, grappling hook, gliding, multiple Batsuits
  • Jim Gordon — Foam gun, police-related interactions
  • Batgirl — Unique combat and tech abilities
  • Nightwing — Acrobatic movement, escrima sticks
  • Robin — Technology and puzzle-solving
  • Catwoman — Whip, stealth, wall climbing
  • Talia al Ghul — League of Shadows training

TT Games deliberately chose a smaller roster to make each character feel meaningfully different. Learn what each hero can do — you'll need all of them.

4. Explore the Open World Between Missions

Between story missions, take time to explore Gotham City's open world. Previews have described the game's scope as significantly larger than previous TT Games titles, with a more immersive city than ever before. Hidden secrets, side activities, and collectibles are scattered throughout the city.

5. Customize the Batcave

Your home base, the Batcave, can be customized with vehicles, Batsuits, trophies, and collectibles found throughout the game. It serves as your hub between missions and reflects your progress. Check in regularly to see what new customization options are available.

6. Play Local Co-Op With a Friend

The game supports local couch co-op (two players on the same screen). There is no online multiplayer. If you have a friend or family member who wants to join, grab a second controller — LEGO games are known for being great co-op experiences.

7. Pay Attention to Character Skins

The game features character skins inspired by Batman actors across film history, including Michelle Pfeiffer's Catwoman (Batman Returns), Heath Ledger's Joker (The Dark Knight), Danny DeVito's Penguin (Batman Returns), and Jeffrey Wright's Jim Gordon (The Batman). These aren't just cosmetic — they reflect the game's celebration of the entire Batman franchise.
