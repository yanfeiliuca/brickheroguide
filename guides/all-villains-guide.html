Rogues Gallery Overview

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight pulls from decades of Batman history — comics, films, animated series, and the Arkham games — to assemble the largest and most diverse villain roster in any LEGO Batman title to date. 14 villains are confirmed in the shipped game, ranging from iconic top-tier threats like the Joker and Bane to deep-cut fan favourites like Egghead and Condiment King.

Two villains from this list — Catwoman and Talia al Ghul — cross over as playable heroes. Joker and Harley Quinn join as playable characters via the Mayhem Collection DLC on September 18, 2026. The remaining cast serve as bosses, open-world bounty targets, and story antagonists across the game's six chapters (plus a prologue).

Community tip: Looking for playable villain content? Joker and Harley Quinn join as fully playable characters with their own movesets in the Mayhem Collection DLC (September 18, 2026). Dataminers have also reportedly uncovered additional Task Force X characters in the game files — check our DLC datamine breakdown for details.

The 3 Main Antagonists

While all 14 villains have a presence in the game, three are elevated to main antagonist status — the driving forces of the central story across the six chapters (plus prologue):

  • The Joker — The primary narrative villain, with an origin tied to the Red Hood gang storyline from the comics.
  • Bane — The muscle of the League of Shadows faction, voiced by Matt Berry in an exaggerated take on the Tom Hardy interpretation.
  • Ra's al Ghul — The mastermind behind Bruce Wayne's early training and the overarching threat connecting the Prologue to the main campaign.

Every Villain — Detailed Breakdown

🤷 The Joker

Main Antagonist
Hideout: Flugelheim Museum Affiliation: Red Hood Gang

The Joker is the game's central villain and gets a mythology twist that ties his origin directly to the Red Hood gang storyline from the comics — the same origin that inspired the 2019 Joker film. His design borrows from multiple film portrayals simultaneously, blending visual nods to both Jack Nicholson's purple-suited showman and Heath Ledger's anarchic chaos agent. His base of operations is the Flugelheim Museum, a direct callback to the Joker's art heist in Tim Burton's 1989 Batman. He becomes a playable character via the Mayhem Collection DLC on September 18, 2026.

🤹 Harley Quinn

DLC — Sep 2026
Real Name: Dr. Harleen Quinzel Affiliation: The Joker

Harley Quinn appears in the base game's story as a supporting presence alongside the Joker but is not a playable boss in the main campaign. She becomes fully playable — with her own unique abilities, gadgets, and takedowns — through the Mayhem Collection DLC, which launches on September 18, 2026 for Deluxe Edition owners (or as a separate purchase for Standard Edition players). The DLC also adds a new story mission where Joker and Harley break out of Arkham Asylum and cause chaos across Gotham.

💪 Bane

Main Antagonist
Hideout: Amusement Mile Affiliation: League of Shadows

Bane is voiced by Matt Berry — a casting choice that leans heavily into parody of the Tom Hardy interpretation from The Dark Knight Rises, with Berry's distinctive theatrical delivery playing up the "masked mumbling" that Bane is famous for. His design combines the venom-tube look from the Arkham games with the tactical vest silhouette from the films. As a League of Shadows member, Bane's story ties directly to Ra's al Ghul's plan and provides some of the game's most demanding combat encounters. His wanted poster lists "Venom Muscle Enhancers" and "Masked Mumbling."

⚔️ Ra's al Ghul

Main Antagonist
Hideout: Nanda Parbat Affiliation: League of Shadows

Ra's al Ghul is the connective thread between the Prologue and the main campaign. As the head of the League of Shadows, he oversees Bruce Wayne's early training — a relationship that eventually fractures into open conflict. His classic design (flowing cape, goatee, martial grandeur) is rendered faithfully in LEGO brick form. Ra's is not a chapter boss in the conventional sense; instead, he functions as the overarching puppet-master whose machinations drive other villains' actions across multiple chapters.

🐈 Catwoman

Also Playable Hero
Real Name: Selina Kyle Hideout: Shreck's Department Store

Catwoman occupies the classic anti-hero slot, introduced as an antagonist during the Shreck's Department Store mission in Chapter 2 — a direct Batman Returns reference. She steals Gordon's Batclaw on her debut, signalling her gadget-heavy playstyle. She transitions into the playable roster during Chapter 2's "Mines" mission, where her whip, wall-crawling, and cat-calling abilities open puzzles and traversal routes that no other character can replicate. She is one of seven permanently playable heroes in the base game.

⛈️ The Penguin

Story Boss
Real Name: Oswald Cobblepot Hideout: The Iceberg Lounge

Oswald Cobblepot wields his trademark umbrella as both a weapon and a symbol of authority. He operates from the Iceberg Lounge, Gotham's most exclusive criminal social club. A political subplot runs alongside his criminal empire: Penguin is actively running for Gotham mayor while juggling his underworld operations, a plotline that intersects with Harvey Dent's fall from grace. His boss encounter involves the Lounge's elaborate defences and a fight sequence across its icy interior.

❄️ Mr. Freeze

Story Boss — Chapter 4
Real Name: Dr. Victor Fries Hideout: Gotham City Observatory

Mr. Freeze is staked out at the Gotham City Observatory and delivers one of the game's most praised boss encounters, according to multiple reviewers. His design and tone draw directly from the Arkham games' tragic take on the character — cold, methodical, and driven by grief rather than pure malice. Mechanically, his fight is the trigger for Nightwing's unlock: Dick Grayson graduates to his Nightwing persona immediately after the Mr. Freeze chapter concludes. Expect environmental ice hazards and a multi-phase encounter requiring gadget switching.

⚖️ Two-Face

Story Boss — Chapter 3
Real Name: Harvey Dent Hideout: City Hall

Harvey Dent's arc traces his fall from Gotham's beloved District Attorney to the coin-flipping villain holed up in City Hall. Rather than beginning the game as Two-Face, the story covers the transition — exploring what breaks a man before showing the monster he becomes. His boss encounter at City Hall involves a courtroom setting and his trademark dual-personality decision mechanics, where the outcome of certain encounters is determined by chance. Reviewers specifically praised the Two-Face sequence for its tonal balance of LEGO humour and genuine tragedy.

🌿 Poison Ivy

Story Boss — Chapter 3
Real Name: Pamela Isley Hideout: Gotham City Botanical Gardens

Pamela Isley begins as a botanist pushing for a greener Gotham before her transformation into Poison Ivy. Her territory — the Gotham City Botanical Gardens — becomes one of the game's most visually distinctive environments, with LEGO plants and vines overtaking the building's architecture. Her wanted poster lists "Monster Plants" and "Persuasive Perfumes," hinting at a boss fight that involves both environmental plant hazards and a crowd-control mechanic tied to her pheromone abilities. She and Two-Face share Chapter 3's villain spotlight.

❓ The Riddler

Open-World Hunt
Real Name: Edward Nygma Hideout: Old Gotham South

The Riddler brings back the trophy-hunt mechanic from the Arkham series, seeding collectible Riddler question marks, trophies, and riddles throughout Gotham's open world. His base is in Old Gotham South, but his influence stretches across every island of the city. Solving his riddles and collecting trophies is core to the 100% completion checklist. Unlike most villains, the Riddler's final confrontation isn't simply a combat encounter — solving a series of environmental logic puzzles is required before he can be taken down. See our 100% Completion Guide for all Riddler trophy locations.

🔥 Firefly

Story Boss — Chapter 4
Real Name: Garfield Lynns Hideout: Gotham City Museum

Garfield Lynns operates out of the Gotham City Museum using a jetpack to set fires across Gotham's rooftops. His story appearances centre on aerial chases and rooftop encounters, making him one of the more kinetic boss fights in the Chapter 4 sequence. He appears in promotional trailers escaping by air after torching the museum — suggesting the encounter involves both ground-level firefighting and a pursuit component. Batgirl's Drone gadget (unlocked in Chapter 4) is particularly useful for tracking Firefly during his aerial phases.

🦁 Talia al Ghul

Also Playable — Chapter 5
Affiliation: League of Shadows Unlocks as hero: Start of Chapter 5

Talia al Ghul walks the same line as Catwoman — villain by alignment, playable hero by gameplay. She's briefly playable during the Prologue's League of Shadows training sequences before joining the permanent roster at the start of Chapter 5, following the Tumbler Chase mission. Her focus on stealth (Ninja Dash, Blowdarts) makes her the most lethal melee fighter in the roster. In story terms, she bridges the gap between her father Ra's al Ghul's grand plan and Batman's personal history with the League.

🥚 Egghead

Easter Egg Cameo
Origin: 1966 Batman TV series Spotted: Gotham Prison

Egghead is a deep cut from the 1966 Batman television series, where he was originally played by Vincent Price as a self-proclaimed genius with an egg-themed criminal persona. His inclusion in Legacy of the Dark Knight — spotted in prison garb in marketing materials — is a direct nod to the game's love of the Adam West era, which also surfaces in several of Batman's available Batsuits. He's not a boss fight; he's a reward for eagle-eyed players who appreciate the franchise's long history. Look for him during any prison-set story sequence.

🌹 Condiment King

Comic Relief Cameo
Real Name: Mitchell Mayo Origin: Batman: The Animated Series Weapon: Ketchup & Mustard Guns

Condiment King is the Rogues Gallery's joke pick — the D-list villain originally created for Batman: The Animated Series who attacks enemies with ketchup and mustard guns. His inclusion is pure LEGO franchise DNA: the series has always found ways to resurrect the most obscure characters for comic relief. He roams Gotham as an open-world encounter rather than a proper boss fight, but defeating him (which requires somehow taking his condiment arsenal seriously) is a small side activity with its own reward. A favourite among fans of the animated series.

League of Shadows Faction

Three villains share the League of Shadows faction tag, forming the game's secondary antagonist bloc and shaping a major portion of the Prologue and main campaign:

  • Ra's al Ghul — Faction leader, Nanda Parbat. Oversees Bruce Wayne's training before their inevitable conflict.
  • Bane — Faction enforcer, Amusement Mile. Executes Ra's al Ghul's ground operations in Gotham.
  • Talia al Ghul — Faction insider and eventual Bat-Family member. Her loyalty to her father versus her relationship with Batman is the faction's central tension.
Story note: The League of Shadows storyline connects the Prologue (Bruce's training) to the final acts of the main campaign. Understanding their hierarchy helps make sense of character motivations across chapters — particularly Bane's seemingly independent attacks on Gotham, which are eventually revealed to be coordinated with Ra's al Ghul's broader plan.

DLC Villains: Joker & Harley Quinn (September 18, 2026)

The Mayhem Collection DLC, launching September 2026, adds Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters — the eighth and ninth additions to the roster. Each brings:

  • A unique set of abilities and gadgets distinct from the base-game heroes
  • A new story mission: Joker and Harley break out of Arkham Asylum and cause chaos across Gotham's streets
  • The Sinister Pack: seven new suits, five new Batcave props, and one new Batmobile
  • Access to "Mayhem Mode" — a new gameplay mode with expanded chaos mechanics

The Mayhem Collection is included with the Deluxe Edition (SRP $89.99) or available separately for Standard Edition owners. Dataminers have also reportedly uncovered references to over a dozen Task Force X characters — including Deadshot, Deathstroke, and Captain Boomerang — in the game's files, though TT Games has not confirmed additional DLC beyond the Mayhem Collection. Tips for Boss Fights

Each major villain boss fight has a specific character or gadget that makes the encounter significantly easier. Red flash attacks are always unblockable — dodge instead of countering when you see the red glow:

  • The Joker (Final Boss): Three distinct phases. In phase one, use the Batclaw to interrupt gadget throws mid-air. Phase two introduces electric floor panels — Robin's insulated boots negate the damage entirely. Phase three is pure combo pressure; keep the counter chain alive to prevent Joker from resetting. Do not let him reach a fallen henchman — he revives them to restore his own health if left alone too long.
  • Ra's al Ghul: His Lazarus Pit revival mechanic resets his health once per fight. The revival is triggered if you knock him out without first destroying the Lazarus Pit terminal at the edge of the arena. Destroy the terminal first — it takes about 10 hits — before delivering the finishing combo. Talia al Ghul's Shadow Dash is uniquely effective for closing distance during his teleport phase.
  • Bane: Venom-enhanced Bane is resistant to direct hits. Use the Batclaw to strip his back armour before attacking. Nightwing's Electric Cable Launcher can stun him mid-charge. When he enters Venom Overdrive (turns red), avoid all contact and wait for the cooldown — countering during Overdrive triggers a one-hit knockdown regardless of your health.
  • Mr. Freeze: Explosive Gel (Batman) shatters his ice barriers. Use Batgirl's Drone to reveal weak points in his Freeze Truck armour. His ice beam has a 1.5-second wind-up; the moment you see his shoulder cannon rise, roll sideways rather than backwards — the beam tracks horizontal movement poorly.
  • The Penguin: His umbrellas deploy different effects (electric, explosive, toxic) in a rotating sequence. Watch which one he opens before closing to melee range — the toxic umbrella leaves a hazard patch on the floor that lasts 10 seconds. The fight's second phase introduces Iceberg Lounge security drones; prioritise them with Batman's Remote Batarang before re-engaging Penguin.
  • Two-Face: Keep your combo meter high — the coin-flip mechanic can interrupt his attack patterns mid-combo, so sustained pressure is key. He always defends his left side (Harvey Dent side) more heavily; target his scarred right side for higher damage outputs.
  • Poison Ivy: Switch to Robin's Cable Launcher to navigate above her plant hazards. Her pheromone clouds are avoidable by staying elevated. Her vines have a 3-second recharge after each attack — that window is your best opening for sustained hits.
  • Firefly: Use Batgirl's Drone to track his jetpack fuel canisters — hitting them triggers a phase transition faster than direct attacks. He strafes in a predictable figure-eight pattern; position yourself at the centre point and let him fly into your Batarang throws rather than chasing him.
  • The Riddler: His boss trophies are missable during story — explore each area thoroughly before engaging each phase, as some trophies require character gadgets that may not be available in story mode. Return in Free Play with Catwoman's lockpick for sealed question-mark boxes.
Universal boss tip — unblockable attacks: Every boss in the game has at least one red-flash unblockable move. When you see the red glow on an enemy, dodge immediately — blocking or countering does nothing and you will take full damage. Mastering dodge timing on red-flash attacks is the single biggest skill gap between players who find boss fights easy and those who find them frustrating.
Free Play tip: Many villain encounters in story mode can only be 100%-completed in Free Play, where you can bring any character's gadget to areas originally restricted to story-assigned characters. If a Riddler trophy or boss challenge feels inaccessible, try returning in Free Play mode.
