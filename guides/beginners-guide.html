Beginner's Guide: 10 Things to Know Before You Start

Jumping into LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight for the first time? This Gotham adventure is packed with secrets, collectibles, and surprises around every corner. Whether you're a LEGO game veteran or a first-timer, these 10 essential tips will help you make the most of your experience from the very start.

1. Explore Everything Before Moving On

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight rewards curiosity. Each area is filled with hidden breakable objects, secret paths, and environmental puzzles. Before following the story marker to the next objective, take a moment to look around, smash objects, and check behind structures. Many collectibles are hidden in plain sight.

Tip: Look for glowing outlines on objects — these indicate interactive elements you can build or destroy for rewards.

2. Switch Characters Often

Each member of the Bat-family has unique abilities that are essential for solving different puzzles and reaching hidden areas. Unlike previous LEGO games with massive rosters, Legacy of the Dark Knight focuses on 7 deeply developed characters, each with upgradeable skill trees. Get comfortable switching between them regularly.

3. Smash Every Object You See

This is LEGO 101, but it bears repeating. Breaking objects drops studs — the game's primary currency. Early on, every stud counts for purchasing upgrades at the Batcave. Some objects also drop Instruction Pages, which are key collectibles for unlocking vehicle and Batcave upgrades.

4. Prioritize Batcave Upgrades Early

The Batcave is your central hub, and investing in upgrades early pays dividends throughout the entire game. Focus on unlocking the Vehicle Display first for extra stud bonuses, then prioritize the gadget workbench for expanded character abilities.

5. Don't Worry About 100% on Your First Run

Many collectibles in each mission require abilities you won't have during your first playthrough. Play through the story first to unlock all characters and abilities, then revisit missions in Free Play mode to mop up everything you missed.

Important: Some minikits can only be reached with specific character abilities. Don't waste time trying to reach them in Story mode if you don't have the right character yet.

6. Understand the Currency System

The game has two main currencies: WayneTech Chips (for upgrading combat, traversal, and gadgets) and Skill Bricks (for unlocking new abilities in each character's skill tree). Both are earned through gameplay, but knowing where to spend them wisely makes a huge difference.

For a detailed breakdown, check our WayneTech Chips & Skill Bricks Currency Guide.

7. Use Photo Mode for Hidden Clues

The game's Photo Mode isn't just for screenshots — it can actually help you spot hidden objects and breakable walls that are hard to see during normal gameplay. Zoom in on suspicious areas to reveal secrets.

8. Co-op Unlocks Bonus Areas

Playing with a friend in local co-op isn't just more fun — certain areas in the game require two players to activate simultaneously. If you're aiming for 100% completion, you'll need a co-op partner for at least some missions.

9. Red Bricks Are Game-Changers

Finding Red Bricks unlocks powerful extras like stud multipliers (2x, 4x, 6x, 8x, 10x), invincibility, and other game-changing modifiers. The stud multipliers stack, so finding all five multiplier Red Bricks means you'll earn studs at an absurd 3,840x rate. For all locations, see our Red Bricks Guide.

10. Replay Missions in Free Play

After completing the story, Free Play mode lets you return to any mission with any unlocked character. This is essential for 100% completion, as many collectibles require specific character abilities you didn't have the first time around. Check our Full Walkthrough for mission-by-mission collectible guides.
