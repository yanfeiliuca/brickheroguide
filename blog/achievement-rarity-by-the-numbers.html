By the Numbers: Only 5.4% of Players Have Every LEGO Batman Legacy Achievement

Published: July 19, 2026 · 7 min read

Steam keeps a public scoreboard for every game it sells, and two months after launch, LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight's scoreboard tells a clearer story about how people actually play the game than any review can. Valve's global achievement stats page for the title breaks down, achievement by achievement, exactly what percentage of the Steam player base has earned each one. The gap between the top and bottom of that list is enormous — and it lines up almost perfectly with our own Trophy Guide's finding that the game is easy to platinum in theory (3/10 difficulty, no missables) but still rarely finished in full.

Our site currently rates the game around a week and a half old as far as Steam concurrency goes: SteamCharts puts the 30-day average at roughly 14,570 concurrent players, down from a May 2026 launch-month average of about 15,045 — a gentle 3% monthly decline, well within the normal post-launch curve for a single-player LEGO title. The all-time concurrent peak sits at 33,053. Against that base, here's what the achievement data shows.

52 Achievements, One Steep Curve

The game ships with 52 Steam achievements, matching the 52 trophies on PS5 tracked in our Trophy Guide. According to Steam's official global stats page, the easiest achievement in the entire list — "Beware the Gray Ghost!" for completing Bruce's Gray Ghost costume — has been earned by 95.8% of players who've opened the game. That's about as close to universal as achievement data gets. From there, the curve drops fast:

Nothing unusual there — those are the achievements tied to the opening hours, before a player has had the chance to bounce off the game or move on to something else.

Where the Story Mode Crowd Thins Out

The most useful read on player retention comes from the five story chapter-completion achievements, since Steam tracks each one individually and they form a clean funnel:

Milestone% of Players
Complete Chapter One ("One Bad Day")63.6%
Complete Chapter Two ("Do I look like I'm joking?")46.3%
Complete Chapter Three ("The Dynamic Duo")36.8%
Complete Chapter Four ("An Endless Winter")31.1%
Complete Chapter Five ("Knightfall")27.1%
Complete Story Mode ("A hero can be anyone.")26.4%

Roughly two-thirds of players who start the game finish Chapter One, but fewer than half make it through Chapter Two, and only about one in four ever see the credits. That's a much steeper drop-off than the game's difficulty rating would suggest — TT Games' LEGO titles have never been about combat challenge, so the attrition here looks less like players getting stuck and more like players setting the game down between sessions and not coming back, which tracks with the length estimates in our How Long to Beat piece.

And at the very bottom: "I am Batman" — the achievement for collecting every single achievement in the game — sits at just 5.4%. That means roughly 1 in 19 players who've touched the game on Steam has gone the full distance, which is a notably small full-completion rate for a title our own guides rate as mechanically forgiving.

The One Everyone Misses: Collecting Evidence

The most interesting entry on that list isn't a grind achievement at all. "Collecting Evidence" just requires rolling the Photo Mode camera past 20 degrees — something that takes about five seconds once you know Photo Mode exists. Its rarity is a discovery problem, not a difficulty problem: our Photo Mode guide covers exactly how to trigger it, and it's the single fastest achievement on this list to knock out once you know where to look.

What This Says About "Easy" vs. "Finished"

Every collectible-clearing achievement in the Under-10% Club maps directly onto content our Collectibles Guide and WayneTech Upgrades Guide already track in detail — 247+ collectibles split across 121 Riddler Trophies and 200 WayneTech Caches, per our internal fact sheet. None of that requires combat skill; it requires time, a map, and the patience to backtrack across all four Gotham islands. The Steam data backs up what our Trophy Guide has said since launch: this isn't a hard platinum, it's a long one, and the achievement curve shows most players stop well before the finish line rather than getting stuck on it.

Bottom line: If you've finished the story and are wondering whether full completion is worth chasing, the numbers say you'd be in a genuinely small club — under 6% of the Steam player base gets there. Our 100% Completion Guide and Trophy Guide are built to make that climb faster than the average player's, since neither requires anything close to precision combat play.

Quick Recap

  • 52 Steam achievements total; easiest ("Beware the Gray Ghost!") sits at 95.8%.
  • Story completion funnels from 63.6% (Chapter One) down to 26.4% (Story Mode complete).
  • Twelve achievements sit under 12%, almost all tied to full collectible or upgrade completion.
  • "I am Batman" (all achievements) is the rarest at 5.4%.
  • "Collecting Evidence" (11.5%) is the easiest of the rare achievements to fix — see our Photo Mode guide.
  • Steam concurrency remains healthy two months out: ~14,570 average players over the last 30 days, 33,053 all-time peak.
