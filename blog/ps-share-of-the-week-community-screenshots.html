PlayStation's Share of the Week Spotlights Stunning LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Fan Screenshots

Published: May 29, 2026 · 4 min read

Just one week after LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight launched on May 22, 2026, the PlayStation community has already produced some extraordinary in-game photography. PlayStation's weekly spotlight series — Share of the Week — put up the Bat Signal for May 29, and the response from players has been remarkable. Six screenshots were selected from community submissions made via #PSshare #PSBlog on social media, and each one captures a different side of what TT Games built.

This Week's Six Featured Screenshots

The official PlayStation Blog post published today highlights six community-captured images, each showcasing a different slice of Gotham. Here's a breakdown of every featured shot:

@thefrostysm
Batman gliding through a fiery tunnel — a kinetic action shot that captures one of the game's most cinematic traversal moments. The cape spread against the orange glow of the flames makes for a striking composition.
@sirevanztheduke
Batman mid-throw, hurling a Batarang directly toward the camera. The tight framing and action pose demonstrate just how expressive TT Games has made the LEGO character animations in this entry.
@CheezecakeNinja
An upside-down POV of Batman approaching a flipped car — one of the more creative angle choices of the bunch. This perspective flip makes Gotham look genuinely disorienting and fresh.
@ErfanManavi
Batman and Catwoman riding together on a motorbike through Gotham's streets. This dynamic duo shot highlights the cooperative spirit the game celebrates, and the motion blur sells the speed convincingly.
@PhikkySixx
Batman swooping into a museum defaced by the Joker's graffiti — a perfect encapsulation of the game's tonal contrast between grim Dark Knight atmosphere and colorful LEGO chaos.
@call_me_xavii
Batman standing on the corner of a rooftop, silhouetted against the full moon. Perhaps the most classically Batman image of the six — timeless iconography recreated perfectly in LEGO form.

What the Community Shots Reveal About the Game

Beyond their visual quality, these six screenshots say something meaningful about the breadth of LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight as a game. The variety of scenarios captured — high-speed escapes, aerial traversal, museum heist sequences, rooftop vigils — reflects how much the game's open-world Gotham has to offer in terms of memorable set pieces and spontaneous moments.

What's particularly notable is the range of tones on display. The Joker-graffitied museum shot (@PhikkySixx) sits at one end of the spectrum — bright, chaotic, and irreverent — while the moonlit rooftop image (@call_me_xavii) sits at the other, evoking the solemnity of Frank Miller or Christopher Nolan's Batman. The fact that a single LEGO game can credibly produce both kinds of screenshot speaks to how successfully TT Games threaded the tone needle here.

For many players, this is also the first time they've had access to a robust in-game capture mode in a LEGO title of this scale. The PS5's native Share button combined with the game's dynamic environments — rain-slicked streets, cathedral spires, neon-lit alleyways — gives players genuinely photogenic material to work with.

Want to be featured? Sony's Share of the Week theme has already moved on to Star Wars Outlaws for the following week, but expect LEGO Batman community showcases to continue through the summer as the game builds its audience. Keep using #PSshare #PSBlog on social media for future chances to be highlighted by PlayStation.

The Broader Context: A Game Worth Photographing

The fact that PlayStation chose LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight for Share of the Week just seven days after launch signals something about how the platform holder views the title's community momentum. Share of the Week themes are selected based on player engagement and the quality of submitted content — and clearly, Legacy of the Dark Knight players showed up with their best work immediately.

The game launched on May 22, 2026, across PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC (Steam and Epic Games Store), with the Deluxe Edition available three days early from May 19. According to sales tracking data reported by GuruFocus, the game generated 1.2 million units and $83 million in gross revenues at launch, with PS5 accounting for 56.4% of that total — making PlayStation by far the dominant platform. With that kind of install base, the pool of potential screenshot submissions was always going to be substantial.

The game currently sits at an 84 on Metacritic and an 85 on OpenCritic, making it the highest-rated LEGO game of all time. Critical reception has been warm, but community enthusiasm — as evidenced by these screenshot submissions — suggests the player audience is equally engaged. Gotham City, it seems, has never looked this good in plastic.

Capture your own: If you're playing on PS5, press the Create button and select "Take Screenshot" for a full-resolution capture, or activate Photo Mode (if available) from the pause menu for additional camera angle control. Share with #PSshare #PSBlog for a chance to appear in a future PlayStation Blog spotlight.

What's Next for the Community

With the Mayhem Collection DLC — adding Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable protagonists — arriving in September 2026 for Deluxe Edition owners, there will be an entirely new wave of community content to look forward to. New environments, new character models, and new story moments mean a fresh batch of screenshot opportunities. For a game that's only been out for one week and is already generating PlayStation-featured photography, the long-term community outlook looks strong.

In the meantime, if you're still working through the main campaign, check out our Mission 1 Walkthrough or the 100% Completion Guide to make sure you're not missing anything in Gotham.
