All 43 Batcave Challenges in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — Complete Guide

Published: June 14, 2026 · 9 min read

Hidden behind the green terminal above the Batmobile garage sits one of the most rewarding progression systems in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — the 43 Batcave Challenges. Complete them all and you'll unlock a spectacular full-wall mural in the Batcave, and pocket a cumulative total of 465,000 Studs in the process. If you're chasing 100% completion, this is one list you cannot afford to ignore.

How to Access the Challenge Board

The challenge board isn't available from the moment you start the game. You can earn challenges from the very beginning — including during the Prologue — but you won't be able to cash them in until you reach the Batcave at the start of Chapter 2.

Once Chapter 2 begins, head to the west side of the Batcave, just above the Batmobile garage. Look for a glowing green computer terminal. Interact with it to pull up the full challenge list, check your progress, and press X (PS5) or A (Xbox) to collect Stud rewards for any challenges you've already completed.

Pro tip: Don't wait too long before checking the terminal. If you've been playing through the story normally, you may already have five or six challenges banked by the time Chapter 2 starts. Redeem them early to fund upgrades at the Workbench.

Rewards and the Batcave Mural

Each challenge pays out between 5,000 and 20,000 Studs when redeemed. With 43 challenges in total, completing the full set nets you 465,000 Studs — a significant boost toward unlocking characters, costumes, and vehicles from Bat-Mite's Shop.

Beyond the Studs, every completed challenge adds a piece to a large mural on the Batcave wall. This mural serves as a visual progress tracker and doubles as an achievement/trophy requirement. There are two achievements tied directly to the challenge board: one for completing your first challenge, and a second for completing all 43.

Combat Challenges

The largest category of challenges revolves around combat encounters. Most of these unlock organically as you fight your way through Gotham, but a few require deliberate setups.

Challenge How to Complete Reward
Brawler Defeat 100 enemies without losing all health. Try an easier difficulty if needed. 10,000 Studs
Brutal Combat Defeat a Brute. You'll encounter your first in Chapter 1's Red Hood Gang mission. 5,000 Studs
Brute-Man Make Brute enemies run into smaller ones 10 times. The Chapter 2 Mimes level has a easily-baiteable Brute. 10,000 Studs
Buddy System Robin — Use the Cable Launcher to tie a smaller enemy to a Brute. 10,000 Studs
Combo Crusader Reach a combat combo of 80+. Stun enemies with Batarangs first, then chain punches. 10,000 Studs
Counter Measures Perform 20 counters without taking damage. Stand in combat and press Triangle/Y when prompted. 10,000 Studs
Demolisher Smash 60+ objects in one minute. Take the Batmobile to the streets. 10,000 Studs
Everyone's a Critic Perform 5 Critical Hits without non-critical attacks in between. Pause between button presses after a ×5 combo. 10,000 Studs
Fall Damage Batman — Use the Batclaw to throw enemies off raised ledges 15 times. 10,000 Studs
Hazard Defeat 50 enemies using environmental objects such as exploding gas canisters. 15,000 Studs
Ledgendary Knock 15 enemies off high ledges using takedowns while hanging from a ledge. 10,000 Studs
Living Dangerously Defeat 10 enemies with only one health heart remaining. Let enemies chip down your health first. 10,000 Studs
Untouchable Defeat 30 enemies without losing any health. Early story missions on Standard difficulty work best. 10,000 Studs
Whiplash Catwoman — Spin enemies with her Whip so they hit others 30 times. 10,000 Studs

Stealth Challenges

Several challenges specifically reward stealth play. If you've been blasting through encounters, you may need to revisit Chapter 1's Infiltration mission, which is purpose-built for stealth approaches.

Challenge How to Complete Reward
Assassin Perform 200 stealth takedowns. Replay the Chapter 1 Infiltration mission repeatedly. 20,000 Studs
Check It Out! Throw Batarangs to lure enemies from their posts 20 times while in stealth. 10,000 Studs
Computer Wiz Batgirl — Distract 10 enemies using the Hackarang on alarms and devices. 10,000 Studs
Ninja Perform 15 stealth takedowns in a row without being spotted. Chapter 1 Infiltration is ideal. 15,000 Studs
Pied Piper Talia — Use blowdarts to stun 7 enemies simultaneously. 10,000 Studs
Stealth tip: The "Assassin" challenge (200 stealth takedowns) is the grindiest challenge in the game. Start early and pick it up organically across the campaign rather than farming it at the end.

Character-Specific Challenges

Many challenges are tied to abilities unique to specific playable characters. Make sure you spend time with every member of the Bat-Family — Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, Catwoman, Jim Gordon, and Talia all have challenges tied to their exclusive gadgets.

Challenge Character How to Complete
9 Lives Catwoman Use Call Kitty (L2/LT) on a rooftop, then jump off as the kitten.
Bouncer Nightwing Hit 3 different enemy types with a single Rebound Bolt.
Dodgeball Jim Gordon Foam Spray an enemy into a ball, then punch them into others.
Hair Trigger Nightwing Use the Electric Birdarang on a Rapid Fire Enemy to make them fire wildly.
Multitasker Robin Unlock Robin in Chapter 3. Activate a cable line, then target 3 enemies with the Birdarang.
Speedy Safecracker Catwoman Crack the Batcave safe in under 10 seconds. Practice the mini-game timing.
Teleporter Talia Upgrade Talia's Ninja Dash gadget, then use the Stun Smoke effect on 3 enemies during a single teleport.

Exploration & World Challenges

Gotham City's open world hides several challenge requirements. Some are as simple as visiting a landmark; others demand you use specific character abilities in specific locations.

Challenge How to Complete Tip
Collector Place 30+ objects in the Batcave after unlocking the expansion. Unlock the Batcave expansion in Chapter 3.
Paranormal Investigator Photograph the Gentleman Ghost using Photo Mode. The Gentleman Ghost is in the northwest area of East End — Amusement Mile, near a tree on the road.
Recycler Use Batman's Batclaw on an enemy and throw them into a dumpster. Dumpsters are common in alleyways throughout Gotham.
The Floor is Lava Glide for 60 seconds without touching the ground. Start from a tall building and use Gotham's wind tunnels to stay airborne.
The High Life Stand on top of the tallest building in Gotham City. Climb to the top of Wayne Tower — stand on the W.
Theatrical Visit the Monarch Theatre while wearing the Gray Ghost costume. Buy the Gray Ghost costume from Bat-Mite's Shop for 50,000 Studs. Theater is in East End — Amusement Mile, southeast.
Treasure Hunter Collect all collectible items in any single story mission. See our Collectibles Guide for full mission breakdowns.
Turbo Boost in a vehicle for 30 seconds without stopping or hitting anything. Use a bridge between districts for a long straight stretch.

Stud & Progression Challenges

A handful of challenges tie directly to your Stud count and upgrade progression. These are worth prioritizing early, as the rewards they unlock feed back into powering up the rest of your playthrough.

Challenge How to Complete
Calculator Reach the maximum Stud multiplier. Redeem Stud Cache codes to hit the target fast.
Focused Fighter Perform 5 Focus Takedowns in a single combo streak. Requires Focus-level gadget upgrades from the Workbench.
Multi-Man Collect a Blue or Purple Stud while at max Stud multiplier. Use the Stud Cache method to reach max multiplier first.
Utility Belt Use the Focus Ability upgrade for every gadget in the game. Requires 168 total WayneTech Cache Chips — a significant investment.
Controlled Blast Use the Focus Blast upgrade to hit 3 enemies with a single explosion. Obtain the upgrade from the Workbench using WayneTech Cache Chips.
High Voltage Nightwing — Use the Focus Shockwave upgrade to damage nearby enemies.

Tips for Efficient Completion

With 43 challenges spread across combat, stealth, exploration, and character-specific tasks, a little planning goes a long way. Here's how to approach the list without feeling overwhelmed:

Play every character intentionally. Character-specific challenges account for roughly a third of the list. As you unlock each Bat-Family member, take them to a combat encounter or open world area and test out their unique abilities. Nightwing's Rebound Bolt, Talia's Ninja Dash, and Catwoman's whip all have dedicated challenges that you won't complete by accident.

Don't rush Dark Knight Mode challenges. Two challenges — "In Blackest Night" and "Tough Guy" — require playing in Dark Knight Mode, the game's hardest difficulty setting. For "In Blackest Night," run the short "New Beginnings" mission with no boss fight. For "Tough Guy," simply replay missions on Dark Knight Mode until you've defeated 100 enemies cumulatively.

Farm "Assassin" early. Two hundred stealth takedowns sounds intimidating, but Chapter 1's Infiltration mission is a short, self-contained stealth level that resets when replayed. Running it five or six times will fill the counter without much effort.

Photograph the Gentleman Ghost before you forget. This is the most missable challenge on the list. The Gentleman Ghost only appears in Photo Mode in the northwest corner of East End — Amusement Mile. It's easy to skip this district entirely during the main story, so flag it for a dedicated side trip.

Use the Stud Cache for multiplier challenges. Both "Calculator" and "Multi-Man" require reaching the maximum Stud multiplier. Redeem the in-game Stud Cache reward (check our Redeem Codes guide for details) to reach the cap quickly, then grab a Blue or Purple Stud to tick off "Multi-Man" in the same session.

Completionist note: The "Utility Belt" challenge (Focus Ability for every gadget) requires 168 WayneTech Cache Chips total — the most resource-intensive challenge in the game. Collect Cache Chips consistently throughout the story rather than leaving this for New Game Plus.

Completing all 43 challenges isn't just about Studs and murals — it's a full tour of every mechanic the game has to offer. If the main story flew by too fast, the challenge list will push you to engage with every character, every gadget, and every corner of Gotham. That's what makes it one of the best reasons to revisit Legacy of the Dark Knight after rolling the credits.
