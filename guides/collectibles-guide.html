LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has over 99 collectibles spread across 6 chapters and 29+ main missions, plus more in open-world Gotham. Nothing is permanently missable — the Batcomputer in the Batcave lets you replay any mission. This guide covers every collectible type, what each one rewards, which characters you need to reach them, and the most efficient order to collect everything.

Collectible Types Overview

  • Red Bricks — unlock gameplay modifiers and costume colour schemes
  • Batcave Minikits — build display models in the Batcave hub
  • WayneTech Caches — provide WayneTech Chips for character upgrades
  • Gold Bricks — unlock bonus content and Batcave upgrades
  • Stud Caches — large one-time Stud deposits, some resettable via save-reload
No missables: Every collectible can be retrieved by replaying via the Batcomputer. Play through the story first, then mop up in Free Play — you will have the full character roster and all abilities needed to reach everything.

Red Bricks (23 Total)

Red Bricks

23 total — 14 in-world · 9 in Bat-Mite Store

Each Red Brick unlocks either a gameplay modifier (Stud Multipliers, Invincibility, score bonuses) or a new colour scheme for all 101 base-game costumes. Of the 23 total, 14 are hidden within missions and the open world; 9 must be purchased from the Bat-Mite Store using Studs you have already earned.

Why Red Bricks Matter

Red Bricks are the single most impactful collectible type in the game. The Stud Multiplier bricks stack multiplicatively — finding even two or three of them cuts your Stud grind by 80% or more. Every other collectible type either costs Studs (store Red Bricks) or becomes far easier to reach once you have upgraded traversal from WayneTech Chips, which themselves require Studs to unlock. Getting Stud Multipliers early is the keystone move of any efficient 100% run.

There is also a Golden Red Brick obtainable only by redeeming QR codes from physical LEGO Batman sets (76330–76333). It grants a unique golden colour palette and does not count toward the standard 23. See our QR Codes & Redeem Rewards Guide for the full process.

Red Brick Chapter Breakdown

According to GamesRadar, there is roughly one to three Red Bricks hidden in each of the game's six chapter areas, with additional bricks scattered across open-world Gotham. VGC maintains a full Red Brick location guide with mission-by-mission screenshots.

Character Requirements for Red Bricks

Several in-world Red Bricks are locked behind specific character abilities. You will need Free Play access (available after completing each mission's story run) to retrieve them. Key ability gates include:

  • Batgirl's hacking ability — required to access several terminal-locked rooms where Red Bricks are stored
  • Nightwing's wall-jump — reaches elevated platforms in later chapter areas that are inaccessible to Batman alone
  • Catwoman's lockpick — opens locked containers and side doors throughout Gotham's open world
  • Robin's tech suit — needed for certain puzzle sequences in the Gotham industrial district
Plan ahead: During your first story playthrough, note any locked door, hackable terminal, or high platform you pass that you cannot reach. These are almost certainly hiding a Red Brick or Gold Brick. Return in Free Play once you have the needed character.

Batcave Minikits (10 Total)

Batcave Minikits

10 total — hidden in the Batcave hub across multiple rooms

Unlike older LEGO games where Minikits were found inside story missions, Legacy of the Dark Knight places all 10 Minikits within the Batcave hub itself. Finding all 10 assembles a large Batmobile display model in the main Batcave chamber and unlocks a trophy/achievement. They are distributed across Batcave rooms that unlock progressively as you advance through the story.

Why Minikits Matter

Beyond the display model and the associated trophy, completing the Minikits is required for 100% completion. Because they are spread across unlockable Batcave rooms, you cannot collect all 10 until late in the story — at least two rooms remain locked until Chapters 5 and 6. Do a sweep of each new room as it becomes available rather than leaving it all to the end.

Minikit Search Tips

  • Every Batcave room has at least one Minikit. Scan walls, ceiling fixtures, and behind equipment racks.
  • Some Minikits are hidden inside destructible objects — smash everything in each room before moving on.
  • The Batcomputer room and the Trophy Room tend to have the most hidden spots; check above the monitors and behind display cases.
  • The map does not track individual Minikit positions, so a systematic room-by-room sweep is more reliable than randomly searching.

WayneTech Caches (10 Total)

WayneTech Caches

10 total — also hidden in the Batcave hub

WayneTech Caches contain WayneTech Chips, the upgrade currency for character abilities, gadgets, and traversal skills. All 10 are hidden in the Batcave alongside the Minikits. According to Game Rant, they are tied to specific Batcave rooms that unlock with story progress — so the same room-by-room sweep strategy applies.

Why WayneTech Caches Matter

WayneTech Chips fund upgrades that directly expand where you can go and what you can do. Wall-run distance upgrades, extended grapple range, and combat efficiency upgrades all open shortcuts and puzzle solutions throughout the open world. Prioritise finding these early because some traversal upgrades are effectively required to reach otherwise-awkward Red Brick and Gold Brick locations. The most valuable upgrades to unlock first are grapple range (extends reach on all rope-swing collectibles) and suit endurance (reduces how often you lose combat momentum while hunting enemies near collectibles).

Upgrade Priority Order

  1. Grapple range upgrade — opens the most new collectible spots in the open world
  2. Wall-run distance — required for several upper-level Red Bricks in Chapters 3–5
  3. Detective vision range — reveals hidden interact points and breakable walls you would otherwise miss
  4. Stud magnet radius — passive income boost while collecting anything in the open world

Gold Bricks

Gold Bricks are the broadest collectible category and the one that takes the most varied effort to complete. They are earned through four main sources:

  • Story mission completion — one Gold Brick per main mission upon first completion
  • Open-world side activities — zoo animal rescues, AR Trials, and Riddler Puzzle Rooms each reward a Gold Brick upon completion
  • Mission replay Stud scores — achieving a Stud target (shown on the mission select screen) on a replay run rewards a bonus Gold Brick
  • In-mission challenges — hidden objectives within certain missions unlock a Gold Brick when completed, separate from simply finishing the mission

Gold Bricks unlock Batcave upgrades and pieces of bonus content. The Batcave garage, trophy display alcoves, and the secondary Batmobile bay all require a threshold number of Gold Bricks to access. See our 100% Completion Guide for the full Gold Brick checklist with sources for each one.

Stud Caches

Stud Caches are hidden containers in Gotham City that release a large Stud burst when broken. Some reset after saving and reloading, making them ideal for farming. The most effective cache location yields 20,000–30,000 Studs per opening with Stud Multiplier Red Bricks active. See our Stud Farming Guide for the best cache locations and the save-reload method, plus the exact circuit route that hits five caches in under two minutes.

Optimal Collection Route

The most efficient path to 100% is not to collect everything as you encounter it — it is to sequence your runs so each pass through the game is purposeful. Here is the recommended order:

  1. Complete the full story without backtracking. This unlocks the full character roster in Free Play, all Batcave rooms (and their Minikits and WayneTech Caches), and gives you a baseline of Studs and Gold Bricks from story missions alone.
  2. Sweep every Batcave room for Minikits and WayneTech Caches. Do this immediately after the credits — all rooms are now open. This takes 15–20 minutes and gives you all WayneTech Chips to spend on upgrades.
  3. Spend WayneTech Chips on grapple and traversal upgrades first, then replay the earliest missions in Free Play to grab locked Red Bricks (Batgirl hacks, Catwoman locks, Nightwing wall-jumps).
  4. Find the Stud Multiplier Red Bricks as your top priority in these replays. Once you have x2 and x4 multipliers stacked, your Stud income from everything else explodes.
  5. Clear open-world Gotham district by district. The map tracks side activity completion per district, so set a goal of 100% in one district before moving to the next rather than skipping around.
  6. Buy the remaining 9 store Red Bricks from the Bat-Mite Store last. By this point your multipliers are active and you will have more than enough Studs.
Bonus finds along the route: While sweeping each district for side activities, you will naturally pass most open-world Stud Caches. Break everything you see — a single Stud Cache can be worth more than an entire short mission replay with multipliers active.

Character Requirement Risk Warnings

Several collectibles are locked behind character abilities that may not be available during your story playthrough. These are the most commonly missed — note them down so you know to return in Free Play with the right character:

  • Batgirl (Hack): Required for all Batgirl-locked Red Bricks and several WayneTech Cache rooms with electronic keypads. Batgirl unlocks as a playable character part-way through the story — any collectible behind a hack panel before that point requires a Free Play revisit.
  • Catwoman (Lockpick): Required for locked vault Red Bricks and some Gotham side-activity chests. Catwoman is unlocked via story progression but her lockpick ability is gated behind a WayneTech upgrade. Do not spend chips elsewhere before buying the Lockpick upgrade.
  • Nightwing (Wall-Jump): Required for several Red Bricks placed on high ledges reachable only via wall-jump chains. Wall-Jump is Nightwing's base ability, so once he is unlocked (Chapter 3) the ledges become accessible in Free Play.
  • Jim Gordon (Foam Spray / Rebound Launcher): Some puzzle Red Bricks require Gordon's unique gadgets. Gordon is available from early in the game but his upgraded Rebound Launcher is a WayneTech purchase.
  • Talia al Ghul (Shadow Dash): A small number of Red Bricks in later districts require Shadow Dash to phase through cracked walls. Talia is unlocked at story completion.
Free Play rule of thumb: On your first pass through any mission or district, skip collectibles behind coloured-glow ability gates. Return via the Batcomputer or open world once you have every character unlocked and upgraded. This avoids the frustration of being locked out mid-collection run.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I miss any collectibles permanently?

No. Every collectible in the game can be retrieved via mission replay on the Batcomputer, including open-world collectibles that are tied to Gotham districts you have already visited. Nothing is locked behind a one-time story choice.

Do I need online access for any collectibles?

The Golden Red Brick from QR code redemption requires an internet connection at the time of redemption, but the code itself comes from physical LEGO sets and does not expire (as of May 2026). All other 99+ collectibles are fully offline.

How long does 100% take?

Community estimates on platforms like HowLongToBeat put the 100% completion time at approximately 20–30 hours depending on how freely you explore during the story. Following the optimal route above can bring this closer to 18–22 hours for experienced LEGO game players.

Which collectibles are easiest to miss during the story?

Red Bricks hidden behind Batgirl terminal puzzles are the most commonly overlooked — many players complete missions without realising there is a hackable terminal in a side room. Riddler Puzzle Rooms in Gotham's open world are also frequently skipped because they are not marked on the minimap until you get close enough to trigger the waypoint.
