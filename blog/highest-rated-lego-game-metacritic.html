LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Is Now the Highest-Rated LEGO Game Ever on Metacritic

Published: May 28, 2026 · 6 min read

History has been made. LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has officially become the highest-rated LEGO game of all time on Metacritic, surpassing LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga after the latter held the record for four years. With a Metascore of 84 — two points clear of The Skywalker Saga's 82 — Batman has now claimed the crown that LEGO Star Wars 2: The Original Trilogy held for sixteen years before that.

84

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — Metacritic Score

Based on 47 critic reviews · 91% positive · 4 mixed · 0 negative

User Score: 8.9 from 396 ratings · OpenCritic: 85

The Full LEGO Game Metacritic Rankings

To understand what this achievement means, it helps to see where Legacy of the Dark Knight sits relative to its predecessors. The LEGO franchise has never been a critical darling in the traditional sense — its games consistently earn solid, respectable scores rather than the heights reserved for genre-defining titles. That's what makes clearing 84 so meaningful.

Rank Game Year Metascore
🥇 1 LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight 2026 84
🥈 2 LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga 2022 82
🥉 3 LEGO Star Wars 2: The Original Trilogy 2006 82*

*LEGO Star Wars 2 and The Skywalker Saga share the same raw score; The Skywalker Saga held the active record due to more reviews and current-gen prominence before Legacy of the Dark Knight launched.

Milestone context: According to GamesRadar+, LEGO Star Wars 2 held the franchise's critical record for 16 years — from 2006 to 2022 — before The Skywalker Saga took over. Legacy of the Dark Knight has now moved the needle again just four years later.

What Critics Are Saying

Of the 47 reviews counted by Metacritic at time of writing, only four fall into the "Mixed" category — and all four of those are 7/10 scores, which is far from a negative verdict. The game has received zero negative reviews. That near-unanimity is unusual for any title, let alone one in a franchise as family-friendly and niche-adjacent as LEGO games.

GamesRadar+ gave the game four stars out of five. US Managing Editor Rollin Bishop wrote: "Lego Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight remixes classic stories and games to provide a single straightforward explanation for who Batman is, how he came to be, and where he and his extended found family has ultimately found themselves — without taking itself too seriously. Condiment King appears more than once, and you get to use Batman's shark repellent."

The consensus across outlets points to a game that successfully threads a difficult needle: it plays like a Batman: Arkham game — open-world Gotham, fluid combat, detective sequences — while never losing the playful LEGO spirit that has defined the franchise since 2005. Critics who expected a compromise between those two tones came away impressed that TT Games largely delivered on both.

Why This Score Matters for the LEGO Franchise

The LEGO game series has been at a crossroads for most of the last decade. After flooding the market with licensed titles through the mid-2010s, TT Games and Warner Bros. shifted strategy toward larger, more ambitious games. LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga was the first true signal that the new direction was working — a sprawling, 300+ character epic that overhauled the core gameplay formula and earned some of the best reviews the franchise had ever seen.

Legacy of the Dark Knight follows that same philosophy: fewer releases, higher production values, and a genuine attempt to engage players who might normally dismiss LEGO games as children's content. The Arkham-DNA approach — cape glide, grapnel traversal, predator stealth sections, and a detective vision-style gadget scanner — gives the game a mechanical depth that earlier LEGO titles rarely attempted.

The result on Metacritic suggests critics responded to that ambition. A 91% positive review rate is exceptional for any major release. Combined with an 8.9 user score from nearly 400 ratings, Legacy of the Dark Knight has cleared the rare bar of satisfying both press and players simultaneously — something even well-reviewed games frequently struggle with.

The OpenCritic Picture

On OpenCritic, the game sits at 85, according to available data — one point higher than its Metacritic aggregate. That slight variation reflects differences in the review pools each aggregator draws from, but the directional story is the same: this is a game the critical community has embraced warmly. The OpenCritic score also makes Legacy of the Dark Knight the highest-rated LEGO title on that platform as well.

Switch 2 note: The Nintendo Switch 2 version of Legacy of the Dark Knight is scheduled to launch later in 2026. Its reviews will be tallied separately on Metacritic, so the PS5/Xbox/PC aggregate seen now may shift slightly if the Switch 2 version earns notably different scores.

A New Benchmark for Future LEGO Games

For TT Games and Warner Bros. Games, Legacy of the Dark Knight's critical reception sets an expectation baseline that future titles will be measured against. The Arkham-style approach has now been validated by both reviewers and players — it's no longer an experiment but a proven direction.

What comes next is an open question. The Mayhem Collection DLC, arriving in September 2026, will add Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters with their own gadgets, abilities, and a new story mission. Whether TT Games follows Legacy of the Dark Knight with another DC property, a return to Star Wars, or something entirely new, the bar has now been set: any future LEGO game will need to clear 84 on Metacritic to claim the top spot.

After sixteen years under one record-holder and four years under another, the franchise's critical ceiling appears to keep rising. For longtime fans of plastic bricks and playful takes on beloved properties, that's an encouraging sign.
