LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is the fourth installment in the LEGO Batman video game series, developed by Traveller's Tales and published by Warner Bros. Games. Here's everything confirmed about its release.

Official Release Date

The game launches on May 22, 2026 for PlayStation 5, Windows (PC), and Xbox Series X/S.

The game was originally scheduled for May 29, 2026, but on March 16, 2026, Warner Bros. announced that the release date was being brought forward by one week to May 22. TT Games's head of production Jonathan Smith expressed relief about having "some space" around the release date after Grand Theft Auto VI was delayed from May to November 2026.

The game was officially declared "gone gold" on May 1, 2026, meaning development work is complete and the game is ready for manufacturing and digital distribution.

Source: Release date information from official Warner Bros. Games announcement (March 16, 2026). Gone gold confirmed by Push Square (May 1, 2026). The game was announced on August 19, 2025, with the release date revealed at The Game Awards in December 2025.

Deluxe Edition Early Access

Purchasing the Deluxe Edition included 72-hour early access, allowing players to start on May 19, 2026 — three days before the official launch on May 22. The early access window has now concluded; the game is available at its regular price on all platforms.

🚨 Note: The Deluxe Edition remains available for purchase and includes the Legacy Collection DLC packs and the Season Pass for the Mayhem Collection DLC (arriving September 18, 2026). See our Deluxe Edition guide for a full breakdown of what's included.

Multiplayer: Local Co-Op Only

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight supports local split-screen co-op for two players on the same system. Connect a second controller, pause the game, and press the drop-in button shown in the top-right corner of the pause screen. However, the game does NOT feature native online multiplayer. PC players can use Steam Remote Play Together; PS5 players can use PlayStation Share Play as workarounds. See our Co-Op Guide for full details.

Post-Launch Reception & Updates

The game launched May 22, 2026 and has now been out for nearly four weeks. Post-launch data points as of June 17, 2026:

  • Critical reception: 84 Metacritic score from 47 critics — the highest-rated LEGO game of all time. Critics highlight the Arkham-style combat and open-world Gotham as the strongest elements. See our Launch Day Reception post for the full review roundup.
  • Steam performance: 33,449 concurrent players at peak — surpassing Batman: Arkham Knight's all-time Steam high. See our Steam Player Count Analysis.

FAQ

Is this game available on PS4 or Xbox One?

No. Legacy of the Dark Knight is a current-generation exclusive built on Unreal Engine 5. PS4, Xbox One, and original Nintendo Switch are not supported and have not been announced.

Is the Nintendo Switch 2 version a full version of the game?

Warner Bros. has confirmed the Switch 2 version is the same game as the PS5/Xbox/PC release. No content cuts or feature differences have been announced. The delay versus the other platforms is understood to be a porting timeline issue, not a content scope reduction.

Can I upgrade from Standard to Deluxe Edition after launch?

Yes. The Deluxe Edition upgrade (which adds the Legacy Collection DLC and the Mayhem Collection Season Pass) is sold separately on all platform stores. The 72-hour early access benefit no longer applies post-launch, but all other Deluxe content is available via the upgrade purchase.

Is there a physical edition?

Physical editions are available at retail for PS5 and Xbox Series X/S. PC is digital-only (Steam and Epic Games Store). Physical editions include the base game only; Deluxe content requires a separate digital purchase or upgrade code.

Nintendo Switch 2 physical edition: The Switch 2 version uses Game Key Cards — a physical card containing a download code rather than traditional game data on the card itself. Both Standard ($69.99) and Deluxe ($89.99) physical editions are available for pre-order. The Switch 2 Deluxe Edition physical copy includes an exclusive Retro Video Game Batman Minifigure (available while supplies last), based on Batman's appearance in the 1989 Sunsoft NES game Batman: The Video Game. This physical minifigure is unique to the Switch 2 Deluxe physical edition and is not included with any digital purchase or with PS5/Xbox physical copies. (Source: StoneWars, June 4, 2026)

Is the game on Game Pass or PlayStation Plus?

As of June 15, 2026, the game has not been announced for Xbox Game Pass or PlayStation Plus. This is standard for major Warner Bros. releases at launch — previous LEGO games have historically appeared on subscription services 6–12 months post-launch.
