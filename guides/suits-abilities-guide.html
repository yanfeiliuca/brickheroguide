LEGO Batman Legacy of the Dark Knight — Confirmed Batsuits & Character Skins

Last updated: May 19, 2026 · 6 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight features a wide variety of character skins and Batsuits inspired by decades of Batman media. TT Games has confirmed several actor-based character skins drawn from iconic Batman films, alongside multiple Batsuit variations for Batman himself. This page tracks what has been officially confirmed ahead of the game's May 22, 2026 release.

Confirmed Actor-Based Character Skins

TT Games has revealed that several playable character skins are directly inspired by specific actors from Batman's cinematic history. These are not generic versions of the characters — they are modeled after the actors' likenesses and costumes from their respective films.

Michelle Pfeiffer's Catwoman (Batman Returns, 1992)

The iconic stitched-together catsuit from Tim Burton's Batman Returns makes its LEGO debut. Pfeiffer's Catwoman skin features her signature blonde hair, pale complexion, and the unmistakable white-stitched black bodysuit. This skin is expected to come with whip-based combat abilities and acrobatic movement options.

Danny DeVito's Penguin (Batman Returns, 1992)

DeVito's grotesque and unforgettable portrayal of Oswald Cobblepot is rendered in LEGO form with the character's top hat, monocle, flipper-like hands, and black bile drool. This skin captures the tragic, sewer-dwelling version of the Penguin and is likely to feature umbrella-based gadgets and penguin minion summons.

Heath Ledger's Joker (The Dark Knight, 2008)

Heath Ledger's Oscar-winning performance as the Clown Prince of Crime is honored with a skin featuring the character's purple overcoat, green vest, smeared face paint, and disheveled green hair. Expect chaos-themed abilities including explosive gadgets, playing card projectiles, and unpredictable combat animations faithful to Ledger's interpretation.

Jeffrey Wright's Jim Gordon (The Batman, 2022)

Jeffrey Wright's grounded, detective-focused Commissioner Gordon from Matt Reeves' The Batman joins the LEGO roster. This skin features Gordon's tactical police vest, glasses, and a detective's toolkit. As a GCPD ally, Gordon's abilities are expected to include crime scene investigation mechanics and police radio coordination.

Source: Vice published an article on April 18, 2026 titled "All Confirmed Batsuits in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight So Far" with additional details on the skin selection and design process.

Multiple Batsuits for Batman

In addition to character skins from the films, Batman himself can wear multiple Batsuits inspired by various Batman media. While the full roster of Batsuits has not been finalized publicly, TT Games has confirmed that players will be able to switch between different Batsuit variations, each drawing from a different era or interpretation of the Dark Knight across comics, films, and animated series.

The Batsuit system allows players to customize Batman's appearance while retaining his core ability set. Details on whether specific Batsuits come with unique abilities or are purely cosmetic have not been fully clarified by the developers. More information is expected alongside or shortly after the game's launch.

Batcave Customization & Display

The Batcave serves as your central hub and can be customized with vehicles, Batsuits, trophies, and collectibles on display. As you progress through the game and unlock more content, these items populate the Batcave, transforming it from a simple starting space into a showcase of your accomplishments across Gotham City.

Vehicles you unlock — including the Batmobile and other iconic transports — appear on display platforms. Batsuits and character skins you collect are visible in the Wardrobe area. Trophies and collectibles fill out dedicated display sections, giving the Batcave a lived-in feel that reflects your progress.
