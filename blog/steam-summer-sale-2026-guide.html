Steam Summer Sale 2026: Will LEGO Batman Legacy of the Dark Knight Get Discounted?

📅 June 23, 2026  |  ⏱ 5 min read  |  💬 News

Steam’s biggest annual sale of the year kicks off in two days. The Steam Summer Sale 2026 runs from June 25 to July 9, 2026 — 14 days of discounts across thousands of titles, opening at 10 AM Pacific Time on June 25. For anyone who has been on the fence about picking up LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, the natural question is: will it be cheaper by the end of the week?

The short answer is that a deep Steam discount is unlikely on the base game — it launched just 32 days ago on May 22, 2026. But the longer answer involves a few nuances worth understanding before the sale goes live, including third-party key options already below full price, what the Deluxe Edition gets you, and what Valve’s current sale policies mean for brand-new releases.

TL;DR: The Steam Summer Sale 2026 starts June 25. LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is unlikely to receive a steep official Steam discount this close to launch, but third-party key sites already offer savings of up to 28%. The Deluxe Edition is worth it for long-term players given it includes the September Mayhem Collection DLC.

Steam Summer Sale 2026: Key Dates and What to Expect

According to gHacks Tech News and the official Steamworks documentation, the 2026 Steam Summer Sale runs as follows:

Detail Information
Start Date June 25, 2026 at 10 AM Pacific (17:00 UTC)
End Date July 9, 2026
Duration 14 days
Discount Structure All discounts active for the full sale period — no flash deals or daily rotations
Historical Average Discount ~45% off, with common tiers at 50%, 57%, and 75%

Valve’s current policy prevents games from being discounted on Steam within 30 days of a price change or within 30 days of launch. LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight launched May 22, 2026, making the 30-day window barely clearing by June 21 — technically eligible for the Summer Sale. However, publisher discounting decisions are separate from Valve’s minimum wait periods. Warner Bros. Games historically does not apply steep discounts to titles in their first major sale appearance, preferring to wait until the game has been available for several months.

Expectation management: Steam historically averages a 45% discount across participating titles, but brand-new AAA releases typically see modest discounts of 10–20% in their first sale window, if they participate at all. A 50% off scenario for Legacy of the Dark Knight in June 2026 would be unusually aggressive for a title this new with a Metacritic score of 84.

Current Best Prices — Before the Sale Opens

If you’re price-conscious, there are already savings available without waiting for Valve’s official sale. According to GG.deals, the Standard Edition can currently be purchased at roughly 28% below the Steam list price via authorized third-party key retailers. The best price on record for the Standard Edition was $48.11, recorded on June 13, 2026 via GAMESEAL.

For the Deluxe Edition ($80 on Steam), similar third-party savings are available through GG.deals Deluxe Edition listings and DLCompare. These sites aggregate keys from authorized resellers and are generally considered safe for Steam key purchases.

💰 The Fanatical Mystery Egg Bundle Deal (June 5)

Third-Party — Limited Availability

On June 5, 2026, Game Rant reported that Fanatical’s Mystery Egg Bundle offered a slim chance of receiving the LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Deluxe Edition for as little as $1. The bundle was a randomised draw — most keys resolved to smaller indie titles, but the premium “Golden Egg” tier included a shortlist of major AAA games: LEGO Batman Deluxe, Death Stranding 2: On the Beach, Invincible VS, Far Far West, and Doom: The Dark Ages.

  • Entry price: $1 per mystery key; $0.66/key for 30-key bundles
  • No guarantee of receiving any specific game
  • That specific bundle has since reset; check Fanatical.com for current offerings

Standard vs. Deluxe: Which Edition to Buy on Sale?

This is the more important question for anyone deciding to pull the trigger during the Summer Sale window. Here’s what each edition currently includes:

Edition Steam Price What’s Included
Standard Edition ~$59.99 Base game (main campaign, open-world Gotham, all 7 playable characters)
Deluxe Edition $80.00 Base game + Legacy Collection DLC (30 items across 3 themed packs) + Mayhem Collection DLC (September 18 — Joker & Harley Quinn, Arkham story mission, Sinister Pack)

The value calculus hinges on the Mayhem Collection. The DLC launches September 18, 2026 and will be sold separately for Standard Edition owners — pricing unconfirmed, but comparable DLC in past LEGO games has ranged from $9.99 to $19.99. If standalone DLC pricing lands at $15–$20, the $20 Deluxe upgrade is mathematically close to neutral, with the Legacy Collection’s 30 in-game items thrown in as additional value.

For players who are confident they will play through the Mayhem Collection content — the Arkham Asylum story mission and Mayhem Mode are genuinely novel additions for the series — the Deluxe Edition is the better long-term purchase, particularly if it receives a small Summer Sale discount on top of the base price.

What If There’s No Steam Discount for LEGO Batman?

Warner Bros. Games may simply choose not to participate in the Summer Sale for this particular title, or may opt for only a token 10% discount. That’s a plausible outcome for a 32-day-old game with strong critical reception (84 on Metacritic, 93% of critics recommend per OpenCritic). Publishers are generally reluctant to aggressively discount titles that are still selling well at full price.

If you’re committed to buying at the best possible price and don’t mind waiting, the game’s most significant Steam discounts will likely come during the Steam Autumn Sale 2026 (typically late November) or the Steam Winter Sale 2026 (December–January), when it will have been on sale for six-plus months. Games in the 84–90 Metacritic range with solid ongoing player counts commonly hit 25–40% off by their first major seasonal sale after the summer window.

Alternatively, with the Mayhem Collection DLC arriving September 18, there is a reasonable chance Warner Bros. runs a promotional discount on the Standard Edition to coincide with the DLC launch — a common publisher strategy for driving DLC sales by temporarily reducing the barrier to entry for new players. Keep an eye on the Steam store page and SteamDB for price history updates.

Where to Track the Best Price

Whether or not an official Steam discount materialises, the following tools will help you find the best price during and after the Summer Sale:

  • GG.deals — aggregates prices from authorised Steam key retailers, shows historical lows and price alerts
  • SteamDB Price History — tracks the official Steam price and all past discounts with timestamps
  • DLCompare — real-time comparison of key prices across authorised retailers
  • Fanatical.com — watch for bundle deals; the Mystery Egg Bundle has rotated in high-profile games previously

Setting a price alert on GG.deals or SteamDB is the most efficient way to ensure you’re notified the moment the price drops below your target threshold, without having to check manually every day.

The Bottom Line

The Steam Summer Sale 2026 opening on June 25 is worth watching, but temper your expectations for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight specifically. A small official discount (10–20%) is possible; a steep 33–50% cut this close to launch would be a genuine surprise. Third-party key sites are already offering meaningful savings over the full Steam retail price for both editions. And for anyone planning to play the September Mayhem Collection, the Deluxe Edition’s value proposition is strong regardless of whether the Summer Sale adds to it.

We’ll update this article once the sale goes live on June 25 and official discount figures are confirmed — or if Warner Bros. announces any standalone promotional pricing ahead of the DLC window.
