Mayhem Collection DLC Confirmed: September 18 Release Date & Full Content Breakdown

Published: June 21, 2026 · 6 min read

When we reported on the early Mayhem Collection leak back in May, the details were promising but unverified. Now, with official confirmation from Warner Bros. Games, we have a locked-in release date and the full content list. The Mayhem Collection DLC arrives on September 18, 2026, and it's a substantial expansion of LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight.

Here's everything that's been officially confirmed.

What Is the Mayhem Collection?

The Mayhem Collection is a post-launch DLC expansion included with the LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Deluxe Edition. Players who purchased the Standard Edition can acquire it separately (pricing to be announced). The expansion centers on the game's two most iconic villains — The Joker and Harley Quinn — and adds a significant chunk of new content across both story and gameplay.

According to the official press materials, the DLC introduces a brand-new game mode called Mayhem Mode, which is distinct from the base game's campaign. Rather than playing as the heroic side of Gotham, you'll be unleashing chaos on the city's streets.

Release date: September 18, 2026 — included in the Deluxe Edition; available as a standalone purchase for Standard Edition owners.

Playable Characters: Joker & Harley Quinn

The Joker and Harley Quinn are the headline additions as fully playable characters. Each comes with their own distinct set of abilities, gadgets, and takedown animations — reflecting their contrasting personalities and fighting styles.

The Joker

The Clown Prince of Crime brings his signature arsenal of trick gadgets and unpredictable combat moves. Based on official descriptions, his combat style emphasizes chaos and crowd control — fitting for a villain who thrives on disorder. Expect gadgets that confuse enemies and create environmental hazards, consistent with how the character has been portrayed in the Batman: Arkham series that clearly influenced Legacy of the Dark Knight's design.

Harley Quinn

Harley's abilities reportedly favor agility and physical power. Her background as a trained psychiatrist-turned-acrobatic villain gives her a unique combat signature: fast, unpredictable attacks paired with her distinctive oversized mallet. She's described as having her own gadgets and takedowns separate from The Joker, making the two characters genuinely distinct rather than palette swaps.

Note: These two characters join the seven base-game playable characters (Batman, Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, Gordon, Catwoman, and Talia al Ghul). They appear to be exclusive to the Mayhem Collection content rather than integrated into the main campaign Free Play.

The New Story Mission: Arkham Asylum Breakout

The centerpiece of the Mayhem Collection is a brand-new story mission set inside Arkham Asylum. The premise: The Joker and Harley Quinn need to break out of Arkham and get back to terrorizing the streets of Gotham City.

This is a smart narrative flip. The base game's campaign follows Batman and his allies working to restore order to Gotham; the DLC puts you on the opposite side of that equation, playing as the very villains the heroes have been fighting. Whether the events of the DLC connect directly to the main campaign's timeline hasn't been specified, but the Arkham Asylum setting is thematically rich — the institution is already referenced and depicted in the base game.

After the escape, the action moves to Gotham's streets for Mayhem Mode, where you cause as much chaos as possible. The details of what that entails mechanically — whether it's score-attack, mission-based, or open sandbox — haven't been fully described in official materials. More specifics are expected closer to the September launch.

The Sinister Pack: Suits, Batcave Items & Batmobile

Beyond the characters and story mission, the Mayhem Collection includes what's being called the Sinister Pack — a collection of cosmetic content for the base game's characters and your Batcave.

Sinister Pack Contents

  • 7 new suits — one per original playable character (Batman, Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, Gordon, Catwoman, Talia al Ghul)
  • 5 new Batcave items — decorative trophies and items for your Batcave hub
  • 1 new Batmobile — a new vehicle variant for your Batcave garage

The specific suit designs haven't been fully revealed, but given the "Sinister" theming, they may draw on villain-adjacent aesthetics or darker variants of the heroes' existing looks. The Legacy Collection (which launched with the Deluxe Edition) included suits inspired by the Batman: Arkham games, the Batman Beyond animated series, and a party-themed pack — suggesting TT Games has been creative with the suit sources.

How This Compares to the Legacy Collection

The Deluxe Edition's launch content — the Legacy Collection — was primarily a cosmetic and vehicle expansion: new suits for all seven characters, new Batcave items, and Batmobile variants drawn from the Arkham series, Batman Beyond, and a party pack. It was a solid value-add for fans who wanted to customize their experience, but it didn't add new story content.

The Mayhem Collection is a meaningfully different proposition. Two fully playable characters with unique move sets, a new story mission with its own setting, a new game mode, and the Sinister Pack cosmetics represent a considerably larger content footprint than the launch DLC. For players who've already reached 100% completion on the base game, the Mayhem Collection gives them a reason to come back to Gotham City.

Who Gets It Automatically?

If you purchased the LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Deluxe Edition (either at launch or as an upgrade via the Steam Deluxe Edition Upgrade listing), the Mayhem Collection is included at no additional cost. It will download and become available on September 18, 2026.

Standard Edition players will need to purchase the DLC separately. Warner Bros. Games has not yet announced the standalone price for the Mayhem Collection, though that information is expected to surface in the weeks ahead of the release date.

What We're Still Waiting For

A few details remain unconfirmed as of this writing. The full mechanical breakdown of Mayhem Mode — scoring, objectives, and how it integrates with the rest of the game — hasn't been described in detail. The Sinister Pack's specific suit designs are also still unrevealed, as are the five new Batcave items. Pre-release marketing typically ramps up four to six weeks before launch, so we can expect a more detailed reveal in August.

We'll update this article as new information becomes available. If you want to get ahead of the DLC, check our Legacy Collection breakdown to understand the existing Deluxe Edition content, and the full suits guide for the base game's 63+ outfits.

Mark your calendar: September 18, 2026 — Mayhem Collection DLC release. Deluxe Edition owners will get it automatically; Standard Edition players should watch for pricing announcements.
