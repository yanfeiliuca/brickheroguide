3 Secret Playable Characters You Probably Missed in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight

Published: June 13, 2026 · 6 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight officially features seven playable characters — Batman, Robin, Nightwing, Batgirl, Catwoman, Red Hood, and Commissioner Gordon. But the community has uncovered three more that TT Games quietly tucked away behind a specific D-pad trick: Alfred Pennyworth, Lucius Fox, and Bruce Wayne (without the cowl).

None of these are listed in the game's menu or character selection screen. There's no in-game prompt that tells you they exist. Discovered by players digging through the game's mission-select mechanics, these bonus characters have since been confirmed by multiple outlets, including a June 10 feature from Brick Fanatics cataloguing the game's best-hidden surprises. Here's exactly how to unlock all three.

Why These Characters Are Hidden

Alfred, Lucius Fox, and Bruce Wayne are all NPCs in the main game — they appear in cutscenes and at key story moments, but they were never intended to be part of the playable roster. What TT Games appear to have left in (intentionally or otherwise) is the ability to force the game's character system to select them during specific mission sections where they appear as interactable figures.

The trick itself is consistent: enter a mission section that features the character, select them via the character swap screen, hold Down on the D-pad, and then exit the mission. The game registers them as having been "selected" before your exit, and adds them to your free-roam playable roster. It's a quirk of how the game tracks character state — and it works reliably across all platforms.

Platform note: This trick works on PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC. The inputs are slightly different: PS5 uses the D-pad Down button, Xbox uses the same, and PC players should map D-pad controls or use "S" on the keyboard if using keyboard/mouse.

How to Unlock Alfred

Alfred Pennyworth

Mission: The Mimes — Section: “Home Sweet Home”

The Wayne family butler. Alfred has no unique combat abilities — he fights with a basic broom-swipe attack and cannot use gadgets — but he's fully functional in free-roam Gotham and is an absolute delight to drive the Batmobile as.

Step-by-step: Unlock Alfred

  1. From the main menu, go to Missions → The Mimes
  2. Select the completed section titled “Home Sweet Home” (this section must be previously completed)
  3. When the mission loads and the character swap screen becomes accessible, scroll to Alfred
  4. Highlight Alfred, then hold Down on the D-pad for approximately two seconds
  5. While still holding Down, select Exit Mission or Quit to Batcave
  6. Alfred will now appear in your free-roam character roster

If Alfred doesn't appear in your roster after the first attempt, try again — some players report needing to hold the D-pad input a moment longer before triggering the exit prompt.

How to Unlock Lucius Fox and Bruce Wayne

Both Lucius Fox and Bruce Wayne are unlocked using the same method, from the same mission section. The key difference from Alfred is that you need to unlock them one at a time — the trick only registers one character per exit.

Lucius Fox

Mission: Wayne Industries — Section: “WayneTech R&D”

Head of WayneTech R&D and the man behind Batman's gadgets. Lucius has a unique passive ability in free roam: WayneTech terminals around Gotham that normally require Batman's authorization can also be accessed by Lucius, making him unexpectedly useful for collectible hunting. He has no combat moves and will flee from enemies, which makes clearing combat zones an adventure.

Bruce Wayne

Mission: Wayne Industries — Section: “WayneTech R&D”

Bruce Wayne in his civilian suit — the man behind the mask. Like Alfred, Bruce has no combat abilities and cannot use any of Batman's gadgets. He does, however, have one unique interaction: Bruce Wayne can enter the Wayne Manor drawing room that's locked to all other characters, where a fully decorated room with easter eggs awaits. Worth visiting at least once.

Step-by-step: Unlock Lucius Fox (then repeat for Bruce Wayne)

  1. From the main menu, go to Missions → Wayne Industries
  2. Select the completed section titled “WayneTech R&D”
  3. When the character swap screen appears, scroll to Lucius Fox
  4. Hold Down on the D-pad for approximately two seconds, then exit the mission
  5. Lucius Fox will be added to your free-roam roster
  6. Repeat the entire process, this time selecting Bruce Wayne instead of Lucius Fox
Important: You must unlock Lucius and Bruce in separate runs of this trick. Attempting to hold both simultaneously or quickly switching between them before exiting will not register either character. One exit, one character.

What Can You Actually Do With These Characters?

In free-roam Gotham, all three bonus characters are fully mobile — they can run, drive any vehicle, swim, and interact with most environmental objects. Their limitations are in combat and gadget use:

  • Alfred attacks with a broom and has no gadgets. He can't grapple or glide. Enemies will still pursue him normally.
  • Lucius Fox has no combat moves at all. He runs from enemies. He can access WayneTech terminals, which is genuinely useful for collectible completion.
  • Bruce Wayne has no combat moves. He can enter the Wayne Manor drawing room (exclusive interaction). Hitting the combat button causes Bruce to raise his fists ineffectually.

None of these characters can be used in story missions or replayed chapters — they're purely a free-roam novelty. But for players hunting collectibles, Lucius Fox's terminal access and the general novelty of exploring Gotham as a completely unprepared civilian makes the unlock worthwhile.

Collectible tip: Lucius Fox can unlock WayneTech terminals that otherwise require Batman's Batclaw or specific gadgets. If you're stuck on a collectible that seems gated behind an ability you don't have, try switching to Lucius first.

Other Hidden Surprises Worth Finding

The three bonus characters are the most mechanically interesting discovery, but Brick Fanatics' deep-dive piece identified several other surprises that most players will miss. A few highlights worth seeking out:

The Keyboard Cat is tucked inside "The Party Man" mission. When Catwoman summons her cat to enter a security room, hopping up to the keyboard on a desk in front of a sleeping guard recreates the classic internet meme — the monitor reacts to each keypress. Gentleman Ghost appears as an optional photo challenge near Arkham Asylum: find the white ectoplasm puddles on the road, stand in them, open Photo Mode's edit screen, and the ghost will appear in your capture. The One Ring sits hidden in the rocks at the base of the Batcave — a nod to another Warner Bros. franchise.

The Batcave customisation also goes deeper than most players realize. Each giant Bat-logo in the cave can have its background color and symbol design changed independently via the console in front of it. The Batcomputer offers multiple free screensavers and even different computer models when you press down on the right thumbstick — a level of personalisation detail that TT Games never publicized.

These discoveries speak to the sheer density TT Games packed into Legacy of the Dark Knight. Three weeks after launch, players are still uncovering secrets. Given how many secrets the Arkham games contained — many of which weren't found for years — it's likely that Gotham is hiding quite a bit more.
