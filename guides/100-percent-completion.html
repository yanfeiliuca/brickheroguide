Reaching 100% completion in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight requires clearing every mission, collecting every Red Brick and Batcave collectible, completing all open-world side activities across Gotham's districts, unlocking all suits, and fully upgrading the Batcave. The good news: nothing is permanently missable. The Batcomputer in your Batcave lets you replay any mission at any time. Here is a phase-by-phase guide to getting there efficiently.

Overview & Most Efficient Order

The biggest mistake players make is trying to collect everything during the story playthrough. Many collectibles are locked behind character abilities (Batgirl's hacking terminal access, Catwoman's lockpick, Nightwing's wall-jump) that you will not have until late in the story or until Free Play unlocks after each mission. Hunting them early costs time and causes frustration. The most efficient order is:

  1. Full story, casual collection — unlock all characters, all Batcave rooms, all traversal abilities
  2. Batcave sweep — all 10 Batcave Minikits and 8 Batcave-located WayneTech Caches while all rooms are open
  3. WayneTech upgrades — invest Chips into grapple range and wall-run first, as these open the most collectible spots
  4. Stud Cache circuit — loop the open world's respawning Stud Caches for fast Stud income (no multiplier bricks exist in this game)
  5. Open-world sweep — district by district, all side activities
  6. Mission replay mop-up — any remaining Red Bricks and Gold Brick challenges
  7. Store purchases — suits and store Red Bricks once your Stud income is maxed
Key insight: Step 3 (WayneTech mobility upgrades) is the unlock that makes everything else faster — more grapple range and wall-run distance means faster access to collectible spots. There is no Stud Multiplier Red Brick in this game; the Stud Cache circuit (see our Stud Farming Guide) is the fastest way to fund the long open-world grind.

Phase 1: Story Playthrough (12–18 hours)

Play all 6 chapters and 21 main missions in order. Focus on enjoying the story and learning the combat system. Collect Studs naturally but do not hunt for collectibles — you will need story-unlocked abilities to reach many of them on replay.

At the end of this phase you will have: all 7 playable characters unlocked for Free Play, the Batmobile, grapple and glide traversal abilities, access to the full Gotham open world, and all Batcave rooms unlocked. The story alone delivers 6 story trophies and a foundation of Gold Bricks from mission completions.

What to Do During the Story

  • Break everything — environmental Studs accumulate passively and count toward Stud milestone trophies
  • Note locked doors, hackable terminals, and high platforms you cannot reach — these mark future collectible spots
  • Complete every mission's main objective; do not worry about optional challenge goals on the first pass
  • Visit the Batcave between chapters to check if new rooms have unlocked — sweep any newly opened room immediately

Phase 2: Open-World Gotham Completion (8–15 hours)

Gotham City is divided into distinct districts. Work through each district completely before moving on — the in-game map shows per-district completion percentages. Side activities in each district include:

  • Escaped Zoo Animals — use Detective Vision to find animals hiding across each district, then return them to the Gotham Zoo. Animals can be on rooftops, in alleys, or along waterfront areas. Each returned animal earns a Gold Brick.
  • AR Trials — timed combat and traversal challenges accessed via glowing AR terminals distributed across Gotham. They test grapple, glide, and combat timing. Completing each trial earns a Gold Brick; some have bonus time targets for a second reward.
  • Riddler Puzzle Rooms — environmental logic puzzles hidden throughout the city, marked with a green question mark when you are nearby. These reward Gold Bricks and occasionally unlock suits. They are the most commonly missed side activity because their map markers only appear when you are close.
  • Villain Encounters — scripted open-world boss fights in each district. These pop as you explore and reward Gold Bricks plus a short character vignette on completion.
  • Falcone Fortunes — 14 heist puzzle rooms hidden across Gotham's districts. Each one involves cracking a safe inside a puzzle room and rewards 2 WayneTech Chips (28 chips total across all 14). They unlock progressively: complete the introductory heist during the story, then work through the available Fortunes district by district to gradually unlock the remaining ones. Almost all require Batman + Catwoman; one ("No Heist Too Tough") also requires Batgirl. (Full location list: GameRant)

Completing all open-world activities is the main time investment for 100% and covers the majority of Gold Bricks not tied to mission content. According to community guides at Push Square, this phase takes 8–15 hours depending on thoroughness.

District Sweep Order (Recommended)

Start with the districts closest to the Batcave hub entrance — these tend to have the most forgiving activity density for learning the side activity formats. Then work outward. The game's map clearly marks which districts border each other, so a geographic sweep minimises fast-travel time between activities.

Phase 3: Mission Replay & Collectible Mop-Up (3–6 hours)

Use the Batcomputer to replay any missions where you missed collectibles. Key targets in mission replay:

  • In-world Red Bricks — replay in Free Play to use Batgirl, Catwoman, Nightwing, and Robin for character-locked spots
  • Gold Brick challenge objectives — each mission has optional challenge goals (defeat X enemies a certain way, finish under a time limit, avoid damage) that each reward a Gold Brick separate from story completion
  • Stud score targets — reaching the Stud threshold on mission replay earns a bonus Gold Brick per mission

The Batcomputer mission select screen shows which Gold Brick objectives you have completed and which remain, so you can instantly identify which missions need a second run.

Phase 4: Batcave & Store Completion (2–4 hours)

Finish the remaining items that require accumulated Studs and resources:

  • Purchase all remaining suits from the Bat-Mite Store — total cost across all 101 base-game suits is approximately 1,000,000 Studs; there is no Stud Multiplier in this game, so budget genuine playtime for the Stud Cache circuit (see our Stud Farming Guide)
  • Buy the 9 store Red Bricks not found in the world — these are cosmetic skins sold rather than hidden, same as the 14 found in story missions
  • Find all 10 Batcave Minikits and the 8 WayneTech Caches located within the Batcave (best done in Phase 1 post-story sweep, but any missed ones can be grabbed now — the remaining 192 of the game's 200 total WayneTech Caches are out in Gotham's open world)
  • Complete all Batcave structural upgrades using Gold Bricks — these unlock display rooms, vehicle bays, and the Trophy Room
  • Max out all WayneTech upgrades using all collected Chips
  • Fill the Batcave's vehicle display and the wardrobe (done naturally by purchasing suits)

Use the Stud Farming Guide if you need additional Studs — the Stud Cache fast-travel circuit is the fastest method and takes about 10–15 minutes per cycle.

Easy to Miss: Items Players Most Often Skip

These are the items that community players on r/legogaming and trophy tracking sites most frequently report missing on their first 100% attempt:

Riddler Puzzle Rooms

These do not appear on the minimap until you are within a short radius of their entrance — no waypoint, no indicator until you are already close. Players who fast-travel around Gotham and move quickly between marked objectives commonly miss 3–5 Puzzle Rooms per district. The fix: walk each district on foot (or at low altitude in glide mode) on your final sweep to let proximity markers pop. Detective Vision highlights the green question mark entrances through walls and obstacles.

Gold Brick Mission Challenges

The Batcomputer mission select screen shows story completion for each mission but the challenge objective descriptions can be easy to overlook. Each of the 21 missions has at least one optional challenge goal that awards a separate Gold Brick — completing the mission does not automatically complete the challenge. Check the mission detail screen before replaying and identify the specific challenge condition before starting so you can target it intentionally.

Batcave Minikits in Late-Unlocking Rooms

Two Batcave rooms remain locked until Chapters 5 and 6. Players who rush the story and do a single Batcave sweep early will miss the Minikits in these rooms. If your Minikit count is below 10 after the story, the late-unlock rooms are the first place to check.

WayneTech Caches Behind Upgrade-Gated Areas

A few WayneTech Caches in the Batcave are in spots that require specific WayneTech upgrades to reach — a wall-run segment or a grapple-and-swing route. If you sweep the Batcave before spending any Chips on traversal upgrades, you may miss these. Invest in at least one level of grapple range and wall-run before doing your Batcave sweep.

AR Trial Bonus Time Targets

Each AR Trial has a standard completion reward (Gold Brick) and a bonus time target that rewards additional Studs and sometimes a secondary collectible. Many players complete the trial and leave without noticing the bonus target on the results screen. These are not required for 100% but count toward certain Stud milestone trophy thresholds.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the game consider "100%"?

The in-game completion tracker measures: all missions completed, all collectibles found (Red Bricks, Minikits, WayneTech Caches, Gold Bricks), all open-world side activities cleared, all suits purchased, and all Batcave upgrades completed. When the tracker reads 100%, the Platinum trophy pops automatically on PS5 (or the final achievement unlocks on Xbox).

Do I need to replay every mission in Free Play?

Only the missions where you missed collectibles during the story. Not every mission has an in-world Red Brick requiring Free Play characters — many collectibles during story missions are reachable with the default characters. Use the Batcomputer's mission completion display to identify exactly which missions still have outstanding Gold Brick challenges or uncollected items before replaying.

Can I carry over progress if I start a new game?

Legacy of the Dark Knight uses a single continuous save file — there is no new game plus that resets progress. Your Stud totals, collectibles, and suit unlocks all persist. The only reason to replay missions is to meet challenge objectives you skipped, not to restart your progress.

How long does 100% take compared to just finishing the story?

The story alone runs 12–18 hours. Full 100% is 25–40 hours. The difference is almost entirely open-world side activities (Phase 2) and mission replay for challenge Gold Bricks (Phase 3). Looping the Stud Cache circuit (see our Stud Farming Guide) keeps the Stud grind for suits and store Red Bricks from adding much to the top end of the estimate.

Is there any DLC that affects 100%?

DLC suits and the Mayhem Collection content (if purchased) appear in the Bat-Mite Store as separate catalogue entries and are tracked separately from the base-game 101 suits. The base-game 100% counter does not require DLC — you can reach 100% without purchasing any additional content.
