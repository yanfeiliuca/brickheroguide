LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has the largest costume roster of any LEGO game ever made. The base game includes 101 suits and outfits across all seven playable characters, with the Deluxe Edition's DLC pushing that number to 126. Here's everything you need to know about unlocking them all.

Overview

Every playable character — Batman, Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, Catwoman, Jim Gordon, and Talia al Ghul — has their own dedicated wardrobe of unlockable outfits. Suits are purely cosmetic in most cases, meaning they change your character's appearance without altering their core moveset. A small number of film-inspired skins come with unique idle animations and combat finishers that reflect their source material, but the core abilities remain the same across all outfits for a given character.

Cost to 100%: Buying every suit from the Bat-Mite Store alone would cost over 1,000,000 Studs. Prioritise Red Bricks and Stud Multipliers early — see our Stud Farming Guide for the fastest methods.

3 Ways to Unlock Suits

1. Mission Rewards

Every story mission and major side mission awards one new suit for each character you played during that mission. This is the primary unlock path and requires no Studs. Play through the full story to unlock the bulk of the roster automatically. You cannot miss these — just complete the missions.

2. Bat-Mite Store

The Bat-Mite Store in the Batcave sells additional suits using Studs (the in-game currency you collect by smashing everything in sight). Prices vary by outfit. Stock up on Studs through free-roam Gotham and Stud Multiplier Red Bricks before shopping here. The store is available from early in the game.

3. Side Activities & Collectibles

Some suits are tied to completing specific side activities — solving Riddler Puzzle Rooms, catching escaped zoo animals, finishing AR Trials, or finding hidden Stud Caches. These tend to be the rarer film-inspired and event-specific outfits.

DLC Note: Pre-order bonuses (such as the "Dark Knight Returns" Batsuit) and the Mayhem Collection DLC add 25 additional suits not included in the base 101. See the DLC Suits section below.

How to Change Suits

To change your outfit at any time while in the open world (not inside a vehicle):

  1. Press Start / Options to open the menu
  2. Press R1 / RB to navigate to the Characters tab
  3. Press Square / X to open Suit Options
  4. Browse and select any unlocked suit

You can also change suits inside the Batcave's Wardrobe room at any time. Suits can be changed freely between missions without any penalty or cost.

Film-Inspired Skins

A standout feature of Legacy of the Dark Knight is its collection of actor-likeness skins modeled on specific Batman film performances. These are among the most memorable outfits in the game and are confirmed to be in the base roster:

  • Michelle Pfeiffer's Catwoman — from Batman Returns (1992)
  • Danny DeVito's Penguin — from Batman Returns (1992)
  • Heath Ledger's Joker — from The Dark Knight (2008)
  • Jeffrey Wright's Jim Gordon — from The Batman (2022)
  • Adam West Batman — from the 1966 TV series
  • Michael Keaton Batman — from Batman (1989)

Film skins often include unique idle animations and special finisher animations that reference the character's cinematic portrayal. According to GamesRadar, these skins are among the most sought-after unlocks in the game.

DLC Suits

The Deluxe Edition includes two DLC packs that add suits beyond the base 101:

  • Legacy Collection (available at launch with Deluxe Edition): suits inspired by the Batman: Arkham game series, Batman Beyond animated series, and a party-themed cosmetic pack.
  • Mayhem Collection — Sinister Pack (September 2026): 7 new suits, one per original playable character, plus 5 new Batcave props and a new Batmobile. Comes with Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters in a new story mission. See our Mayhem Collection DLC Guide for full details.

Tips for Collectors

  • Unlock Red Brick Stud Multipliers first. Some Red Bricks double or quadruple your Stud income. The fastest path to buying all shop suits is stacking multipliers early.
  • Replay missions via the Batcomputer. You can return to any completed mission to clean up missed collectibles and earn any suit you skipped.
  • Don't ignore side activities. Zoo animals, AR Trials, and Riddler rooms each tie to specific outfit unlocks. Check your map for outstanding activities in each district.
  • Suits display in the Batcave Wardrobe. As you unlock outfits, they populate the wardrobe room — a great visual progress tracker for completionists.
