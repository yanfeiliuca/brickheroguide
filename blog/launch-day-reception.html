LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Launches to "Overwhelmingly Positive" Reviews — 84 on Metacritic, 97% on Steam

📅 May 22, 2026  |  ⏱ 5 min read  |  BrickHeroGuide

Today is the day. LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is now fully available to all players on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC — and if the numbers are anything to go by, TT Games has pulled off something genuinely special. After three days of Deluxe Edition early access starting May 19, the general launch has arrived to a chorus of near-universal praise from both critics and players alike.

Here's everything we know about how the game has landed on its official release day.

🏆 Historical Milestone: With a Metacritic score of 84, Legacy of the Dark Knight is the highest-rated LEGO game ever released — surpassing LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga (83), which held the record for four years. Source: GamesRadar+

What Critics Are Saying

The consensus among professional reviewers is that Legacy of the Dark Knight succeeds by combining the accessible charm of classic LEGO titles with a surprisingly deep combat system inspired by the Batman: Arkham series.

The Praise

PC Gamer awarded the game 83/100, according to their review, highlighting the revamped combat, detailed open world, and charming story.

NME called it "a block-buster", noting it "leaves no meat on the bone, with no corner of Batman's mythology left unturned as it depicts every version of the Caped Crusader fans have come to love."

Game Informer titled their review "Batman, Built Different" — a nod to how TT Games has evolved the LEGO formula beyond what fans expected.

GamesRadar+ awarded it 4/5, describing its combat as "Arkham-lite" — familiar gadget use and combo chaining in a more family-friendly package. Their review also highlighted how the game was built on the Skywalker Saga tech stack, giving it a noticeably polished open-world feel.

Push Square gave it 8/10 and Shacknews scored it 9/10, making it one of the more unanimously praised games of the year so far.

The Criticisms

No game is without its rough edges. Critics pointed to two recurring issues: stealth sections that feel underdeveloped compared to the main combat, and minor technical glitches. VGC (4/5) noted that collectibles are "very slightly excessive" — though as one Steam reviewer put it, "that's just part of the LEGO formula." Game Informer singled out the stealth mechanics as a weak point in an otherwise strong package.

📋 Platform Score Variation: The Xbox Series X|S version scores 86 on Metacritic — two points higher than PS5 (84) and three points higher than PC (83). The variation likely reflects platform-specific performance and frame rate differences, not content changes. All versions contain identical game content.

What Players Are Saying on Steam

Professional critics aren't the only ones impressed. As of launch day, Legacy of the Dark Knight holds an "Overwhelmingly Positive" rating on Steam with 97% positive reviews from over 1,700 players. That's a remarkably high rating for a major release, where anything above 95% in the first 24 hours is considered exceptional.

A few standout reactions from the Steam community:

  • One player called it "probably my biggest gaming surprise of 2026" — they "expected absolutely nothing from it" and were blown away.
  • Deluxe Edition buyers reported it was "completely worth it," with several specifically excited about the September DLC featuring Joker as a playable character.
  • The most common criticism echoed the professional reviewers: too many collectibles — though most players treated this as an observation rather than a dealbreaker.

Source: Steam user reviews

Is the Game Also Playable on Steam Deck?

For handheld players, there's good news. According to Steam Deck HQ, Legacy of the Dark Knight is "surprisingly playable" on the Steam Deck — a notable bonus for players who prefer portable gaming.

What's Next: The Mayhem Collection DLC

For players who want more after launch, the confirmed DLC is The Mayhem Collection, arriving in September 2026. It adds a Joker and Harley Quinn story mission, 7 new suits, a new Batmobile variant, and 5 Batcave props. Deluxe Edition buyers already have access to this content included in their purchase.

The Verdict from Launch Day

It's early hours, but the picture is already clear: LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is not just a good LEGO game — it may be the best one TT Games has ever made. A Metacritic score of 84 with "Generally Favorable" reviews, 100% recommendation rate on OpenCritic, and 97% positive Steam reviews within hours of general launch paint the picture of a rare, genuine crowd-pleaser.

Whether you're a LEGO veteran, a Batman fan, or someone who's never touched a LEGO title before, the consensus seems to be: this one is worth your time.
