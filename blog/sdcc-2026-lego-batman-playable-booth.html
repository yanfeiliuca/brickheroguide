LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Gets a Playable Booth at San Diego Comic-Con 2026

Published: July 8, 2026 · 6 min read

DC published its full lineup for Comic-Con International: San Diego 2026 this morning, and LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has a confirmed spot on the show floor. The game will be playable at the DC Booth (#4544) for the entire run of the convention, July 22 through July 26, with giveaway items reserved for attendees who sit down and play.

It's the first confirmed public appearance for the game since its May 22 launch, and it lands right in the middle of the countdown to two other milestones: the Mayhem Collection DLC and the Nintendo Switch 2 release, both set for September 18.

DC Booth #4544: Dates, Location, and What's Around It

According to DC's official press release, published July 8, the DC Booth (#4544) opens on the SDCC convention floor Wednesday, July 22 and runs through Sunday, July 26. This year's booth theme centers on a 1950s Silver Age celebration of DC's publishing history, complete with a Daily Planet-styled newsstand giving away a free MAD Magazine #1 facsimile. LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight gets its own dedicated activation within that space, as part of DC's ongoing collaboration with WB Games.

The booth isn't exclusively about the game — it's shared with creator signings (Jim Lee, Scott Snyder, Mark Waid, G. Willow Wilson, Matt Fraction, and others), a Superman Experience photo-op, and activations from partners including Upper Deck, Shoe Palace, and Warner Bros. Studio Tour Hollywood. But the game receives explicit, named billing in DC's own announcement, which stands out given how packed the SDCC schedule usually is.

Playable Stations and Giveaways: What's Confirmed

Per DC's press materials, "the DC Booth will include stations to play LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight," with giveaway items available on-site for attendees who play the game, while supplies last. DC hasn't specified exactly what the giveaway items are, how many demo stations will be set up, or which mission or level slice will be featured. Those specifics are typically confirmed closer to the event or revealed on-site.

What is confirmed is that this is a hands-on activation rather than a static merchandise table. Convention-goers will be able to actually play the game at Booth #4544, which is a meaningful marketing push for a title roughly two months past launch rather than a brand-new release needing a splashy reveal.

Quick Facts

  • Booth: DC Booth #4544, San Diego Convention Center show floor
  • Dates: Wednesday, July 22 – Sunday, July 26, 2026
  • Activation: Playable demo stations for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight
  • Reward: On-site giveaway items for attendees who play, while supplies last
  • Partner: Collaboration between DC and WB Games

Why This Matters: The September 18 Double Release

The timing lines up with a busy back half of the year for the game. Two major milestones both land on September 18, 2026: the Nintendo Switch 2 version of the base game, and the Mayhem Collection DLC, which adds The Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, a new Arkham Asylum story mission, and Mayhem Mode for Deluxe Edition owners across Switch 2, PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC.

An SDCC presence in late July gives WB Games and DC a high-visibility moment roughly seven weeks ahead of that September launch window — a natural spot to build anticipation with a convention crowd, even though DC's announcement doesn't confirm that any new Mayhem Collection reveals will happen at the booth itself.

For background on what's coming in September, see our full Mayhem Collection breakdown and the DLC guide.

Where the Game Stands Two Months After Launch

Heading into SDCC, LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is still drawing a healthy crowd on Steam. Live Steam Charts data shows the game averaging just over 14,570 concurrent players over the last 30 days, with a 24-hour peak of 17,601 and an all-time peak of 33,053 concurrent players recorded around launch. That's a normal post-launch cooldown curve for a single-player-focused LEGO title rather than a red flag — most LEGO games see the bulk of their playtime concentrated in the first few weeks after release.

On the review side, the game holds an 8/10 from IGN and 4/5 from GamesRadar, and Steam's user reviews remain in "Overwhelmingly Positive" territory with more than 11,600 reviews logged.

Note: DC's announcement does not mention any playable Switch 2 build or Mayhem Collection demo at the booth — the confirmed activation is for the current base game, available now on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC (Steam & Epic Games Store).

What to Expect If You're at SDCC

If you're attending Comic-Con International in San Diego this year, the DC Booth (#4544) is your spot for hands-on time with LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, plus a shot at whatever giveaway items WB Games and DC bring along. DC says more details on booth activations will be revealed in the coming weeks, so specifics on the giveaway items and demo setup are still to come. We'll update this post if WB Games or DC releases more information before the show opens.
