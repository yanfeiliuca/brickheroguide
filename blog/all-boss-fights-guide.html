All 10 Boss Fights in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — Chapter-by-Chapter Strategies

Published: June 28, 2026 · 6 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight packs 10 boss encounters across its Prologue and six chapters, each with distinct attack patterns and multi-phase mechanics that go well beyond the standard LEGO game formula. The difficulty is never brutal — even on the hardest Dark Knight setting — but knowing what each villain telegraphs before they attack makes every encounter faster and cleaner. Here is every boss fight in story order, with the key strategies that actually matter.

Universal tip: When fighting any boss, simply aim at an interaction point instead of manually swapping gadgets — the game auto-equips the correct tool. This removes gadget-juggling pressure during high-stakes phases. (Source: Kotaku)

Prologue: Ra's al Ghul

Prologue — Origins & The League of Shadows
Ra's al Ghul

Phase 1: Ra's telegraphs a spinning sword attack at close range, ranged shuriken throws, and a teleport-reappear combo. Don't chase him when he vanishes — wait for the landing animation and punish with a 3-hit combo. Keep mobile between phases to avoid the shurikens, which track slightly on the horizontal axis.

This is the game's tutorial boss and the forgiving difficulty reflects that. Ra's introduces the dodge-and-punish rhythm that every later boss builds on.

Chapter 1: Red Hood One

Chapter 1 — Returning Home / Falcone / Red Hood
Red Hood One (Joker Origin)

Phase 1: Red Hood One opens with a rocket-launcher spread. The lock-on reticule goes red just before each burst fires — roll perpendicular to the beam, not away from it. After depleting his first health bar, he switches to melee rushes with electric punches. Time a Batarang stun at mid-range just before contact to interrupt the charge.

This is the most mechanically interesting early fight: rockets require spatial awareness, while the melee phase rewards Batarang timing over button-mashing. Playing on Caped Crusader (middle difficulty) keeps this fun without turning it into a spam-fest.

Chapter 2: The Penguin & The Joker

Chapter 2 — Mimes / Penguin / Joker
The Penguin

Penguin uses an umbrella melee jab with a distinct windup animation — the umbrella tip glows before the strike. Punish the windup with a Catwoman whip pull or Batman's 3-hit combo. Between melee phases, he drops proximity bombs from his umbrella hat; keep moving in a wide arc to avoid the explosion radius.

Chapter 2 — Mimes / Penguin / Joker
The Joker

The Joker fight takes place across moving parade floats, which is the real hazard here. Stay mobile to avoid the Smilex gas plumes he fires from his lapel flower — the cloud lingers for several seconds after deployment. When Joker jumps between floats, use the momentary landing stagger to land 2–3 hits before he recovers into his next gas attack. His hand taser deals bonus stun damage to whichever character you're not controlling, so swap characters frequently to spread the pressure.

Chapter 3: Two-Face & Poison Ivy

Chapter 3 — Two-Face / Kite Man / Poison Ivy
Two-Face

Phase 1 (Heads): When the coin-flip lands heads, Two-Face charges directly at you. Sidestep left or right and counter immediately with Robin's bo-staff combo — it hits on both sides and triggers the knockdown faster than Batman's slower grapple setup. Phase 2 (Tails): Two-Face calls in armed henchmen and hangs back with a shotgun. Prioritise clearing henchmen first using crowd-control gadgets; only attack Two-Face when the arena is clear.

Chapter 3 — Two-Face / Kite Man / Poison Ivy
Poison Ivy

Phase 1 (Dr. Pamela Isley): Pre-transformation, Ivy uses vine-whip melee swings in a wide arc. Her tells are slow, so most players can simply combo through this phase on Normal. Phase 2 (Snapdragon plant-creature): The Snapdragon form adds a ground-spike AoE attack — jump just before ground contact to clear the spikes. Use Batgirl's electric Batarang (unlocked if you've been investing WayneTech chips in ranged upgrades) to stun the creature between ground-slam phases and land extended combos.

Chapter 4: Firefly & Mr. Freeze

Chapter 4 — Firefly / Batgirl Begins / Mr. Freeze
Firefly

Firefly opens by torching the museum entrance hall. Before engaging him directly, douse the floor flames using the overhead water pipes — activate them with Batman's Batclaw. Fighting Firefly on a burning floor triggers persistent damage-over-time that stacks quickly on Dark Knight difficulty. Once the floor is clear, Firefly cycles between dive-bomb passes and hovering flame blasts; punish the post-dive landing with a Batarang stun followed by a combo.

Chapter 4 — Firefly / Batgirl Begins / Mr. Freeze
Mr. Freeze

Phase 1 (on foot): Freeze channels a wide freeze AoE — jump just before the ice wave lands to avoid getting frozen solid. Staying frozen for more than 2 seconds on Dark Knight difficulty results in a one-hit kill from his follow-up beam. The jump window is generous. Phase 2 (frost machine): Freeze enters a cryo-walker suit and gains a shield that blocks frontal damage. Attack from behind or use Batgirl's EMP gadget to stagger the suit briefly and open a damage window. Repeat three times to end the fight. Note: a bug in this fight was blocking story trophy unlocks prior to patch 1.006 (June 2, 2026), which fixed the crash. Make sure you've applied the update before attempting this encounter if you're on console.

Mr. Freeze patch note: Update 1.006 (June 2, 2026) fixed a crash during the Mr. Freeze boss fight that was preventing story-related trophies from unlocking. If you experienced this on PS5 or Xbox, replay the encounter post-patch to collect your trophy. See our full 1.006 patch breakdown for details.

Chapter 6: Bane & Talia al Ghul (Final Boss)

Chapter 6 — The League of Shadows Returns
Bane

Bane straight-line-charges with zero deviation — keep to the sides of the arena. Throw a Batarang just as he's about to reach you to stun him at the end of each charge; this interrupts his follow-up grab attack. After enough charges, Bane pumps Venom and enlarges: his move-set slows but his damage output spikes significantly. Stick to quick 1-2 hit combos and retreat before the Venom-phase grab comes out.

Chapter 6 — The League of Shadows Returns (Final Boss)
Talia al Ghul

Phase 1: Sidestep her straight-line charge; on her platform-jump tell (yellow surge beneath her feet), jump immediately to avoid the shockwave. Punish her slow short-range slashes with 3-hit combos followed by a quick retreat. Phase 2: Talia gains a Lazarus-enhanced form — her attacks speed up and she adds a tracking projectile. The Batclaw is your best friend here: grapple her mid-charge to reset her position and negate her momentum. Stay aggressive between projectile volleys and don't let her recover into the enhanced form's full attack cadence.

General Boss Fight Tips

A few universal principles hold across all 10 encounters. First, difficulty matters more than players expect: on Dark Knight mode, boss arenas spawn additional minions during key phases that don't appear on lower settings — fighting two-on-one in the Joker parade fight while also managing platform gaps is a genuinely different experience than Caped Crusader. If a fight feels unfair, check your difficulty setting before assuming a bug.

Second, unlock Batman's grapple slam early through WayneTech upgrades. Shield-carrying minions appear in several boss arenas (especially Two-Face and Bane) and the grapple slam bypasses shields entirely — it lets you hop over an enemy and slam them to the ground with the right trigger. Without it, shield enemies become a slog that drags out already-extended boss phases. For a prioritised WayneTech upgrade order, see our WayneTech Upgrades Guide.

Third, use the Batcomputer in the Batcave to replay any boss fight you missed collectibles in. All 10 encounters are replayable at any time, so there's no need to retry on the spot if you're stuck — lower the difficulty, collect what you need, and move on.

For trophy hunters: five of the 10 boss fights are tied to story trophies on PS5 (52 trophies base) and Xbox (51 achievements). None require perfect no-hit runs — they simply require defeating the boss, making this one of the more accessible trophy categories in the game. Full details are in our Trophy & Achievement Guide.
