LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Confirmed for Nintendo Switch 2 on September 18

Published: June 4, 2026 · 5 min read · News

Nintendo Switch 2 players now have a firm date to look forward to: Warner Bros. Games officially announced on June 3, 2026 that LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight will launch on Nintendo Switch 2 on September 18, 2026. Pre-orders are live now on the Nintendo eShop for both the Standard Edition ($69.99) and the Deluxe Edition ($89.99).

The timing is significant for more than just Switch 2 fans. According to the official Warner Bros. Games press release, September 18 is also the day the long-anticipated Mayhem Collection DLC — featuring The Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters — arrives on all platforms simultaneously. That means PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC players will be getting major new content on the same day the Switch 2 version launches.

What's Included in the Switch 2 Deluxe Edition

The Switch 2 Deluxe Edition ($89.99) is the most content-packed version of the game available at launch on the platform. Buyers get everything in one package on day one, including DLC that PS5 and Xbox owners have been waiting for since May.

Switch 2 Deluxe Edition — Day One Content

Available September 18, 2026
  • Legacy Collection DLC — New suits, vehicles, and Batcave items inspired by the Batman: Arkham series, Batman Beyond, and a party music-themed pack
  • Mayhem Collection DLC — Mayhem Mode with The Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, a new story mission, and the Sinister Pack (7 new suits, 5 Batcave items, 1 new Batmobile)
  • Free LEGO Retro Video Game Batman Minifigure — Physical edition only, while supplies last
Pre-Order Bonus: All Nintendo Switch 2 pre-orders (Standard and Deluxe) include The Dark Knight Returns in-game Batsuit, inspired by the acclaimed Frank Miller comic book series. This is available at launch for those who pre-order before September 18.

The Mayhem Collection DLC: What to Expect

The Mayhem Collection is the headliner of the September 18 update, and it's coming to every platform at the same time. According to the official announcement, the DLC centers on a brand-new Mayhem Mode in which players take control of The Joker and Harley Quinn rather than Batman and his allies. This represents a significant tonal shift — Mayhem Mode is described as featuring a new story mission built around Gotham's most chaotic duo.

Beyond the mode itself, the Sinister Pack bundled with the Mayhem Collection adds seven new suits (one for each of the main game's playable characters), five new Batcave display items, and a new Batmobile variant. For Deluxe Edition owners on PS5, Xbox, and PC who have been playing since May, this will be a free automatic unlock on launch day.

Note for current owners: If you own the Standard Edition on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, or PC (Steam/Epic), you can purchase the Deluxe Edition upgrade for $24.99 to receive the Legacy Collection DLC now and the Mayhem Collection DLC when it drops on September 18.

Free Batsuits via Linked Accounts

Players on any platform — including Switch 2 at launch — can unlock additional Batsuits for free by linking their Warner Bros. Games account and HBO Max account. According to the official announcement, three suits are available through this route:

  • Golden Age Batsuit — Based on Batman's debut look from Detective Comics #27 (1939). Available to anyone who creates or already has a Warner Bros. Games account.
  • Dark Knights of Steel Batsuit — Inspired by DC's Dark Knights of Steel comic series. Requires a linked HBO Max account.
  • Black Lantern Batsuit — Inspired by HBO's Lanterns. Also requires a linked HBO Max account.

These account-linked Batsuits are available regardless of which edition (Standard or Deluxe) players own, and there's no additional cost beyond having the relevant accounts.

LEGO Set Tie-Ins with In-Game Content

Warner Bros. Games also highlighted four physical LEGO DC Batman sets that include redeemable in-game content codes, applicable now on PS5/Xbox/PC and at launch on Switch 2. Each set unlocks a playable in-game vehicle or exclusive cosmetic:

  • LEGO DC Batman: Batman Logo (76330) — Unlocks an exclusive gold Batsuit in-game
  • LEGO DC Batman: The Batman Batmobile (76332) — Unlocks the Batmobile from The Batman (2022) plus an exclusive gold variant
  • LEGO DC Batman: Batman v Superman Batmobile (76331) — Unlocks the BvS Batmobile plus gold variant
  • LEGO DC Batman: Batman & Robin Batmobile (76333) — Unlocks the 1997 Batmobile plus gold variant

It's worth noting that the gold Batsuit and gold vehicle variants are only available through the physical LEGO set codes — they cannot be purchased or unlocked any other way.

Why the Switch 2 Wait?

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight launched on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC on May 22, 2026, with no Switch or Switch 2 version available at that time. The nearly four-month gap to September 18 is typical for ports targeting Nintendo's hardware, which often requires additional optimization work. The Switch 2 is a capable machine, but adapting an Unreal Engine 5 open-world game to any new platform takes time.

From a business perspective, the simultaneous Mayhem Collection DLC release on all platforms on September 18 is a smart move — it gives Switch 2 players the fullest experience at launch, while also giving the existing player base on other platforms a compelling reason to return to the game after a few months away. According to Nintendo Everything, a new release date announcement trailer is also available on YouTube.
