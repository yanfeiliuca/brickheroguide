LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight features 7 playable characters, each with distinct abilities that unlock different puzzle solutions, collectible spots, and combat approaches. During your story playthrough, character choice is mostly locked to whoever the mission assigns — but in Free Play (available after completing each mission) you can bring any unlocked character and revisit every area. This guide covers both: what each character does, and who to bring on each mission replay to clean up collectibles efficiently.

Character Ability Overview

Batman — The All-Rounder

Primary protagonist · Story-default character

Batman is the most versatile character thanks to his interchangeable Batsuits. Base Batman has access to the Batarang (ranged stun), grapple hook (traversal and combat), and Detective Vision (highlights interactable objects and hidden clues through walls). Suit swaps grant additional abilities: the Thermal suit for heat-based puzzles, the Stealth suit for undetected sections, and the Dark Knight suit for the final chapter.

He is your workhorse for the story — most missions are designed around his toolkit. For Free Play collectible runs, Batman's Detective Vision is invaluable for spotting hidden wall breakpoints and interactable terminals you may have walked past.

Robin — The Agile Technician

Acrobatic combat · Tech puzzle specialist

Robin's standout abilities are his tech suit (required for certain Gotham industrial district puzzle sequences) and his acrobatic movement, which lets him reach elevated platforms and traverse narrow environments faster than other characters. In combat, his staff attacks have wider sweep arcs than Batman's punches, making him slightly better at crowd control in open areas. He is the go-to character for any mission or Free Play section with a glowing tech panel that Batman cannot interact with.

Batgirl — The Stealth Hacker

Hacking · Stealth takedowns · Tower activation

Batgirl is arguably the most important character for 100% completion. Her hacking ability activates terminal-locked doors and collectible-revealing towers throughout Gotham — towers that, once activated, light up nearby Red Bricks and Gold Bricks on the minimap for an entire district. Several in-world Red Bricks sit behind hackable terminals that no other character can open. She also has a stealth finisher that neutralises enemies silently, useful in sections where combat triggers reinforcements.

Catwoman — The Infiltrator

Whip · Call Kitty · Wall Climbing · Safe Cracker · Glass Cutter

Catwoman has the broadest ability set of any non-Batman character. Her Whip rotates LEGO screws and pulls distant objects, doubling as crowd control in combat. Call Kitty deploys a cat companion that navigates ventilation shafts and tight spaces no playable character can enter — essential for duct-hidden collectibles. Wall Climbing reaches textured vertical surfaces marked with claw scratch marks. Safe Cracker opens LEGO safes scattered across every district (often containing WayneTech Chips). Glass Cutter slices through reinforced glass panels blocking hidden rooms and shortcuts. She is essential for any Free Play run in Chapters 2 and beyond — of the seven characters, she gates the most content after Batgirl. See our full Catwoman Complete Character Guide for ability details and puzzle-type breakdowns.

Commissioner Gordon — The Support Specialist

Foam gun · Evidence gathering · Crowd control

Gordon's foam gun can neutralise specific enemy types that resist normal melee attacks — particularly armoured enforcers in Chapters 1 and 2. His evidence-gathering ability highlights clue trails that lead to optional Gold Brick rewards in open-world investigation sequences. He is not a primary collectible-gating character (unlike Batgirl or Catwoman) but shines in specific combat encounters and is worth having available during Chapter 1 Free Play runs.

Talia al Ghul — The Melee Specialist

Sword combat · Parkour · League of Shadows access

Talia brings the fastest and most aggressive melee moveset in the roster. Her sword combos deal higher per-hit damage than any other character, making her the most efficient choice for combat-heavy Free Play sections. Her parkour traversal is also faster than standard grapple movement in open environments. Certain League of Shadows-themed puzzle elements in Chapter 6 areas specifically respond to Talia's presence, opening shortcuts unavailable to other characters.

Nightwing — The Acrobatic Fighter

Wall-jump · Escrima combat · Crowd control

Nightwing's wall-jump reaches elevated platforms that neither Batman's grapple nor Catwoman's wall-run can access — specifically certain upper-level Red Brick locations in Chapters 3 through 5. His escrima stick combos are designed for handling multiple enemies simultaneously, making him the best combat character against groups. For Free Play, bring Nightwing to any mission where you noticed an elevated platform or wall-jump surface you could not use during the story.

Prologue — Wayne Manor & League of Shadows

Missions: Wayne Manor Grounds, Falcone Confrontation, The League of Shadows, Infiltration.

Story character: Batman only

The Prologue is a controlled tutorial — Batman is the only available character and his abilities are introduced progressively. Focus on mastering the grapple hook timing and Detective Vision rhythm. There are no character-locked collectibles in the Prologue that require Free Play; anything missed can be retrieved with Batman alone on replay.

Chapter 1 — Red Hood Gang

Missions: New Beginnings, Tricorner Investigation, Riddle Me This, Riddler, Carmine Falcone, The Subwayne, Spelunking, A Family Friend, WayneTech R&D, Glider Calibration, The Red Hood Gang.

Story characters: Batman, Gordon (mid-chapter)

Gordon's foam gun is the key tool here. Falcone's armoured enforcers resist Batman's Batarang — Gordon's foam neutralises them cleanly. The Riddler puzzle chain (Riddle Me This → Riddler missions) is worth completing in sequence to earn WayneTech tokens that fund early traversal upgrades.

Free Play priority: Bring Batgirl for terminal-locked rooms in The Subwayne and WayneTech R&D. Catwoman's lockpick opens side containers in Tricorner Investigation. Robin's tech suit is needed for one puzzle panel in Spelunking. This chapter has the highest density of character-locked collectibles of any in the game — budget extra time for Chapter 1 Free Play runs.

Chapter 2 — The Joker

Missions: Mimes, The Penguin, Flugelheim, Joker.

Story characters: Batman, Catwoman (unlocked during chapter)

Catwoman unlocks mid-chapter and her whip makes the Flugelheim Museum's multi-level puzzle sections significantly faster. Batman's Detective Vision is critical in the Joker mission for tracking Joker-gas vents before they incapacitate the team — scan each room before engaging.

Free Play priority: The Penguin mission has two Catwoman lockpick doors leading to Red Bricks. Batgirl's hacking opens a terminal in Flugelheim that reveals a hidden suit collectible on an upper balcony. Nightwing's wall-jump reaches a platform above the Joker's final arena that contains a Gold Brick challenge reward.

Chapter 3 — Rogues Gallery

Missions: Two-Face, Kite Man, Poison Ivy.

Story characters: Batman, Robin, Gordon

Gordon's upgraded foam projectile counters Two-Face's coin-flip shield mechanic efficiently. For Poison Ivy, Robin's tech suit interacts with plant-based environmental puzzles, cutting two puzzle sections in half. Kite Man is the most platforming-heavy mission of the chapter — Catwoman's wall-run (if available in story) helps, but she may not be accessible here on first playthrough.

Free Play priority: Nightwing's wall-jump is essential for upper-level Red Bricks in both the Two-Face and Kite Man missions. Batgirl unlocks a large collectible cache via a hacking terminal near Poison Ivy's greenhouse entrance. Talia's parkour clears a rapid-transit route in Kite Man that shaves 5+ minutes off a collectible sweep.

Chapter 4 — Freeze and Batgirl

Missions: Firefly, Batgirl Begins, Out of Commission, Mr. Freeze.

Story characters: Batman, Batgirl (playable from Batgirl Begins onward)

Batgirl becomes playable in "Batgirl Begins" and is essential for Chapter 4's tower puzzles. Her tower-activation ability is introduced here — activating each tower reveals all collectibles in its district on the minimap, making her the single most valuable character for completionists doing the open-world sweep. For Mr. Freeze, Batman's Thermal Batarang suit is the recommended loadout to melt ice barriers during the boss sequence.

Free Play priority: Immediately after unlocking Batgirl in the story, replay any earlier missions with hackable terminals — she retroactively opens all of them. Out of Commission has a Catwoman lockpick door in a side corridor leading to a Red Brick that many players miss entirely.

Chapter 5 — Arkham

Missions: Tumbler Chase, Arkham Asylum.

Story characters: Batman, Nightwing

Nightwing's acrobatic speed advantages appear in Tumbler Chase's on-foot sections — he traverses the rooftop sequences noticeably faster than Batman. Arkham Asylum is the most combat-intensive chapter; Batman's fully upgraded grapple slam (unlocked mid-game via WayneTech Chips) makes the shield-carrying asylum guards manageable. Without it, use Robin's wider sweep attacks.

Free Play priority: Nightwing wall-jump points appear throughout Arkham Asylum's upper tiers and lead to three Red Bricks. Batgirl's hacking opens a security door in the warden's office area that contains a hidden suit. Talia's combat efficiency helps speed through the wave-dense encounters in Tumbler Chase Free Play.

Chapter 6 — League of Shadows

Missions: The League of Shadows Returns, The Dark Knight Returns.

Story characters: Batman, Talia al Ghul

Talia is the recommended character for League of Shadows combat encounters — her sword combat is specifically designed to match the League's fighting style and she deals more damage per combo than Batman against League enemies. Batman in the full Dark Knight suit is required for the final confrontation; do not switch away from it during the boss sequence. Certain League environmental puzzles respond only to Talia, opening shortcuts during story and Free Play alike.

Free Play priority: Chapter 6 has the fewest character-locked collectibles of any chapter. Most items are accessible with Batman alone on replay. The main exception is one Batgirl terminal near the League's inner sanctum and one Catwoman lock on a lateral storage chamber. Both are visible from the main path — you will not miss them if you are actively looking.

Free Play Strategy for 100%

Free Play unlocks after completing each mission's story run. In Free Play you can pause the mission and switch to any character you have unlocked globally — you are not limited to that mission's story roster. Here is how to run Free Play efficiently:

Build Your Free Play Team

You do not need every character for every mission. The three characters that unlock the most content across the entire game are Batgirl (hacking terminals and tower activations), Catwoman (lockpicks and whip traversal), and Nightwing (wall-jump platforms). If time is limited, prioritise getting these three into every mission replay before running others.

Use Detective Vision First

At the start of every Free Play run, switch to Batman and activate Detective Vision before doing anything else. Walk through the mission area slowly — Detective Vision highlights every interactable object, breakable wall, and hidden terminal in the environment. Map out where each character-locked spot is before switching characters, so you can plan an efficient route through the mission rather than backtracking.

Batgirl Tower Activations Multiply Your Efficiency

Whenever you enter a new Gotham district in Free Play, immediately find and activate Batgirl's tower if you have not already done so in that district. A single tower activation reveals all nearby Red Bricks, Gold Bricks, and Riddler Puzzle Rooms within its radius on the minimap. Without tower activation, you are searching blind; with it, your sweep becomes a simple checklist.

Free Play rule of thumb: Story run first, collectibles second. Do not stress about missing character-locked items during the story — you are supposed to go back in Free Play. That is the game's intended loop, and everything is designed to be retrievable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which character is most important for 100% completion?

Batgirl, by a significant margin. Her hacking terminals are the most common character-exclusive interaction in the game, and her tower-activation ability is the fastest way to reveal collectibles across entire districts. If you only have time to run one character on every mission replay, make it Batgirl.

Can I complete the story without switching characters?

Yes — the story is designed so Batman can complete every main objective in each mission without relying on other characters' exclusive abilities. Other characters make certain sections easier or faster but are not required to reach the end credits. Character-exclusive abilities only gate collectibles, not story progress.

When do I get access to all 7 characters?

All 7 characters are available for Free Play by the end of Chapter 6. Batgirl unlocks partway through Chapter 4, Talia and Nightwing in Chapter 5–6. Gordon and Catwoman unlock during Chapters 1–2. Robin is available from early Chapter 1. Batman is available from the start.

Do character upgrades carry over between missions?

Yes. WayneTech upgrades are global — any ability you unlock via the WayneTech tree in the Batcave applies to that character in every mission replay and open-world session. Upgrading Batgirl's hacking range once applies everywhere she is used.
