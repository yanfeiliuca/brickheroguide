LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Trophy Guide — Road to Platinum "I am Batman"

📅 June 7, 2026  |  ⏱ 7 min read  |  BrickHeroGuide

The Platinum trophy in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is called "I am Batman" — and earning it is less of a punishing grind than you might expect. With 52 trophies total, no missables, a difficulty of 3/10, and an estimated 40–50 hours to completion, this is one of the more accessible Platinum runs in the modern LEGO library. Here's everything you need to know to do it efficiently.

Recommended Roadmap: Two Phases

The fastest path to Platinum follows a clean two-phase structure. Trying to 100% each level as you go in Story Mode is tempting but inefficient — many collectibles and challenge areas require characters, suits, or gadgets you won't have until late in the game. According to PowerPyx's roadmap and HTG's guide, the two-phase approach saves significant time.

Phase Focus Estimated Time
Phase 1 Story Mode — complete all campaign levels, grab obvious collectibles, unlock characters naturally 10–15 hours
Phase 2 Free Play & Open-World Cleanup — revisit levels with full roster, collect all Riddler trophies, Batcave props, achieve True Hero status on all levels 25–35 hours
💡 Key insight: Complete the story before any serious collectible hunting. Your full roster of characters — Batman, Gordon, Catwoman, Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, and Talia al Ghul — unlocks progressively through the campaign. You'll need all seven (plus any unlockable side characters) to access character-gated collectibles in Free Play.

Phase 1: Story Mode

Your goal in Story Mode is simple: finish the campaign and unlock as many characters as possible without worrying about perfect completion. The campaign is structured across multiple chapters and takes 10–15 hours on a focused playthrough, as outlined in our game length guide.

During the story, you'll naturally earn a significant chunk of the 44 Bronze trophies — those tied to story beats, first-use of gadgets, defeating boss characters, and reaching key narrative moments. These unlock automatically and require no extra effort.

What to do during Story Mode

  • Collect studs passively: Grab studs as you go without detouring. You don't need True Hero status yet — that's a Free Play task.
  • Buy stud multipliers early: Once you access the Batcomputer, purchase every available Stud Multiplier upgrade. Stacking them dramatically reduces the grind for True Hero status in Phase 2. This single tip, highlighted by KeenGamer's guide, can shave hours off your total time.
  • Unlock as many characters as possible: Some side characters unlock via open-world Gotham challenges rather than story missions. Doing these as you encounter them in Phase 1 saves backtracking in Phase 2.
  • Explore Gotham between missions: The city is dense with visible Riddler trophies, Batcave props, and challenge markers. Grab anything in easy reach without committing to systematic hunts.

Phase 2: Free Play & Open-World Cleanup

Phase 2 is where the bulk of your Platinum time goes — and where the game truly opens up. With every character unlocked, the character-gated sections of each level and many areas of open-world Gotham become accessible for the first time.

Riddler Trophies

Riddler trophies are scattered throughout both the campaign levels (accessible in Free Play mode) and the open-world Gotham districts. Each district has its own cluster of trophies, many requiring specific character abilities: Batgirl's hacking, Nightwing's acrobatic wall-jump, or Catwoman's lockpick. Without those characters in your roster, the trophy simply cannot be reached. This is why collecting them in Phase 2 is so much more efficient than trying in Phase 1.

True Hero Status

Achieving True Hero status means collecting enough studs in a level to hit a set threshold — a core LEGO game mechanic. With Stud Multipliers fully stacked from Phase 1, this becomes much less of a grind. According to NeonLightsMedia's achievement guide, stacking all available multipliers is the single most impactful optimization for True Hero cleanup runs.

Batcave Props

Batcave props are collectible trophies that populate Batman's Batcave as you discover them across Gotham and levels. They serve as a satisfying visual reward for completionists — your Batcave gradually fills up with memorabilia from Batman's history. Tracking which props you've found is easiest via the in-game collection menu, which flags undiscovered items by district.

Suit Unlocks

Each of the seven playable characters has an extensive suit wardrobe. Some suits unlock through story progression, others through collectibles, challenge completions, or specific in-game conditions. Trophy trackers may require completing all suit unlocks for specific characters — checking your progress via the character select menu in the Batcave will show completion percentages per character.

Open-World Gotham Challenges

Each Gotham district hosts unique challenge activities beyond the main missions — time trials, combat arenas, stealth courses, and more. These challenges often tie directly to Bronze and Silver trophy requirements. Tackling them district-by-district with a guide ensures nothing slips through the cracks.

💾 Track your progress: Use the in-game collection menus (Batcomputer → Collections) and the trophy list on your console to identify exactly what's missing. The game's own tracking is reliable and specific enough to guide your cleanup without needing an external checklist for most categories.

Gold Trophies: What to Expect

The three Gold trophies represent the game's major milestone accomplishments. While PowerPyx's full roadmap details the exact unlock conditions, Gold trophies are generally tied to: completing the main story campaign, reaching a significant collectible threshold (such as collecting all Riddler trophies or all Batcave props), and one further challenge likely related to 100% completion of the open-world Gotham districts. None are missable, and all three flow naturally from the two-phase approach described above.

How Long Will It Actually Take?

The honest answer is 40–50 hours for most players. The range comes down to familiarity with LEGO game mechanics and how efficiently you approach Phase 2. Players who have completed Platinums in LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga or LEGO DC Super-Villains will likely land in the 35–40 hour range. First-time LEGO completionists should budget for 45–50.

That's a meaningful time investment — but the game's 84 Metacritic score and consistently high player satisfaction suggests those hours are well spent. The Platinum is achievable by any player willing to commit, which is exactly how a 3/10 difficulty rating should feel.

For reference, the overall game's length by playstyle is covered in detail in our How Long to Beat guide. And if you're hunting collectibles, our Collectibles Guide maps out optimal routes to minimize backtracking.

Trophy Guide for Xbox / PC Achievements

Xbox and PC players have the equivalent Achievement list — 52 achievements mirroring the PlayStation trophy structure, worth a total of 1,000 Gamerscore. The unlock conditions, difficulty, and roadmap are identical across platforms. The same two-phase approach applies regardless of where you're playing.
