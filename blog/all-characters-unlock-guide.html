All 7 Playable Characters in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — How to Unlock Each One

Published: May 27, 2026 · 6 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight takes a fundamentally different approach to its roster than any previous LEGO game. Instead of dozens of interchangeable characters, the game features just seven deeply designed playable heroes — each with a unique gadget set, a distinct combat style, and a story arc that earns their place in the Bat-Family. If you're wondering when your favorite character unlocks, or which gadgets they bring to the table, this guide has everything you need.

The Full Roster at a Glance

Unlike earlier LEGO titles where dozens of characters could be swapped in at will, Legacy of the Dark Knight locks characters behind story chapters. Roster progression is tied entirely to the main campaign — you cannot skip ahead to grab a favorite hero early. According to Game Rant, the seven heroes are as follows:

Character Unlocks Gadgets
Batman Prologue Batarang, Batclaw
Jim Gordon Chapter 1 Foam Sprayer, Rebound Launcher
Catwoman Chapter 2 — Mines Whip, Call Kitty
Robin Chapter 3 — Haley's Circus Birdarang, Cable Launcher
Batgirl Chapter 4 Hackarang, Drone
Nightwing Chapter 4 — Mr. Freeze Electrorang, Electric Cable Launcher
Talia al Ghul Chapter 5 Blowdarts, Ninja Dash
DLC note: The Joker and Harley Quinn will be added as playable characters with the Mayhem Collection DLC, launching September 2026 for Deluxe Edition owners.

Character-by-Character Breakdown

Batman

Unlocks: Prologue · Always Player 1
Batarang Batclaw Explosive Gel (acquired later)

Batman is technically the first character you unlock — you play as Bruce Wayne through the Prologue sections before receiving the cowl and batsuit at the start of Chapter 1. He is the game's permanent protagonist and always occupies the Player 1 slot, so you can never swap him out of your party. His Arkham-inspired combat and driveable Batmobiles make him the most versatile hero in the roster. Over the course of the story, his gadget loadout expands to include Explosive Gel alongside his signature Batarang and Batclaw.

Jim Gordon

Unlocks: Chapter 1
Foam Sprayer Rebound Launcher

Gotham's finest joins the adventure at the start of Chapter 1 as your default Player 2 partner for that chapter's story. Gordon is particularly useful in environmental puzzles: his Foam Sprayer can block pipes and immobilize active machinery (and enemies, in a satisfyingly sticky fashion). The Rebound Launcher opens up ranged puzzle solutions that Batman's more direct gadgets can't handle. As a supporting character rather than the protagonist, Gordon cycles in and out of your active party in subsequent chapters.

Catwoman

Unlocks: Chapter 2 — Mines mission
Whip Call Kitty

Catwoman unlocks during the Chapter 2 main mission "Mines" — it's impossible to miss, as she makes a memorable entrance by stealing Gordon's Batclaw. Her kit fills a genuine gap in the roster: the Whip can cut through glass surfaces and reach high grapple points that Batman can't access, while the Call Kitty gadget deploys a LEGO cat through tight spaces completely inaccessible to larger characters. She can also crawl up certain walls, opening vertical traversal routes that reward exploration.

Robin

Unlocks: Chapter 3 — Haley's Circus
Birdarang Cable Launcher

Dick Grayson steps into the Robin suit at the start of Chapter 3, following a training montage after you visit Haley's Circus. While Robin's brawling style is similar to Batman's, his Birdarang can hit multiple enemies in a single throw — a key advantage against groups. The Cable Launcher creates a tightrope that Robin can walk across, enabling puzzle solutions and combat positioning not available to any other character. Note that Robin remains in your roster even after Dick's later upgrade to Nightwing.

Batgirl

Unlocks: Chapter 4
Hackarang Drone

Barbara Gordon joins the Bat-Family at the start of Chapter 4 — much to her father's obliviousness. Where Batman and Robin favor direct combat, Batgirl's gadgets lean heavily into technology and disruption. The Hackarang can interface with electronic panels and stun enemies mid-combat. Her Drone serves a dual scanning function: it can survey areas remotely to reveal hidden paths and interact with arrow spots on the environment. She's the go-to character for any puzzle involving circuitry or surveillance.

Nightwing

Unlocks: Chapter 4 — Mr. Freeze (final mission)
Electrorang Electric Cable Launcher

Dick Grayson graduates to Nightwing in the final mission of Chapter 4, following the Mr. Freeze confrontation. Mechanically, he's Robin with an electrified upgrade: the Electrorang replaces the Birdarang, hitting multiple targets and adding a zap that can jumpstart electronics. The Electric Cable Launcher functions like Robin's but delivers a live current along the rope — useful for activating power conduits and electrifying puddles to deal area-of-effect damage. Both Robin and Nightwing remain in your roster as separate characters.

Talia al Ghul

Unlocks: Chapter 5
Blowdarts Ninja Dash

Talia al Ghul is playable briefly during the Prologue's League of Shadows training sequences, but she doesn't join your permanent roster until Chapter 5. She's the stealth specialist of the group: the Ninja Dash lets her vanish and reposition instantly, while her Blowdarts can stun enemies before they can raise an alarm. She excels in encounters that reward patience and misdirection over Batman's front-line brawling. Her inclusion also ties directly into the game's story, making her feel earned rather than arbitrary.

About the Suits System

Beyond the seven base characters, Legacy of the Dark Knight features over 100 suits and outfits (not counting DLC), according to Game Rant's suits guide. Outfits are unlocked in several ways: purchasing from Bat-Mite's Batcave Shop using collected Studs, completing specific in-game collectibles and open-world activities, or earning through DLC content. Notably, some suits — including certain Batgirl variants tied to the Arkham Trilogy, Batman Beyond, and 1966 television series themes — require purchasing physical LEGO sets released in March 2026 to obtain their digital codes.

Gold Batgirl tip: The Gold Batgirl suit reportedly requires a physical LEGO set redemption code. Check the Redeem Codes & QR Rewards guide for full details on which sets unlock which rewards.

Upcoming: Joker & Harley Quinn (September 2026)

The September 2026 Mayhem Collection DLC will add the Joker and Harley Quinn as the eighth and ninth playable characters, each with their own abilities, gadgets, and takedowns, plus a new story mission set around a breakout from Arkham Asylum. Dataminers have also reportedly found references to over a dozen additional Task Force X characters in the game's files — though these remain unconfirmed by TT Games or Warner Bros. Games. The Mayhem Collection is included with the Deluxe Edition or available as a separate purchase for Standard Edition owners.

With the base game's seven-character roster now fully documented, keep an eye on BrickHeroGuide for updates as the September DLC approaches. For now, the Bat-Family roster — from Batman's Arkham-style brawling to Talia's shadow-step stealth — offers enough mechanical variety to reward experimenting with different character combinations across the open world and story missions alike.
