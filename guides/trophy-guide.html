LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is an excellent game for trophy hunters and achievement chasers. The list has 52 trophies on PS5 (including a Platinum) and 51 achievements on Xbox, with zero missable entries — you can replay any mission or revisit any area to clean up whatever you skipped. The list skews toward open-world completion and collectible hunting rather than skill-based challenges, making it very achievable for dedicated players.

Overview

The PS5 trophy list breaks down as follows: 1 Platinum, 3 Gold, 4 Silver, and 44 Bronze trophies. There is no difficulty-related trophy — completing the game on Classic earns the same trophy access as Dark Knight mode. This means you can play on any difficulty you prefer without affecting your completion progress. According to multiple post-launch guides including Game Rant and Happy Thumbs Gaming, the list is considered fair and well-suited to the LEGO game format.

Platinum Trophy: "I am Batman"

🏆 "I am Batman" — Platinum

Unlock all other 51 trophies to earn the Platinum. The name is a nod to one of Batman's most iconic lines, fitting for a game that celebrates 85+ years of the Dark Knight across every medium. Estimated completion time based on community guides is 25–40 hours for a thorough first playthrough plus mop-up.

Trophy Categories

Story Trophies

One trophy per story chapter — 6 chapters means 6 automatic story trophies simply by completing the campaign. These cannot be missed and require no additional effort.

Open-World & Side Activity Trophies

The majority of the list focuses on Gotham's open world. These include clearing all side activities in a district (zoo animals, AR Trials, Riddler Puzzle Rooms), finding all Gold Bricks from side content, and completing the Batcave's upgrade progression. According to The Gamer, the harder trophies ask you to clear all Gotham side activities, collect every major collectible, unlock all vehicles, and fully explore the open world.

Collectible Trophies

Several trophies are tied to collecting Red Bricks, Batcave Minikits, WayneTech Caches, and reaching specific Stud milestones. See our Collectibles Guide for locations.

Combat Trophies

A handful of Bronze trophies require specific combat feats — performing stealth takedowns, executing counter chains of a certain length, defeating enemies with particular gadgets, and so on. These can be targeted during free-roam rather than requiring specific missions.

Character & Suit Trophies

Some trophies reward unlocking specific characters, equipping film-inspired skins, or filling out the Batcave wardrobe. These overlap naturally with the suit-collection and mission-completion process.

Recommended Strategy

  1. Play through the full story on any difficulty. Collect casually as you go but do not stress about missing items — nothing is missable. Story completion unlocks all traversal abilities needed for mop-up.
  2. Find Stud Multiplier Red Bricks immediately after each chapter. These dramatically reduce the Stud grind required for shop suits and the remaining Gold and Silver trophies.
  3. Tackle open-world activities district by district. The map clearly shows completion percentage per district. Work through each zone completely before moving on.
  4. Replay missions via the Batcomputer for missed Red Bricks and Gold Brick chapter objectives.
  5. Clean up combat trophies during free roam. The open world provides unlimited opportunities for combat feat trophies without needing to replay specific missions.

Key Tips

No missables at all. The Batcomputer in your Batcave lets you replay every mission freely. If you finish a mission without a Red Brick or challenge objective, just replay it — there is no penalty and no time limit.
Difficulty does not matter for trophies. Play on Classic if you want a relaxed experience. Play on Dark Knight if you enjoy a challenge. The Platinum is available either way.
External resources: Game Rant's full trophy list and Keen Gamer's trophy guide provide detailed unlock conditions for every individual trophy.
