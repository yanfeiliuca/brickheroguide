LEGO Batman Legacy of the Dark Knight -- PC System Requirements

Last updated: May 19, 2026 · 3 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is built on Unreal Engine 5, marking the first LEGO game to use this engine. Official system requirements have not yet been published by Warner Bros. Games or TT Games. For the most accurate and up-to-date specs, check the game's store page on Steam, the Epic Games Store, or the Xbox store.

Key takeaway: As a UE5 title targeting current-gen consoles only (PS5, Xbox Series X/S), expect higher hardware demands than previous LEGO games. A mid-range GPU paired with an SSD is the sweet spot for smooth 1080p gameplay.

What to Expect from a UE5 LEGO Game

Unreal Engine 5 brings significant visual improvements -- Lumen dynamic lighting, Nanite geometry, and higher texture fidelity. With that comes higher hardware demands compared to previous TT Games titles, which used older, simpler engines. Here is some general guidance based on Unreal Engine 5's typical scaling:

General UE5 Hardware Guidance

  • GPU: A modern 6 GB+ VRAM card is strongly recommended for 1080p / 60 FPS gameplay. 4 GB cards may be limited to Low-Medium settings.
  • CPU: Any modern 6-core processor (Intel Core i5-10400 / AMD Ryzen 5 3600 or newer) should handle this type of game comfortably.
  • RAM: 16 GB is the comfortable baseline for UE5 titles. 8 GB may work but expect occasional stuttering.
  • Storage: An SSD is strongly recommended. UE5's asset streaming and Nanite geometry benefit significantly from fast storage. Expect noticeably longer load times on HDD.
  • OS: Windows 10/11 64-bit. The game is not available on macOS or Linux natively.

Console Availability

The game releases on PS5, Windows, and Xbox Series X/S on May 22, 2026, with a Nintendo Switch 2 version coming at a later (TBA) date. There are no PS4, Xbox One, or original Nintendo Switch versions. The move to current-gen only reflects the UE5 engine's hardware requirements. If you pre-ordered the Deluxe Edition, 72-hour early access began on May 19, 2026.

Steam Deck & Handheld Compatibility

Compatibility with Steam Deck and other handheld PCs (ASUS ROG Ally, Lenovo Legion Go) has not been officially confirmed. UE5 titles have had mixed results on the Steam Deck -- some run well at low settings, others struggle. Check the Steam store page for the official Steam Deck verification status closer to launch.
