Epic Games Store Players Are Still Waiting on LEGO Batman Legacy's July Update

Published: July 24, 2026 · 6 min read · Patch Watch

If you're playing LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight on the Epic Games Store, you're currently behind. Not behind on collectibles or trophies — behind on the game itself. WB Games' own support page for the "July 2026 Update" lists a full page of fixes for PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC via Steam — and then, in a single footnote at the bottom, confirms the Epic Games Store build still hasn't gotten any of it: "July 2026 Update will be available for Epic Games Store later this month." With this article publishing on July 24, that leaves one week of July for WB to make good on that promise, and the gap between platforms has only widened since the note was first published.

What we verified: This article is based on the exact wording of WB Games' official LEGO Games Support article for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, fetched directly on July 24, 2026, cross-checked against MP1st's reporting on the July 14 and July 21 patches. No speculation on why Epic's certification is taking longer — WB hasn't said, and we're not going to guess.

The Widening Gap: A Timeline

Here's how the platforms have diverged since the start of the month:

Date Update PS5 / Xbox / Steam Epic Games Store
July 14, 2026 Update 1.007 ("July 2026 Update") Live Not yet released
July 21, 2026 Update 1.008 (13GB, no official changelog) Live Not yet released
July 24, 2026 (today) — Two updates ahead Still on pre-July build

We covered Update 1.008 last week as a bit of a mystery patch — a 13GB download on Steam with no changelog of its own from TT Games. Whatever is in it, Epic Games Store players don't have it yet, on top of not having the July 14 fixes either.

What Epic Games Store Players Are Actually Missing

The July 2026 Update's confirmed fix list, per WB Games' own support article, covers ten separate issues. These are the fixes that PS5, Xbox, and Steam players have had for ten days and that Epic Games Store players are still waiting on:

  • Fixed issues related to the "On the Prowl" mission
  • Fixed issues related to the "Haly's Circus" mission (LBAT-134)
  • Fixed camera issues related to the Batpod (LBAT-118)
  • Fixed an interaction issue with the Riddler Trophy Batcave item (LBAT-121)
  • Fixed issues related to aiming gadgets after control remapping
  • Fixed issues related to Talia al Ghul's gadget/ability upgrades (LBAT-64)
  • Fixed a UV Vision issue during "Case of Waylon Jones" side activities (LBAT-179)
  • Fixed an issue related to the "Party Select" menu
  • Fixed vehicles becoming invisible in the Batcave (LBAT-602)
  • Fixed a WayneTech Cache Chest not spawning in Tricorner Island (LBAT-446)

Two of these are worth flagging specifically for completionists. LBAT-446, the missing Tricorner Island WayneTech Cache Chest, is the same bug we detailed in our post-game 100% completion checklist — it can block a full WayneTech Cache count on affected saves. LBAT-602, the vehicles-turning-invisible-in-the-Batcave bug, is the one we covered in our Batcave hub guide after cosmetic vehicle displays started disappearing for some players. Both fixes are live everywhere except Epic Games Store.

Why This Matters More Than It Might Seem

Cross-platform patch staggering isn't unheard of — storefront certification pipelines genuinely do run on different schedules, and WB has been upfront that this is coming rather than staying silent about it. But the gap here has gone from "a few days" to "an entire second patch's worth of undocumented changes" without any updated timeline from WB beyond the original "later this month" language. For Epic Games Store owners chasing 100% completion or a clean Diamond Brutal Bat trophy run, that means playing on a build that's now missing fixes for two confirmed progression-relevant bugs (LBAT-446 and LBAT-602) with no firm date for when that changes.

What Epic Games Store Owners Can Do in the Meantime

  • Avoid the Tricorner Island WayneTech Cache Chest route flagged in LBAT-446 if you're actively tracking a 100% completion save, since the chest may still fail to spawn on the pre-patch Epic build.
  • Don't rely on Batcave vehicle displays for photo mode or collection-checking purposes until LBAT-602 lands on your platform.
  • Check for updates manually via the Epic Games Launcher's Library tab periodically rather than assuming auto-update will notify you the moment it ships.
  • Watch the LEGO Games Support Announcements section directly, since that's the same page WB has been updating with each platform's rollout status.

We'll update this post the moment WB Games confirms the Epic Games Store build has caught up. Given the "later this month" language is now seven days from expiring on its own terms, that update should be coming one way or another before August.
