Nightwing in LEGO Batman Legacy: Unlock, Abilities & Is He Worth Switching To?

Published: July 12, 2026 · 6 min read

Nightwing is the last member of the Bat-Family to join your roster in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, arriving right as the story hits one of its toughest boss fights. He's also the character players argue about most: some find his electrified gear a genuine upgrade over Robin, others say he's close enough to a reskin that switching barely matters. This guide covers exactly when Dick Grayson's older alter ego unlocks, what his kit actually does differently from Robin's, and whether he's worth keeping in rotation once you have him.

How to Unlock Nightwing

Nightwing becomes playable at the end of Chapter 4, during the Mr. Freeze boss encounter. Unlike Robin, who joins automatically at the start of a chapter, Nightwing's introduction is tied directly to that fight, so there's no way to miss him if you're progressing through the story normally. Once the encounter wraps, he's added permanently to your character-select wheel for both story missions and Free Play.

Tip: Because he arrives mid-boss-fight, Nightwing is specifically useful for finishing that Mr. Freeze encounter — his traversal kit is built for the vertical, ice-covered arena you're fighting in.

Nightwing's Abilities

Nightwing shares his general moveset with Robin (both are Bat-Family acrobats with cable-based traversal), but his signature gadget is an electrified upgrade rather than a completely new tool.

⚡ Electric Cable Launcher

Combat + Traversal

Nightwing's version of Robin's Cable Launcher adds an electrical charge to the line. In practical terms, this is the same grapple-and-swing tool Robin uses, but with a combat and puzzle-solving edge — enemies and switches that respond to electricity react differently to Nightwing's cable than to a plain grapple.

💄 Wall-Jumping

Traversal

Nightwing can wall-jump between parallel surfaces, letting him climb narrow gaps and rooftop corridors that other characters have to route around. This is his standout traversal advantage and the main reason he's useful outside of story-mandated sections.

🚶 Faster Ground Movement

Traversal

Nightwing simply moves faster on foot than most of the roster, which matters more than it sounds like across Gotham's open-world hub, where a lot of time is spent just getting from one collectible or side quest to the next.

Nightwing vs. Robin: How Different Is He, Really?

This is the debate that follows Nightwing around. In COGconnected's full ranking of all seven playable characters, reviewer Michael Chow placed Nightwing sixth out of seven, writing: "After playing as Robin for a while, Nightwing just felt a bit redundant for me... His unique abilities add to Robin's 'birdarang' to become an 'electrorang,' and his cable launcher adds electricity. These do ultimately make a difference in terms of puzzle-solving, but by that point, it felt more like an upgraded ability in combat than a different character." The same piece notes Chow preferred Nightwing's character design over Robin's, even while ranking his kit lower.

That's a fair summary of the trade-off: Nightwing isn't a new toolkit so much as Robin's toolkit with an electrical upgrade layered on top, plus the wall-jump and speed bonuses that Robin doesn't have. Whether that counts as "worth switching for" depends on what you're doing — it matters a lot more for electricity-gated puzzles and rooftop traversal than it does for general combat, where the two characters play almost identically.

Full roster context: Nightwing joins a seven-character playable lineup that also includes Batman, Jim Gordon, Catwoman, Robin, Batgirl, and Talia Al Ghul. For where he unlocks relative to the rest of the cast, see our All Characters & Unlocks Guide.

Who Voices Nightwing?

Adult Dick Grayson / Nightwing is voiced by Hyoie O'Grady, with his younger self voiced by Greg Jones, according to cast listings compiled by Behind The Voice Actors. Splitting the character across two actors for different life stages lines up with the game's flashback-heavy narrative structure, which revisits Dick Grayson's history with Batman at multiple points in the story.

Practical Tips for Using Nightwing

  • Swap to him for electricity-gated content. Any switch, door, or enemy that specifically calls for an electrical charge is where the Electric Cable Launcher earns its keep over Robin's plain version.
  • Use his wall-jump for rooftop collectible routes. If a Riddler trophy or WayneTech Cache sits on a ledge that looks unreachable, try Nightwing before assuming you need a different character's gadget.
  • Lean on his speed in the open world. For pure point-A-to-point-B travel around Gotham's four districts, Nightwing's faster ground movement shaves real time off side-quest running.
  • Don't expect a combat overhaul. If you're hoping for a meaningfully different fighting style from Robin, temper expectations — the core combo system carries over almost unchanged.

Verdict: Is Nightwing Worth Using?

Nightwing is a situational upgrade rather than a must-play character. He's not going to replace Batman's gadget variety or give you a dramatically different combat experience from Robin, but his Electric Cable Launcher, wall-jump, and movement speed make him the right pick for a specific set of tasks: electricity puzzles, rooftop traversal, and covering ground quickly in Free Play. Keep him in your rotation for those situations rather than expecting him to be a wholesale reinvention of the Bat-Family moveset.
