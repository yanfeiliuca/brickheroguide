How Long to Beat LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — Story, 100%, and Free Play Times

📅 June 5, 2026  |  ⏱ 6 min read  |  BrickHeroGuide

Whether you have a weekend to spare or want to go for a full completionist run, knowing how long LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight actually takes is useful before you dive in. The short answer: somewhere between 10 and 50 hours, depending entirely on how much of Gotham you want to explore. Here's the full breakdown.

These figures are based on aggregated data from GamesRadar, VGC, Insider Gaming, and Game8. Individual times will vary based on platform, difficulty approach, and how much time you spend exploring open-world Gotham between missions.

💡 Quick tip: If you just want to see the story before tackling collectibles, go straight through the campaign first. Many collectibles and suits require abilities unlocked by later-game characters — you'll save yourself significant backtracking by doing a clean story run first, then a second Free Play pass.

Story Mode: 10–15 Hours

The main campaign of Legacy of the Dark Knight consists of over 30 playable levels spread across multiple chapters. According to GamesRadar's review, a focused player who doesn't obsess over every secret and simply follows the critical path will reach the credits somewhere in the 10–15 hour window. Most reviewers landed around 12 hours for a clean first run.

This is notably longer than earlier LEGO games. LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga, by comparison, took around 15–20 hours for story mode but spread that across nine full films. Legacy of the Dark Knight achieves a similar runtime with a single, original narrative — a sign of how much TT Games has expanded the scope of a single LEGO title.

The open-world Gotham City is accessible between missions and adds to that runtime organically. Even if you're not actively hunting collectibles, you'll naturally explore some of the city's districts while traveling between objectives, which can stretch story playtime toward the upper end of that range.

What Affects Story Length

A few factors can push your story time higher or lower:

  • Puzzle difficulty: Some environmental puzzles are genuinely tricky. Players who get stuck on a puzzle can lose 20–30 minutes without noticing.
  • Open-world exploration: Gotham is densely packed and easy to wander. Even on a story-only run, many players detour to grab visible studs or investigate a Riddler trophy they've spotted.
  • Cutscene watching: The game has a strong emphasis on cinematic storytelling, and its cutscenes are longer and more frequent than earlier LEGO titles. Watching everything adds real time.
  • Combat fluency: Unlike older LEGO games, Legacy of the Dark Knight has a deeper combat system inspired by the Batman Arkham series. Players new to that style of counter-based combat may spend more time in encounters early on.

Average Playthrough: ~16 Hours

Most players who play naturally — completing story missions while grabbing obvious collectibles and exploring Gotham somewhat freely — report finishing in around 16 hours, according to Brick Fanatics. This "average" category is the most common experience: you're not rushing to the credits, but you're also not methodically hunting every hidden item.

This is also the most replayable bracket. Because many areas have collectibles locked behind characters you don't have yet in story mode, a natural first run tends to leave a healthy chunk of content for a second Free Play pass. That second pass — where you revisit levels with a full roster of characters and their unique abilities — is often where the real satisfaction comes from in LEGO games, and Legacy of the Dark Knight is no exception.

100% Completion: 25–50 Hours

This is where Legacy of the Dark Knight reveals its true scope. Getting to 100% requires collecting every Riddler trophy, unlocking all suits, finding all Batcave props, completing every challenge in the open-world Gotham, and finishing every level with True Hero status in both Story and Free Play modes.

Sources range from 25–35 hours on the lower end to upwards of 50 hours for trophy/achievement completion on consoles. The variance comes down to player efficiency and how much time is spent hunting obscure collectibles without a guide.

What's Required for 100%

  • Riddler Trophies: Scattered throughout open-world Gotham and within levels. Many require specific character abilities to access.
  • All Suits: Both Batman and other playable characters have extensive suit wardrobes, some unlocked via story progression, others via collectibles or challenges.
  • Batcave Props: Decorative collectibles that populate Batman's Batcave as you find them — a satisfying visual reward for completionists.
  • True Hero status: Accumulating enough studs in each level on both Story Mode and Free Play. Stud multiplier upgrades (unlockable via the Batcomputer) make this substantially faster.
  • Open-world challenges: Gotham districts each have unique challenge activities beyond the main missions.
  • Character unlocks: The roster is large, and some characters are only accessible after fulfilling specific in-game conditions.
💡 Efficiency tip: Unlock Stud Multipliers as early as possible via the Batcomputer. Stacking them dramatically reduces the grind for True Hero status on each level and can shave several hours off the total 100% time.

How It Compares to Other LEGO Games

To put these numbers in context, here's roughly how Legacy of the Dark Knight stacks up against other recent LEGO titles for a story-only run:

Game Story Mode 100% Completion
LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight (2026) 10–15 hrs 25–50 hrs
LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga (2022) 15–20 hrs 60–80 hrs
LEGO Marvel Super Heroes 2 (2017) 8–12 hrs 20–30 hrs
LEGO Batman 3: Beyond Gotham (2014) 6–8 hrs 15–20 hrs

Legacy of the Dark Knight is a substantial step up in length from older LEGO Batman titles. The game's Arkham-inspired open-world design, deeper combat system, and denser collectible structure all contribute to a game that's meaningfully larger than its franchise predecessors — while still being manageable in scope compared to the sprawling nine-film scope of Skywalker Saga.

Is It Worth the Time Investment?

Given that the game currently holds an 84 on Metacritic — the highest score ever for a LEGO title — the answer for most players is yes. Whether you play through the story in a weekend or chip away at 100% over several weeks, the game has been designed to reward both approaches.

For parents playing with children, the 10–15 hour story is a comfortable commitment at a reasonable pace — a level or two per session over a couple of weeks. For dedicated LEGO fans aiming for the full completionist experience, 50 hours is a serious but achievable target, especially with a collectibles guide in hand.

The Mayhem Collection DLC, confirmed for September 18, 2026 alongside the Nintendo Switch 2 launch, will add a new story mission featuring The Joker and Harley Quinn — extending total playtime further for Deluxe Edition owners.
