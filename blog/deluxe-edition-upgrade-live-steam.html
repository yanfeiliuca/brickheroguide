The Deluxe Edition Upgrade Is Now Live on Steam for $24.99 — Here's Exactly What You Get

Published: July 3, 2026 · 6 min read

If you bought the Standard Edition of LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight at launch and have been wondering whether you'd ever be able to add the Deluxe content without buying the whole game a second time, that question now has a concrete answer. A standalone Deluxe Edition Upgrade listing is live on the Steam store, priced at $24.99, sitting under the base game's Downloadable Content section. It requires the base game on Steam to install and play.

What This Actually Is

We've covered the $24.99 Standard→Deluxe upgrade path before as a confirmed-but-not-yet-purchasable option in our Mayhem Collection DLC guide. What changed is that it's no longer just an announced price point — there's now an actual product page you can add to your cart. Per Steam's listing, the Deluxe Edition Upgrade is explicitly built "for existing owners of LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight," and its own review section already shows 28 user reviews, 100% positive as of this writing (a small sample, but a clean one).

What You Get Right Now: The Legacy Collection

Buying the upgrade today immediately unlocks the Legacy Collection, which Steam breaks down into three Themed Packs:

  • Arkham Trilogy Pack — suits, Batmobile skin, and Batcave props themed around the Arkham games
  • Batman Beyond Pack — suits, Batmobile skin, and Batcave props themed around the animated future-Batman series
  • Party Music Pack — suits, Batmobile skin, and Batcave props with a lighter, celebratory theme

Each of the three packs includes 7 suits (one for every playable character), 1 Batmobile skin, and a set of 5 Batcave customization props — more than 30 cosmetic items available to equip immediately after purchase, no story progress required.

What Arrives Later: The Mayhem Collection

The same $24.99 purchase also pre-loads the Mayhem Collection, which unlocks automatically on September 18, 2026 — no second purchase, no separate download to track down. That DLC adds Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters (raising the roster from 7 to 9), a new Arkham Asylum breakout story mission played from the villains' perspective, the open-world Mayhem Mode sandbox, and the villain-themed Sinister Pack — 7 more suits, 5 Batcave decorations, and 1 additional Batmobile skin.

Why bundle it this way: Buying the upgrade now locks in day-one Mayhem Collection access in September at today's $24.99 price, while also giving you the full Legacy Collection to use immediately — you're not paying twice or waiting for a second listing to show up closer to the DLC's release.

What's Still Unconfirmed

The Steam listing does not change one thing we flagged in our earlier DLC coverage: there is still no confirmed standalone Mayhem Collection purchase for players who don't want the Legacy Collection cosmetics. The Deluxe Edition Upgrade remains the only route currently available on Steam for Standard Edition owners, and we have not seen equivalent standalone upgrade listings appear yet on PS Store, Xbox Marketplace, or the Epic Games Store — worth checking your platform's store directly before assuming parity.

Should You Buy It Now or Wait?

If you already know you want Joker and Harley Quinn playable in September, buying the upgrade now costs the same $24.99 you'd pay later, but you get the Legacy Collection's 30+ cosmetic items to use for the next two and a half months instead of waiting. If you're only lukewarm on the villain-focused DLC, there's no urgency — the price hasn't moved and nothing suggests it's a launch-window discount that expires.
