Update 1.008: The 13GB LEGO Batman Legacy Patch Nobody Explained

Published: July 22, 2026 · 6 min read

Eight days after Update 1.007 shipped, LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight quietly picked up a second July patch. According to MP1st, Update 1.008 went live on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC on July 21, 2026, and it's not a small one — the PC download alone clocks in at over 13GB on Steam. What makes it unusual is what's missing: as of publication, TT Games has not released a changelog for build 1.008 specifically. Here's what's actually confirmed, and what's still a mystery.

What's Confirmed: Size, Date, Platforms

The facts that are nailed down are narrow but real. MP1st's report, dated July 21, states the update rolled out same-day across all three current-gen platforms, and that the Steam build size alone is "at least" 13GB — a hefty download for what TT Games is calling, per the outlet, an "unannounced patch." SteamDB's patch tracker corroborates that a build went out with changed depot files but explicitly notes there are "no official patch notes available for this build besides the list of changed files."

That's an important distinction from Update 1.007, which shipped July 14 with a full, itemized changelog on WB Games' official support page. As of this writing, that same support page still only documents the July 14 fix list — it has not been updated to reflect whatever changed in the July 21 build. If you want the full rundown of what 1.007 actually fixed, including the Talia al Ghul upgrade bug and the Batpod camera issue, we covered that in our Update 1.007 patch notes breakdown.

Bottom line: if you saw a 13GB+ download queue up on July 21 and no in-game patch notes screen explained it, that's expected right now — not a bug on your end. TT Games simply hasn't published what changed.

The Known Issues List Is Still Long

MP1st also compiled a running list of player-reported bugs still active around the 1.008 release window, gathered from Steam Community discussions and support tickets. The list includes:

  • Golden Batmobile / Batsuit redemption codes failing because WB's account services can't be reached
  • A Twitch Drops and survey-reward bug
  • Reduced WayneTech chip totals for players who use the mission-skip feature
  • 100% completion becoming unobtainable for some players after the Day 1 patch
  • Random crashes, with at least one report of a deleted save file
  • Players stuck at 49 of 50 Bat Symbol collectibles
  • The long-running Diamond Brutal Bat trophy bug
  • A bugged Clue Master train puzzle

Several of these overlap with issues we've flagged before. The Diamond Brutal Bat trophy bug, for instance, is one we tracked in our Trophy & Achievement Guide — as of the July 14 patch notes, it still wasn't listed as fixed, and nothing in the public 1.008 information changes that assessment. Until TT Games confirms otherwise, treat that trophy as still affected.

Why a Silent Patch Isn't Actually Strange

Live-service-adjacent post-launch support for single-player LEGO titles has always been a bit looser about communication than a full-blown multiplayer game. TT Games shipped a 1.006 stability patch in early June with modest notes, then a much more detailed 1.007 changelog in mid-July covering ten distinct fixes. A same-week follow-up build that only patches a handful of loose ends — without a full public writeup — is a common pattern for smaller hotfixes squeezed in between the marquee updates. The size of the download (13GB+) suggests more than a one-line hotfix, though, which is exactly why the lack of a changelog stands out enough for outlets to write about it.

It's also worth noting the timing: this build lands roughly two months before the Mayhem Collection DLC is due on September 18, 2026. It wouldn't be unusual for some of this download to include groundwork for that release even if the patch notes don't say so — though that's speculation on our part, not a confirmed detail, so treat it as a theory rather than a fact.

What to Do If You're Affected

  • Update anyway. Even without notes, a 13GB build almost certainly includes bug fixes and stability work rather than regressions.
  • Don't expect the known issues above to be resolved yet. None of them appear in any confirmed changelog for 1.007 or 1.008.
  • Keep save backups if you're on PC. The crash-with-save-loss reports are still circulating; back up your save folder before jumping into a long session post-patch.
  • Check back for an official changelog. If WB Games publishes a full 1.008 writeup, we'll update our Post-Launch Patch Tracker with the details.
