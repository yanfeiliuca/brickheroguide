Mayhem Collection DLC Leak: 13 Suicide Squad Characters Reportedly Datamined for LEGO Batman Legacy

Published: May 25, 2026 · 6 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has only been out for a few days, but dataminers are already uncovering what lies ahead. According to multiple sources reporting on leaked game files, the upcoming Mayhem Collection DLC — set for September 2026 — reportedly contains references to 13 DC characters strongly associated with the Suicide Squad and Task Force X. Here's everything that's reportedly been found, and what it could mean for the game's future.

⚠ Rumor Warning: The information below is based on datamined and leaked game files. None of these details have been officially confirmed by TT Games or Warner Bros. Games. Treat all specifics as rumor until an official announcement is made. Datamined content may change before final release.

What Is the Mayhem Collection?

The Mayhem Collection was announced as part of the LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Deluxe Edition at the game's reveal. It was confirmed to include a new "Mayhem Mode" starring The Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters, each with unique abilities, gadgets, and takedowns distinct from the base game's seven-hero roster. A new story mission centered on the duo breaking out of Arkham Asylum and unleashing chaos on Gotham City was also confirmed officially.

Beyond the new characters, the Deluxe Edition listing confirmed a "Sinister Pack" containing seven new suits (one per original playable character), five new Batcave decoration props, and one additional Batmobile variant for use in Gotham's open world. This was the extent of what WB Games officially revealed ahead of the game's May 22 launch.

Official DLC timeline: The Mayhem Collection is scheduled for September 2026. Deluxe Edition owners receive it at no additional cost; standard edition players can purchase it separately.

The Datamine: Task Force X Comes to Gotham

Shortly after launch, a dataminer going by the handle X0XLEAK posted findings from the game's files that went significantly beyond the confirmed content. According to reports from Game Rant, Brick Fanatics, and GamingBolt, the leaked character list suggests players in Mayhem Mode will be going up against a roster of classic Task Force X / Suicide Squad members as enemies or secondary characters.

The reportedly datamined character list includes 13 names from DC's Suicide Squad mythology:

Bronze Tiger
Captain Boomerang
Deadshot
Deathstroke
Javelin
Katana
Killer Croc
King Shark
Lester (The Electrocutioner?)
Mongal
Polka Dot Man
Rick Flag
A.R.G.U.S. Cops

If accurate, this represents a considerably larger character expansion than what was officially announced. Several names on the list — Deadshot, King Shark, Captain Boomerang — are among the most recognizable in the Suicide Squad mythology, suggesting TT Games may be drawing on both the comics and the recent film adaptations for inspiration.

A Note on "Lester"

One intriguing entry in the reported list is simply "Lester," which several outlets speculate could refer to The Electrocutioner, a minor Batman villain whose civilian name is Lester Buchinsky. The Electrocutioner appeared memorably in Batman: Arkham Origins, and including him would be a neat nod to the Arkham series that clearly influenced Legacy of the Dark Knight's combat design.

Additional Leaked Details: Gameplay Structure

Beyond the character list, the datamined files reportedly also contain structural details about how Mayhem Mode will actually play. According to the same leakers:

  • Mayhem Mode will feature its own dedicated in-game currency, separate from the studs used in the main game
  • There will reportedly be "7 or 9 objectives and/or missions" with bonus objectives for completionists
  • A new "Mayhem Hideout" hub area is referenced, described as featuring a costume shop and a challenge board
  • Harley Quinn's iconic pet hyenas, Bud and Lou, are reportedly present in the DLC content

The mention of a separate currency system is particularly interesting. It suggests the DLC won't simply bolt onto the existing open-world progression — Mayhem Mode may function more like a self-contained experience with its own loop, similar to how some games handle major story expansions.

Why Task Force X Makes Narrative Sense

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight already uses a story that blends multiple eras of Batman's history, pulling from films, comics, TV shows, and games. Introducing the Suicide Squad/Task Force X in DLC fits naturally into that multi-era tapestry. The Joker's prominence as a Mayhem Mode lead character immediately suggests a storyline where Amanda Waller might deploy Task Force X to counter a Gotham threat — or where the Squad itself becomes part of the chaos the Clown Prince is engineering.

The inclusion of Katana is notable because she has historically been associated with Batman and the Outsiders rather than the Suicide Squad in comics, hinting that TT Games may be mixing team affiliations loosely in the interest of building the most interesting roster possible — very much in keeping with Legacy of the Dark Knight's broader "everything Batman" approach.

Character comparison: The base game includes Batman, Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, Red Hood, Catwoman, and Commissioner Gordon as playable heroes. The Mayhem Collection adds Joker and Harley Quinn, potentially alongside 13+ enemy/supporting characters from the Suicide Squad roster.

How This Compares to Other LEGO DLC

For context, post-launch DLC in the LEGO franchise has historically been relatively modest. The Skywalker Saga's character packs added playable characters without major new story content. Legacy of the Dark Knight's Mayhem Collection, if the datamined details hold, would be significantly more substantial — a new playable story, a new hub area, new currency systems, and a roster of characters that nearly doubles the base game's lineup.

That scope would make the Mayhem Collection one of the most ambitious LEGO game DLC packages ever released, which may explain why WB Games chose to hold it for September rather than launching it alongside the base game.

What We're Watching For

As with any datamine, the gap between what's found in game files and what ships in the final product can be significant. Characters get cut, mechanics change, and content discovered in early builds sometimes never reaches players. The key signals to watch for:

  • An official Mayhem Collection trailer from WB Games or TT Games (likely Summer 2026)
  • Official character confirms on the LEGO Batman social channels
  • Any patch notes that add or remove the referenced content from game files

For now, the datamined details paint a picture of a DLC expansion far larger than what was advertised at launch. If even half the leaked character list makes it into the final product, September 2026 could be a very busy month for LEGO Batman fans.
