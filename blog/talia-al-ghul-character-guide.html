Talia al Ghul in LEGO Batman Legacy: Unlock, Abilities & Why Update 1.007 Finally Fixed Her

Published: July 21, 2026 · 6 min read

Talia al Ghul is the last of the base game's seven playable characters to join the roster, and for the first seven weeks after launch, she was also the buggiest. Warner Bros. Games' official July 2026 Update patch notes list a fix for “issues related to Talia al Ghul's gadget/ability upgrades” under bug ticket LBAT-64 — a quiet but meaningful fix for anyone who tried to invest WayneTech Chips into her kit early and watched the upgrades not stick properly. With that bug now squashed on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC, this is a good moment for a full rundown of who Talia is, how to unlock her, and what her two-ability kit actually does.

How to Unlock Talia al Ghul

Talia joins the roster later than every other hero in the base game. According to Game8's unlock guide, she becomes playable once you reach the Tumbler Chase mission in Chapter 5, and stays unlocked after you complete it. Deltia's Gaming's Chapter 5 walkthrough lays out the setup: midway through the Tumbler Chase, Nightwing's motorcycle is destroyed along with the Tumbler itself, leaving Batman to keep pursuing Joker alone on the Tumbler's bike attachment. A mysterious rider then pulls up alongside him on her own motorcycle to help finish the chase — that rider is Talia al Ghul, and she's controllable for the rest of the mission and every mission after it.

Like every other character, she carries over into Free Play once unlocked, so any chapter you've already cleared becomes accessible with her toolkit from that point forward.

Tip: Because Talia unlocks so late, plan a dedicated Free Play sweep of early chapters once you have her — several League of Shadows-themed puzzles in the Prologue and early chapters are built specifically around abilities she doesn't get to use until Chapter 5 in the main story.

Talia's Two Core Abilities

Where characters like Catwoman have five distinct gadgets, Talia's kit is narrower and built entirely around her League of Assassins training. Per Dexerto's character wiki and Game8's breakdown, she has two abilities:

👤 Ninja Dash

Traversal + Combat

Talia can teleport a short distance in the direction she's facing. It closes gaps other characters have to walk or grapple around, lets her cross broken bridges and other environmental hazards other characters can't, and doubles as a mobility tool in combat to close distance on enemies or slip out of a surrounded position quickly.

🏹 Blowdarts

Combat + Stealth

Talia's ranged option puts enemies to sleep at a distance rather than knocking them out through direct combat. It's the closest thing to a stealth takedown tool in her kit and is particularly useful for thinning out a group before wading in, or for silently disabling a guard that would otherwise trigger an alarm.

Upgrading Talia — and the Bug That Just Got Fixed

Like every playable character, Talia's Ninja Dash and Blowdarts can be upgraded at the Workbench in the Batcave using WayneTech Chips, which come from completing missions and cracking open WayneTech Caches scattered across Gotham's four islands. Game8's list of Talia al Ghul upgrades covers the specific chip costs for extending her dash range and expanding what the Blowdarts can affect.

Until this month, that upgrade path had a real problem: WB Games' own July 2026 Update notes confirm bug LBAT-64 covered issues specifically with Talia's gadget and ability upgrades not functioning correctly. The same patch also fixed a related interaction bug with the Riddler Trophy Batcave item (LBAT-121) and a WayneTech Cache Chest that failed to spawn on Tricorner Island (LBAT-446) — both relevant if you're doing a completion-focused Talia run. If you invested chips into her before mid-July and the upgrades didn't seem to change anything, that wasn't in your head; it's worth reopening the Workbench post-patch to confirm her upgrades are now applying properly.

Talia's Story Role: Villain, Ally, Playable Hero

Talia occupies unusual territory in Legacy's cast. She's introduced as a League of Shadows figure tied to her father Ra's al Ghul's larger plan for Gotham, but her personal history with Bruce Wayne — the two trained together under Ra's in Nanda Parbat — puts her in a genuinely ambiguous position rather than a simple turncoat arc. Game Rant's unlock roundup groups her alongside Catwoman as one of two characters in the base game who cross over from the villain roster to the permanent playable lineup, which is part of what makes her the most narratively distinct addition to the cast.

Practical Tips for Playing Talia

  • Save Ninja Dash upgrades for late-game Free Play sweeps. A longer dash range pays off most once you're backtracking through chapters looking for WayneTech Caches and Ace Cards you couldn't reach on your first pass.
  • Use Blowdarts before engaging groups. Thinning a pack of enemies down by one or two with a ranged sleep dart before wading in with melee makes crowd fights noticeably more manageable, especially on Dark Knight Mode.
  • Check the Workbench after Update 1.007. If you upgraded Talia before the July patch, re-verify her gadget upgrades are active now that LBAT-64 is resolved.
  • Pair her with a melee-heavy character in co-op. Her dash-and-dart kit is more about positioning and crowd thinning than raw damage, so she complements a partner built around direct combat.

Verdict: Worth the Wait?

Talia arrives later than anyone else in the roster, but she's not a filler unlock — her Ninja Dash is one of the more satisfying traversal tools in the game, and her League of Shadows connection gives Chapter 5 onward some of Legacy's more interesting character writing. With Update 1.007 fixing the upgrade bug that dogged her for the first seven weeks post-launch, now is a reasonable time to go back and actually invest WayneTech Chips into her kit if you'd been holding off.
