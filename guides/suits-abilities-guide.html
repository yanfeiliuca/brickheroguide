LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has the largest costume roster of any LEGO game ever made. The base game includes 100 suits and outfits across all seven playable characters, with all Deluxe Edition DLC pushing that number to 128. Here's everything you need to know about unlocking them all.

Overview

Every playable character — Batman, Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, Catwoman, Jim Gordon, and Talia al Ghul — has their own dedicated wardrobe of unlockable outfits. Suits are purely cosmetic in most cases, meaning they change your character's appearance without altering their core moveset. A small number of film-inspired skins come with unique idle animations and combat finishers that reflect their source material, but the core abilities remain the same across all outfits for a given character.

Cost to 100%: Buying every suit from the Bat-Mite Store alone would cost over 1,000,000 Studs. Prioritise Red Bricks and Stud Multipliers early — see our Stud Farming Guide for the fastest methods.

How to Unlock Suits

1. Main Story Missions

Completing story chapters automatically unlocks suits for multiple characters at once. This is the primary unlock path — playing through the full story will earn the majority of your wardrobe without any extra effort. Suits earned this way include the Batman Begins, Batman '89, The Dark Knight, and Batgirl of Burnside outfits, among many others.

2. Bat-Mite Store

The Bat-Mite Store in the Batcave sells suits using Studs. Prices range from 30,000 to 55,000 Studs per outfit. Stock up with Stud Multiplier Red Bricks before shopping — see our Stud Farming Guide for the fastest methods. The store is available from early in the game.

3. Lost Zoo Animals

Finding escaped zoo animals scattered across Gotham unlocks suits at milestone counts: 3 animals (Commissioner Gordon suit for Jim Gordon), 9 animals (Absolute Batman), and 15 animals (Batgirl Bronze Age). Worth collecting during open-world exploration.

4. WayneTech Caches

Unlocking WayneTech Caches scattered across Gotham City rewards suits at cumulative milestones: 10, 30, 50, 70, 80, 90, 110, 140, 150, 160, and 180 caches. Talia Al Ghul's wardrobe is almost entirely gated behind high cache counts, making her the most grind-heavy character for completionists.

5. AR Trials

Completing the Augmented Reality challenge courses scattered across Gotham rewards suits at 2, 5, and 15 trial completions — including Knightfall Batman and The LEGO Batman Movie Robin.

6. Riddler & Cluemaster Puzzles

Solve all 61 Riddler Puzzles to unlock the LEGO Batman: The Videogame suit for Batman. Solve all 60 Cluemaster Puzzles to unlock the Batman Serial (1943) suit. Both are long-term goals best saved for a dedicated completionist pass.

7. Wanted Posters & Side Missions

Completing Wanted Poster activities (2 for Knightmare Suit, 4 for Jim Gordon's Batsuit) and Falcone side missions (3 for Catwoman Costume Party, 9 for Catwoman BTAS suit) unlock specific character outfits. Waylon Jones cases reward Robin's World's Finest suit.

For a complete character-by-character breakdown of every suit and exact unlock requirement, see our All Suits Unlock Guide.

DLC Note: The Legacy Collection (21 suits across 3 packs) and the Mayhem Collection Sinister Pack (7 suits) add 28 additional suits beyond the base 100, bringing the full total to 128 with all DLC. See the DLC Suits section below.

How to Change Suits

To change your outfit at any time while in the open world (not inside a vehicle):

  1. Press Start / Options to open the menu
  2. Press R1 / RB to navigate to the Characters tab
  3. Press Square / X to open Suit Options
  4. Browse and select any unlocked suit

You can also change suits inside the Batcave's Wardrobe room at any time. Suits can be changed freely between missions without any penalty or cost.

Film-Inspired Skins

A standout feature of Legacy of the Dark Knight is its collection of actor-likeness skins modeled on specific Batman film performances. These are among the most memorable outfits in the game and are confirmed to be in the base roster:

  • Michelle Pfeiffer's Catwoman — from Batman Returns (1992)
  • Danny DeVito's Penguin — from Batman Returns (1992)
  • Heath Ledger's Joker — from The Dark Knight (2008)
  • Jeffrey Wright's Jim Gordon — from The Batman (2022)
  • Adam West Batman — from the 1966 TV series
  • Michael Keaton Batman — from Batman (1989)

Film skins often include unique idle animations and special finisher animations that reference the character's cinematic portrayal. According to GamesRadar, these skins are among the most sought-after unlocks in the game.

Video Game Legacy Skins

Beyond film tributes, the game also celebrates Batman’s video game history with at least one confirmed legacy unlock:

  • 1990 NES Batman — Purple 8-Bit Batsuit (Unlockable): The purple Batsuit from the 1990 NES Batman game by Sunsoft is available as an unlockable costume. Confirmed by DC.com’s post-launch feature (June 8, 2026). For many players this is one of the most nostalgic unlocks in the entire game — the purple silhouette jumping across Axis Chemical is a formative gaming memory that spans 36 years of Batman interactive history.

DLC Suits

The Deluxe Edition includes two DLC content drops that add 28 suits beyond the base 100:

  • Legacy Collection (available at launch with Deluxe Edition): 21 suits across three themed packs — Arkham Trilogy Pack (7 suits, Arkham Knight Batmobile), Batman Beyond Pack (7 suits, Batman Beyond Batmobile), and Party Music Pack (7 suits, party "Bat-Buggy"). Each pack also includes 5 Batcave decorative props. See our Legacy Collection DLC Breakdown for full details on every item.
  • Mayhem Collection — Sinister Pack (September 18, 2026): 7 new suits, one per original playable character, plus 5 new Batcave props and a new Batmobile. Comes with Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters in a new story mission. See our Mayhem Collection DLC Guide for full details.
  • Nintendo Switch 2 Pre-Order Bonus — The Dark Knight Returns Batsuit: All Switch 2 pre-orders (Standard and Deluxe) receive this suit at launch, inspired by Frank Miller's 1986 comic series. This suit is a Switch 2 pre-order exclusive and is not available on PS5, Xbox, or PC. Counts as an additional suit for Switch 2 players who pre-order. (Source: WB Games, June 3, 2026)

LEGO Set QR Redemption Suits

Four additional bonus suits are available through real-world LEGO set redemption — one per eligible Batman LEGO set, each tied to a QR code in the set's building instructions. These suits are cosmetic-only and do not count toward the 100 base-game completion total, so players chasing 100% do not need them. However, they are the only route to some exclusive Batsuit designs not found anywhere else in the game.

The four eligible sets and their in-game rewards, according to LEGO's official DLC page:

  • Batman Logo (76330) — 1 exclusive Batsuit + matching Batmobile variant
  • Batman v Superman Batmobile (76331) — 1 exclusive Batsuit + matching Batmobile variant
  • The Batman Batmobile (76332) — 1 exclusive Batsuit + matching Batmobile variant
  • Batman & Robin Batmobile (76333) — 1 exclusive Batsuit + matching Batmobile variant

How to redeem: Scan the QR code in the set's building instructions on LEGO.com (requires a free LEGO Insiders account). Then, on the game's main menu, select Redeem Code and enter the code that appears in your LEGO account. Each code is a single-use redemption valid until March 1, 2029. As of June 2026, there are no universally free codes — all four require owning a physical LEGO set. For a full walkthrough of the redemption process, see our QR Rewards Explained blog post.

These suits count toward the Wardrobe display in the Batcave but are tracked separately from the 100 base suits and 28 DLC suits. Players who redeem all four will see four additional entries in the Batman wardrobe slot.

Tips for Collectors

  • Unlock Red Brick Stud Multipliers first. Some Red Bricks double or quadruple your Stud income. The fastest path to buying all shop suits is stacking multipliers early.
  • Replay missions via the Batcomputer. You can return to any completed mission to clean up missed collectibles and earn any suit you skipped.
  • Don't ignore side activities. Zoo animals, AR Trials, and Riddler rooms each tie to specific outfit unlocks. Check your map for outstanding activities in each district.
  • Suits display in the Batcave Wardrobe. As you unlock outfits, they populate the wardrobe room — a great visual progress tracker for completionists.
📌 Post-Launch Bug Fix: A patch released in late May 2026 fixed an issue where players with 100 out of 101 suits unlocked saw an incorrect progress percentage of 99.8% instead of the correct value. If your completion counter was stuck just below 100%, update your game to the latest version — the display should now be correct. Reported and confirmed by JayShockblast. See our Post-Launch Patch Tracker for all known bugs and fix status.

Extra Suits via PC Mods

PC players have a fourth route to more outfits: the modding community. Within days of launch, modders on Nexus Mods published skin packs adding comic-accurate suits not available in the base game — including New 52 and Rebirth-era designs for Batman, Nightwing, and Batgirl. These are cosmetic replacements for existing in-game suits rather than new wardrobe slots, so they work best for players who've already unlocked the suit they want to reskin.

For a full rundown of what's available and how to install, see our Best PC Mods guide. Note that mods are PC-only; no mod support exists for PS5 or Xbox Series X|S versions.
