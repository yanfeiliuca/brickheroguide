33K Steam Peak: LEGO Batman Legacy Dethrones Arkham Knight — But Skywalker Saga Still Reigns Supreme

📅 May 26, 2026  |  ⏱ 5 min read  |  BrickHeroGuide

Four days into launch, LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has a record to its name: its all-time peak of 33,449 concurrent players on Steam is the highest figure ever recorded by any Batman game on the platform. That's enough to surpass Batman: Arkham Knight's long-standing all-time high of 27,432 — a record the Rocksteady classic had held since 2015.

But before you hand TT Games the trophy, there's a catch. Those 33K players are barely a third of what LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga achieved at its own launch peak of 82,517 in April 2022. And in the wider world of LEGO games on Steam, the gap between Batman and Star Wars turns out to be quite revealing.

Here's a full breakdown of the numbers — what they mean, and what they say about the LEGO franchise's place in PC gaming.

📊 Data sourced from: SteamCharts, Steambase, and TheGamer. Player counts were recorded around May 23–25, 2026.

Put differently: Legacy of the Dark Knight attracted roughly 22% more peak players than Arkham Knight — the Rocksteady game widely considered the gold standard of Batman gaming — while falling about 59% short of the LEGO series' all-time Steam peak set by Skywalker Saga in 2022.

Why Legacy Beats Arkham Knight — And Why That Matters

Batman: Arkham Knight launched in June 2015 with a notoriously troubled PC port. Warner Bros. pulled the game from Steam for six months shortly after release due to widespread performance issues, and the rough launch almost certainly suppressed its player count permanently. The game's all-time peak of 27,432 was achieved amid those chaotic early days and was never surpassed despite subsequent optimization patches.

By contrast, Legacy of the Dark Knight launched cleanly. Press previews highlighted smooth frame rates across hardware tiers, and the Steam Deck community reported surprisingly playable performance out of the box. A clean launch almost always translates into a higher organic player peak, and the 33K figure reflects that healthy technical state.

📋 Context: LEGO Marvel Super Heroes (2013) held the LEGO series' previous Steam peak record at just 5,953 concurrent players. Legacy of the Dark Knight is more than 5× larger than that figure — a sign of how dramatically PC gaming has grown, and how much more aggressively TT Games marketed this release.

The Batman brand also plays a role. The Caped Crusader consistently attracts more mainstream interest than the niche LEGO Marvel titles, and Legacy of the Dark Knight benefited from marketing that leaned into the cinematic Batman legacy — Christopher Nolan's trilogy, Tim Burton's films, the Animated Series. That cross-generational appeal likely pushed the game's audience beyond the typical LEGO demographic.

The Skywalker Saga Gap: Why 82K Is Still a Mountain to Climb

The elephant in the room is LEGO Star Wars. The Skywalker Saga's 82,517 peak in April 2022 was achieved on the back of one of the biggest entertainment franchises on the planet, years of anticipation, and a lengthy delay that generated enormous pent-up demand. The game also launched at a time when the LEGO franchise had been absent from major releases for several years, creating a genuine "return event" feeling.

Legacy of the Dark Knight didn't have those same tailwinds. Batman is popular, but Star Wars is a different category of cultural phenomenon. More practically, the game had a relatively short hype window — TT Games only announced the title in August 2025, giving it less than a year of buildup versus the years of pre-release coverage Skywalker Saga generated.

Notably, despite the lower player ceiling, Legacy of the Dark Knight actually holds a significantly better review score than Skywalker Saga: 97.2% positive on Steam versus Skywalker Saga's current 90.2%. In terms of player satisfaction, Batman may actually be the stronger release — it's simply playing to a smaller initial audience.

What Does 33K Actually Mean for the Game's Health?

Peak concurrent player counts are a snapshot metric — they tell you about launch buzz, not long-term engagement. The more telling number may be the 30-day average of 25,044 players, which suggests that a substantial portion of the launch audience is sticking around rather than bouncing off after the opening weekend. That's a healthy retention signal for a single-player focused title.

For comparison, many critically acclaimed single-player games see their average player counts drop to less than 20% of their peak within the first month. Legacy of the Dark Knight maintaining roughly 75% of its peak average at day four is encouraging, though it will be worth watching whether that figure holds as players complete the main story over the coming weeks.

🏆 The Real Win: Dethroning Arkham Knight is symbolic, but the more meaningful milestone is that Legacy of the Dark Knight has demonstrated LEGO games can attract mid-tier mainstream PC audiences. With a September DLC bringing Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, a second player spike is plausible — and could push the all-time peak even higher.

What to Watch For

A few factors could materially change the trajectory of the game's Steam numbers over the next few months. The Mayhem Collection DLC in September 2026 adds Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters with new story content — a significant enough addition to bring lapsed players back. DLC drops routinely cause player count spikes on Steam, and a September sale during autumn discounts could amplify that effect further.

The Nintendo Switch 2 version, currently listed with a 2026 release date and no confirmed window, will also expand the total playerbase when it arrives — though it won't affect Steam numbers directly. What matters on PC is whether the September DLC delivers on the datamined promise of 13 Suicide Squad characters, which would give the game the kind of extended content runway to compete more seriously with Skywalker Saga's long tail.

For now, the scoreboard stands: Legacy of the Dark Knight has planted its flag above every Batman game that came before it on Steam. Whether it can eventually challenge the Star Wars titan is a question worth revisiting in late 2026.
