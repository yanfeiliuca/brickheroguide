You have finished LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight's main story. The credits have rolled. Now what? The answer is: quite a lot. The story is designed to be the first act of your time with the game — most of the content, collectibles, and completion activities open up after the story ends. This checklist covers everything you need to do, ordered by efficiency, to reach 100% and earn the "I Am Batman" platinum trophy.

The Good News — Nothing is Missable

Before you worry about what you skipped during the story: nothing in this game is permanently missable. The Batcomputer in the Batcave hub lets you replay any mission at any time. You will have the full 7-character roster available in Free Play mode during replays, so character-gated collectibles you could not reach during Story Mode are all accessible now. Take a breath — you have not locked yourself out of anything.

Free Play explained: Every mission has a Story Mode version (with the characters assigned by the narrative) and a Free Play version (where you can choose any two characters from your full roster). Collectibles behind character-specific locks — Batgirl hacks, Catwoman lockpicks, Nightwing cables — are the targets for Free Play sweeps.

Immediate Priority — Do These First

⚡ Do Immediately After Credits

Activate all 9 District Towers 30–45 min

Batgirl is now fully unlocked with her Hackarang. Activate every district tower across all 9 areas — this reveals every open-world collectible on your map simultaneously. Do this before anything else. It is the highest ROI action in the post-game.

Buy the Stud Multiplier upgrades Immediate

If you have not already acquired the Stud Multiplier Red Bricks, get them now before starting your collectible sweep. Multiplied studs during collection runs make every subsequent purchase trivial. Check our Stud Farming Guide for locations.

Cash in all pending Batcave Challenges 10 min

Visit the green terminal in the Batcave and cash in every challenge you have completed. You likely have at least 15–20 from the story — that is 75,000–200,000 free Studs waiting for you.

Collectibles & Open World

🌎 Open World Sweep

Collect all 160 open-world WayneTech Caches 2–4 hours

With towers activated, all cache icons are visible on your map. Work island by island — Old Gotham first (highest density), then The Cauldron, Gotham Village, Newtown, and finally East End and Tricorner. For puzzle caches, bring Batgirl (hacking), Catwoman (lockpick), and Nightwing (electric) as your rotation.

Complete all open-world side activities 2–3 hours

Crime events, AR Challenges, and zoo animal recoveries across all 9 districts. Crime events respawn on a timer, so you can grind these efficiently. AR Challenges are one-time and reward Gold Bricks — Jim Gordon with his upgraded Rebound Launcher is the best pick for combat AR challenges.

Complete all 14 Falcone Fortunes 2–3 hours

Falcone Fortunes are heist puzzle rooms hidden across Gotham's districts — each involves cracking a safe inside a multi-room puzzle. They unlock progressively (you can't do all 14 at once), and almost all require Batman + Catwoman working together. One room also needs Batgirl. Reward: 2 WayneTech Chips per Fortune, 28 chips total — a major contribution to the upgrade grind. See our Falcone Fortune locations list for all 14 map coordinates.

Find all 50 Bat-Symbols (any 25 count toward completion) 1–2 hours

Bat-Symbols are the only collectible type NOT revealed by tower activation. Use Detective Mode and listen for audio pings while exploring rooftops — Bat-Symbols produce the same proximity ping as WayneTech Caches. You only need 25 of 50 for completion credit.

Find all open-world Gold Bricks Revealed by towers

Gold Brick icons appear on your map after tower activation. Some require puzzle completion, others are awards from completing side activities. Gold Bricks are used to purchase exploration Skills (Stud Magnet, Hyper Combo) from the Batcave skill board.

Batcave Completion

🏼 Batcave Hub

Build all Batcave platform expansions Studs required

The Batcave hub has multiple buildable platforms that expand the available space and unlock new facilities. Each expansion costs Studs — check the Batcave for glowing build points you have not yet constructed.

Place 30+ objects for the Collector Batcave Challenge 15 min

If you have not yet completed the Collector challenge (place 30+ objects in the Batcave), spend some time building on the available platforms — this is the easiest Batcave challenge and the one most people overlook.

Complete all 43 Batcave Mural Challenges Varies — see challenge guide

Work through remaining challenges from our Batcave Mural Challenges Guide. You need 20 for the achievement and all 43 to fully complete the mural. Utility Belt (requiring all Focus ultimates) is the most expensive and the one to plan last.

Mission Replay (Free Play)

🎮 Batcomputer Replays

Replay all 8 missions in Free Play for missed caches 2–3 hours

Each mission has 5 WayneTech Caches = 40 story-mission chips total. You likely missed several on first pass, especially ones behind Batgirl hacks and Catwoman lockpicks. Use our mission walkthrough pages for individual cache locations.

Replay missions for Red Bricks Varies per mission

Red Bricks found inside missions are only accessible in Free Play with the full roster. Check your completion percentage on the Batcomputer — missions showing under 100% still have items to find.

Complete the "Treasure Hunter" Batcave Challenge 30 min

Get all collectibles in any one story mission in a single run. Replay Mission 1 (the shortest mission) in Free Play — it has the fewest caches. Use Detective Mode to find everything before exiting.

Suits & Vehicles

👔 Bat-Mite Shop

Purchase all suits from the Bat-Mite Shop 1M+ Studs required

The Bat-Mite Shop sells character suits for Studs. Most suits are cosmetic, but the Gray Ghost suit is required for the Theatrical Batcave challenge. Budget significant Stud farming time if you want all suits — the full set costs over a million Studs total.

Unlock all vehicles Studs + Gold Bricks

Vehicles are purchased from the Batcave garage using Studs and Gold Bricks. Some vehicles are unlocked through story progression; others must be bought. Check the garage for any greyed-out vehicles showing their unlock requirements.

Batcave Challenges & Trophy Cleanup

🏆 Trophy Push

Complete Tough Guy (100 enemies in Dark Knight Mode) 4–6 hours

The longest grind in the game. If you did not play on Dark Knight Mode during the story, you will need to replay missions on this difficulty and accumulate 100 defeats. Dark Knight Mode can be selected from the pause menu at any time.

Earn the "I Am Batman" platinum / full completion Final unlock

The platinum trophy on PS5 (or the equivalent 100% achievement on Xbox/PC) unlocks when all other trophies are earned. See our full Trophy Guide for the complete roadmap.

Upcoming DLC

The Mayhem Collection DLC — adding Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters with their own upgrade trees and exclusive missions — is scheduled for September 18, 2026. If you purchased the Deluxe Edition, you will have immediate access when it launches. Standard Edition players can purchase it separately. DLC collectibles and trophies will be tracked separately from the base game completion percentage.

Known bug (Tricorner district): As of the June 2026 patch (1.006), a disappearing world chest in the Tricorner district affects some players' completion counts. If your Tricorner completion shows under 100% but you cannot find the final chest, reload a save from before entering the district or check for a new patch.
