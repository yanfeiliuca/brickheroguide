LEGO Batman Returns Set 76355: Everything We Know About the Rumoured 1992 Batmobile

Published: June 24, 2026 · 6 min read · LEGO Sets

September is shaping up to be a massive month for Batman LEGO fans. While everyone knows about the Mayhem Collection DLC dropping September 18 alongside the Nintendo Switch 2 version of Legacy of the Dark Knight, a separate rumour has been circulating in the LEGO community: a physical set called LEGO Batman Returns (76355) — reportedly a full-scale 1992 Batmobile model landing on September 1.

The rumour originated from leaker Brick Tap on Reddit, citing multiple sources, and was subsequently reported by StoneWars.com in late May 2026. Neither LEGO nor Warner Bros. has officially confirmed the set. Here is a full breakdown of what the rumour claims and what the broader context tells us about how plausible it is.

Rumour status: Everything in this article is based on unconfirmed leaks. Treat all details as provisional until LEGO makes an official announcement.

According to the leak, set 76355 is based on the Batmobile as it appeared in Batman Returns (1992), Tim Burton's sequel to his 1989 original. The vehicle is visually similar to the 1989 model — long, low-slung, jet-turbine aesthetic — but with small differences in bodywork and fin design that distinguish it from the version already immortalised in LEGO set 76139.

How Does It Compare to Previous LEGO Batmobile Sets?

The rumoured piece count of 2,272 puts set 76355 squarely between the classic TV Series Batmobile and the landmark 1989 version. That is roughly 1,000 fewer pieces than the 76139 — which measured a striking 60 cm (23.9 in) in length. According to StoneWars, this suggests the new set will be noticeably shorter and narrower, likely closer to the proportions of the 76328 TV Series model at around 50 cm.

The $220 price point (compared to $229.99 for the 1989 set in 2019) feels plausible given inflation, even with the lower piece count. Modern large LEGO sets tend to use more specialised parts and more complex internal structure per piece than their predecessors, which pushes prices up independently of piece count.

The Batman Returns Batmobile: A Visual Refresher

For those whose Batman Returns knowledge is a little hazy: the 1992 Batmobile is essentially the same long, jet-turbine design as the 1989 original, but with a slightly different engine cowl and updated bat-fin arrangement. In the film, the vehicle is eventually hijacked by the Penguin and turned into a weapon, leading to one of the more memorable sequences in the Tim Burton era.

The Batmobile from Batman Returns has appeared in LEGO form before, most recently at a very small scale inside the 2023 set 76252 Batcave – Shadow Box, which recreated the Batcave diorama from the film. A full-size standalone model has never been released, which makes the 76355 rumour particularly exciting — it would fill a notable gap in the LEGO Batman vehicle lineup.

Why the Visual Differences Matter to Collectors

To the casual observer, the 1989 and 1992 Batmobiles look nearly identical. But collectors and Batman film fans will notice the distinctions: the engine intake shapes differ, the rear fin arrangement is subtly changed, and certain panel lines are unique to the Returns version. LEGO set designers have historically been meticulous about capturing film-accurate details on their large Batmobile models, so a 76355 release would presumably reflect those differences with care.

Whether LEGO can communicate those visual differences clearly at a scale 1,000 pieces smaller than the 76139 remains to be seen. At 2,272 pieces, there is less room for surface detailing. The likely tradeoff is a more display-friendly footprint (smaller shelf space required) at the cost of some fine detail.

What About Minifigures?

The rumour is silent on minifigures, but precedent is instructive. The 1989 Batmobile (76139) included Batman, The Joker, and Vicki Vale — all characters central to the 1989 film. Applying the same logic to a Batman Returns set, the obvious candidates would be Batman, Catwoman, and the Penguin — the three central characters of the sequel.

A Penguin minifigure would be the real draw for collectors. The character has appeared in LEGO sets before but never in a form specifically referencing Danny DeVito's memorable portrayal. Given that Legacy of the Dark Knight's Mayhem Collection DLC features Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, a physical set releasing the same month that spotlights the Penguin would create an interesting cross-product Batman villain moment for the franchise.

Cross-product timing: If the rumour holds, September 2026 would see the Switch 2 game launch (Sept 18), the Mayhem Collection DLC (Sept 18), and potentially the 76355 Batmobile set (Sept 1) all arriving within the same 3-week window. That's an unusually coordinated Batman commercial moment.

How Credible Is the Rumour?

The Brick Tap leaker on Reddit has a reasonable track record within the LEGO fan community, and StoneWars.com — the German LEGO news outlet that reported the rumour — has solid editorial standards and notes when sources are unverified. The specific details (set number, piece count, price, date) are precise enough that this doesn't read like idle speculation; those figures suggest someone with access to early product planning information.

There are also structural reasons to find the rumour plausible. LEGO has been leaning heavily into Batman IP throughout 2026, timing physical sets to coincide with the video game's momentum. The 1992 Batmobile represents the one major Tim Burton-era Batman vehicle that has never received a standalone large-scale treatment, making it an obvious product to greenlight. A September 1 date — three weeks before the game's Switch 2 launch — fits the pattern of physical and digital product launches clustering together.

When Will We Know for Certain?

LEGO typically confirms sets officially 6–8 weeks before their release date. If September 1, 2026 is the real target, an official announcement would be expected sometime in July, possibly coinciding with LEGO's summer fan media events. Keep an eye on LEGO's official channels and this blog for confirmation.

What This Means for Legacy of the Dark Knight Players

For players of LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, the Batmobile from Batman Returns is already part of the game's DNA. The Tim Burton films — both 1989 and 1992 — are explicitly referenced through the legacy Batsuit collection and various Easter eggs scattered throughout Gotham. Our Easter Eggs and Hidden References guide documents several nods to the Burton era.

If you want to get your Batmobile fix in-game right now, the Batman Returns–era vehicle styling appears most clearly in the Legacy Collection DLC (included with the Deluxe Edition), which draws on the broader film and TV history of the Batman franchise. Whether LEGO's physical set and TT Games' digital Batmobile share any design DNA remains to be seen — but the timing of both arriving in September is hard to ignore.
