LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is an excellent game for trophy hunters and achievement chasers. The list has 52 trophies on PS5 (including a Platinum) and 51 achievements on Xbox, with zero missable entries — you can replay any mission or revisit any area to clean up whatever you skipped. The list skews toward open-world completion and collectible hunting rather than skill-based challenges, making it very achievable for dedicated players.

Overview

The PS5 trophy list breaks down as follows: 1 Platinum, 3 Gold, 4 Silver, and 44 Bronze trophies. There is no difficulty-related trophy — completing the game on Classic earns the same trophy access as Dark Knight mode. This means you can play on any difficulty you prefer without affecting your completion progress. According to multiple post-launch guides including Game Rant and Happy Thumbs Gaming, the list is considered fair and well-suited to the LEGO game format.

Platinum Trophy: "I am Batman"

🏆 "I am Batman" — Platinum

Unlock all other 51 trophies to earn the Platinum. The name is a nod to one of Batman's most iconic lines, fitting for a game that celebrates 85+ years of the Dark Knight across every medium. Estimated completion time based on community guides is 40–50 hours for a thorough first playthrough plus mop-up. See our full Platinum roadmap for the recommended two-phase approach.

Trophy Categories in Detail

Story Trophies (6 Bronze)

One trophy per story chapter — completing all 6 chapters automatically earns 6 Bronze trophies. These pop at the end of each chapter's final mission regardless of how you played. No special conditions, no collectible requirements — just finish the story and they are yours. Because nothing in this game is missable, you cannot accidentally lock yourself out of story trophies even if you skip cutscenes or rush through sections.

Open-World & Side Activity Trophies

This is the largest trophy category and the one that takes the most clock time. Gotham's open world is divided into districts, each containing a set of repeating side activities: zoo animal rescues (track down escaped animals and return them to Gotham Zoo), AR Trials (timed obstacle courses using Batman's suit systems), and Riddler Puzzle Rooms (environmental logic puzzles hidden throughout the city). According to The Gamer, the harder trophies ask you to clear all Gotham side activities, collect every major collectible, unlock all vehicles, and fully explore the open world.

Clearing all activities in a single district typically takes 30–60 minutes depending on familiarity with the area. The game's district completion tracker on the map makes it easy to see when you have finished each zone — aim for 100% in one district before moving on rather than bouncing around the city.

Collectible Trophies

Several trophies are tied to collecting specific types of items: finding all 23 Red Bricks, collecting all 10 Batcave Minikits (which assembles a display model), recovering all 10 WayneTech Caches, and reaching particular Stud milestones. These trophies pop automatically when you hit the required count — the game tracks your totals in the Batcomputer pause menu so you can monitor progress at any time.

The Stud milestone trophies deserve special attention. The highest milestone — the “Life’s been good to me” trophy — requires accumulating a total of 1,000,000 Studs across your playthrough (confirmed by multiple post-launch trophy guides including Game Rant and Happy Thumbs Gaming). With Stud Multiplier Red Bricks active, this is comfortably reached during normal open-world exploration. Without multipliers, it typically requires a short dedicated farming session. See our Collectibles Guide for Red Brick locations and our Stud Farming Guide for the fastest income methods.

Combat Trophies

A cluster of Bronze trophies requires specific combat feats. Common ones across LEGO Batman games include stealth takedown chains, counter combos of a set length, defeating a number of enemies with a specific gadget (Batarang, grapple slam, explosive gel), and performing a certain number of takedowns without being hit. These can all be targeted during free-roam across Gotham rather than requiring specific mission replays — enemy encounters respawn on re-entering areas, giving you unlimited attempts.

Combat trophy tip: The industrial district in central Gotham tends to have the densest enemy spawns, making it the best location to chain stealth and combat trophies in a single session. Enemies here respawn within seconds of clearing a group.

Character & Suit Trophies

Some trophies reward progression through the character and costume systems — unlocking specific ally characters, equipping film-inspired suits (based on Tim Burton, Joel Schumacher, Christopher Nolan, and Zack Snyder versions of the costume), or filling a percentage of the Batcave wardrobe. These layer naturally onto the normal 100% completion path. If you are hunting collectibles and completing side activities, most character and suit trophies will pop on their own without needing a separate targeted run.

Batcave Upgrade Trophies

Upgrading the Batcave hub using Gold Bricks and WayneTech Chips unlocks trophy notifications tied to specific milestones — opening a new Batcave room, completing the Trophy Room display, and reaching maximum WayneTech upgrade level. These are natural byproducts of the 100% process and require no specific strategy beyond collecting the necessary resources.

Most Challenging Trophies

While no trophy in this game is particularly punishing, a few require more focused effort than the rest:

“Life’s been good to me” — Collect 1,000,000 Studs (Gold)

This is the trophy most players are last to earn. The 1,000,000 Stud target is large enough that an active story playthrough with open-world exploration typically gets you to 600,000–750,000 Studs at most. The solution is to activate Stud Multiplier Red Bricks — stack the x2 and x4 bricks at minimum — then run a Stud Cache circuit in the open world. With multipliers active, a 15-minute circuit through the top Stud Cache locations (detailed in our Stud Farming Guide) can yield hundreds of thousands of Studs per run. The trophy pops automatically once you cross the threshold; you do not need to hold the Studs in your wallet.

All Side Activities Cleared (Gold)

This Gold trophy requires finishing every zoo rescue, AR Trial, and Riddler Puzzle Room across all Gotham districts. The main difficulty is not the activities themselves but finding them all — Riddler Puzzle Rooms in particular are not marked on the minimap until you are physically close to their entrance. Use the district completion tracker and walk each area slowly on your last few percentage points. Detective Vision highlights nearby interactable objects, which can surface Puzzle Room entrances you might otherwise miss.

Perfect Combat Challenge (Bronze)

Some trophies require completing a combat encounter without taking damage or using a specific technique. The Batcave's Challenge Mode (unlocked with a Gold Brick threshold) offers isolated arenas for practicing these without open-world interruptions. Completing the challenge arenas also nets additional Gold Bricks, making them worth doing anyway.

Complete All WayneTech Upgrades (Silver)

Maxing out every upgrade branch in the WayneTech tree requires finding all 10 WayneTech Caches in the Batcave plus accumulating a significant Stud total for the later, more expensive upgrade tiers. Get the Caches first (detailed in our Collectibles Guide), then use your Stud Multiplier income to fund the remaining upgrades. This trophy naturally pairs with the Stud milestone trophy.

Trophy Roadmap: 3 Passes

The most efficient path to Platinum is a structured three-pass approach rather than trying to do everything at once:

Pass 1 — Story Playthrough (8–12 hours)

Play through all 6 chapters without pressure. Collect anything obvious but do not backtrack for missed items. Your goals are: earn the 6 story chapter trophies, unlock Free Play for all missions, open all Batcave rooms, and build a baseline Stud total. By the end of the story you will have unlocked the full character roster, which is necessary for Free Play collectible runs.

Pass 2 — Batcave Sweep + Red Brick Priority (2–3 hours)

Immediately after credits: sweep every Batcave room for all 10 Minikits and all 10 WayneTech Caches (all rooms are now open). Then replay the earliest story missions in Free Play and retrieve every Stud Multiplier Red Brick you can find. Even x2 and x4 stacked will transform your Stud income for Pass 3. Spend WayneTech Chips on grapple range and wall-run distance first — these open the most additional Red Brick locations.

Pass 3 — Open World 100% + Mop-Up (12–18 hours)

Clear Gotham district by district: zoo rescues, AR Trials, Riddler Puzzle Rooms. Use your Stud Multipliers throughout. Replay any missions still missing Red Bricks via the Batcomputer. Target the remaining combat trophies during enemy encounters in the open world. Buy the 9 store Red Bricks from Bat-Mite once your Studs are high enough. The Platinum drops automatically when the final trophy pops.

Trophy order tip: Do not buy store Red Bricks until Pass 3. With multipliers active, the Stud cost becomes trivial. Buying them early in Pass 1 wastes Studs you have not yet efficiently farmed.

Recommended Strategy

  1. Play the full story on any difficulty — no difficulty trophies exist. Classic mode is perfectly valid for Platinum.
  2. Find Stud Multiplier Red Bricks as your first post-story priority. Every minute you spend earning Studs without multipliers is less efficient than it will be once you have them.
  3. Sweep Batcave rooms immediately after credits while they are fresh — all 10 Minikits and 10 WayneTech Caches in one pass.
  4. Clear open-world districts one at a time. The completion tracker shows exactly what is left; do not move on until a district reads 100%.
  5. Target combat feat trophies in the industrial district where enemy density is highest and respawn is fastest.
  6. Use Batcave Challenge Mode arenas for damage-free and technique-specific combat trophies — isolated and repeatable.
  7. Let the Stud milestone come naturally. With multipliers running during your open-world sweep, you will likely hit the threshold before finishing the last district.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is any trophy missable?

No. Every trophy is available at any point in the game via mission replay on the Batcomputer or open-world free roam. There are no one-time story choices, timed events, or new-game-plus exclusive trophies.

Can I get Platinum on a first playthrough without a guide?

Technically yes, but it will take longer because you will need to make several return trips for collectibles locked behind character abilities unavailable during the story. Following the three-pass roadmap above keeps total time to around 25–30 hours. Without structure, community reports suggest 35–45 hours on a blind run.

Does difficulty affect trophies?

No. Dark Knight difficulty offers no trophy advantages over Classic. Choose whatever difficulty you enjoy — the Platinum path is identical on both settings.

Do online features affect trophies?

The QR code redemption (for the bonus Golden Red Brick) requires a one-time internet connection but the Golden Red Brick is not required for any trophy — it is a cosmetic bonus from physical LEGO sets. All 52 trophies can be earned entirely offline.

How does this compare to previous LEGO Batman games?

Community consensus on platforms like PSNProfiles rates the list as comparable in difficulty to LEGO Batman 3's trophy list, with a slightly more generous time estimate due to the open-world design. LEGO Batman 1 and 2 had no Platinum on PS3; this is only the second LEGO Batman title to include one (after LEGO Batman 3).

External resources: Game Rant's full trophy list and Keen Gamer's trophy guide provide detailed unlock conditions for every individual trophy.
