Mayhem Collection DLC: Everything Officially Confirmed — Joker & Harley Quinn, Arkham Asylum Mission & September 18 Release

📅 June 21, 2026  |  ⏱ 7 min read  |  💬 News

When early datamines and press leaks pointed to a post-launch Joker and Harley Quinn expansion for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, the details were speculative. Now they’re not. Warner Bros. Games has officially confirmed the Mayhem Collection DLC in full, including a firm release date of September 18, 2026, a previously undetailed story mission set inside Arkham Asylum, and an all-new cosmetic bundle called the Sinister Pack. Here is every confirmed detail in one place.

TL;DR: The Mayhem Collection DLC releases September 18, 2026 on all platforms. It adds Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, a new Arkham Asylum story mission, Mayhem Mode, and the Sinister Pack (7 villain-themed suits, 1 new Batmobile, 1 Batcave prop). It is included with the Deluxe Edition and will be available as a standalone purchase.

The Release Date: September 18, 2026

According to the official Warner Bros. press release, the Mayhem Collection DLC launches on September 18, 2026 across PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, PC via Steam and Epic Games Store. This date is not coincidental: it is the same day the Nintendo Switch 2 version of the base game launches, making September 18 a significant date across the entire Legacy of the Dark Knight ecosystem.

Deluxe Edition owners on PS5, Xbox, and PC have the Mayhem Collection included at no additional charge. It was part of the Deluxe Edition’s original promise at launch, so no further purchase is required for those players. For Standard Edition owners who want access, the DLC is expected to be available as a standalone purchase, though pricing has not yet been confirmed by Warner Bros. as of this writing.

Switch 2 note: The Nintendo Switch 2 Deluxe Edition, launching on the same September 18 date, also includes the Mayhem Collection. Switch 2 Standard Edition players will be in the same position as their PS5/Xbox/PC counterparts regarding standalone purchase options.

The New Story Mission: Break Out of Arkham Asylum

The headline content addition in the Mayhem Collection is a brand-new story mission featuring The Joker and Harley Quinn as the protagonists — a significant shift from the main game’s hero-focused narrative. According to the Game8 breakdown of official materials, the mission puts players in control of the iconic villain duo as they attempt to break out of Arkham Asylum.

This framing is genuinely interesting from a narrative standpoint. The main game’s story is set in a Gotham City shaped by the Red Hood Gang and the emergence of organised crime, and seeing Arkham Asylum as a mission location suggests the DLC explores a different corner of Gotham’s criminal ecosystem. Playing as the villains — trying to escape confinement rather than enforce justice — inverts the main game’s structure in a way that fits the “Mayhem” branding perfectly.

The mission is described as a standalone addition rather than a direct continuation of the main campaign’s story, which means players won’t need to have completed the base game to dive in. That said, the Arkham Asylum setting will likely include callbacks and easter eggs for players familiar with the main game’s world-building.

Mayhem Mode: Causing Chaos in Gotham City

Beyond the story mission, the DLC introduces Mayhem Mode — a new gameplay mode in which players take to the streets of Gotham City as the Joker and Harley Quinn and simply cause chaos. Official descriptions emphasise that each character has their own distinct abilities, gadgets, and takedowns, giving both Joker and Harley mechanically differentiated playstyles rather than treating one as a reskin of the other.

The Joker’s toolset is described as leaning into improvisation and unpredictability — fitting for a character defined by chaos. Harley Quinn’s abilities reportedly emphasise acrobatics and close-quarters combat, reflecting her gymnastic background and aggressive fighting style from across her many media appearances. Specific gadgets have not been detailed beyond the broad strokes, but the official marketing materials confirm each character has a unique kit.

Mayhem Mode is framed as an open-ended sandbox experience within Gotham City’s existing map, which suggests it will give players a chance to revisit familiar locations from the base game from a villain’s perspective. Whether it includes structured challenges, score targets, or purely freeform mayhem remains to be confirmed ahead of the September launch.

The Sinister Pack: Villain-Themed Cosmetics

😈 Sinister Pack

Included in the Mayhem Collection DLC
  • 7 Villain-Themed Suits — One for each of the main game’s seven playable characters, designed around a dark, villain-inspired aesthetic
  • 1 New Batmobile — A new villain-themed vehicle variant added to the Batcave display and driveable roster
  • 5 Batcave Prop Items — A set of five villain-themed decorative items for the Batcave hub, according to the official WB Games press release

The Sinister Pack is the Mayhem Collection’s cosmetic component, following the same structural logic as the Legacy Collection packs from the base Deluxe Edition: character suits for each of the seven playable characters, a Batmobile addition, and Batcave customisation content. The villain-themed angle is a natural fit given the DLC’s Joker and Harley Quinn framing — players getting to dress their heroes in outfits inspired by the dark side of Gotham.

Specific suit designs have not been officially revealed as of June 2026, though preview materials suggest a palette of deep purples, acid greens, and Gothic blacks consistent with the Joker’s visual identity. Whether the suits draw directly from Joker and Harley Quinn’s iconography (e.g., Batman in a Joker-coloured cowl) or take a more abstract “villainous” approach remains to be seen in pre-release screenshots.

Like the individual Legacy Collection packs (each of which also includes 7 suits, 1 Batmobile, and 5 Batcave props), the Sinister Pack matches that cosmetic structure while adding the far more substantial gameplay additions — the Arkham Asylum story mission and Mayhem Mode — on top.

Why This Is Bigger Than a Typical LEGO DLC

Most post-launch DLC for LEGO games historically fell into a predictable pattern: a handful of new characters dropped into the hub, accessible in the existing game but without unique story content. The Mayhem Collection breaks from that template in a meaningful way. Two fully playable villain characters with distinct mechanical identities, a dedicated story mission with a new narrative perspective, and an entirely new gameplay mode is a substantially more ambitious offering than the series has typically delivered at this post-launch stage.

The comparison that comes to mind is the “villain campaign” additions that have appeared in other open-world games — the chance to experience Gotham from the other side of the law. For a LEGO game, this is genuinely novel. Previous LEGO Batman titles allowed villain character selection in Free Play, but dedicated villain-protagonist story missions are a different proposition entirely. The DLC signals that TT Games is willing to experiment with the format in ways that go beyond cosmetic additions.

It also cements the Deluxe Edition’s value retroactively. At launch, the $30 premium over the Standard Edition bought you the Legacy Collection (21 suits, 3 Batmobiles, 15 Batcave props) and early access. With the Mayhem Collection now fully revealed, that $30 also covers a second playable pair of characters, a new story mission, and a new game mode. For fans who purchased the Deluxe Edition in May, the wait until September will be rewarded with something substantive.

Already on Standard Edition? If you’re considering the upgrade, now is a good time to weigh the full Deluxe Edition value: Legacy Collection cosmetics (all available now) plus the Mayhem Collection in September. The Deluxe Edition Upgrade is available on all platforms and activates both DLC bundles on your existing save.

What We Still Don’t Know

Several details remain unconfirmed ahead of September 18. The standalone price for players who want the Mayhem Collection without the full Deluxe Edition upgrade has not been announced. The specific gadgets available to the Joker and Harley Quinn in Mayhem Mode have only been described in broad terms. The Sinister Pack suit designs have not been officially revealed, and the exact nature of the new Batmobile has not been detailed beyond its existence.

Pre-release coverage and possibly a dedicated trailer are expected before the September launch — TT Games and Warner Bros. typically ramp up marketing in the weeks before a major release date. Given the significance of September 18 as both the Mayhem Collection launch and the Nintendo Switch 2 base game debut, a substantial promotional push seems likely in July and August 2026.

We will update this article as new confirmed details emerge. For now, the September 18 date is locked in, the Arkham Asylum story mission is confirmed, and Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters are officially part of Legacy of the Dark Knight’s post-launch roadmap.
