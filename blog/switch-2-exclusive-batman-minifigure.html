Switch 2 Deluxe Edition Comes with an Exclusive Retro Video Game Batman Minifigure

Published: June 11, 2026 · 5 min read

When Warner Bros. Games officially confirmed the September 18, 2026 release date for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight on Nintendo Switch 2, the headline figure wasn't just the date — it was a small plastic one. The physical Deluxe Edition of the game for Switch 2 will ship with an exclusive "Retro Video Game Batman" minifigure, and the LEGO and gaming communities have both taken notice.

What Is the Retro Video Game Batman Minifigure?

The exclusive figure is described as a "Retro Video Game Batman" minifigure — a physical LEGO collectible designed after the pixelated Batman from the 1989 NES game Batman: The Video Game, developed by Sunsoft. That game featured an 8-bit Batman running and jumping through side-scrolling levels based on Tim Burton's classic film, and it holds a beloved place in gaming history for many players who grew up in the late 1980s and early 1990s.

According to multiple sources including The Brick Fan and Stonewars, the minifigure is available with physical Deluxe Edition copies only, and only while supplies last. It is not included with the digital Deluxe Edition, nor with the physical Standard Edition.

Important: The Retro Video Game Batman minifigure is a physical-copy exclusive. Digital Deluxe Edition buyers and Standard Edition buyers do not receive it. If you want the figure, you need the physical Switch 2 Deluxe Edition — which retails for $89.99 USD.

Why This Minifigure Matters to LEGO Fans

To understand why this particular figure is generating buzz, a bit of history helps. The Sunsoft 1989 Batman NES game predates the LEGO Batman franchise by nearly two decades — it was a licensed tie-in to Tim Burton's film, released at a time when the idea of LEGO video games didn't yet exist. Turning that specific era of Batman's video game history into a collectible minifigure is a meta-referential move that rewards fans who know both worlds.

From a pure LEGO collecting standpoint, video-game-exclusive minifigures have a strong track record of becoming sought-after items. The LEGO Dimensions series (2015–2017) produced numerous game-exclusive figures that now command significant prices on the secondary market. While it's too early to speculate about this figure's long-term collectibility, the precedent exists for game-bundle LEGO figures to appreciate in value once in-store availability dries up.

Note for collectors: The "while supplies last" qualifier in the official press release is standard language for pack-in exclusives, but it does signal that once initial print runs sell through, the figure will no longer be available at retail. First-print physical copies are typically the safer bet for securing the bundle.

The Switch 2 Version's In-Game Features

Beyond the physical collectible, the Switch 2 version of Legacy of the Dark Knight has several platform-specific features worth noting. The game supports the Nintendo Switch 2's enhanced Joy-Con 2 haptic feedback, and the Deluxe Edition includes the same digital content as other platforms: the Legacy Collection DLC (Arkham Trilogy Pack, Batman Beyond Pack, and Party Music Pack), plus access to the upcoming Mayhem Collection DLC arriving in September 2026, which adds Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters.

According to the official Warner Bros. press release, Deluxe Edition buyers on Switch 2 also receive 72 hours of early access before the September 18 launch date — putting the early access start at approximately September 15, 2026.

A Broader Trend: Physical Gaming Collectibles Are Back

This announcement fits into a wider resurgence of physical collectibles bundled with video game releases. The past few years have seen limited-edition game bundles include statues, art books, steelbooks, and now LEGO minifigures. For a franchise that straddles gaming and physical toys, bundling a minifigure with the Switch 2 version is an intuitive fit — LEGO's core audience likely skews toward Nintendo hardware, and the physical toy element reinforces the LEGO brand's tactile identity.

Sites covering both LEGO news and gaming, including Brick Fanatics and Hoth Bricks, have both picked up the story — a sign that the crossover appeal is real and that the announcement is reaching both communities simultaneously.

Should You Buy the Physical Switch 2 Deluxe Edition?

If you're already planning to pick up the Switch 2 version and you care about physical LEGO collectibles, the physical Deluxe Edition at $89.99 is arguably the most compelling version of the game across any platform. You get the full Deluxe Edition digital content (Legacy Collection DLC + Mayhem Collection DLC access), 72-hour early access, and a physical minifigure that won't be available any other way.

If you're not a physical media collector, the digital Deluxe Edition at the same $89.99 price point is functionally identical in terms of in-game content. And if you're already playing on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, or PC, there's no platform-equivalent minifigure offer — the figure is currently Switch 2 exclusive.

Pre-orders for the physical Deluxe Edition are live now ahead of the September 18 launch. Given the supply-limited nature of the minifigure inclusion, pre-ordering sooner rather than later is the safer choice for collectors who don't want to chase it on the secondary market.
