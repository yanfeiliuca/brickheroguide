Complete Voice Cast Guide: Every Actor in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight

Published: June 25, 2026 · 7 min read

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight arrived on May 22, 2026, with one of the most notable cast shakeups in franchise history. Gone are the familiar voices from previous LEGO DC titles — including Will Arnett's irreverent take on the Dark Knight — replaced entirely by a fresh ensemble of British and international voice talent. Here's the complete breakdown of every actor behind the cowl, the cape, and the chaos.

Why a Completely New Cast?

The most common question fans have asked since launch is straightforward: why didn't TT Games bring back Traveller's Tales' longtime voice talent? The answer is the 2024–2025 SAG-AFTRA video game strike. The strike, which ran for over a year and primarily affected major studio productions, meant that actors affiliated with the union were not available to reprise their roles from previous LEGO games and DC titles.

The result is that every significant voice in Legacy of the Dark Knight is performed by non-SAG talent, primarily sourced from the UK voice acting pool where TT Games' Knutsford studio is based. Far from being a limitation, the new cast brings a distinctly British theatrical sensibility to Gotham — one that, according to critics, suits the game's more serious Arkham-influenced tone.

Archive tribute: Two legendary Batman actors are still present in the game through archive audio. Kevin Conroy, who died in November 2022, is heard as the Arkham Asylum Announcer, and Adam West's footage from the 1960s TV series appears within the game's Batman legacy tributes. Both inclusions were widely praised as respectful nods to Batman's history.

Standout Performances and Casting Notes

Shai Matheson as Batman / Bruce Wayne

Shai Matheson

Batman / Bruce Wayne

Matheson is perhaps the least immediately recognizable name on this list, which is part of TT Games' apparent intent. Rather than leaning on an established Batman voice — Kevin Conroy's definitive animated take or Ben Affleck's gravelly growl — the studio cast fresh. Matheson has credits in Final Fantasy XVI and Xenoblade Chronicles 3, suggesting a background in epic, emotionally layered game narratives. His Batman is reportedly measured and weighty without the campy irreverence of Arnett's LEGO Movie version, which critics say suits the Arkham-adjacent tone of Legacy perfectly.

Matt Berry as Bane

Matt Berry

Bane

This is the casting choice that generated the most pre-launch buzz — and for good reason. Berry, best known for his comedic work in What We Do in the Shadows and The IT Crowd, brings a theatrical, almost operatic quality to Bane that reviewers describe as leaning into Tom Hardy's voice from The Dark Knight Rises while dialing up the absurdity to match the LEGO setting. The result, according to multiple outlets, lands perfectly: a Bane who sounds simultaneously threatening and faintly ridiculous, which is exactly what a LEGO game needs.

Colin McFarlane as Commissioner Gordon

Colin McFarlane

Commissioner Jim Gordon

McFarlane is the cast member with the deepest real-world Batman connection outside of archive recordings. He played Commissioner Gillian B. Loeb in Christopher Nolan's The Dark Knight and Batman Begins — meaning the Dark Knight trilogy's Gotham essentially had the same actor on both sides of the law. His Gordon in Legacy is described as authoritative and warm, with the kind of lived-in authenticity you'd expect from someone who has literally inhabited the Nolan-verse. It's a quiet piece of casting continuity that rewards attentive fans.

Ewan Bailey as The Joker

Ewan Bailey

The Joker

Perhaps the most scrutinized casting in the game, since The Joker carries enormous expectations from Mark Hamill's benchmark performance. Bailey brings a different energy: reviews suggest a Joker who is genuinely unsettling in his cheerfulness rather than broadly comic, which aligns with the game's darker narrative ambitions. Notably, Bailey also voices Carmine Falcone in several scenes, demonstrating the ensemble's versatility under a tight production budget. The Joker becomes playable in the Mayhem Collection DLC arriving September 18, 2026, so Bailey will have much more material to work with later this year.

The Archive Tributes: Conroy and West

The most emotionally resonant voice acting in Legacy of the Dark Knight doesn't come from living actors. Kevin Conroy, who voiced Batman in Batman: The Animated Series for three decades before his passing in November 2022, is present as the Arkham Asylum Announcer via archive recordings. TT Games has not disclosed which specific recording sessions were used, but the inclusion was widely reported as a deliberate tribute organized in consultation with his estate.

Adam West's voice — or more precisely, footage from the original 1960s Batman television series — appears during the game's Batman Legacy Tributes section, which chronicles the Dark Knight's evolution across decades of media. West, who died in 2017, is presented in archival context rather than as a character voice, making the sequence feel more like a loving museum exhibit than a posthumous cameo.

The Mayhem Collection: What Happens to the Cast?

When the Mayhem Collection DLC launches on September 18, 2026, The Joker and Harley Quinn become fully playable characters with their own story mission set around an Arkham Asylum breakout. Ewan Bailey (Joker) returns to a much larger role, and while TT Games has not yet confirmed who voices Harley Quinn in the DLC, the same ensemble casting approach is expected to apply.

The Nintendo Switch 2 version of Legacy of the Dark Knight also launches on the same date, meaning the full cast will debut on a new platform simultaneously with the DLC content expansion.

How the Cast Compares to Previous LEGO Games

Previous LEGO Batman games leaned on licensed talent from animated and theatrical productions. LEGO Batman 2 (2012) brought in the voice cast from Batman: The Animated Series, while LEGO DC Super-Villains (2018) featured Troy Baker, Laura Bailey, and Mark Hamill reprising his definitive Joker performance. The decision to move entirely away from established LEGO DC voice talent is the most significant such departure in the franchise's history.

Whether this becomes the new norm for LEGO games — or whether future titles return to marquee licensed voices when the SAG-AFTRA situation permits — remains to be seen. What's clear from Legacy of the Dark Knight's reception is that the new ensemble delivered a performance worthy of the franchise's highest-rated entry. An 84 on Metacritic, with only four mixed reviews out of 47 total, suggests that players and critics have accepted the new voices as definitively as any that came before.
