What to Do After Beating LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight (Post-Credits Guide)

Published: June 29, 2026 · 5 min read

You've rolled credits on LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight after clearing all 21 main missions. Now what? Unlike many action games, there's no New Game Plus mode waiting for you — but the post-credits landscape is rich enough that many players have logged more hours after the campaign than during it. Here's a structured breakdown of everything available and the order that makes the most sense to tackle it.

First: No New Game Plus — Here's Why That's Fine

Let's address the question searched most often after credits roll. According to Game8's dedicated NG+ entry, LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight does not include a New Game Plus mode, which is consistent with how TT Games has approached the series historically. There's no option to carry your upgrades or unlocked suits into a fresh campaign run.

In practice, this matters less than it sounds. The game's post-credits content is structured around a different loop: Free Play mode opens up fully after campaign completion, allowing you to replay any of the 21 missions with any character in your roster. This is where most collectibles that were gated behind specific character abilities become accessible, and it's the primary engine that drives the 100% completion grind.

Free Play unlocked: After credits, every completed mission becomes replayable with any character or suit in your collection. This is the correct time to go back and collect anything story-mode locked you behind character-specific abilities during your first run.

The Collectible Mountain: 247+ Items Across Four Islands

The real post-game is the collectible sweep. The game's total collectible count stands at 247+, broken into several categories across Gotham's four islands:

121 Riddler Trophies
200 WayneTech Caches
247+ Total Collectibles
4 Gotham Islands

The 200 WayneTech Caches are the most systematically rewarding category to pursue because each cache feeds into the WayneTech upgrade tree, and hitting specific milestone totals unlocks new upgrade tiers. The confirmed milestones are at 10, 30, 50, 70, 80, 90, 110, 140, 150, 160, and 180 caches collected. If you finished the campaign and still have upgrade points you couldn't spend because you hadn't unlocked the higher tiers, the cache hunt is the direct path to those unlocks. Our full WayneTech Upgrades Guide has the complete milestone breakdown.

The 121 Riddler Trophies are the second major category. These are the most puzzle-heavy collectibles in the game, typically requiring specific suit abilities, environmental manipulation, or character switches to solve. After campaign completion, the full open world is accessible across all four islands simultaneously, which means you can approach the Riddler hunt geographically rather than being locked to story-unlocked zones. For a full map and location breakdown, see our Collectibles Guide.

Recommended order: Use Free Play to sweep missed in-mission collectibles first (these are finite and easy to track per-mission), then shift to the open-world collectible hunt. Doing it in this order keeps your progress stats accurate and prevents double-counting confusion.

Dark Knight Difficulty: The Cleaner Replay Option

If you want a reason to re-experience the campaign's 21 missions rather than collecting, Dark Knight Mode is the answer. Unlike NG+, it doesn't start you over from scratch — you can toggle it from the pause menu at any point and apply it immediately to any mission you replay in Free Play. Your suits, upgrades, and unlocked characters carry over.

What changes: a hard lives system applies to most missions, enemy crowd sizes increase, and advanced combat variants appear earlier. According to guides at Neon Lights Media and Games.gg, there's also a 20% stud penalty per death — meaning unspent studs are at risk. The upside: Dark Knight Mode is the sharpest the combat system gets in the base game, and replaying missions you already know well gives you the mental space to focus on the combat challenges rather than navigation.

Critically, there are no trophies or achievements locked to Dark Knight Mode completion. You can attempt it purely for personal challenge without worrying about progress implications if you switch back.

Trophy and Achievement Hunting

For completionists with a platform goal, the numbers are 52 trophies on PS5 (including platinum) and 51 achievements on Xbox. As documented by PowerPyx's trophy roadmap, the platinum is largely driven by collectible completion and suit unlocks rather than difficulty-gated challenges. Most players who do a thorough collectible sweep will be within range of the platinum without any dedicated trophy-hunting sessions — the trophies and the completion loop align naturally.

The one area where targeted effort helps: suit-specific trophies. The game has 101 base suits and 129 total suits including all DLC, and several trophies require you to use specific suits to perform actions. Our Suits & Abilities Guide lists which suits have unique abilities and which trophies they're tied to, which helps prioritize your unlock order if the platinum is the goal. See also the Trophy Guide for the full roadmap.

Looking Ahead: Mayhem Collection DLC (September 18)

The biggest post-credits reason to maintain your save file rather than starting fresh: the Mayhem Collection DLC drops on September 18, 2026 — the same day as the Nintendo Switch 2 launch. It adds Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters, an Arkham Asylum Jailbreak mission, the new Mayhem Mode, and the Sinister Pack (7 suits, 5 Batcave decorations, 1 Batmobile skin). Players with the Deluxe Edition get it automatically; everyone else can purchase it separately.

If you're planning on playing the DLC, finishing your collectible sweep and suit unlocks before September means you'll be fully upgraded when the new content arrives. The two new playable characters are also expected to open new Free Play options in existing missions, potentially unlocking collectibles that are currently inaccessible.

DLC prep tip: The Mayhem Collection is confirmed to add 7 new suits to the roster. If you're tracking your suit collection for trophies, the DLC will push the total to 129. Hold off on any suit-count trophies that might be affected until after September 18 if you're planning to get the DLC.

The Short Answer

After the credits: run Free Play on your weaker missions to grab missed collectibles, then sweep the open world for WayneTech Caches (prioritize milestone totals at 10, 30, 50, 70, 80, 90, 110, 140, 150, 160, 180) and Riddler Trophies. If you want a combat challenge, toggle Dark Knight Mode. If you're platinum-hunting, the PowerPyx roadmap will get you there without wasted effort. Then hold your save for September 18 when the Mayhem Collection arrives.
