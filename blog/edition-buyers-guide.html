LEGO Batman Legacy: Standard vs. Deluxe vs. Switch 2 — Which Edition Should You Buy?

Published: June 23, 2026 · 6 min read

With the Nintendo Switch 2 version of LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight officially dated for September 18 and pre-orders now open across all platforms, there has never been more edition options to navigate. There is the Standard Edition, the Deluxe Edition, the Deluxe Edition Upgrade for people who already own the Standard, and two separate Switch 2 editions — one of which comes with a physical LEGO minifigure. Add in multiple pre-order bonuses and it quickly gets confusing.

This guide cuts through all of it. Here is exactly what each edition contains, who it is for, and where it makes sense to spend the extra money — or where it does not.

Standard Edition

Standard

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight

Available on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC (Steam & Epic) — launched May 22, 2026
  • Full base game — main campaign, open-world Gotham, all 7 playable characters
  • All free post-launch content (suits distributed via HBO Max tie-in, patch updates)
Best for: Players who want to try the game first without committing to a long-term DLC plan, or anyone who prefers to wait and see whether the Mayhem Collection DLC is worth purchasing separately at launch.

The Standard Edition gives you everything you need to experience the core game. The main campaign runs roughly 12 to 15 hours for a first playthrough, and a full 100% completion run clocks in between 30 and 40 hours according to community reports. There is no content cut from the base game — the Deluxe Edition does not unlock missions or story content that Standard players cannot access. The difference is entirely cosmetic DLC and early access timing.

Deluxe Edition

Deluxe — Best Value

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Deluxe Edition Recommended

$89.99 SRP — PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC (Steam & Epic) — available now
  • Full base game
  • 72-hour early access (May 19 instead of May 22 at launch)
  • Legacy Collection DLC — available now: Arkham Trilogy Pack (7 suits + 1 Batmobile), Batman Beyond Pack (7 suits + 1 Batmobile), Party Music Pack (Batcave DJ booth, jukebox, dance floor, and more)
  • Mayhem Collection DLC — releasing September 18, 2026: Joker and Harley Quinn as fully playable characters with unique movesets, Arkham Asylum story mission, Sinister Pack (7 new suits, 5 Batcave props, 1 new Batmobile)
Best for: Anyone planning to spend significant time in the game. Two full DLC packs with a combined 21+ suits, multiple Batmobiles, and an additional playable story mission represent strong value at the price premium over Standard.

The Legacy Collection DLC content is already playable right now. If you purchased the Deluxe Edition at launch, the Arkham Trilogy and Batman Beyond suit packs have been available since May 19. The Mayhem Collection DLC adds the two most-requested post-launch features: Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, which TT Games confirmed in the pre-launch trailer, and an entirely new story mission set in Arkham Asylum.

According to the Mayhem Collection DLC content breakdown, the Sinister Pack component specifically adds one suit for each of the seven original playable characters (Batman, Catwoman, Robin, Nightwing, Batgirl, Red Hood, and Commissioner Gordon), five new Batcave decorative items, and one additional Batmobile for use in open-world Gotham. These items carry over into the main game rather than being isolated to a DLC-exclusive mode, which increases their practical value for collectors and completionists.

Deluxe Edition Upgrade ($24.99)

If you already own the Standard Edition on PC, PS5, or Xbox, you can purchase the Deluxe Edition Upgrade for $24.99 via Steam or your platform's store. This grants both the Legacy Collection and Mayhem Collection DLC packs. The early access window has obviously already passed, but every other piece of Deluxe content is included. This is the most efficient upgrade path for existing Standard Edition owners who decide later that the DLC is worth it.

Note on Mayhem Mode: The new Joker and Harley Quinn Mayhem Mode is only available through the Mayhem Collection DLC, which is part of the Deluxe Edition or purchasable separately. Standard Edition owners will not have access to these characters or the Arkham Asylum mission without the upgrade or standalone DLC purchase.

Nintendo Switch 2 Editions

Switch 2 — Launching September 18, 2026

Nintendo Switch 2 Standard Edition

$69.99 SRP — Nintendo Switch 2 — pre-orders open now
  • Full base game (optimized for Nintendo Switch 2 hardware)
  • Pre-order bonus: Dark Knight Returns in-game Batsuit (available at launch)
Best for: Switch 2 owners who want the base experience at a lower entry point and are comfortable skipping the DLC content.
Switch 2 Deluxe — Physical Only for Minifigure

Nintendo Switch 2 Deluxe Edition

$89.99 SRP — Nintendo Switch 2 — pre-orders open now
  • Full base game
  • Legacy Collection DLC (available at launch on Sept 18)
  • Mayhem Collection DLC (available at launch on Sept 18, same day)
  • Pre-order bonus: Dark Knight Returns in-game Batsuit
  • Physical edition only: Exclusive LEGO Retro Video Game Batman Minifigure (while supplies last)
Best for: LEGO collectors and Nintendo fans. Switch 2 Deluxe Edition players get a meaningful advantage over their PS5/Xbox counterparts at launch: both DLC packs are available on day one, September 18, at the same time as the Switch 2 release.

The Nintendo Switch 2 launch on September 18 is timed to coincide with the Mayhem Collection DLC release across all platforms. This means Switch 2 Deluxe Edition buyers get the complete package — base game plus both DLC packs — all at once. PS5 and PC Deluxe Edition owners who have been waiting since May for the Mayhem Collection will receive it on the same date.

The physical Deluxe Edition exclusive minifigure deserves special mention. The Retro Video Game Batman Minifigure is based on the 1989 NES game Batman: The Video Game, published by Sunsoft, as confirmed by Stonewars. It is a physical LEGO collectible included only with the physical retail version of the Switch 2 Deluxe Edition, and it will only be available while supplies last. There is no digital equivalent. This makes the physical edition meaningfully different from a digital purchase of the same SKU — an unusual distinction in modern gaming that LEGO collectors in particular should factor into their decision.

Pre-Order Bonuses Explained

All pre-orders for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — on any platform, in any edition — include the Dark Knight Returns Batsuit, a Frank Miller-inspired costume unlocked at launch. According to ComicBook.com, the suit features the chunky bat symbol from Frank Miller's iconic 1986 comic run rather than the streamlined symbol used on Batman's default Batsuit in the main game. For Switch 2 pre-orders, this bonus applies to both Standard and Deluxe editions.

Pre-order window: The Dark Knight Returns Batsuit pre-order bonus is available now for all Switch 2 editions. If you are planning to pick up any edition at any point before September 18, consider locking in a pre-order specifically to secure this bonus, as it is unlikely to remain available after launch.

Verdict: Which Edition Is Right for You?

If you are on PS5, Xbox, or PC and have not yet purchased the game, the Deluxe Edition at $89.99 is the better long-term value — both DLC packs combined add more than 21 suits, multiple Batmobiles, new Batcave content, and an additional story mission. The gap between Standard and Deluxe is meaningful in the long run, particularly once the Mayhem Collection lands in September with Joker and Harley Quinn.

If you already own the Standard Edition on those platforms, the $24.99 upgrade is a clean and efficient path. There is no need to repurchase the base game.

If you are a Nintendo Switch 2 owner, the physical Deluxe Edition is the obvious choice for any LEGO collector, not just for the DLC content but for the Retro Video Game Batman Minifigure. Supply for that minifigure is almost certain to be limited. Pre-ordering now, while stock is available, is the safest move. The Switch 2 Deluxe also benefits from getting both DLC packs on day one rather than in two separate waves.

For casual players who only want to sample the main story before committing, the Standard Edition on any platform delivers the complete core experience. The Deluxe content is all cosmetic and supplemental — nothing in those DLC packs unlocks gameplay or campaign content that Standard players are missing. The base game is a full, complete release on its own.
