LEGO Batman Legacy Soundtrack Deep Dive: Simon Withenshaw, Danny Elfman & 39 Tracks Explained

Published: June 27, 2026 · 7 min read

The original soundtrack for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight has been quietly one of the best video game scores of 2026 — and it deserves a proper deep dive. Released digitally on May 22, 2026 through WaterTower Music, the 39-track, 114-minute score by composer Simon Withenshaw is both a love letter to Batman's entire musical heritage and a substantial standalone achievement. If you've been playing the game without headphones, you've been doing it wrong.

The Composer: Simon Withenshaw's LEGO Career

Simon Withenshaw is not a new name in the LEGO music universe. According to NOWPLAYING, Withenshaw has been with TT Games since 2009 and is credited on nearly every LEGO game the studio has released since then — Marvel, Star Wars, Lord of the Rings, Harry Potter, DC Super-Villains. He has described himself as a "musical chameleon," and the breadth of his output proves it.

His connection to Batman specifically goes back further than this game. Withenshaw served as a sound designer on LEGO Batman 2: DC Super Heroes in 2012, making Legacy of the Dark Knight a kind of homecoming — one where he was finally handed the full compositional reins for a standalone Batman title. The result, according to reviewers on Album of the Year, is his most ambitious work to date: highly orchestral, tightly woven through the game's narrative beats, and genuinely enjoyable to listen to outside of a gaming context.

The Danny Elfman Connection — Track 30

The biggest story in this OST isn't on the cover. Buried at track 30 — "Rough and Tumble" — is a co-composer credit for Danny Elfman. According to the official track notes on VGMdb, Elfman shares composer credit with Withenshaw specifically on that one track, with Matthew Arnold handling the mix for it separately from the rest of the album.

For anyone familiar with Batman's cinematic history, the significance of Elfman's name on a Batman game is hard to overstate. He composed the iconic themes for Tim Burton's Batman (1989) and Batman Returns (1992) — two of the most recognizable superhero scores ever written. Legacy of the Dark Knight draws heavily on Batman's film and television heritage, so incorporating Elfman-associated material into track 30 is both a deliberate creative choice and a meaningful nod to the character's musical legacy.

Listen for it: "Rough and Tumble" sits in the game's mid-chapter action sequences and carries a distinct brass-heavy texture that stands apart from Withenshaw's other tracks. The tonal shift is noticeable once you know to listen for it.

Tracklist Highlights: 39 Tracks, One Story

The full tracklist, available on Spotify, Amazon Music, and VGMdb, maps almost perfectly to the game's narrative arc. Location and character names run through it like a breadcrumb trail — "Crime Alley," "Nanda Parbat," "Ra's al Ghul," "ACE Chemicals," "Caught Red Hooded" — which means the OST functions as a light spoiler document for anyone who hasn't finished the story.

A few tracks worth isolating:

01

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Theme (2:52)

The main theme that plays over the title screen. Per the NOWPLAYING review, it's "really catchy" — a claim that holds up after repeated listens. Withenshaw opens with a fully orchestral statement that sets the tone for everything that follows.

12

Caught Red Hooded (2:54)

The confrontation music that plays around the Red Hood storyline. One of the darker, more brooding tracks on the album — the point where the score shifts from action-adventure into something closer to tragedy.

30

Rough and Tumble (3:00) — feat. Danny Elfman

The only track with an additional composer credit. Co-written with Danny Elfman and mixed separately by Matthew Arnold. The most distinct-sounding cue on the album.

31

A Serious House… (3:02)

An Arkham Asylum reference in both title and tone. The ellipsis in the name is a clue — "A Serious House on Serious Earth" is the subtitle of Grant Morrison's landmark Arkham Asylum graphic novel. Withenshaw clearly did his homework.

39

LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight End Credits (2:20)

A shorter reprise that bookends the main theme. Worth staying through the credits for.

Production Team Behind the Score

The album's credits on VGMdb reveal a substantial production apparatus behind Withenshaw's compositions. Producers include Ari Taitz, Jason Linn, Sandeep Sriram, and Simon Withenshaw himself, with Matthew Arnold producing track 30 alongside them. Creative direction was handled by Kate Lambert. Mastering was split between Justin Fleuriel and Stephen Marsh.

The scale of that team explains the polish. This isn't a solo effort or a game OST padded out with ambient loops — it's a 39-track orchestral album produced to full commercial release standards, available at $9.99 digital from WaterTower Music, the same label that distributes major Warner Bros. film scores.

Where to Listen

The LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Original Video Game Soundtrack by Simon Withenshaw is available now on all major platforms:

  • Spotify
  • Amazon Music
  • YouTube (Full Album)
  • iTunes / Apple Music

The full album runs 1 hour and 54 minutes — long enough to serve as a background score for an extended gaming session, or a solid companion listen while you grind collectibles in Gotham's open world.

Why This Score Matters

LEGO games are not typically known for their music. They're known for brick-smashing, stud-collecting, and co-op chaos — the audio is usually functional background dressing. Legacy of the Dark Knight changes that. Withenshaw's score is narrative-aware, emotionally calibrated, and composed to a level of craft that holds up outside the game entirely. The Danny Elfman co-credit on track 30 anchors it to Batman's deeper musical canon, and the tracklist's chapter-by-chapter structure means the OST rewards listeners who've completed the story.

If you've been sleeping on the soundtrack, the Spotify album is free. Give the main theme 30 seconds and see if you keep scrolling.
