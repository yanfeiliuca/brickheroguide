The Best Interactive Maps for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight — Every Collectible, Every Secret

Published: June 19, 2026 · 6 min read · Community

Gotham City in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is enormous. Four distinct islands spread across an open world that takes hours to fully traverse on foot, crammed with 121 Riddler Puzzles, over 160 WayneTech Caches, dozens of Batcave Trophies, Red Bricks, hidden costumes, and more. Trying to hunt these down without help is a recipe for missing half of them. Thankfully, the community has stepped up fast, and within weeks of launch, several outstanding interactive maps have appeared that pin every collectible across the entire game.

Whether you're chasing the platinum trophy, filling out your Batcave, or just trying to unlock a specific character ability upgrade, these maps are the fastest path from wherever you are to 100% completion. Here is a breakdown of the best ones available right now and what each covers.

What You Are Actually Looking For

Before diving into the maps themselves, it helps to understand what you are hunting. Legacy of the Dark Knight has several distinct collectible categories, and each rewards you differently. Here is a quick breakdown:

Collectible Type Total Count What It Unlocks
Riddler Puzzles 121 total (9 on Tricorner Island alone) Puzzle boxes that reward WayneTech Chips, costumes, and character unlocks when solved
WayneTech Caches 160 in the open world + 40 in story missions WayneTech Chips, which are spent at workbenches to upgrade gadgets and Batman’s abilities
Batcave Trophies 20+ per district (Sand Timers and other types) Decorative items placed in your Batcave; some unlock story content and Batcave expansions
Red Bricks Hidden across all four islands Gameplay modifiers: stud multipliers, invincibility, and cosmetic cheats
Costumes Scattered throughout open world New Batsuits and character skins, including some tied to DLC and pre-order bonuses
Pro tip: WayneTech Caches are the most directly useful collectible for gameplay. Prioritize finding these early so you can upgrade Batman’s gadgets before tackling later chapters on higher difficulties.

The Best Interactive Maps Right Now

All four maps below track real confirmed collectible locations, not estimates or guesses. The community has been thorough: within three weeks of launch, most major collectible types across all four islands had been documented and added to at least one of these resources.

GameRant Interactive Map

gamerant.com — Updated regularly

GameRant’s map is the most comprehensive currently available. It covers all four islands, with filters for Riddler Puzzles, WayneTech Caches, Batcave Trophies, Red Bricks, and costumes. Each pin is clickable and shows a brief description of how to reach or solve the collectible. The interface is clean and loads quickly, making it easy to use on a second screen while you play. For players systematically working through a single district at a time, this is the go-to resource.

Steam Community Guide — [INTERACTIVE MAP]

steamcommunity.com — Community maintained

The Steam community has produced a detailed pinned guide that currently focuses on Tricorner Island and South Island, with the remaining islands being updated as contributors document them. What sets this apart is the level of detail in the text descriptions: puzzle solutions are explained step-by-step rather than just pinned on a map, which is invaluable for Riddler Puzzles that require solving before the collectible appears. The map is filterable by collectible type and by district, so you can focus on exactly what you need without visual clutter.

Shacknews Interactive Map via Shackmaps

shacknews.com — Official partner map

Shacknews partnered with the Shackmaps team to produce an in-house interactive map that covers all major collectible categories across the full game. The Shackmaps format is particularly mobile-friendly, which matters if you are using your phone as a reference while playing on console. The map also includes coverage of points of interest, mission-gated collectibles (noting which require specific characters or chapter completion to access), and side activity locations such as Gotham crimes and district challenges.

Gamemappers.com

gamemappers.com — Dedicated mapping site

Gamemappers has built a clean, standalone map that covers the entire Gotham open world. Its main advantage is loading speed and interface simplicity — if you just want to find the nearest Red Brick or confirm the location of a trophy you spotted on a rooftop, this map gets you there faster than the larger aggregators. It is less detailed in individual collectible descriptions but excellent as a quick reference during gameplay sessions.

Understanding Gotham’s Four Islands

Legacy of the Dark Knight divides its open world into four distinct islands, each with its own visual identity and collectible density. Knowing which island you are on helps you navigate all of the above maps more efficiently, since most use island names as their primary filter categories.

Tricorner Island is where the majority of early-game story missions are concentrated, so it tends to be the island players document first — and as a result, the Steam community map coverage here is the most complete. South Island opens up as the story progresses and contains several story-gated collectibles that cannot be reached until certain chapters are unlocked. The two remaining islands cover Gotham’s outer districts and the areas surrounding iconic locations like Ace Chemicals, Shreck’s Department Store, and Arkham Asylum.

Note on story-gated collectibles: A number of Riddler Puzzles and Batcave Trophies in certain districts require completing specific chapters before they become accessible. All four maps above flag these where known, but if a collectible shown on a map is not appearing in your game, a chapter gate is the most likely explanation.

Mobalytics All Secrets & Collectibles Guide

Beyond the visual maps, the Mobalytics guide is worth bookmarking as a text-based companion. While not a clickable map, it organizes all secrets and collectibles by category with precise written location descriptions and puzzle solutions. For players who find it faster to read a description and navigate there manually rather than cross-referencing a map, it covers the full game in detail and is regularly updated as the community discovers new secrets.

You can find it at mobalytics.gg/news/guides/lego-batman-all-secrets-and-collectibles.

Tips for Using These Maps Efficiently

A few strategies that make hunting collectibles with these resources much faster than pure exploration:

Work district by district rather than island by island. Each map lets you filter by district and collectible type simultaneously. Clearing all Riddler Puzzles in one district before moving to the next prevents the confusion of backtracking and losing track of which areas you have already searched.

Use the Batcycle for open-world traversal once you have it. The Batcycle is the fastest overland vehicle and handles Gotham’s rooftop-level terrain well when combined with Batman’s grapple. Getting from a pinned map location to the actual collectible in the game world takes far less time once you are comfortable with how the two environments map onto each other.

Check whether a Riddler Puzzle requires a specific character ability before spending time on it. Several puzzles gate behind Batgirl’s hacking ability, Nightwing’s acrobatics, or Catwoman’s lock-picking. The GameRant and Shackmaps resources both flag these requirements in the pin descriptions, so you can skip those in a first pass and return in Free Play once you have the right character unlocked.

Free Play is your friend: Legacy of the Dark Knight allows you to replay any story mission in Free Play mode with any unlocked character. Collectibles that require specific abilities are much easier to approach in a second pass once your roster is larger — do not try to 100% each mission on the first playthrough.

The Community Behind the Maps

One of the most impressive things about Legacy of the Dark Knight’s launch has been the speed with which the community has organized around it. The game only released on May 22, 2026, yet multiple comprehensive maps were already substantially complete by early June. The official LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Discord has been central to this effort, with players pooling location data and coordinating to verify collectible positions before adding them to public resources.

For players who want to contribute to ongoing documentation — particularly for the mystery collectibles and hidden secrets that the community is still piecing together — the Discord is the place to start. The Steam community guide in particular accepts contributions from players who have found collectibles not yet documented, so early explorers can leave a tangible mark on the resource that will help every future player who attempts 100% completion.

As with any living community resource, these maps will become more complete over the coming weeks. The game’s more obscure secrets, including some Easter eggs noted by DC.com’s June 8 piece that have not yet been pinned, are still being tracked down. Check back on all of these resources regularly if you are working through the game at a slower pace.
