Chapter Overview

Chapter 1, The Red Hood Gang, is the opening story chapter of LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight. Set in the early days of Batman's crime-fighting career, the chapter follows Batman and Jim Gordon as they pursue the Red Hood Gang — a violent criminal organization tied to the origins of Gotham's emerging villain problem. The chapter takes place across Falcone-controlled Gotham territory, eventually ending with a confrontation that reveals deeper conspiracy threads running through the game's story.

This is the tutorial-adjacent chapter of the game. It introduces core mechanics including combo combat, Detective Vision, Jim Gordon's foam-based puzzle toolkit, and basic traversal. Don't rush it — collectibles here establish habits you'll need for the rest of the game, and the Red Brick unlocked in this chapter provides a stud bonus that pays off throughout your playthrough.

Story Mode vs Free Play: Some collectibles in Chapter 1 require characters you don't have access to in Story Mode. The 5 Ace Cards and 5 WayneTech Caches are all obtainable on a first pass, but the Red Brick requires returning in Free Play with Catwoman's lockpick ability. See the Red Brick section for details.

Characters & Abilities Needed

Chapter 1 gives you Batman and Jim Gordon as your playable pair. Their combined ability set covers almost everything in this chapter:

  • Batman — Batarang throws, grapple hook, glide, Detective Vision (click R3 / RS, or Middle Mouse Button on PC), melee combo
  • Jim Gordon — Foam Canister (disables speaker/electrical hazards), police credentials for certain barriers

The chapter's key puzzle mechanic is Gordon's Foam Canister, which is required to deactivate the amplified speaker arrays protecting key areas. When you see large speaker stacks with yellow electrical indicators, swap to Gordon before approaching.

Free Play requirement: The Red Brick is locked behind a Catwoman lockpick door. You cannot obtain it in Story Mode on your first pass. Return in Free Play after unlocking Catwoman to collect it. All Ace Cards and WayneTech Caches are reachable without any special character abilities.

Step-by-Step Walkthrough

The following covers the main story path through Chapter 1. Collectible callouts are embedded at the points where they appear. For a dedicated collectibles-only run, jump to the tables in the sections below.

1

Falcone's Ballroom Entrance. The chapter opens with Batman and Gordon infiltrating a Falcone-controlled venue. Fight through the initial Red Hood Gang members using basic combos — this is the game's combat tutorial. Stun enemies with Cape Swipe (Circle/B) and follow up with the combo string. Your first Ace Card is visible near the ballroom entrance on a decorated table — smash the nearby LEGO structure to reveal it.

2

The Ballpit Area. After clearing the ballroom, you move into a recreation area with a large ballpit. The environment is deliberately absurd — this is the game's LEGO humor at full volume. Fight through all enemies in the area. Look up and to the left of the ballpit for a grapple point — use Batman's Grapple Hook (hold Square/X near a white grapple indicator) to reach a raised ledge where the second WayneTech Cache is located.

3

Slide and Grapple Sequence. A slide section leads into the next area. As you descend, look for a grapple point on the right wall partway down — immediately grapple onto the ledge mid-slide. This grabs a hidden Ace Card placed on the ledge and is easy to miss on a first playthrough if you slide through without looking.

4

Enemy Wave Room. Defeat all enemies in the room beyond the slide. This is a larger combat encounter — use Glide Kick (jump then Square/X near an enemy) to open the combo string on the first enemy and cut through the wave quickly. One of the Red Hood Gang members drops a special red stud on defeat — this is not a collectible, just bonus currency.

5

The Speaker Room — Gordon Puzzle. This is the chapter's signature puzzle. A room with two large speaker arrays blocks your path — approaching them as Batman triggers an audio stun effect that prevents progress. Switch to Jim Gordon (D-pad or character wheel) and use his Foam Canister on both speakers to disable them. Each speaker takes one foam application. Once both are disabled, smash the LEGO musical equipment around the room to collect the build pieces.

6

Build the Drill. Use the collected LEGO pieces to assemble a massive drill using the build prompt (hold Square/X near the LEGO pile). The drill bores through several floors of the building. During the drilling sequence, button prompts appear on screen — hit each one on time to maintain drilling speed. Missing a prompt doesn't fail the mission but slows progress slightly.

7

Underground Vault Area. The drill deposits you in a lower section of the building containing Falcone's vault. This area contains two of the chapter's WayneTech Caches — activate Batman's Detective Vision to highlight them in orange. One is tucked behind a breakable wall section, the other is inside a small side room accessible via a low crawl gap (Batman automatically ducks through).

8

Red Hood One's Betrayal. A story cutscene triggers: Red Hood One — the gang's masked leader — turns on Falcone mid-confrontation. Falcone flees. This moment kicks off the chase sequence that closes out the chapter. The reveal is one of the story's early hooks and connects directly to the game's larger origin narrative.

9

Falcone Chase Sequence. The chapter's final act is a pursuit through Falcone's escape route. See the Boss section below for the full breakdown. Before entering the chase, check the room for the final Ace Cards — they are placed in the pre-chase staging area and are missable if you trigger the chase cutscene without exploring first.

All 5 Ace Card Locations

Ace Cards are Chapter 1's themed collectible (each chapter uses a different collectible type). Collect all 5 to earn the "Ace Up Your Sleeve" Batcave Trophy and contribute toward your overall collectible completion percentage.

#LocationHow to Get It
1Ballroom EntranceSmash the decorated LEGO table near the main doors. The card is inside the debris.
2Ballpit Room — Upper GalleryGrapple to the upper gallery overlooking the ballpit. The card is on the far right ledge.
3Slide Corridor — Mid-Section LedgeGrapple the wall ledge mid-slide. Standing on the ledge automatically picks up the card.
4Speaker Room — Behind Disabled SpeakerAfter using Gordon to foam both speakers, smash the left speaker stack completely. The card is behind the debris.
5Pre-Chase Staging AreaBefore triggering the Falcone chase cutscene, check the right-side wall alcove. The card is in a breakable LEGO crate.

All 5 WayneTech Cache Locations

WayneTech Caches appear in orange when Batman's Detective Vision is active (click R3 / RS, or Middle Mouse Button on PC — see our full Detective Mode guide for every platform's control). They contain upgrade components that feed into the Batcave's upgrade system. Detective Vision is essential — several caches are hidden inside breakable walls or above standard eye-level sightlines.

#LocationDetective Vision Tip
1Ballroom — Behind the Stage CurtainThe cache glows orange behind the stage curtain on the left side of the ballroom. Smash through the curtain props to access it.
2Ballpit Room — Raised Ledge (right side)Visible from the ballpit floor with Detective Vision. Grapple up to reach it.
3Post-Drill Underground Area — Breakable WallA cracked LEGO wall section in the underground vault area. Hit it with a Batarang to break it, then collect the cache inside.
4Underground Area — Low Crawl Side RoomFollow the low crawl passage off the main vault corridor. The cache is at the back of the side room.
5Chase Sequence — Mid-Point PlatformDuring the Falcone chase, a platform on the left side contains the final cache. Brief window to collect — use Detective Vision continuously through the chase section.
Detective Vision habit: Get into the habit of keeping Detective Vision active during every new area. Caches give a brief orange pulse even when not in Detective Vision mode — if you see an orange flicker in your peripheral, turn on Detective Vision immediately.

Red Brick Location — Free Play Required

Chapter 1 contains 1 Red Brick. It is located in the underground vault area, behind a Catwoman lockpick door — a golden padlock icon indicates a Catwoman-required interaction. You cannot access this in Story Mode.

To collect the Red Brick:

  1. Unlock Catwoman through the story (she becomes available mid-game) or purchase her from the Batcave character roster
  2. Return to Chapter 1 via Free Play (select the chapter from the Batcave mission board)
  3. In the underground vault area, locate the gold padlock door — it is on the wall opposite the main vault entrance
  4. Switch to Catwoman and interact with the door to pick the lock
  5. The Red Brick is inside the small room behind the door

The Chapter 1 Red Brick is a cosmetic unlock, like all 23 Red Bricks in Legacy of the Dark Knight — there is no Stud Multiplier mechanic in this game. See our Red Bricks Guide for what it unlocks, and our Stud Farming Guide for the fastest way to build up Studs.

Boss: Red Hood One & Falcone Chase

Chapter 1 closes with a multi-stage boss encounter rather than a conventional one-on-one fight. After Red Hood One's betrayal cutscene, Carmine Falcone attempts to flee through the building's lower levels with Red Hood Gang reinforcements protecting his escape route.

Phase 1 — Gang Reinforcements

A wave of Red Hood Gang members blocks the pursuit corridor. Use area-clearing combos rather than focusing on single targets — the Glide Kick into multi-enemy knockdown is most efficient here. Gordon can also contribute with his baton strikes; don't leave him idle during the wave phases.

Phase 2 — Falcone's Armored Vehicle

Falcone reaches an armored vehicle and the encounter transitions into a brief vehicular chase. Target the glowing orange weak points on the vehicle's rear with thrown Batarangs. Three hits disable the vehicle and trigger the chapter-ending cutscene. The vehicle's attack patterns are simple at this stage — dodge left or right when the exhaust vents flash red to avoid the blast.

Chapter End

The chapter concludes with a story cutscene and Batman returning to the Batcave. Any uncollected Ace Cards or WayneTech Caches from the chapter are now inaccessible until you return via Free Play. The Red Brick is always a Free Play item — no additional access is lost by completing the chapter.
