Catwoman in LEGO Batman Legacy: Complete Character Guide — Abilities, Puzzles & Story Role

Published: June 26, 2026 · 7 min read

Of the seven playable characters in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, Catwoman is the one most players underestimate — and then spend hours backtracking with once they realize how many paths only she can open. Her toolkit is the broadest of any supporting character in the roster, and understanding it early is the difference between a smooth 100% run and a frustrating second sweep of Gotham.

This guide covers everything: how and when she unlocks, each of her five core abilities, the specific puzzle types where she shines, her role in the story, and some practical tips for making the most of her time on-screen.

How to Unlock Catwoman

Catwoman is not available from the start. According to NoobFeed's unlock guide, she becomes a playable character during the story campaign — specifically through a chapter where her anti-hero allegiances put her temporarily in Batman's corner. You do not need to purchase her separately; story progression unlocks her automatically.

Once unlocked, she is available for free play in all previously completed chapters, which is when her puzzle-solving value really kicks in. Many WayneTech Caches and Ace Cards in early chapters are gated behind abilities she doesn't have during your first pass.

Tip: Don't stress about missed collectibles during your story run. Free Play mode with Catwoman is the intended method for mopping up most of Gotham's hidden items.

Catwoman's Five Core Abilities

According to GamesRadar's skills breakdown and Screen Rant's character rankings, Catwoman has five distinct abilities that set her apart from every other character in the roster.

🙌 Whip

Combat + Puzzle

Catwoman's signature move serves double duty. In combat, the Whip makes enemies spin and collide with each other — it's one of the most effective crowd-control tools in the game and can chain hits across multiple minifigures simultaneously. In puzzle contexts, the Whip can rotate LEGO screw bolts and grab highlighted LEGO bricks to pull structures apart, opening paths that ordinary attacks can't budge.

🐱 Call Kitty (Cat Control)

Exploration + Combat

This two-part ability is arguably Catwoman's most unique. Activating Call Kitty summons a small cat companion that can enter ventilation shafts and crawl spaces too small for any playable character. You guide the cat through these tight areas to flip switches, reach hidden items, or trigger environmental puzzles from the inside. In combat, the Cat Laser stuns enemies while the cat deals scratch damage — useful for isolating a single target while you deal with the rest of a group.

🏃 Wall Climbing

Traversal

Catwoman can scale walls that have rough or textured surfaces — the game visually signals these with a subtle shimmer. This gives her access to elevated platforms, rooftop shortcuts, and hidden ledges that other characters can only reach via grapple points or specific gadgets. In Free Play, wall-climbable surfaces frequently lead to Ace Card locations that would otherwise require a second character with a different traversal ability.

🔐 Safe Cracking

Collectibles

Scattered throughout Gotham are LEGO safes that only Catwoman can open. These typically contain high stud payouts, WayneTech Chips, or rare collectibles. No other base-game character shares this ability, which makes Catwoman mandatory for 100% completion. Safe locations are marked on the in-game map once you've been within range of them, so keep an eye out during your story run.

🔧 Glass Cutting

Puzzle

Certain windows and glass panels throughout Gotham are marked with a faint diamond pattern — only Catwoman's retractable claws can cut through them cleanly. Behind these panels you'll often find shortcuts between areas, hidden rooms, or collectible containers. It's a narrower ability than her others but essential in specific chapters.

Where Catwoman Matters Most

According to GameSpot's character overview, Catwoman has more unique puzzle interactions than any character besides Batman himself. Here are the game areas where she's indispensable:

Gotham City Open World

The open-world districts of Gotham are where Catwoman earns most of her reputation. She's needed for a significant portion of the side stories available in Free Play — particularly the heist-themed street missions scattered across the Diamond District and the East End. Her combination of safe cracking, vent access via the cat, and wall climbing means she can clear entire side quest chains solo that other characters would need multiple ability swaps to complete.

The Red Hood Gang Chapter

This early chapter — which doubles as an origin story for the Joker — has several vents and a glass-cutting puzzle that are skipped during your story run because Catwoman isn't available yet. Returning in Free Play with her reveals a hidden room behind a glass panel and a WayneTech Cache tucked inside a duct, according to walkthroughs on Game8 and Push Square.

Batcave Challenge Rooms

Several of the Batcave's optional challenge rooms have Catwoman-specific puzzles built around her cat companion navigating tight LEGO mazes. Completing these yields Red Bricks — permanent multipliers and quality-of-life modifiers that carry through your entire playthrough. These are optional but highly recommended if you're aiming for the Platinum trophy.

Catwoman's Role in the Story

Without going into spoilers, Catwoman occupies the classic anti-hero position in Legacy's narrative — she's pursuing her own goals in Gotham that happen to align with Batman's for a stretch of the campaign. The game handles her moral ambiguity well, giving her story moments that acknowledge her theft-focused lifestyle while letting players enjoy her as a protagonist rather than a nuisance.

Her arc intersects with both Jim Gordon (who views her with professional suspicion) and Nightwing (who has a more complicated read on her intentions). These interactions give Legacy's story some of its most entertaining dialogue exchanges — the writing team clearly enjoyed the opportunity to play Selina Kyle's persona against straight-laced cast members.

Character ranking note: Screen Rant's ranking of all seven playable characters places Catwoman second only to Batman in terms of overall utility and unique puzzle value, specifically citing her five-ability toolkit as unmatched in the base roster.

Practical Tips for Using Catwoman

A few things worth knowing before you start free-play sweeps with her:

  • Prioritize WayneTech upgrades for her Whip. The Whip's puzzle utility expands with upgrades — reaching further screw bolts and pulling heavier LEGO bricks. Invest in it early if you plan to use her extensively in Free Play.
  • Use Call Kitty before switching areas. When you enter a new room, deploy the cat first and scan for vent openings. Many players miss these because the vents are designed to be subtle and the game won't always prompt you.
  • Safes emit a faint clicking sound. The audio cue is consistent — if you hear a mechanical ticking in a room, there's likely a safe nearby. Worth learning early to avoid missing them during Free Play runs.
  • Glass-cuttable panels have a diamond etching. The visual marker is small but distinct. Look for a faint crosshatch pattern on transparent surfaces, usually at eye level on a wall or door.

Verdict: Is Catwoman Worth Investing In?

Absolutely. Catwoman is not a character you can skip if you're aiming for 100% completion — her safe cracking ability alone makes her mandatory. But she's also genuinely enjoyable to play outside of collectible hunts. Her combat style (Whip crowd control plus cat-based distractions) is distinctive enough to feel different from the rest of the roster, and her cat companion mechanic adds a puzzle layer that no other character has.

If you've been saving your WayneTech Chips without knowing where to invest, Catwoman's Whip and Cat Laser upgrades are among the highest-return purchases in the game. And if you haven't started your Free Play pass yet, start your Gotham sweep with her — you'll cover more ground per session than with almost any other character combination.
