LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight Speedrun Leaderboards Open June 7 — What the Community Has Built

Published: June 6, 2026 · 5 min read

Two weeks after LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight launched on May 22, 2026, the speedrunning community is ready. The official Speedrun.com leaderboards for the game have been in an embargo period since launch — a standard practice that gives moderators time to establish categories and rules before competitive submissions open. That embargo ends on June 7, 2026, meaning the race for the first official world record starts tomorrow.

The embargo system exists for good reason. Without it, early runs submitted the day after launch can lock in world records that later get invalidated because the category rules weren't finalized yet. For a game as content-rich as Legacy of the Dark Knight — with 18 individual missions, a large open-world Gotham hub, and multiple playable characters — establishing fair and consistent category rules takes meaningful community work. The moderator team has spent the past two weeks doing exactly that.

Who's Running the Community

Six moderators are overseeing the LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight community on Speedrun.com. Leading the team as super moderators are chimkin and gustative, with CaptainPaxo, colten8, kwazrr, and Siedemnastek serving as moderators. This is a strong and reasonably large moderation team for a newly launched game, which suggests the community has organized itself thoughtfully ahead of the leaderboards going live.

Moderators in speedrunning communities are responsible for verifying run submissions, enforcing category rules, and making judgment calls about edge cases — such as whether a particular glitch or skip is permitted in a given category. Their work in the embargo period has included watching pre-submission practice runs shared in the Discord, stress-testing routes, and agreeing on timing conventions (when a run starts and ends).

Join the community: The official speedrunning Discord for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight is available at discord.gg/zgtEyFvrmA. Community streams are also hosted on Twitch at twitch.tv/legospeedruns.

What Categories to Expect

While the full category list hasn't been officially published yet, the 18 individual level boards that are already set up give a strong indication of the structure. Most LEGO game speedrun communities organize their leaderboards around a few standard categories.

Any% (Full Game)

The marquee category. Players complete the game as fast as possible, allowing any glitches or skips discovered that don't violate stated rules. For a LEGO game with a hub world and mission structure, this typically means completing all required story missions in the minimum number of steps, optimizing cutscene skips, and finding route shortcuts through Gotham.

100% (Full Game)

All collectibles, all characters unlocked, all studs milestones achieved, all Batcave trophies collected. This is a significantly longer category — based on the 100% completion guide estimates, a casual completion runs 15–20 hours, so a competitive 100% run will likely clock in somewhere between 6–10 hours depending on how many skips runners discover.

Individual Level (IL) Runs

With 18 level boards already established, players can submit times for individual missions without needing to record a full-game run. This lowers the barrier to entry significantly — a runner can practice Mission 3 until they nail the optimal route and submit that time, rather than committing to an hours-long full-game attempt.

What Makes This Game Interesting for Speedrunners

LEGO games have a long speedrunning history, and Legacy of the Dark Knight brings several mechanics that the community has already identified as interesting from a routing perspective.

Character Abilities and Routing

The game features multiple playable characters — Batman, Robin, Batgirl, Nightwing, and others — each with distinct abilities. Some abilities that are optional for casual play (like Nightwing's wall-jump or Catwoman's lockpick) can open shortcuts or bypass puzzle sequences entirely. Routing decisions around which character to use and when will be a major optimization axis in competitive runs.

Stud Multiplier Skips

In LEGO games, acquiring the stud multiplier Red Bricks early can dramatically speed up collecting the studs needed to unlock certain doors or pay off costs. Runners will likely spend time early in a run hunting specific multipliers that pay dividends throughout the rest of the game — this is a routing consideration that separates optimized runs from beginner attempts.

The Open-World Hub

Legacy of the Dark Knight features an open Gotham City hub between missions, which introduces route decisions that don't exist in older LEGO games. Navigating the hub efficiently — using the Batcycle, Batwing, or foot movement optimally — will matter particularly in any% runs where every second of travel time counts.

Practicing before submission: If you want to try speedrunning before submitting an official time, focus first on learning cutscene skip timing and the fastest path through Gotham's hub. Both will save significant time in full-game runs and are consistent skills to develop regardless of which category you eventually compete in.

The Broader LEGO Speedrunning Context

LEGO games are a staple of the speedrunning community. Titles like LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga, LEGO Marvel Super Heroes, and the original LEGO Batman trilogy have active leaderboards with competitive scenes that have lasted years after release. Legacy of the Dark Knight's combination of an original story, a large character roster, and an open-world hub gives it the content depth to sustain long-term speedrunning interest rather than burning out quickly.

The game's Metacritic score of 84 — the highest in LEGO game history — also means the install base is larger than typical, which directly translates into a larger pool of potential runners. More players means more route discoveries, which means faster times and a more competitive scene.

The Speedrun.com page launched roughly 13 days ago and already has 68 followers without a single official run on the board. By comparison, many LEGO games take several months to reach that level of pre-launch community engagement on the platform. When leaderboards open tomorrow, the first wave of submissions will paint a picture of where current estimates sit — and likely spark an immediate competitive push to claim early world record status.

How to Submit a Run

When the leaderboards open on June 7, submitting a run requires a Speedrun.com account, a video of your run uploaded to a public platform (YouTube or Twitch VODs are standard), and adherence to the category rules posted on the game's page. Runs go through a moderation queue before appearing on the leaderboard — typically 24–72 hours for a newly active game.

For players who want to compete but haven't started yet, now is a good time to revisit your playthrough with routing in mind. Our Mission 1 Walkthrough and All Characters & Unlocks guide can help you understand the mechanics deeply enough to start thinking about optimization. Whether you end up submitting a competitive time or just chasing your personal best, the opening of the leaderboards marks a genuinely new chapter for the Legacy of the Dark Knight community.
