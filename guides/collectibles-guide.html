LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight features seven types of collectibles spread across 8 story missions and the open-world Gotham City. This guide explains what each collectible does, how many there are, and the most efficient order to hunt them down for 100% completion.

Minikits — The Main Collectible

Minikits are the iconic LEGO game collectible. Each of the 8 story missions contains 10 Minikits, for a total of 80. They appear as small, glowing LEGO model kits hidden throughout each level.

How to Find Minikits

Minikits emit a soft blue glow and a faint humming sound when you're nearby. In your first playthrough (Story Mode), you can typically find 3-5 per mission using just the characters available at that time. The remaining minikits require abilities from characters or suits you unlock later, making them accessible only in Free Play mode.

Minikit Rewards

Collecting all 10 minikits in a single mission rewards you with that mission's Gold Brick. Additionally, minikit collection milestones unlock cosmetic rewards in the Batcave Trophy Room:

  • 10 Minikits: Batcave Trophy Case (small)
  • 30 Minikits: Classic Batsuit Display
  • 50 Minikits: Joker Card Trophy
  • 80 Minikits (all): Golden Batsuit Display + final Gold Brick
Minikit Strategy: Don't stress about minikits on your first playthrough. Complete the story first to unlock all characters and suits, then use our All Minikit Locations guide in Free Play for efficient cleanup.

Red Bricks — Game-Changing Extras

Red Bricks are the most valuable collectibles in the game. Each one unlocks a powerful modifier (called an "Extra") that you can toggle on or off from the Batcave's Batcomputer. There are 20 Red Bricks total.

Critical Tip: Stud multipliers stack. With all five active, you earn 2 × 4 × 6 × 8 × 10 = 3,840x studs. Finding the five stud multiplier Red Bricks should be your top priority after completing the story. They make affording every upgrade trivial.

Instruction Pages — Vehicle & Base Upgrades

Instruction Pages are parchment-like collectibles found exclusively in the open world. Each page you collect unlocks either a new vehicle for the Batcave Vehicle Bay or a cosmetic upgrade for the Batcave itself. There are 40 total, split roughly 50/50 between vehicles and base cosmetics.

Unlike minikits, Instruction Pages are found through exploration and puzzle-solving in Gotham's open-world districts. Many are hidden behind breakable walls, on rooftops, or underwater. Batgirl's Oracle Drone and Commissioner Gordon's Evidence Scanner are especially helpful for tracking them down.

WayneTech Chips — Upgrade Currency

WayneTech Chips are the primary currency for upgrading Batman's suits and gadgets at the Batcave's Armor Workbench. They're earned through:

  • Collecting studs (every 10,000 studs = 1 WayneTech Chip)
  • Completing missions (bonus awarded at end screen based on studs collected)
  • Open world activities (Riddler Challenges, Bat-Signal Races)
  • Smashing special WayneTech crates (marked with a "W" logo)

Fully upgrading all six Batsuits costs approximately 120,000 WayneTech Chips. With all five stud multiplier Red Bricks active, you can farm this in about 30 minutes at the Steel Mill stud farming spot in the Industrial District.

Skill Bricks — Character Progression

Skill Bricks are character-specific collectibles that unlock nodes in each character's skill tree. Each of the 7 playable characters has a skill tree with roughly 7-8 nodes, and each node costs 1 Skill Brick to unlock. There are exactly 50 Skill Bricks — enough to fully unlock every character's tree.

Skill Bricks are earned through:

  • Completing story missions (guaranteed 2-3 per mission)
  • Riddler Challenges in the open world (20 total, 1 brick each)
  • Batcave Training Room combat challenges (10 total, 1 brick each)
  • Finding them as rare drops from elite enemies in Arkham Island

Character Tokens

Character Tokens are story-gated and unmissable. Each of the 7 playable characters (except Batman, who starts unlocked) has a Character Token that you automatically earn by completing the mission where that character joins the story. There's no way to miss these — finish the story, and you'll have all 7 characters unlocked.

100% Completion Roadmap

Here's the most efficient order to tackle 100% completion:

  1. Complete the 8-mission story on any difficulty. Don't stop for collectibles.
  2. Hunt the 5 stud multiplier Red Bricks first. These make everything else faster.
  3. Collect the Minikit Detector and Red Brick Detector Red Bricks next.
  4. Activate all 5 stud multipliers and farm WayneTech Chips at the Steel Mill for 30 minutes. Max out all suit upgrades.
  5. Replay all 8 missions in Free Play with detectors active. Collect all remaining minikits and red bricks mission by mission.
  6. Clear the open world: All Instruction Pages, Riddler Challenges, Bat-Signal Races, Vehicle Stunt Jumps, and Photo Spots.
  7. Max all skill trees with your remaining Skill Bricks.
  8. Buy all remaining Batcave upgrades.

Estimated time to 100%: 25-35 hours (15-20 hours if following our guides).
