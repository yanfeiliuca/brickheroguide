What Is the Mayhem Collection?

The Mayhem Collection is the first major paid DLC expansion for LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight, releasing on September 18, 2026 — confirmed by Warner Bros. Games on June 3, 2026. This is roughly four months after the game's May 22, 2026 launch, and launches simultaneously on Nintendo Switch 2 (which also launches that day), PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC. It adds a substantial chunk of villain-focused content that the base game deliberately left out: Joker and Harley Quinn as playable characters, a brand-new story chapter, an open-world villain sandbox mode, and a cosmetic Sinister Pack.

The DLC was announced alongside the Deluxe Edition at launch. Deluxe Edition and Season Pass holders receive the Mayhem Collection at no additional cost when it releases.

📅 Release date: September 18, 2026 — confirmed by Warner Bros. Games on June 3, 2026. Launches simultaneously on Nintendo Switch 2, PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC. Deluxe Edition / Season Pass holders receive it at no additional cost automatically.

New Playable Characters

The Mayhem Collection adds two fully playable characters — Joker and Harley Quinn — raising the roster from 7 to 9 characters. Both are unlocked by completing the new Arkham Breakout story mission.

🃏
The Joker
Unlocked: Arkham Breakout mission
  • Joy Buzzer Electric melee attack that stuns enemies
  • Acid Flower Melts gold LEGO objects (replaces laser)
  • Bang Gun Fires comedy flag for puzzle interactions
  • Gag Bomb Area distraction grenade (clears crowds)
  • Laugh Track Passive: nearby enemies occasionally freeze laughing
🔨
Harley Quinn
Unlocked: Arkham Breakout mission
  • Giant Mallet Heavy smash — breaks cracked LEGO objects
  • Acrobatics Triple jump + wall run (unique to Harley)
  • Party Popper Confetti blast crowd-control projectile
  • Hyena Call Summons pet hyena to dig hidden areas
  • Tumble Dash Somersault through tight crawlspaces

Both characters are immediately available in Free Play for all previous story missions once unlocked. Harley Quinn's acrobatics make her particularly useful for reaching hidden Minikits in earlier levels.

New Story Mission: Arkham Breakout

🏥 Arkham Breakout

The new story chapter takes place entirely within Arkham Asylum. You play as Joker and Harley Quinn — not Batman — as the duo engineer a mass breakout. The mission is designed as a villain perspective chapter, reversing the usual gameplay dynamic: enemies are Gotham's police and asylum security rather than costumed villains.

Key details confirmed:

  • Plays from start to finish as Joker/Harley co-op (no switching to hero characters)
  • Estimated 45–60 minutes for first playthrough
  • Has its own set of Minikits, Red Bricks, and collectibles separate from the main game
  • Completing it unlocks both characters for the main game's Free Play mode
  • Canonically set between Chapter 4 and Chapter 5 of the base game story

Mayhem Mode

💥 Mayhem Mode — Villain Open World Sandbox

Mayhem Mode is the biggest new addition. It repurposes the existing Gotham City open world as a villain playground. Playing as Joker, Harley Quinn, or the Sinister Pack villain suits, you cause chaos across Gotham's districts while police and Batman's allies respond to stop you.

How it works:

  • Access from the main menu or Bat-Cave (a "Villain Lair" hub replaces the Bat-Cave in this mode)
  • Chaos Meter rises as you destroy objects, defeat police, and complete Mayhem Challenges
  • Higher Chaos levels unlock faster police response waves — more enemies, more Studs
  • Mayhem Challenges are short objective bursts: "Destroy 10 vehicles in 60 seconds", "Reach Chaos Level 5", etc.
  • Studs earned in Mayhem Mode carry back into the main game economy
  • All Stud Multiplier Red Bricks from the base game apply here — excellent for late-game farming
💡 Farming tip: Mayhem Mode with all five Stud Multipliers active (×3,840 total) is reportedly the fastest Stud-per-minute method in the game — faster than any base-game Free Play mission.

🔍 Unconfirmed Extras — Datamine Findings

⚠️ Note: The core Mayhem Collection contents (Joker & Harley Quinn characters, Arkham Breakout story mission, Mayhem Mode, Sinister Pack) are all officially confirmed by WB Games (June 3, 2026 press release). The items below are additional elements found in datamines that have not been confirmed by TT Games or WB Games, and may or may not appear in the final September 18 release.

Shortly after launch, dataminers reportedly uncovered additional character and gameplay data in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight's game files — beyond what was announced in the official DLC press release. According to reports from Game Rant and Brick Fanatics, leaked files reportedly reference a 13-character Task Force X / Suicide Squad roster that may appear as enemies or supporting characters in Mayhem Mode:

  • Bronze Tiger, Captain Boomerang, Deadshot, Deathstroke
  • Javelin, Katana, Killer Croc, King Shark
  • Lester (possibly The Electrocutioner), Mongal, Polka Dot Man
  • Rick Flag, A.R.G.U.S. Cops

The leak also reportedly references a dedicated Mayhem Mode currency system, a "Mayhem Hideout" hub with a costume shop and challenge board, and "7 or 9" objectives or missions with bonus objectives for completionists. Harley Quinn's pet hyenas Bud and Lou are also mentioned in the files.

For a full breakdown of the datamined content, see our Mayhem Collection DLC leak analysis.

Is It Worth Buying?

The Mayhem Collection is the most substantial piece of DLC on the roadmap. If you bought the Standard Edition and enjoy the base game, the decision comes down to how much you value Joker/Harley Quinn as playable characters and whether Mayhem Mode's villain sandbox appeals to you.

From a pure value standpoint, the Deluxe Edition upgrade (if available at a discount) is almost always the better deal than waiting to buy the Mayhem Collection standalone — you also get the Legacy Collection suit packs and Party Music Pack as part of the Deluxe bundle.

If you primarily care about 100% completion, note that Mayhem Mode is expected to have its own collectibles. The base game platinum does not require any DLC content, and TT Games has confirmed no DLC trophies are required for the base platinum — so completionists can safely defer the Mayhem Collection purchase until its September 18, 2026 release — pre-orders are now live on PS Store, Xbox Marketplace, Steam, and Epic Games Store.

🏆 Platinum note: The base game's platinum is achievable without the Mayhem Collection — no DLC content is required for the base game's 52 PS5 trophies or 51 Xbox achievements. TT Games has not yet confirmed whether the Mayhem Collection will include its own separate trophy/achievement list (a common practice for LEGO game DLC), but based on precedent with LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga's Character Collection packs, a dedicated DLC list is plausible. Check the Trophy Guide for updates after the September 18, 2026 release.
